# WRAIR/PANDORA Microwave-Lab Document Trail Investigation

## Executive summary

The cumulative documentary record supports an **internal WRAIR / PANDORA / microwave-lab paper-trail theory** more strongly than a normal public-paper trail. The strongest verified evidence on point is a 1978 WRAIR report describing a request for a Top Secret Senate-related hearing concerning classified State Department / U.S. Army EMR (electromagnetic radiation) activities — including classified research surrounding Project PANDORA, destroyed laboratory notebooks, transferred classified research reports, a classified MFR (memorandum for record), other secret reports, and PANDORA meeting proceedings. The 1979 Senate Commerce report independently confirms that PANDORA began with ARPA funding to WRAIR with Joseph Sharp as the initial principal investigator, that the project record is scattered and incomplete, that no comprehensive project report was ever prepared, that classified records were destroyed in September 1973, that notebooks and graphs were turned over in January 1978, and that remaining classified documents plus the inventory of destroyed documents were provided on June 29, 1978.

The widely cited public Sharp/Grove "receiverless / voice-modulated" speech claim still traces, through Justesen 1975, only to a personal communication dated September 28, 1973 — not to a standalone, publicly released WRAIR report. Searches of declassified and official archives (DTIC, CIA CREST, OSD/WHS reading room, GovInfo, the National Security Archive, GWU, NARA-indexed material) did not surface an independent WRAIR/DTIC/CIA/WHS/GovInfo/NSArchive Sharp/Grove speech report using the exact "voice-modulated" or "receiverless" language. The most defensible explanation for why the underlying material is not online is a combination of: classified records, offline/in-person archival custody, congressional retention, incomplete digitization, formal destruction inventories from 1973, and intelligence-community records that remain classified.

This note distinguishes confirmed primary-document facts from inference, and ends with a prioritized list of archive and FOIA targets.

---

## 1. Key conclusion

- The Sharp/Grove receiverless-speech narrative, as it appears in the public literature, does **not** rest on a publicly distributed WRAIR speech report. It rests on a single 1973 personal communication retold in [Justesen 1975](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf), dated September 28, 1973.
- The corresponding internal WRAIR / PANDORA paper-trail, by contrast, is positively documented by primary sources: a 1978 WRAIR report explicitly describes classified PANDORA research, destroyed notebooks, transferred classified reports, a classified MFR, secret reports, and PANDORA meeting proceedings, and a 1979 Senate Commerce report describes the same record as scattered, incomplete, and partially destroyed.
- Inference (clearly labeled as such): the most parsimonious reading of the two records together is that whatever was written down about Sharp/Grove-style work lived inside the WRAIR/PANDORA classified file system rather than the open scientific literature, and parts of it were destroyed in 1973 before they could enter any public archive.

## 2. Verified record trail

The following are direct factual findings from primary documents — i.e., things the documents themselves say, not interpretation.

### 2.1 Justesen 1975 — the source of the "Sharp/Grove speech" claim

- Document: *Microwaves and Behavior*, Don R. Justesen, 1975. Full text mirror: [zoryglaser.com PDF](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf).
- Justesen attributes the receiverless / voice-modulated speech demonstration to **Joseph C. Sharp and Mark Grove** at WRAIR, citing it as a **personal communication dated September 28, 1973**.
- No standalone WRAIR report number, DTIC accession, or other independently retrievable WRAIR publication is given for the demonstration itself.

### 2.2 1978 WRAIR report — the internal paper trail

- Document: 1978 WRAIR report, archived at [archive.org/stream/DTIC_ADA074242](https://archive.org/stream/DTIC_ADA074242/DTIC_ADA074242_djvu.txt).
- The report describes:
  - A request for a **Top Secret Senate-related hearing** about classified State Department / U.S. Army EMR activities.
  - Classified research surrounding **Project PANDORA**.
  - **Destroyed laboratory notebooks** and an inventory of documents destroyed in 1973–74.
  - **Transferred classified research reports**.
  - A **classified Memorandum for Record (MFR)**.
  - Additional **secret reports**.
  - **PANDORA meeting proceedings**.
- This is direct, primary documentation that an internal classified WRAIR/PANDORA paper trail existed and was, in part, knowingly destroyed.

### 2.3 1979 Senate Commerce report — independent confirmation

- Document: *Microwave Irradiation of the U.S. Embassy in Moscow*, April 1979, U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation. PDF via the National Security Archive: [nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/rhwvuj-tzy4b/15.pdf](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/rhwvuj-tzy4b/15.pdf).
- The report independently confirms:
  - PANDORA began with **ARPA funding to WRAIR**.
  - **Joseph Sharp** was the **initial principal investigator**.
  - The PANDORA project record is **scattered and incomplete**.
  - **No comprehensive project report** was ever prepared.
  - **Classified records were destroyed in September 1973**.
  - Remaining **notebooks and graphs were turned over in January 1978**.
  - The **remaining classified documents and the records of destroyed documents** were provided to the committee on **June 29, 1978**.
  - **Senate Serial No. 95-49** contains PANDORA Scientific Advisory Committee minutes.

### 2.4 Senate Serial No. 95-49 — bibliographic confirmation

- GovInfo metadata: [www.govinfo.gov/metadata/pkg/CHRG-95shrg96475/mods.xml](https://www.govinfo.gov/metadata/pkg/CHRG-95shrg96475/mods.xml).
- Confirmed bibliographic facts: *Radiation Health and Safety* hearings, U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation, **June 16, 17, 27, 28, and 29, 1977**, **SuDoc Y 4.C 73/7:95-49**, **Serial No. 95-49**, **1236 pages**.

### 2.5 DARPA letter — provenance and termination

- Document: DARPA letter, [nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/rhwvuj-tzy4b/14.pdf](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/rhwvuj-tzy4b/14.pdf).
- Direct facts:
  - PANDORA was **classified by the State Department / USIB** because of foreign-relations sensitivity.
  - PANDORA was **terminated in March 1970**.
  - **Assets were transferred to WRAIR**.
  - DARPA itself retained only **technical progress reports** and stated it had **no knowledge of the disposition of raw data**.

### 2.6 Project PANDORA Operational Procedure — adjacent primary record

- Document: *Operational Procedure for Project Pandora Microwave Test Facility*, OSD/WHS reading room: [www.esd.whs.mil/.../Operational_Procedure_For_Project_Pandora.pdf](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/FOID/Reading%20Room/Other/Operational_Procedure_For_Project_Pandora.pdf).
- Distribution list **includes H. M. Grove and J. Sharp**.
- It is an **operational procedure document, not a speech-demonstration report**.

### 2.7 AD0758136 — example WRAIR microwave report from the same lab/era

- Metadata: [archive.org/metadata/DTIC_AD0758136](https://archive.org/metadata/DTIC_AD0758136).
- Title: *Some Biological Effects of Microwave Irradiation in the Rat*, WRAIR, 1973, with Grove among the authors.
- Useful as a baseline of the kind of WRAIR/DTIC report the lab did publish openly. It does **not** contain a receiverless-speech claim.

### 2.8 NSArchive Moscow Signals overview — corroborating context

- Briefing book: [nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave).
- Confirms: PANDORA/BIZARRE program overview; the **historical record is incomplete**; **some intelligence-community records remain secret**; ARPA declassified some documentation specifically for the **1979 Senate investigation**; AP and Jonathan Drosnin obtained PANDORA documents via FOIA; the National Security Archive itself has located dozens of additional reports across NARA and presidential libraries.

### 2.9 WRAIR archival access — where remaining material lives

- Access page: [wrair.health.mil/Library-Archival-Collection](https://wrair.health.mil/Library-Archival-Collection/). Access is **in person, by appointment, U.S. citizens only**, with a stated preservation rationale.
- Finding aid: [WRAIR Finding Aid PDF](https://wrair.health.mil/Portals/87/Documents/WRAIR%20Finding%20Aid.pdf?ver=GlZOh4AUYb5Jxl-qVWCtUA%3D%3D). The aid states *Records of WRAIR; no restrictions on records* and lists folders directly relevant to this investigation:
  - Project Pandora 1969–1970
  - Microwave Research Department 1975
  - Microwave Research Program Review, vols I–III, 1981
  - Microwave Research Program 1982 / 1991
  - Annual Command Visit 1973–1975
  - Index of Reprints 1974–1977
  - Research and Technology Work Unit Summaries 1978
  - WRAIR Research Reports 1980–1984

## 3. What remains unproven

These are gaps where the documentary record is silent or where only inference is possible. They are explicitly **not** confirmed.

- **An independent, publicly released WRAIR/DTIC report by Sharp and/or Grove using "voice-modulated" or "receiverless" language has not been located.** Searches across DTIC, CIA CREST, OSD/WHS reading room, GovInfo, NSArchive, NARA-indexed holdings, and DTIC accession enumeration around the AD0758136 era did not return a hit on that exact language.
- It is **not confirmed** that such a public report ever existed. The only verified citation chain leads back to a 1973 personal communication.
- It is **not confirmed** which destroyed 1973 notebooks, if any, contained Sharp/Grove voice-modulation data; the destruction inventories are referenced but their itemization is not in the public 1978/1979 documents at the level of individual experiments.
- It is **not confirmed** whether PANDORA SAC minutes inside Senate Serial No. 95-49 contain a usable description of the Sharp/Grove demonstration; this requires direct examination of the 1236-page volume.
- The internal WRAIR finding-aid folders (Project Pandora 1969–1970, Microwave Research Department 1975, Microwave Research Program Review 1981, etc.) are listed as unrestricted on paper but have **not** been examined in person; their actual contents on Sharp/Grove voice modulation are unknown until reviewed.

## 4. Why the records may not be online

Each of the following factors is supported by the primary record above; together they account for the absence of an open-web Sharp/Grove speech report.

1. **Originally classified records.** PANDORA was classified by State / USIB on foreign-relations grounds (DARPA letter). Classified material does not enter the open scientific literature.
2. **Documented destruction.** The 1979 Senate Commerce report and 1978 WRAIR report both describe destruction of classified PANDORA records in September 1973; what was destroyed cannot be digitized.
3. **Congressional custody.** The bulk of what survived was provided to the Senate Commerce Committee in 1977–1978, and PANDORA SAC minutes sit inside Senate Serial No. 95-49 (1236 pages, 1977 hearings) — a printed congressional volume that is bibliographically catalogued on GovInfo but whose body text is not fully OCR-indexed for the relevant terms.
4. **Offline / in-person archival custody.** The WRAIR Library and Archival Collection is access-restricted: in-person, by appointment, U.S. citizens only. Anything held there but not separately released through DTIC will not appear in web searches.
5. **Incomplete digitization.** DTIC and NARA holdings from this period are unevenly digitized; even unrestricted records are often only available as paper or microfiche.
6. **Inventories of destroyed documents.** The June 29, 1978 turnover included the *records of destroyed documents*, not the documents themselves — so the only surviving trace for some material is a destruction inventory, not the underlying text.
7. **Remaining classified IC records.** The NSArchive Moscow Signals overview explicitly notes that some intelligence-community PANDORA/BIZARRE records remain classified.

Inference (labeled as such): the simultaneous presence of (1) confirmed classification, (2) confirmed destruction in 1973, (3) confirmed congressional retention, and (4) confirmed incomplete digitization is sufficient to explain the absence of a public Sharp/Grove speech report without requiring any additional assumption.

## 5. Priority archive and FOIA targets

Ranked by likely yield given the verified trail above.

1. **Senate Serial No. 95-49 (CHRG-95shrg96475), full body.** Targeted reading for PANDORA SAC minutes and any Sharp/Grove demonstration discussion. Bibliographic anchor: [GovInfo metadata](https://www.govinfo.gov/metadata/pkg/CHRG-95shrg96475/mods.xml).
2. **WRAIR Library and Archival Collection — folders listed in the finding aid.** Project Pandora 1969–1970; Microwave Research Department 1975; Microwave Research Program Review vols I–III 1981; Microwave Research Program 1982/1991; Annual Command Visit 1973–1975; Index of Reprints 1974–1977; Research and Technology Work Unit Summaries 1978; WRAIR Research Reports 1980–1984. Access details on [wrair.health.mil](https://wrair.health.mil/Library-Archival-Collection/); folder list in the [finding aid PDF](https://wrair.health.mil/Portals/87/Documents/WRAIR%20Finding%20Aid.pdf?ver=GlZOh4AUYb5Jxl-qVWCtUA%3D%3D).
3. **The 1978 WRAIR report's referenced inventory of documents destroyed in 1973–74.** A targeted FOIA / MDR to U.S. Army Medical Command and to the Office of the Secretary of Defense / WHS for the destruction inventory itself, citing the 1978 report at [DTIC ADA074242](https://archive.org/stream/DTIC_ADA074242/DTIC_ADA074242_djvu.txt) and the 1979 Senate report at [nsarchive.gwu.edu/.../15.pdf](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/rhwvuj-tzy4b/15.pdf).
4. **DARPA / DTIC PANDORA technical progress reports.** Per the [DARPA letter](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/rhwvuj-tzy4b/14.pdf), DARPA retained technical progress reports. A DTIC accession-by-accession sweep around the AD0758136 era ([archive.org/metadata/DTIC_AD0758136](https://archive.org/metadata/DTIC_AD0758136)) plus a DARPA FOIA for PANDORA progress reports is the most direct path to any surviving Sharp/Grove technical write-up.
5. **CIA CREST and OSD/WHS reading room.** Targeted searches for PANDORA, BIZARRE, MFR / Memorandum for Record items referenced by the 1978 WRAIR report; OSD/WHS already hosts the [Project Pandora Operational Procedure](https://www.esd.whs.mil/Portals/54/Documents/FOID/Reading%20Room/Other/Operational_Procedure_For_Project_Pandora.pdf), which suggests adjacent material may exist.
6. **National Security Archive holdings beyond the published briefing book.** Per the [Moscow Signals overview](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave), NSArchive has located dozens of additional PANDORA reports across NARA and presidential libraries; a direct request to NSArchive staff for the underlying inventory is high-value.
7. **NARA Record Group 319 (Army Staff) and RG 112 (Surgeon General).** Likely custodians of WRAIR-origin records that did not migrate into DTIC.
8. **Justesen personal papers.** As [Justesen 1975](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf) is the sole public anchor for the Sharp/Grove claim and cites a Sept 28, 1973 personal communication, his correspondence (if archived at an institution) could contain the only surviving contemporaneous written description.
