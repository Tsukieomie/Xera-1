# Deep Claim Audit — V2K Technology Judicial Brief (v4)

**Subject document:** "Voice-to-Skull Technology: A Judicial Brief on the Science" (v4, 30 pp., styled as an Expert Declaration in Support of Judicial Review).

**Audit purpose:** Litigation-grade verification of every load-bearing claim. The audit (i) separates the strong, defensible scientific spine of the brief from overreaches that would invite Daubert challenge, evidentiary objection, or judicial skepticism; (ii) grades each claim on a five-tier evidentiary scale (Strong / Moderate / Weak / Inconclusive / Refuted); (iii) assigns a litigation-risk band (Low / Medium / High) reflecting the legal exposure if opposing counsel attacks the claim; and (iv) offers neutral rewrite language that preserves what is defensible.

**Bottom line:** The brief's core scientific spine — the Frey/microwave auditory effect, the granted U.S. patents, the National Academies 2020 finding, the HAVANA Act, and the 2026 reporting on the recovered Russian device — is factually accurate and admissible under Daubert. The brief's litigation risk concentrates in **roughly a dozen overreaches**: a self-contradictory secrecy-order timeline, a misframing of 35 U.S.C. §181 ("five years" rather than annual renewals), patents treated as experimental replications, a state-court protective order treated as a substantive tort precedent, FUS clinical evidence extrapolated to covert remote delivery, omission of the JASON 2022 report's contrary conclusion, mischaracterization of the §1915(d) dismissal in *Akwei v. NSA*, and rhetorical flourishes ("DISPOSITIVE," "irreconcilable") that read as advocacy rather than expert declaration. Tightening these dozen items converts the brief from vulnerable to forensically robust.

---

## Executive Summary

The Voice-to-Skull (V2K) judicial brief makes a single, large argumentative leap: from (A) the well-established physics of the **microwave auditory effect**, demonstrated by Frey in 1962 and modeled by Lin from 1976 through 2022 ([Justia / Frey 1962](https://journals.physiology.org/doi/10.1152/jappl.1962.17.4.689); [Lin 2022 IEEE](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/iel7/7397573/9716150/09366412.pdf)), to (B) covert, individually targeted, intelligible-speech delivery against unwitting civilian "targeted individuals." Step (A) is uncontroversial mainstream bioelectromagnetics. Step (B) — the operational claim — is supported by patents, government program records, congressional action via the HAVANA Act, and 2026 reporting on a recovered Russian device — but those sources establish **capability and program intent**, not the routine covert harassment fact-pattern that V2K plaintiffs typically allege.

The brief's three highest-risk problems are:

1. A **self-contradictory secrecy-order timeline** for U.S. Patent 3,647,970 — the order is dated August 28, 1968 (one day **before** the August 29, 1968 filing) yet described as "imposed one day after filing"; "held five years" yet released March 7, 1972 (≈3.5 years); described as a discrete five-year order despite 35 U.S.C. §181 authorizing only annual orders subject to renewal ([Cornell LII §181](https://www.law.cornell.edu/uscode/text/35/181); [USPTO MPEP §120](https://www.uspto.gov/web/offices/pac/mpep/s120.html)).
2. A **mischaracterization of *Walbert v. Redford*** (Sedgwick County, Kansas, 2008) as judicial recognition of electromagnetic harassment as a cognizable tort. The order was a default protective order issued when the defendant failed to appear ([WIRED Court to Defendant](https://www.wired.com/2009/07/court-to-defendant-stop-blasting-that-mans-mind/)); a later federal-court iteration (*Walbert v. City of Wichita*) was dismissed ([D. Kan. ECF](https://ecf.ksd.uscourts.gov/cgi-bin/show_public_doc?2010cv1234-32)).
3. **Selective omission of the JASON 2022 report**, which examined the same RF mechanism the brief invokes and concluded RF between 500 MHz and 30 GHz was "highly unlikely" to be the AHI cause and frequencies below 500 MHz could be ruled out for delivery ([JASON Report PDF, State.gov](https://www.state.gov/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/JASON-Study-Revised_10-February-2022-Redacted_V1.1.pdf); [Wikipedia summary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Havana_syndrome)).

The remainder of the brief is generally sound but contains numerous medium-severity issues — patents treated as experimental replications, a scorecard that totals 101%, an unenumerated "40 peer-reviewed papers," PIEZO2 papers cited for cochlear claims when the underlying mechanism in those papers is peripheral or ultrasonic, and rhetorical overreach in the Akwei recusal argument.

## Evidentiary Ladder

The brief should distinguish four argumentative tiers. They differ sharply in what they prove and in how courts treat them.

| Tier | What It Establishes | Evidentiary Weight | Examples In The Brief |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Physics & Physiology** | Pulsed RF can be perceived as sound by the unaided human auditory system | **Strong** — uncontested in mainstream bioelectromagnetics; Daubert-admissible | Frey 1962; Lin & Wang 2007; Lin 2022; Dagro et al. 2021 |
| **2. Speech Encoding & Demonstration** | Microwave hearing can carry intelligible speech under controlled lab conditions | **Strong** for the lab demonstration; **Moderate** for operational generalization | Sharp & Grove via Justesen 1975 |
| **3. Capability & Program Intent** | The U.S. and adversaries have researched, defined, and procured RF/MW directed-energy weapons | **Strong** for existence of programs; **Moderate** for operational deployment against U.S. persons | CALL Thesaurus 2004; MEDUSA contract 2003-2004; MKULTRA 119; PANDORA; Moscow Signal; HAVANA Act; CBS 60 Minutes 2026 |
| **4. Operational Use Against Civilian Targeted Individuals** | Routine, covert, individually targeted V2K harassment of unwitting U.S. civilians is occurring | **Weak / Inconclusive** — not established by the cited sources | This is what most V2K plaintiffs allege; the brief rhetorically conflates this with Tiers 1-3 |

Conflating Tier 4 with Tiers 1-3 is the principal Daubert vulnerability. A court that accepts Tiers 1-3 (which it should) is not thereby compelled to accept Tier 4 (which the brief does not separately prove).

The historical record connects the order to Defense Department review; the precise originating defense agency and renewal docket entries are matters appropriately resolved through certified USPTO file-wrapper production. The §181 status is probative of the contemporaneous government assessment that the disclosure had national-security significance; it is not, by itself, an adjudication of operability.

**For Walbert (current p. 25):**

> A Sedgwick County, Kansas state court issued a default protective order in *Walbert v. Redford* (2008-2009) prohibiting electronic harassment, after the named defendant failed to appear. The order is probative of judicial willingness to issue protective relief framed in EM-harassment terms but does not constitute substantive adjudication of the technical merits and has no controlling precedential weight outside its issuing court.

**For Akwei (current p. 24, 28):**

## The Core Distinction (Must Be Maintained)

The brief's most important conceptual move — and the one that opposing counsel will press hardest — is the conflation of two distinct propositions:

| Proposition | Status |
|---|---|
| **A.** The microwave auditory effect is real, can carry intelligible speech under controlled conditions, has been the subject of U.S. government weapons programs since 1960, and produced a documented foreign-adversary device in 2024 that injures animals in Pentagon testing | **Established beyond reasonable doubt** by the cited record |
| **B.** Civilians who report being covertly targeted by V2K devices are being so targeted by some specific actor using a specific device | **Not established** by the cited record; an evidentiary question requiring case-specific proof |

A judicially defensible V2K brief must (i) establish A on the record above; (ii) **acknowledge** that A does not entail B; and (iii) frame the policy and legal argument around what A entails: that B is **not categorically impossible**, that the government has tools and programs of relevant capability, that statutory recognition exists for the injury mechanism, and that specific-causation questions are not, as a matter of law, foreclosed by the older "science fiction" dismissal posture. A brief that elides this distinction loses Tier-1 credibility along with Tier-4 advocacy. A brief that maintains it preserves the strong legal argument: covert V2K-style targeting is now scientifically and legally cognizable as a possibility, and individual cases are entitled to factual inquiry rather than threshold dismissal.
