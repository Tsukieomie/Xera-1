# V5 Revision Plan — V2K Technology Judicial Brief

**Source:** v4 PDF (`docs/judicial-brief/source/V2K_Technology_Judicial_Brief_v4.pdf`)
**Audit:** `docs/judicial-brief/audit/v4_deep_claim_audit.md` (deep claim audit, 2026-05-05)
**Annotated review:** `docs/judicial-brief/audit/V2K_Technology_Judicial_Brief_v4_reviewed.pdf`
**Goal:** Convert v4 from HIGH aggregate-litigation-risk into MEDIUM-risk by fixing the
items rated CRITICAL (RED) or HIGH (ORANGE) in the audit, while preserving the
defensible scientific spine (Frey, Sharp & Grove, CALL Thesaurus, MEDUSA, NAS 2020,
HAVANA Act, FUS clinical record, AFSA v. State, 60 Minutes 2026).

This plan is intentionally narrow: it changes only what the audit flags. It does
not invent citations, does not soften the strong spine, and does not introduce
new factual claims. All replacement language uses neutral, expert-declaration tone
consistent with FRCP 26(a)(2) / 28 U.S.C. §1746.

## Workflow once an editable v4 source is recovered

1. Open the editable v4 source side-by-side with `V5_REDLINE_PATCHLIST.md`.
2. Apply each patch in the order listed (Priority 1 → 2 → 3 → 4). Each patch
   identifies the exact string to replace and the replacement text.
3. Update the cover page (delete "Expert Declaration in Support of Judicial
   Review" if no §1746 declarant), and the recurring footer banner per §FT-A.
4. Re-tabulate the Section XII scorecard with raw counts only and reconciled
   grades; verify that the body text matches the grade for each row.
5. Append `V5_EXHIBIT_LIST.md` as the final page.
6. Re-export to PDF; bump version to v5; archive the v4 PDF as a "preceding
   version" exhibit if useful.

## Constraints carried from the parent task brief

- **No invented citations.** Every replacement clause cites only sources
  already used in v4 or already cited in the deep audit (Cornell LII §181,
  USPTO MPEP §120, JASON 2022 State.gov PDF, Wired 2009 Walbert coverage,
  D. Kan. ECF, Cornell LII §455, *Neitzke v. Williams* 490 U.S. 319 (1989),
  CBS 60 Minutes 2026, Washington Examiner March 2026).
- **Preserve legal usefulness.** The Frey, Sharp & Grove, NAS 2020, HAVANA
  Act, FUS clinical record, AFSA v. State, and 60 Minutes 2026 anchors are
  unchanged in substance; only their framing is tightened.
- **Neutral expert-declaration language.** No "DISPOSITIVE", "irreconcilable",
  "defunct", "fully operational" rhetoric. State the record, mark the
  evidentiary tier, let the court draw conclusions.

P2 (HIGH) patches takes aggregate litigation risk from **HIGH** to **MEDIUM**.
