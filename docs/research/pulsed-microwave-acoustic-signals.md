# Pulsed Microwave Acoustic Signals and the Microwave Auditory Effect

## 1. Executive Summary

- The historical "Grove speech" thread is actually **Joseph C. Sharp and H. M. Grove** at Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, not a researcher named "Grove" alone.
- The strongest public account of speech transmission is **Don Justesen's 1975 article** ([Justesen 1975](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf)), which states Sharp and Grove recorded spoken digits, used the negative zero-crossings of the speech waveform to trigger microwave pulses, and reported that listeners could hear and distinguish 9 spoken words. Importantly, this account appears as a **personal communication** rather than a standalone peer-reviewed speech paper, which limits its evidentiary weight.
- The hard scientific core of the phenomenon is **not "mind control"**; it is pulsed microwave absorption producing **thermoelastic acoustic pressure waves** in absorbers and tissue. This mechanism explains the microwave auditory effect (clicks, buzzes, chirps).
- The strongest validated technological descendants are **thermoacoustic imaging, microscopy, materials sensing/NDE, and ablation monitoring** — not covert remote speech delivery.

## 2. Mechanism Spine

- **Frey 1962** — Modulated electromagnetic energy induced sound perception in normal and deaf humans. The effect could be induced several hundred feet from the antenna. Peak power density was approximately **275 mW/cm²** at 425 MHz and 1,310 MHz under about 80 dB acoustic noise; average power density was at least as low as **400 μW/cm²**. Source: [Frey, "Human auditory system response to modulated electromagnetic energy," J. Appl. Physiol. 1962](https://journals.physiology.org/doi/abs/10.1152/jappl.1962.17.4.689).
- **Sharp, Grove, Gandhi 1974** — "Generation of Acoustic Signals by Pulsed Microwave Energy." Used 14 μs pulses, roughly 3 pulses/s, about 4500 W/pulse, at frequencies of 1200, 1500, and 2450 MHz, with a carbon-impregnated polyurethane absorber. Acoustic timing delays matched sound propagation speed, ruling out RF artifact. Threshold audibility under their conditions was about **275 W peak**. Source: [Sharp, Grove & Gandhi 1974 (PDF)](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/GENERATION-OF-ACOUSTIC-SIGNALS-BY-PULSED-MICROWAVE-ENERGY.pdf).
- **Justesen 1975** — "Microwaves and Behavior." Reported the Guy Morse-code demonstration and the Sharp/Grove speech-word demonstration. Noted that the **short rise time and first derivative of the waveform** are critical to the effect. Source: [Justesen 1975 (PDF)](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf).
- **Lin 2022 review** — Absorption of a microwave pulse launches a pressure wave that travels by **bone conduction to the inner ear** and activates cochlear receptors. Source: [Lin 2022, IJERM](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2022IJERM...6...16L/abstract).
- **Foster, Garrett & Ziskin 2021** — The microwave auditory effect is thermoacoustic, but **weaponization is unlikely** due to weak effect size, source practicality, and lack of public evidence supporting a Havana-syndrome–style deployment. Source: [Foster, Garrett & Ziskin 2021, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/).

## 7. Evidence Grading

| Tier | What it covers | Examples |
|------|----------------|----------|
| **Solid** | Pulsed microwaves generate acoustic waves in absorbers/tissue; microwave auditory clicks/buzzes; thermoacoustic imaging and microscopy. | Frey 1962; Sharp, Grove & Gandhi 1974; Lin 2022; US6567688B1; US7266407B2; CN102058416B; CN114587326B |
| **Plausible but thin** | Intelligible word perception via Sharp/Grove personal communication; Air Force speech patents that describe a chain but lack public reduction-to-practice. | Justesen 1975 (personal communication); US6470214B1; US6587729B2; US4877027A |
| **Speculative / weak** | MEDUSA-style incapacitation/crowd-control claims; Russian trespasser pain-resonance patent asserted without experimental evidence. | RU2526478C2; popular-press MEDUSA coverage |
| **Not the same mechanism** | Electrode/transdermal RF hearing patents, auditory cortex mode patents, conventional ultrasound/laser photoacoustic systems, ADS skin-heating pain systems. | Active Denial System (ADS); transdermal/electrode "RF hearing"; laser-induced photoacoustics |

## 8. Bottom Line

- The **acoustic-signal physics is well supported**: pulsed microwave absorption produces thermoelastic pressure waves, which is the established explanation for the microwave auditory effect.
- The **speech branch exists and is historically important**, but the public evidence for reliable, practical remote-speech transmission is **thin** — it largely rests on a single personal communication relayed by Justesen and on patent disclosures that have not been publicly reduced to practice.
- The **real technical descendants** of this line of work are **biomedical thermoacoustic imaging, microscopy, ablation monitoring, and materials NDE**, not covert remote-speech weapons.

## 9. Addendum (2026-05-01) — Deeper Dig: Foundational Replications, Patent Clusters, and Modern Derivatives

This addendum extends the note with (a) the foundational psychophysics and animal/material replications that anchor the thermoelastic mechanism, (b) a fully classified patent landscape across direct-hearing and thermoacoustic-imaging clusters, (c) the thermoacoustic tomography (TAT) literature bridge, (d) modern derivative clusters (ablation monitoring, transcranial imaging, deep-learning reconstruction, contrast agents, hardware), and (e) a refined exclusion guide.

### 9.1 Foundational Mechanism Anchors and Experimental Replications

These references close several gaps left by the Frey 1962 / Sharp-Grove-Gandhi 1974 / Lin 2022 chain in §2. They establish the parameter dependence and the mammalian-system case for the thermoelastic mechanism.

- **Frey & Messenger 1973** — "Human perception of illumination with pulsed ultrahigh-frequency electromagnetic energy." Psychophysical study of sound induced by pulse-modulated UHF energy. Found perception **primarily depended on peak power, secondarily on pulse width**, and that **average power did not significantly affect perception**; pitch and timbre were functions of modulation. Source: [Frey & Messenger 1973, PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4719908/). This pins down the parameter-space of the effect and is consistent with a thermoelastic, not thermal-bulk, origin.
- **Foster & Finch 1974** — "Microwave hearing: evidence for thermoacoustic auditory stimulation by pulsed microwaves." Demonstrated that **acoustic transients are thermally generated in water by pulsed microwave energy**, with peak pressure in the audible band and a pulse-parameter dependence sufficient to explain clicks heard by people exposed to microwave radiation. Source: [Foster & Finch 1974, PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4833827/). This is the classic in-vitro physical replication of the mechanism.
- **Guy, Chou, Lin, Christensen 1975** — "Microwave-induced acoustic effects in mammalian auditory systems and physical materials." Mammalian/material acoustic effects under pulsed microwave exposure. Source: [Guy et al. 1975, NYAS Annals](https://nyaspubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/j.1749-6632.1975.tb35996.x) (DOI 10.1111/j.1749-6632.1975.tb35996.x).
- **Chou, Guy, Lin, Christensen 1975** — Cochlear microphonics evidence consistent with cochlear, not central, origin of the perceived sound. DOI 10.1080/00222739.1975.11688973.
- **Chou & Guy 1979** — "Microwave-induced auditory responses in guinea pigs: Relationship of threshold and microwave-pulse duration." Establishes the threshold–pulse-duration relation in an animal model. DOI 10.1029/RS014I06SP00193.
- **Chou, Guy, Galambos 1982** — "Auditory perception of radio-frequency electromagnetic fields." Animal auditory perception/physiology evidence. DOI 10.1121/1.387852.
- **Lin & Wang 2007** — "Hearing of microwave pulses by humans and animals: effects, mechanism, and thresholds." Review consolidating the accepted mechanism: a **thermoelastic acoustic pressure wave launched by absorption in soft tissue and conducted by bone to the inner ear**, not direct nerve or CNS stimulation. Source: [Lin & Wang 2007, PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17495664/).
- **Foster, Garrett & Ziskin 2021** — "Can the Microwave Auditory Effect Be 'Weaponized?'" Modern safety/feasibility skeptical assessment. Useful as a recent ceiling on public claims, **not as a technology proof**. Source: [Foster, Garrett & Ziskin 2021, Frontiers in Public Health](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpubh.2021.788613/full) (DOI 10.3389/fpubh.2021.788613). Note this is the same paper cached at PMC and cited above in §2; the Frontiers DOI link is included here for completeness.

### 9.2 Patent Clusters — Classified

#### 9.2A Direct RF-hearing / audio-to-pulsed-microwave / RF-acoustic claims

These patents describe transmitter chains intended to produce perceived sound or speech in a target via the RF hearing effect. Patent disclosures are not the same as public reduction-to-practice; treat as engineering claims, not validated demonstrations.

- **US4877027A — Brunkan, "Hearing system"** (expired). Carrier 100 MHz–10 GHz, preferred 1 GHz; pulse width 10 ns–1 μs; bursts 500 ns–100 μs; 10–20 pulses/burst; burst repetition roughly 1–100 kHz; FM burst density modulated by audio envelope; claims intelligible sound perceived in head. Source: [US4877027A](https://patents.google.com/patent/US4877027A/en).
- **DE10222439A1 — Leyser, microwave hearing device** (withdrawn). Brunkan-like topology: preferred 1000 MHz carrier, 10 ns–1 μs pulse width (100 ns preferred), 1–25 / up to ~100 kHz burst repetition; direct RF-hearing/audio-to-pulse-microwave claim. Source: [DE10222439A1](https://patents.google.com/patent/DE10222439A1/en).
- **US6470214B1 — U.S. Department of the Air Force** (expired), inventors James P. O'Loughlin and Diana L. Loree, "Method and device for implementing the radio frequency hearing effect." Fully suppressed-carrier AM / SSB / CSAM, **audio predistortion with 40 dB/decade high-frequency de-emphasis plus square-root processing** to compensate the thermal-acoustic demodulation transfer function; claims intelligible speech via the RF hearing effect. Source: [US6470214B1](https://patents.google.com/patent/US6470214B1/en).
- **US6587729B2 — U.S. Air Force, child/divisional of US6470214B1**, "Apparatus for audibly communicating speech using the radio frequency hearing effect." Same Air Force family; apparatus claims rather than method claims. Source: [US6587729B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US6587729B2/en).
- **RU2526478C2 — "Method and device of microwave electromagnetic impact at trespasser"**. Radio-sound / non-lethal deterrent claim. Patent describes amplitude-modulated microwave field at resonant frequencies of head/hearing organs, cites "radio sound," modulation range 6–12 kHz in description, claims painful thermoelastic auditory-apparatus effects. **Legal status fluctuates** (inactive / reinstatement) and the source base is limited; not verified implementation. Source: [RU2526478C2](https://patents.google.com/patent/RU2526478C2/en).
- **US4858612A — "Hearing device"**. Caveat: this patent describes **direct microwave auditory cortex mode-stimulation**, with a microphone/filter bank driving multiple microwave frequencies over the auditory cortex. This is **not the same consensus thermoelastic cochlear mechanism** described in §2 and §9.1; it is a distinct, more speculative theory of operation. Source: [US4858612A](https://patents.google.com/patent/US4858612A/en).
- **US3751605A — "Method for inducing hearing"**. Adjacent / lower relevance: a **cochlear electrode / RF inductive implant system**, not the microwave hearing effect. Useful because later RU/Soviet-tradition patents cite it, but it is not core to pulsed-microwave thermoacoustics. Source: [US3751605A](https://patents.google.com/patent/US3751605A/en).
- **US3586791A** and **US3629521A** — Transdermal RF / facial-nerve hearing systems. Adjacent only; not the pulsed microwave thermoelastic auditory effect. Listed for completeness; do not weight as evidence for the core mechanism.

#### 9.2B Thermoacoustic / photoacoustic medical imaging patents

These patents are the **validated technological line** descending from the same physics: pulsed microwave absorption in tissue produces thermoelastic acoustic waves, which an ultrasonic array reconstructs into an image. They are the strongest evidence that the underlying physics is real, controllable, and engineerable.

- **US5713356A — Optosonics, "Photoacoustic breast scanner"** (expired). EM pulses including microwave 100–1000 MHz (433 MHz and 915 MHz examples), 10 ns–1 μs pulses, acoustic transducer arrays, filtered/time-derivative backprojection, breast cancer imaging. Source: [US5713356A](https://patents.google.com/patent/US5713356A/en).
- **US6104942A — "Thermoacoustic tissue scanner"**. Earlier scanner family: modulated EM radiation produces acoustic signals detected with an acoustic sensor at a focal point, used for imaging or for measuring absorption spectra. Source: [US6104942A](https://patents.google.com/patent/US6104942A/en).
- **WO2000045707A2 / US6216025B1 — Optosonics/Kruger, "Improved thermoacoustic computed tomography scanner" / "Thermoacoustic computed tomography scanner"**. Engineering parameters: ~25 kW peak RF pulse, 0.5 μs pulse, 100 ns rise, effective 200–600 MHz band, 434 MHz and 915 MHz examples, 64 transducers in an imaging bowl, acoustic shielding/noise cancellation, dielectric/acoustic coupling, breast imaging. Sources: [WO2000045707A2](https://patents.google.com/patent/WO2000045707A2/en), [US6216025B1](https://patents.google.com/patent/US6216025B1/en).
- **US6567688B1** — Wang/Ku lineage, microwave-induced thermoacoustic system for imaging biological tissue, 300 MHz–3 GHz short pulses, ultrasonic detection, real-time/multidimensional imaging. (Also referenced in §5.) Source: [US6567688B1](https://patents.google.com/patent/US6567688B1/en).
- **US20050107692A1 / US7266407B2 — University of Florida Research Foundation (Jian Li / Gang Wang), "Multi-frequency microwave-induced thermoacoustic imaging of biological tissue"**. Swept microwave frequency pulses, ultrawideband / swept frequencies roughly 1–10 GHz, adaptive beamforming, breast imaging, skin-effect/clutter compensation. Sources: [US20050107692A1](https://patents.google.com/patent/US20050107692A1/en), [US7266407B2](https://patents.google.com/patent/US7266407).
- **CN101822549B — "Excitation and three-dimensional sensing integrated device for diagnosis of breast cancer or craniocerebral injury"**. 1.2 GHz pulsed microwave through waveguide/horn into breast or cranium/brain with coupling liquid and arc ultrasonic arrays; both breast cancer and craniocerebral injury diagnosis. Source: [CN101822549B](https://patents.google.com/patent/CN101822549B/en).

### 9.3 Thermoacoustic Tomography Literature Bridge

The patent cluster in §9.2B was reduced to practice and benchmarked in the open literature. These papers are the strongest single line of evidence that the underlying physics is real, reproducible, and clinically usable.

- **Kruger et al. 2000** — "Breast cancer in vivo: contrast enhancement with thermoacoustic CT at 434 MHz — feasibility study." Five breast-cancer patients, 434 MHz; tumor-region contrast seen in pre-chemotherapy patients and absent in patients in complete remission. Source: [Kruger et al. 2000, PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10887262/).
- **Kruger 1999** — "Thermoacoustic computed tomography of the breast at 434 MHz." Source: [Kruger 1999, IEEE MTT-S](http://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/779831/) (DOI 10.1109/MWSYM.1999.779831).
- **Xu, Ku, Wang 2001** — "Microwave-induced thermoacoustic tomography using multi-sector scanning." Short-pulsed microwave beam, thermoelastic waves, focused ultrasonic transducer; cross-sectional images by combining 1D sector scans. Source: [Xu, Ku, Wang 2001, PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11585227/).
- **Xu & Wang 2002** — "Pulsed-microwave-induced thermoacoustic tomography: filtered backprojection in a circular measurement configuration." 0.5 μs microwave pulse, circular measurement, filtered backprojection, ~2 mm phantom features at mm-scale ultrasound resolution. Sources: [Xu & Wang 2002, PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12201411/), [Xu & Wang 2002, Caltech PDF](https://www.its.caltech.edu/~coil/epub/2002/XuM_2002_Med_Phys_29_p1661.pdf).

### 9.5 Refined Evidence Grading and Exclusion Guide

The taxonomy in §7 still holds; this section sharpens it for ongoing investigation.

**Core mechanism** (anchor for everything else): pulsed UHF/microwave absorption → rapid thermoelastic expansion → acoustic pressure wave → cochlear/bone-conduction auditory perception (or, in imaging, ultrasonic-array detection and reconstruction).

| Confidence | Coverage | Examples |
|------------|----------|----------|
| **High confidence — core mechanism and mature engineering** | Psychophysics with peak-power-dominated parameter dependence; in-vitro thermoacoustic transients in water; cochlear microphonics; threshold–pulse-duration relations; consensus reviews; thermoacoustic CT at 434 MHz with patient data; reconstruction theory. | Frey & Messenger 1973; Foster & Finch 1974; Guy et al. 1975; Chou et al. 1975, 1979, 1982; Lin & Wang 2007; Lin 2022; Kruger et al. 1999, 2000; Xu, Ku, Wang 2001; Xu & Wang 2002; Optosonics/Kruger and Wang/Ku patent families. |
| **Patent-only or implementation-uncertain** | Engineering disclosures of audio-to-pulsed-microwave speech transmitters with no public reduction to practice or independent replication. | US4877027A (Brunkan); US6470214B1 / US6587729B2 (Air Force, O'Loughlin & Loree); DE10222439A1 (Leyser); RU2526478C2 (deterrent). |
| **Adjacent / lower relevance** | Mechanistically distinct hearing systems; general directed-energy bioeffects that share neither the pulse parameters nor the absorber thermoacoustics; popular-press incapacitation claims. | Transdermal RF / facial-nerve electrode hearing patents (US3586791A, US3629521A); cochlear-implant RF link (US3751605A); auditory-cortex mode patent (US4858612A); ultrasonic parametric speakers; conventional microwave tomography without acoustic detection; Active Denial System (ADS) skin-heating; generic high-power microwave injury studies; popular-press MEDUSA coverage. |

**Deprioritization guide.** Do not weight the following as evidence for the core thermoelastic auditory effect:

- Transdermal RF / electrode hearing patents and cochlear-implant RF links — different mechanism (direct neural/electrode stimulation), not pulsed-microwave thermoacoustics.
- Auditory-cortex direct-stimulation patents (e.g., US4858612A) — propose a different theory of operation and lack the thermoelastic-pressure-wave evidence base.
- Ultrasonic parametric speakers — same end (sound at a target) but acoustic, not RF-induced thermoacoustic.
- Conventional microwave tomography without acoustic detection — same RF physics on the absorption side, but no acoustic transducer in the chain; not in the thermoacoustic family.
- Active Denial System (ADS) — millimeter-wave skin heating for pain compliance, not pulsed-microwave thermoacoustics.
- Generic high-power microwave injury studies — relevant to safety bounds, not to the auditory-effect mechanism.
