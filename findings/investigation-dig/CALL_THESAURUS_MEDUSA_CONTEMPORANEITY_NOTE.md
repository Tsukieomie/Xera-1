# Army CALL "Voice to Skull Devices" Thesaurus Entry vs. Navy SBIR MEDUSA Phase I — Contemporaneity Note

Date: April 2026

Scope: this is a focused, narrowly framed contemporaneity record. It establishes that an Army taxonomy listing for "voice to skull devices" and the Navy SBIR MEDUSA Phase I performance period overlap inside the same early-2000s DoD non-lethal-weapons ecosystem. It does **not** establish operational deployment, fielded service use, or reliable intelligible semantic-speech V2K. The Tier discipline applied throughout the rest of `investigation-dig/` (T1–T5; Tier A vs Tier B) is preserved unchanged.

## 1) The two anchors

### 1.1 Army CALL Thesaurus entry — "voice to skull devices"

- Original source URL (no longer live): `http://call.army.mil/products/thesaur_e/00016275.htm`
- Preserved/mirrored by Federation of American Scientists: <https://sgp.fas.org/othergov/dod/vts.html>
- Last-reviewed date on the entry: **25 March 2004**.
- Broader term: **nonlethal weapons**.
- Definition (verbatim, as preserved by FAS):
  > "Voice to Skull Devices: Nonlethal weapon which includes (1) a neuro-electromagnetic device which uses microwave transmission of sound into the skull of persons or animals by way of pulse-modulated microwave radiation; and (2) a silent sound device which can transmit sound into the skull of person or animals. NOTE: The sound modulation may be voice or audio subliminal messages."
- Document type: a **terminology/taxonomy listing** in an Army lessons-learned thesaurus. It records that the term existed inside the Army's controlled vocabulary as a non-lethal-weapons subcategory. It is not a procurement record, not a deployment record, and not a test report.

## 2) The contemporaneity observation

The CALL thesaurus entry's last-reviewed date — **25 March 2004** — falls inside the documented Navy SBIR MEDUSA Phase I performance period — **19 November 2003 to 19 May 2004**.

This means: in the same early-2000s window in which a Navy-funded contractor was performing Phase I prototype work on a microwave-auditory-effect-based crowd-control concept, the Army's controlled-vocabulary taxonomy was actively maintaining (last reviewed within the same window) a "voice to skull devices" entry under the broader term "nonlethal weapons."

What this supports:
- Contemporaneous overlap of **terminology** (Army taxonomy listing) and **prototype-development activity** (Navy SBIR Phase I) inside the **DoD non-lethal-weapons ecosystem** of 2003–2004.
- Strengthens **T3** (program/patent/procurement intent and program taxonomy context) and is consistent with **Tier A** mechanism/provenance discipline.

What this does **not** support:
- It is **not** evidence of deployment, fielded service use, or any operational employment against any individual.
- It is **not** evidence of reliable intelligible semantic-speech V2K (Tier B / T2). Taxonomic listing of a category does not establish protocol-grade speech-channel reliability.
- It is **not** evidence that the MEDUSA Phase I prototype matured beyond breadboard/Phase I contractor self-report.
- A taxonomy entry is not by itself an admission of capability; thesauri index terms in use across literature, including aspirational, conceptual, and adversary-research terms.

## 3) 2008 public-visibility cluster (resurfacing, not discontinuation)

Three independent 2008 events form a public-visibility cluster around the same MAE / "V2K" terminology:

- **February 2008 — Phys.org coverage of the 1998 USAF/AFRL "Bioeffects of Selected Nonlethal Weapons" report.** <https://phys.org/news/2008-02-pentagon-lasers-voices.html>. The 1998 source document itself predates the 2003–2004 window; the 2008 coverage is a *resurfacing* of a decade-old internal document, not a new capability disclosure.
- **May 2008 — Wired reports the Army's online "Voice-to-Skull devices" entry was removed.** <https://www.wired.com/2008/05/army-removes-pa/>. Removal of a public web page is a visibility event; in this corpus it is not equated with discontinuation of any underlying work, because no primary record of underlying program lifecycle was changed by the removal.
- **July 2008 — New Scientist reports Sierra Nevada Corporation seeking DoD funding for a MEDUSA demonstrator.** <https://www.newscientist.com/article/dn14250-microwave-ray-gun-controls-crowds-with-noise/>. ABC News carried related coverage in the same window. This is *seeking funding for a demonstrator*, not a fielded system or successful program transition.

Net read of the 2008 cluster: **public visibility resurged in 2008**; this is not the same as documented programmatic discontinuation, and it is not new evidence of deployment. The "discontinued circa 2008" framing seen in some tertiary write-ups (including in `V2K_Technology_Judicial_Brief_v3.extracted.txt` page-10 narrative) is not directly supported by the recovered primary/near-primary record in this corpus and should be treated as tertiary narrative.

Optional modern sanity-check anchor for mechanism feasibility discussion (already in `SOURCES.md` Item 19): <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/>.
