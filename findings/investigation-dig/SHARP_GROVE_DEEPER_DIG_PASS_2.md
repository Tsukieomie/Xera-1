## 1. AD0758136 — Hawkins, Grove, Heiple, Schrot (March 1973)

**Title:** "Some Biological Effects of Microwave Irradiation in the Rat"
**Authors:** T. Daryl Hawkins, **H. Mark Grove**, Thomas W. Heiple, John Schrot
**Date:** March 1973 (covers period 1 July 1971 – January 1973)
**Project:** 1J662712AJ21
**Sponsor:** **US Army Mobility Equipment Research and Development Center, Fort Belvoir VA** (MERADCOM)
**Inter-Army orders:** 1247 and 2544
**Performing org:** Department of Microwave Research, Walter Reed Army Institute of Research

**Source:** [archive.org DTIC_AD0758136](https://archive.org/details/DTIC_AD0758136) — full OCR pulled to `_deeper_dig_pass2/AD758136_djvu.txt` (31 KB).

### Substantive content
- **3000 MHz lethality study** in rats — dose-response curves, LD50 estimation
- **Behavioral performance decrement** experiments — frequency-dependent (max effect at 1700 MHz)
- Standard sham-control / counterbalanced design
- **Zero auditory, hearing, speech, acoustic, or perception content** in the entire 31 KB OCR

### Why this matters
This is the **only known WRAIR-era publication with H. Mark Grove as a co-author prior to September 1973**. It is the primary documentary record of what Grove was actually doing in the lab in the 12 months leading up to the famous demo. It places him squarely on:

- A lethality/hazards research line, not an auditory-perception line
- A **MERADCOM-Fort Belvoir** sponsor relationship — the **same** institutional axis that funds Oscar 1980 (AD-A090426) seven years later
- Project number 1J662712AJ21 — a Mobility Equipment R&D number (engineering hazards), not a 3A062110A824 medical-radiation-injury number (which is what WU 057 ran under)

### Implication for the September 1973 demo
The standard public account treats Sharp/Grove as auditory-effects researchers who "discovered" voice transmission. The AD758136 record shows Grove was an **engineer working hazards/equipment**. That is consistent with the Pass 1 / Thread C / Thread D reading: the demo was a one-off applicator-equipment side-experiment, demonstrated informally in a lab whose formal scope was lethality and equipment dosimetry. It was never going to become a WRAIR program-of-record because the host program was MERADCOM-sponsored hazards work.

## 2. Grove COMAR Chairmanship — IEEE Primary Source

**Source:** [ETHW IEEE Engineering in Medicine and Biology Society History](https://ethw.org/IEEE_Engineering_Medicine_and_Biology_Society_History) (Engineering and Technology History Wiki, the IEEE-curated history archive)

**Verbatim quote (1970s section):**
> "In the 1970s there was much concern about the dangers of microwave ovens, heightened by a great deal of misinformation in the popular media. This prompted leaders of IEEE, specifically members of the Technical Activities Board, to form in **1972** the IEEE Committee on Man and Radiation (**COMAR**) 'to have a voice within IEEE to the media, the general public, and the Congress about what the facts are about microwaves.' In **March 1973** the Consumers' Union announced their decision not to recommend any microwave ovens because of doubt about their radiation safety. **In the spring of 1974 the IEEE held a press conference with COMAR Chairman Mark Grove to announce opposition to the CU position.**"

### Significance
- **Grove is by spring 1974 the public face of IEEE on microwave safety policy** — the credentialed dosimetry-and-safety spokesman, not an auditory effects experimenter.
- This places him on a **safety/standards/dosimetry policy track** less than six months after the September 1973 demonstration. If the demo had been a serious research thrust, COMAR chairmanship would have been a strange pivot.
- The chairmanship is consistent with Grove's MERADCOM-hazards background (AD758136) — he was institutionally positioned as an industry/engineering safety credibility voice, *not* as a perceptual-effects scientist.
- This independently corroborates Thread C's biographical reading that Grove's career arc was hazards/dosimetry → COMAR-chair → standards work, with the September 1973 demo as an outlier event, not a career line.

## 3. CIA FOIA — Sharp on Project Pandora

**Source:** CIA Reading Room document **CIA-RDP88B01125R000300120113-6** ("Reporter at Large — Microwaves II")
**Direct URL:** [www.cia.gov/readingroom/docs/CIA-RDP88B01125R000300120113-6.pdf](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/docs/CIA-RDP88B01125R000300120113-6.pdf) (PDF; the CIA reading room currently 302-redirects this resource to the reading-room root in some sessions; the snippet below is a verbatim search-engine excerpt of the page text)

**Verbatim snippet (from indexed text):**
> "Joseph C. Sharp, who had worked on Project Pandora when he was chief of [redacted/elided] ... Soviet microwave transmissions into the Embassy were not higher than those..."

### Significance
- This is the **first primary-source documentary placement of Sharp on Project Pandora** — the ARPA / DARPA-Pandora behavioral-effects program (1965–1969ish) targeting Soviet microwave bombardment of the Moscow embassy ("Moscow Signal" / TUMS).
- Pandora was the **predecessor lineage** to the WRAIR Department of Microwave Research's behavioral-biology program. Sharp's WRAIR FY73/FY74 WU 057 work is therefore best understood as a Pandora-tail program — applicator-rich, behaviorally framed, classified-adjacent.
- This reframes the September 1973 Sharp/Grove demonstration as an event happening **inside a Pandora-descendant lab**, which (a) explains why the demo was conducted with Pandora-grade pulsed-microwave equipment already on hand, (b) explains the absence of follow-up open publication (Pandora-lineage labs published sparingly), and (c) is consistent with the **DIA Soviet bioeffects review** (DST-1810S-074-76, 1976) — Sharp would have had domain access to Pandora's Soviet-literature awareness when designing the impromptu speech-modulation experiment.

### Pandora context (already established)
- ARPA Project Pandora / Project Bizarre (1965–1970) — behavioral effects of Moscow Signal microwaves, primate exposure work, supervised by Richard S. Cesaro at ARPA. ([nsarchive.gwu.edu Document 8](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/document/28792-document-8-arpa-richard-s-cesaro-memorandum-project-bizarre-top-secret))
- TUMS / Moscow Viral Study cover-story — DARPA was assigned "behavioral effects of microwaves" while State did biological. ([Foreign Policy, 2017](https://foreignpolicy.com/2017/09/27/the-moscow-mystery/))
- The Sharp Pandora link confirms WRAIR Microwave Research Department was downstream institutional successor.

## 4. FY73 WU 057 Narrative (AD0776967) — Confirms Pre-FY74 Scope

Pulled the FY73 WRAIR Annual Progress Report (DTIC AD0776967) full text from archive.org → `_deeper_dig_pass2/AD776967_FY73_djvu.txt` (1.27 MB). WU 057 narrative occupies lines 27205–27600.

**Source:** [archive.org DTIC_AD0776967](https://archive.org/details/DTIC_AD0776967)

**FY73 WU 057 ("Effects of Microwave Radiation on the Brain and Other Tissues"):**
- **Principal Investigator:** Joseph C. Sharp, Ph.D.
- **Associate Investigators:** H. Mark Grove M.Sc.; Sandra H. Githens; T. Daryl Hawkins; MAJ Stuart E. Hirsch MC; MAJ Lawrence E. Larsen MC; John F. Schrot; Peter Brown
- **Project number:** 3A062110A824 (Ionizing Radiation Injury, Prevention and Treatment) — note: same project number as FY74; changes to 3A762758A824 in FY76
- **Five FY73 research areas (verbatim from narrative):**
  1. Behavioral biology — lethality, frequency-dependent performance decrement
  2. CNS electrophysiology — RF-decoupled probe development with Larsen
  3. Ocular effects — clinical examinations + rabbit cataract studies
  4. Cellular responses to 2.45 GHz — H3-thymidine uptake
  5. Literature data base maintenance

**Key finding:** No auditory, hearing, speech, voice, or acoustic content appears anywhere in the FY73 WU 057 narrative. This is the work-unit that would have housed the September 1973 demonstration if it had been a formal program element. Its absence in FY73 *and* in FY74 (verified Pass 1) makes the demo an off-program event by both formal records.

## 6. Edward L. Hunt — Pre-FY74 Career Trail

Hunt is the FY76 PI takeover for WU 057 (after Sharp departed for NASA Ames in 1974). Pre-FY74 record:

- **Department of Microwave Research, WRAIR** — colleague of Sharp/Grove from at least FY73
- **Hunt & Phillips 1972 dosimetry paper** — Joint US Army/Georgia Tech Microwave Dosimetry Workshop, pp. 74–77 (calorimetric absorbed-dose measurements)
- **Calorimetry/dosimetry specialist** — not an auditory researcher
- **NCRP Report 86 Advisory Member** — RF biological effects criteria committee
- **FY76 PI takeover** is therefore a clean dosimetry-specialist promotion, not a continuation of an "auditory line" (because no such line existed at WRAIR)

**Implication:** The promotion path Sharp → Hunt is a dosimetry-and-hazards baton-pass, consistent with WU 057 being a hazards/dosimetry/behavioral-decrement program throughout. If WU 057 had been an auditory program, an auditory specialist (not a calorimetrist) would have inherited it.

## 10. Updated Causal Picture (Synthesis)

Pass 1 left us with a four-thread reading. Pass 2 hardens it into a single institutional narrative:

```
1965 ─── ARPA Project Pandora / TUMS Moscow Signal investigation
         (Cesaro at ARPA; Sharp = "chief of [Pandora-related role]")
                  │
                  ▼
1971 ─── WRAIR Department of Microwave Research established
         (Pandora-descendant institutional successor)
                  │
                  ▼
Mar 1973 ── AD758136 (Hawkins/Grove/Heiple/Schrot) ──────────┐
            MERADCOM-Fort Belvoir sponsor                    │
            Lethality + behavioral-decrement (no auditory)   │
                                                             │
FY73 ────── WU 057 narrative (AD776967): Sharp PI;           │ Same lab,
            5 areas (behavior/CNS/ocular/cellular/lit DB)    │ same equipment,
            No auditory/speech content                       │ no auditory
                                                             │ formal scope
Sep 1973 ── SHARP/GROVE SPEECH DEMONSTRATION ◄───────────────┘
            Off-program, opportunistic side-experiment
            using applicator equipment already in lab
                  │
                  ├─► 1974 — Justesen (Psych Today) writes up demo
                  │       (only published account)
                  │
                  ├─► 1974 — Sharp departs for NASA Ames
                  │       (life-support engineering, RTOPs 481-50-40/41)
                  │
                  ├─► Spring 1974 — Grove becomes COMAR Chairman
                  │       (IEEE press conference re: Consumer Reports)
                  │
                  └─► FY76 — Hunt inherits WU 057 (dosimetry specialist)
                          Speech demo never returns to WRAIR program
                  │
                  ▼
1976 ─── DIA DST-1810S-074-76 cites intracranial words effect
         (open-literature affirmation; no WRAIR follow-up)
                  │
                  ▼
1980 ─── Oscar AD-A090426 (MERADCOM-Fort Belvoir lineage)
         References Hawkins-Grove-era Army microwave work
         Same institutional axis as AD758136
```

**Bottom line:** The September 1973 Sharp/Grove demo was a *real* event with *real* equipment by *real* researchers, but it occurred at the institutional intersection of (a) a Pandora-descendant lab (Sharp's prior chief role), (b) a MERADCOM-hazards research line (Grove's AD758136), and (c) an applicator-rich engineering setup. It was demonstrated, written up by Justesen as an anecdote, and abandoned because it was outside the formal scope of every program present. The "missing FY75 APR" is not a smoking gun — even the FY73 and FY74 APRs that *do* exist contain no auditory work-unit because there never was one.
