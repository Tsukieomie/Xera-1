## 1) MEDUSA public record (WaveBand Phase I)

### 1.1 Archived Navy SBIR Phase I record

- URL: https://web.archive.org/web/20080409063721/http://www.navysbirprogram.com/NavySearch/Summary/summary.aspx?pk=F5B07D68-1B19-4235-B140-950CE2E19D08
- Hard fields:
  - Contract: `M67854-04-C-1012`
  - Topic: `N03-163`
  - Solicitation: `03.2`
  - Branch / customer: `MARCOR` (Marine Corps)
  - Phase: `I`
  - Period of performance: `2003-11-19` → `2004-05-19`
  - Award amount: `$99,965.00`
  - Firm: `WaveBand Corporation`, Irvine, CA
  - Principal investigator / firm POC: `Dr. Lev Sadovnik`
  - Government contact: `George Gibbs`
- Stated objective:
  - Breadboard prototype of a temporary personnel-incapacitation system designated MEDUSA, based on the microwave auditory effect (MAE), in which selected microwave pulses produce a strong sound sensation that can be raised to a discomfort level.
- Stated Phase I results (as captured in the archived summary):
  - Operating frequency selected.
  - Hardware requirements established.
  - Hardware designed and built.
  - Power measurements and pulse parameters confirmed.
  - Experimental evidence of MAE observed.
- Stated applications: perimeter protection, hearing-impaired communication, and law-enforcement / military crowd-control / asset-protection roles. Promoted attributes: portable, low-power, controllable radius, crowd or individual coverage, "low probability of fatality or permanent injury."

Interpretation:

- This is the strongest in-corpus primary anchor for **program existence** and the **declared Phase I outcome** of the WaveBand MEDUSA effort.
- The public record supports a Phase I / breadboard / concept stage only. **No public Phase II award for this exact MAE-based MEDUSA line has been recovered.** No primary public record of cancellation or discontinuation "for brain damage" has been recovered.

Cross-reference: this matches the contract / topic / phase / contractor fields independently captured in `medusa-navysbir-wayback.html` and tabulated in `MEDUSA_EVIDENCE_TABLE_PRIMARY.md`.

### 2.4 Retrieved Invocon brief (use carefully)

- URL: https://www.havanasyndrome.nl/static/doc/Invocon-Brief.pdf
- Provenance caveat: the file is hosted on a third-party site (`havanasyndrome.nl`). It is treated here as a **retrieved brief, not an official host of record**. The internal content is consistent with publicly known N03-163 framing, but the host itself is not the program office.
- Internal claims of interest:
  - N03-163 requirements list cited: reduce will and ability to resist; no physical contact; directional; sufficient duration to apply restraints; through-wall capability; no permanent physiological or psychological damage; no gas discharge.
  - Maturity statement: EPIC Phase II would reach approximately TRL 2-3 at the end of the Phase II option in December 2007.
  - Funding road-map cited (Invocon's own forward plan, not a program-of-record commitment):
    - June 2008: mouse vestibular response study, ~$2M.
    - 2009: mammal studies, ~$5M.
    - 2011: human-effects work, ~$10M.
  - Customer alignment named: Navy / JNLWD.

Assessment of MEDUSA vs. EPIC:

- Same problem space (Marine Corps / Navy non-lethal RF personnel-incapacitation requirement, topic `N03-163`) and adjacent contract trail (`M67854-04-C-1012` MEDUSA / WaveBand vs. `M67854-04-C-1013` EPIC / Invocon, both Phase I in the same window).
- Different contractors, different inventors, and different stated mechanism (MAE / silent audio for MEDUSA vs. RF Lorentz-force vestibular disruption for EPIC).
- Public evidence supports treating EPIC as a **sibling / publicly advanced branch / follow-on candidate** under the same Marine Corps requirement family — not as a proven rename or successor of MEDUSA.

## 5) Evidence assessment / confidence levels

- **High confidence:**
  - MEDUSA existed as a Navy SBIR Phase I MAE-based breadboard / prototype / concept effort under contract `M67854-04-C-1012`, topic `N03-163`, performed by WaveBand Corporation in 2003-2004.
  - WaveBand Corporation was acquired by, and ultimately merged into, Sierra Nevada Corporation (announcement May 2005; California "Merged Out" status with inactive date 2008-07-02).
  - EPIC has both a Phase I (`M67854-04-C-1013`, Invocon, 2003) and a Phase II (`M67854-05-C-6509`, Invocon, 2005-2007) public record under the same `N03-163` requirement family.
  - EPIC is a distinct contractor, distinct inventor set, and distinct stated mechanism from MEDUSA.

- **Medium confidence:**
  - EPIC functioned as a sibling / follow-on candidate within the same Marine Corps requirement family (`N03-163`) and may have been the public branch that advanced further on that requirement, given that EPIC has a public Phase II record while MEDUSA does not in the recovered open corpus.

- **Low / unsupported:**
  - That MEDUSA was formally renamed EPIC.
  - That MEDUSA was discontinued specifically because of brain-damage findings.
  - That MEDUSA was operationally fielded.
  - That MEDUSA had a classified or covert "voice-to-skull" semantic-speech function.
  - That the California 2008-07-02 "Merged Out" event, by itself, equals MEDUSA program closeout. (The California event is a corporate-lifecycle event for the WaveBand legal entity, not an evidentiary statement about the MEDUSA program.)
