# Flanagan / Rense Video Clue Review

Date: 2026-05-16

Source video: "Dr Patrick Flanagan interview on Jeff Rense Show: John c lilly wave mind control"  
URL: https://youtu.be/76tt8BfBw4M

## Bottom line

The video adds a strong first-person claim from Flanagan that one of his inventions or patents was placed under secrecy and later released through help from Admiral William "Red" Raborn. It does not prove the agency that caused the secrecy order. The independent documentary record still supports a real secrecy order on US 3,647,970 / serial 756,124, but the video blends the Neurophone story, the later speech-waveform patent, CIA/NSA personal-contact claims, and broader mind-control claims into one narrative.

## What the video corroborates

- **Secrecy-order reality**: The video's "my patent was put under secrecy" claim is consistent with the separate scanned secrecy-order packet for serial 756,124, which identifies Gillis P. Flanagan, the title "Method and System for Simplifying Speech Waveforms," and the legal basis under 35 U.S.C. §§ 181-188. Source: https://img1.wsimg.com/blobby/go/a56ed7e4-a072-4dc8-b939-26ab140309de/downloads/Patrick%20Flanagan%20High%20Security%20Speech%20Scramble.pdf
- **Speech-waveform patent identity**: Google Patents identifies US 3,647,970 as "Method and system for simplifying speech waveforms," inventor Gillis P. Flanagan, filed August 29, 1968, granted March 7, 1972, application US756124A. Source: https://patents.google.com/patent/US3647970A/en
- **Original Neurophone patent identity**: Google Patents identifies US 3,393,279 as "Nervous system excitation device," inventor Gillis Patrick Flanagan, filed March 13, 1962, granted July 16, 1968, application US179337A. Source: https://patents.google.com/patent/US3393279A/en
- **Raborn identity**: The caption-garbled "Red Brain / Raburn / Red Rayburn / Red Rurn" is almost certainly William F. "Red" Raborn Jr., because the CIA record says Raborn was sworn in as Director of Central Intelligence on April 28, 1965 and describes him as the developer of the Polaris missile system. Source: https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp91-00901r000600220001-0
- **Raborn 1961 status**: The Navy history page places Raborn as Director of the Navy's Special Projects Office in a July 19, 1961 interview and says he delivered Polaris A1 in July 1960, three years early. Source: https://www.history.navy.mil/content/history/museums/undersea/explore/Commemorations/StrategicDeterrence/vice-admiral-raborn-interviews.html
- **Lilly Wave technical term**: John C. Lilly's archived page defines the Lilly Wave as a "balanced bidirectional pulse pair" designed to stimulate through implanted electrodes while avoiding thermal/electrolytic injury and irreversible threshold changes. Source: http://www.johnclilly.com/lillywavex.html
- **Flanagan/Navy dolphin-acoustics trail**: A Flanagan-hosted Dolphin Project PDF lists a June 1, 1966 Tufts University final report, "A Study of Acoustical Multipath Systems Section IV Delay Line," by D. W. Batteau and G. Patrick Flanagan, under U.S. Navy Office of Naval Research contract NONR 494(00). Source: https://img1.wsimg.com/blobby/go/a56ed7e4-a072-4dc8-b939-26ab140309de/downloads/Pat%20Flanagan%20Dolphin%20Project.pdf?ver=1685486536436
- **Ultrasonic speech perception clue**: PubMed lists the 1991 Science paper "Human ultrasonic speech perception" by M. L. Lenhardt, R. Skellett, P. Wang, and A. M. Clarke, reporting that bone-conducted ultrasonic hearing could support frequency discrimination and speech detection in normal, hearing-impaired, and profoundly deaf subjects. Source: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2063208/

## What is contradicted or not yet supported

- **"Raborn went on to NSA" is not supported by the checked official records**: The CIA biographical record reviewed here lists Raborn's Navy career, DCI appointment, Navy retirement, and Aerojet role, but contains no NSA role; the Navy page likewise contains no NSA mention. Sources: https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp91-00901r000600220001-0 and https://www.history.navy.mil/content/history/museums/undersea/explore/Commemorations/StrategicDeterrence/vice-admiral-raborn-interviews.html
- **The 1961-1962 "CIA director" timing is wrong as stated**: Flanagan says the Golden Plate/scholarship event was "back in 1961, 1962," but Raborn was not DCI until April 28, 1965. Source: https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp91-00901r000600220001-0
- **The official Academy honoree list does not list Patrick Flanagan**: The Academy of Achievement's all-honorees page lists 1962 honorees including Vice Adm. William F. Raborn Jr. and Murray Gell-Mann, but the fetched page search did not find Patrick Flanagan. Source: https://achievement.org/our-history/golden-plate-awards/all-honorees/
- **Flanagan-affiliated source claims the award anyway**: PhiSciences claims Patrick Flanagan received a Golden Plate Award on December 29, 1962 for the Neurophone and says his omission from the current Academy site is an "oversight" or political; this is useful but not independent. Source: https://www.phisciences.com/pages/patrick-flanagan-history
- **The agency behind the secrecy order remains unproven**: 35 U.S.C. § 181 shows that secrecy orders can involve defense-agency review and that inspection acknowledgments must be entered in the application file, which makes the file wrapper the right place to look for agency attribution. Source: https://uscode.house.gov/view.xhtml?req=granuleid%3AUSC-prelim-title35-section181&num=0&edition=prelim

## V2K / patent timeline side clue

Rense says "voice-to-skull" patents were almost 30 years old and that the originals were being explored in the late 1970s. The clearest patent hit from this pass is US 4,877,027, "Hearing system," filed June 6, 1988 and issued October 31, 1989, which describes inducing sound in a person's head by radiating the head with microwaves from 100 MHz to 10,000 MHz modulated with a waveform. Source: https://patents.google.com/patent/US4877027A/en

US 4,877,027 cites earlier prior art including US 3,629,521, "Hearing systems," issued December 21, 1971 to Henry K. Puharich and Joseph L. Lawrence, which concerns transdermal RF signals applied across the head to stimulate hearing in impaired or deaf persons. Source: https://patents.google.com/patent/US3629521A/en

## Working interpretation

The most conservative reading is that Flanagan's video claim has a real anchor: a genuine Invention Secrecy Act order existed for his 1968 speech-waveform application, and he repeatedly says in the interview that a patent/invention was held under secrecy. The weaker part is attribution: the video points toward Raborn/CIA/NSA as a personal-release story, but the checked official records validate Raborn as a real Polaris/CIA figure while not validating an NSA role or an agency-caused link to the secrecy order.

The video also appears to conflate the original Neurophone patent and the speech-waveform secrecy-order patent. US 3,393,279 is the original "Nervous system excitation device" filed in 1962 and granted in 1968, while the documented secrecy-order packet we have is for US 3,647,970, filed in 1968 and granted in 1972. That does not make Flanagan's claim false, but it means the evidence must be kept patent-specific.
