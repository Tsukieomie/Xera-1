## Provenance facts (period-anchored)

### F-1. Analog Science Fiction / Science Fact, July 1967

- **Bibliographic identifiers.** *Analog Science Fiction / Science Fact* (Condé Nast), Volume LXXIX, Number 5, July 1967, John W. Campbell, Editor.
- **Insert / item.** "LISTENING INCORPORATED — Annual Report, 19 December 1966" appears as embedded organizational/scientific reporting content alongside Campbell's regular editorial features.
- **Named officers.** Dwight Wayne Batteau, President. G. Patrick Flanagan, Vice President of Research. The annual-report block is dated **19 December 1966** and describes Listening Incorporated's research portfolio as of that date.
- **Period framing of the Neurophone.** The report describes the Neurophone (Flanagan invention) as producing **"hearing electrically without mechanical vibration"** — the phrase is the period framing, not a remote-coupling demonstration. The framing is consistent with contact-coupled capacitive-electrode operation as documented in Flanagan's 1968 US 3,393,279 specification.
- **Navy / China Lake contract context.** The annual-report block places one of Listening Incorporated's acoustics-research lanes under U.S. Navy contract activity at China Lake (Naval Weapons Center / Naval Air Warfare Center predecessor at the time), consistent with Batteau's dolphin-acoustics work (cf. NONR 494(00) Tufts University contract, "A Study of Acoustical Multipath Systems Section IV Delay Line," June 1, 1966, Batteau and Flanagan — already logged in `FLANAGAN_RENSE_VIDEO_CLUE_REVIEW_20260516.md`).

## Scope of support — what the Analog source proves

- **Organizational.** Batteau and Flanagan were jointly affiliated with Listening Incorporated in 1966-1967, with Batteau as President and Flanagan as VP of Research. This corroborates the 1968 US 3,393,279 assignment line and the 1966 NONR 494(00) Tufts report.
- **Period framing of Neurophone.** The "hearing electrically without mechanical vibration" phrasing places the contact-coupled Neurophone mechanism in the public-period literature at a date independent of Flanagan's own first-person retrospectives and independent of Begich's later compilation.
- **Navy / China Lake organizational context.** Listening Inc.'s acoustics lane had Navy / China Lake organizational ties, supporting period-source plausibility for any later Navy-channel narrative around Batteau (e.g., the Raborn-era Polaris / Special Projects Office context).

## Scope of non-support — what the Analog source does not prove

- **No remote standoff coupling claim.** The "hearing electrically without mechanical vibration" framing describes the Neurophone's contact-coupled mechanism. It is **not** evidence for standoff dielectric voice-to-brain coupling. See [`STANDOFF_DIELECTRIC_BRIDGE_MATRIX.md`](STANDOFF_DIELECTRIC_BRIDGE_MATRIX.md) for the bridge analysis.
- **No classified-program affiliation for the Neurophone.** The Navy / China Lake context attaches to Listening Inc.'s acoustics-research lane (consistent with Batteau's dolphin-acoustics work under NONR 494(00)); it is **not** evidence that the Neurophone itself was a classified program asset.
- **No DIA / NSA sponsorship of the 1969 secrecy order.** The Analog source describes the 1966-1967 organizational state of Listening Inc.; the April 25, 1969 secrecy order on application 756,124 (a different, later Flanagan patent — US 3,647,970, "Method and System for Simplifying Speech Waveforms") post-dates the annual-report period and is unaddressed by this source. Agency attribution for the 756,124 order remains unresolved per Section 1 / Section 7 of [`index.md`](index.md).
- **No clinical efficacy claim.** Period framing is not a controlled clinical outcome. Clinical-efficacy literature on transdermal hearing-impaired electrostimulation remains mixed per the fourth-pass clinical review.
