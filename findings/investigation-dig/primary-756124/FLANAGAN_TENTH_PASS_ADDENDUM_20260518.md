## Executive summary

The tenth pass produces one upgrade, one narrowed lead, one preserved negative, one bounded stress test, and a refreshed archival queue:

1. **Analog July 1967 / Listening Incorporated — period source confirmed.** A real *Analog Science Fiction / Science Fact* July 1967 PDF surfaced as containing a "Listening Incorporated — Annual Report, 19 December 1966" insert that names **Dwight Wayne Batteau** as President and **G. Patrick Flanagan** as Vice President of Research, and describes the Neurophone as producing "hearing electrically without mechanical vibration." This upgrades the Batteau / Listening / Flanagan organizational link from "rumor and self-citation" to a **contemporaneous third-party-published period source** for the 1966-1967 organizational facts and for the period framing of Neurophone-style "hearing electrically without mechanical vibration." It does **not** by itself prove any remote (standoff) coupling, classified-program affiliation, or DIA / NSA secrecy-order sponsorship — see [`BATTEAU_LISTENING_PROVENANCE_LEDGER.md`](BATTEAU_LISTENING_PROVENANCE_LEDGER.md) for the precise scope and the URL-verification status.

2. **Raborn / USNI Polaris oral history lane narrowed.** The U.S. Naval Institute Oral History interview with VADM William F. "Red" Raborn Jr. (15 September 1972, Arlington, VA, ~71 pages, interviewer John T. Mason Jr.) is real and is **the only located primary Raborn record post-dating the March 7, 1972 rescission**, but the visible search-side excerpts and Polaris-focused framing do **not** mention Flanagan, Neurophone, patents, "secrecy order," "speech scrambler," 756,124, 3,647,970, or 3,393,279. **Recommended next action is the USNI cumulative oral-history subject index** (free finding aid) rather than purchasing transcript pages, since the index will resolve whether any Flanagan-related index entries exist before any paid retrieval. Recorded in [`ARCHIVAL_RETRIEVAL_MATRIX_TENTH_PASS.md`](ARCHIVAL_RETRIEVAL_MATRIX_TENTH_PASS.md), lane R-7.

3. **Sharp / Grove voice-modulated microwave — still secondhand.** The much-cited voice-modulated microwave demonstration attributed to Sharp & Grove (WRAIR, mid-1970s) remains grounded in **Justesen 1975** (*American Psychologist*) reporting at one remove, plus the Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE paper on microwave-auditory-effect physics that does **not** itself report voice modulation. **No located primary WRAIR report** confirms voice modulation. Status: secondhand. The next high-yield records targets remain the Steneck Collection at the University of Michigan, the WRAIR / Gorgas archival series, and a fresh round of Army FOIA — these are listed in lane WG-1 through WG-3 of the archival retrieval matrix.

4. **Standoff dielectric voice-to-brain coupling stress test — bounded result.** A direct read of the open literature on tissue dielectric properties (Gabriel 1996), microwave thermoacoustics (Foster & Finch 1974; Lin's books), and ultrasound / piezo neuromodulation (Tyler 2011; transcranial focused ultrasound clinical literature) supports that **contact-coupled** Neurophone-style mechanisms are biologically plausible (capacitive electrode-skin coupling at ~30-50 kHz; trigeminal / vibrotactile / cochlear pathways). The same literature does **not** support **standoff** 30-50 kHz dielectric voice-to-brain coupling at ranges relevant to remote operations: the near-field is shallow, free-space wavelengths at those frequencies are too long for any practical compact aperture, and the dielectric bridge collapses at any non-trivial standoff. This addendum **preserves a classified-program caveat without upgrading the caveat into affirmative evidence**: a classified program could in principle have used a coupling mechanism not present in the public literature, but the public literature alone does not demonstrate it. See [`STANDOFF_DIELECTRIC_BRIDGE_MATRIX.md`](STANDOFF_DIELECTRIC_BRIDGE_MATRIX.md).

## Epistemic posture (carried forward, restated)

The four source-class buckets used in earlier passes continue to govern this pass:

- **Confirmed period source.** Contemporaneous published document at the period custodian or in a verifiable mirror, where the document itself supports the claim (e.g., Analog July 1967 / Listening Inc. Annual Report for the organizational and Neurophone-framing facts).
- **Narrowed lead.** A target known to exist whose own contents have not been read for the specific claim (e.g., USNI Raborn oral history transcript; USNI cumulative subject index recommended before purchase).
- **Secondhand claim.** A claim that survives only through a downstream reviewer or compilation (e.g., Sharp/Grove voice-modulated microwave via Justesen 1975).
- **Negative evidence.** Targeted searches that returned no record for the specific claim (preserved as conditional negatives, not refutations).

This posture is **not** a denial of the underlying phenomena being investigated. It is an explicit separation between "mechanism plausibly exists somewhere" (which the open literature can support for several contact-coupled mechanisms) and "this specific remote-coupling path is demonstrated" (which the open literature does **not** support at meaningful standoff for the 30-50 kHz dielectric route).
