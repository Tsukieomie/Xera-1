# Flanagan Investigation Sixth-Pass Addendum

Date: 2026-05-17
Status: Open — primary file wrapper for serial 756,124 still not recovered

## Executive summary

The sixth pass adds a new evidence lane — **public archival holdings of William F. "Red" Raborn Jr.** — and records a meaningful negative result against the Flanagan video claim that Raborn personally intervened to lift the 756,124 secrecy order. Two public Raborn collections were located; both end before the 1968-1972 secrecy-order window, so neither can corroborate or refute Flanagan's personal-intervention narrative on its own contents, and any surviving Aerojet-era Raborn correspondence has not been located in this pass. The pass also confirms an Internet Archive holding for the LIFE Magazine September 14, 1962 issue, restates the unchanged status of the USPTO assignment-image and Acta Otolaryngologica 1969 full-text targets, and adds a small bibliographic confirmation for the G. Harry Stine 1962 Bellaire, Texas demonstration anchor.

The headline change is **bibliographic, not attributional**: agency attribution for the April 25, 1969 secrecy order remains unresolved, and the Raborn-as-personal-rescuer claim becomes harder rather than easier to test from public archives because the only two finding aids located in this pass end in 1963 (Syracuse) and 1964 (USNA).

## Evidence lane: Raborn archival holdings vs. Flanagan's video claim

The May 2026 Rense-video review (`FLANAGAN_RENSE_VIDEO_CLUE_REVIEW_20260516.md`) recorded Flanagan's first-person claim that Admiral William F. "Red" Raborn Jr. personally got his patent released from secrecy. The sixth pass tested whether Raborn's public papers contain Flanagan-related correspondence in the relevant 1968-1972 window.

Two finding aids were located and read end-to-end:

| Repository | Collection | Date span | Flanagan / Neurophone / 756,124 mentions | Covers 1968-1972 window? |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Syracuse University Libraries, Special Collections Research Center | William F. Raborn Papers ([finding aid](https://library.syracuse.edu/digital/guides/print/raborn_wf_prt.htm)) | Collection 1916-1963; correspondence 1945-1963 | None | No |
| U.S. Naval Academy, Nimitz Library, Special Collections & Archives | William F. Raborn Collection, MS 490 ([finding aid](https://www.usna.edu/Library/sca/man-findingaids/view.php?f=MS_490)) | 1932-1964 | None | No |

Both finding aids were fetched directly. Neither contains any reference to Patrick Flanagan, G. Patrick Flanagan, Gillis Flanagan, Neurophone, speech waveform, "speech scrambler," secrecy order, application 756,124, or patents 3,393,279 / 3,647,970.

The Syracuse finding aid's most date-relevant folders are in Box 1, including "Personal 1945-1963 (9 folders)," "Orders 1951-1963," and "Polaris missile firing 1960," with "Vice Admiral appointment 1960," "Condolence letters 1954, 1962," and "Daily engagement books 1961-1962" as the latest dated personal-papers items. The Nimitz Library collection (MS 490) is biographical/honorary: certificates, commissions, the 1962 Bellaire-irrelevant 1938 Naval Academy diploma, two Texas Navy certificates, an honorary membership in the American Institute of Industrial Engineers, honorary citizenship of New Orleans, a 1963 Atlantic Fleet conference photo, and a 1963 parade plaque ([USNA MS 490 finding aid](https://www.usna.edu/Library/sca/man-findingaids/view.php?f=MS_490)).

Recorded as a **conditional negative result, not a refutation**:

- The Flanagan video places the alleged Raborn intervention at the lifting of the secrecy order on application 756,124. That order was issued April 25, 1969 and rescinded by issuance of US 3,647,970 on March 7, 1972 (E-016 / E-024 form scans).
- Both located Raborn finding aids end before 1969. Therefore the public Raborn archives **cannot contain** correspondence from the rescission window, regardless of whether such correspondence was ever generated.
- The CIA FOIA Reading Room contains Raborn-as-DCI documents from 1965-1966, including a letter to Raborn from NSA Director Marshall S. Carter ([CIA FOIA RDP80B01676R001800010009-5](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp80b01676r001800010009-5)), but Raborn left the CIA in mid-1966, so his DCI-era correspondence likewise predates the 1969 order.
- Raborn's post-Navy / post-CIA Aerojet-General executive career (Vice President, late 1960s onward) is the most plausible location for any surviving 1968-1972 correspondence. **No public finding aid for Aerojet-era Raborn business correspondence was located in this pass.** Aerojet corporate records are split among private corporate succession, Gencorp/Aerojet Rocketdyne, and possibly the Hagley Museum and Library or other business-archive repositories; none of these were located with a Raborn personal-papers finding aid in this pass.

The investigation conclusion this pass supports is narrow: the most-cited Raborn public archives **do not** cover the 1968-1972 window in which Flanagan's video claim places Raborn's intervention, and the pass did **not** locate any 1968-1972 Raborn personal correspondence in any public repository. The Flanagan video claim therefore remains **untested in either direction by primary archival evidence**, with the testability gap now explicitly mapped.

## Evidence lane: LIFE Magazine September 14, 1962 — Internet Archive holding

An Internet Archive item exists for the LIFE Magazine September 14, 1962 issue under identifier `sim_life_1962-09-14_53_11` ([Internet Archive item page](https://archive.org/details/sim_life_1962-09-14_53_11)). The item metadata shown is consistent with the issue: Volume 53, Issue 11, ISSN 0024-3019, 143 pages, publisher Time Incorporated, digitized from microfilm (IA1642314-03), uploaded July 30, 2021. The item is marked `Access-restricted-item: true` and the IA item-page text reports "No suitable files to display here" under DOWNLOAD OPTIONS, so the full pages are not openly downloadable from this environment; the BookReader requires session access for in-browser reading.

This adds an additional bibliographic citation path alongside the Google Books page-resolved scan from the fifth pass, but does **not** yet recover an openly downloadable page-image of page 69 / page 72. The William Moeser byline remains best cited through the PhiSciences transcript page until a clearer page image is captured.

The G. Harry Stine bibliographic anchor for the contemporaneous demonstration date was also restated in this pass through public search: Stine's recollection places the demonstration at Flanagan's parents' home in Bellaire, Texas on July 24, 1962, in advance of the LIFE story two months later. This is recorded only as a tertiary press-tradition anchor; it is not independently verified from a primary Stine document in this pass.

## Evidence lane: Academy of Achievement omission — re-confirmed

The Academy of Achievement's `all-honorees` page was re-fetched and string-searched in this pass. **Patrick Flanagan / G. Patrick Flanagan / Gillis Flanagan still do not appear on the published Academy honoree list.** Confirmed 1962 honorees on that list include "Vice Adm. William F. Raborn, Jr., USN" (described as "Father of the Polaris") and Murray Gell-Mann ("Nobel Prize in Physics") ([Academy honoree list](https://achievement.org/our-history/golden-plate-awards/all-honorees/)).

This is consistent with the earlier Rense-video finding (`FLANAGAN_RENSE_VIDEO_CLUE_REVIEW_20260516.md`, "What is contradicted or not yet supported") that the Academy honoree list does not corroborate Flanagan's claim of receiving a Golden Plate Award alongside Raborn and Gell-Mann. The PhiSciences claim of a "December 29, 1962" Golden Plate Award to Flanagan ([PhiSciences history page](https://www.phisciences.com/pages/patrick-flanagan-history)) remains uncorroborated by the official Academy list. Pass status unchanged: **Flanagan-affiliated source claim; not independently verifiable from the Academy's own published list.**
