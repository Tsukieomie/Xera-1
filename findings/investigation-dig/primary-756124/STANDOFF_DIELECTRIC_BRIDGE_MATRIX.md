# Standoff Dielectric Voice-to-Brain Bridge Matrix

Companion file to: [`FLANAGAN_TENTH_PASS_ADDENDUM_20260518.md`](FLANAGAN_TENTH_PASS_ADDENDUM_20260518.md)

Date opened: 2026-05-18
Status: Open. Stress-tests whether the open literature supports a **standoff** (non-contact) dielectric voice-to-brain coupling path in the 30-50 kHz band consistent with the Neurophone framing. Preserves a classified-program caveat without upgrading it into affirmative evidence.

## Purpose

Separate two questions that the broader investigation has at times conflated:

- **Q1. Does a biologically plausible mechanism exist somewhere in the open literature that would allow an externally generated electromagnetic / acoustic / piezoelectric signal to elicit auditory or speech-like percepts in a human subject?** The answer in the open literature is qualified yes for several **contact-coupled** mechanisms: capacitive electrode stimulation at ~30-50 kHz (Neurophone-style); microwave-induced thermoacoustic / RF-auditory effect (Frey 1962; Foster & Finch 1974; Lin's books); transdermal electrostimulation (Puharich/Lawrence 1969 *Acta Otolaryngologica*); transcranial focused ultrasound (Tyler 2011 and the focused-ultrasound clinical literature).
- **Q2. Does the open literature demonstrate a *remote, standoff*, dielectric voice-to-brain coupling path in the 30-50 kHz band at ranges relevant to remote operations?** The answer is **no**, on physical-bound grounds laid out below.

This matrix records each candidate mechanism, what the open literature supports at contact distance, what it supports at standoff, the load-bearing physical constraint that bounds the standoff case, and the classified-program caveat that is preserved without affirmative evidence.

## What the open literature does support (summary)

- **Contact-coupled Neurophone-style mechanisms are biologically plausible.** Capacitive electrode coupling at 30-50 kHz can elicit auditory percepts via trigeminal / vibrotactile / cochlear / possibly direct cortical-stimulation pathways, with controlled-trial outcomes mixed on durable clinical efficacy.
- **Pulsed-microwave RF-auditory clicks are physically established.** The thermoacoustic mechanism is well-modeled; primary papers run from Frey 1962 through Foster & Finch 1974 and the Lin and Elder & Chou monographs / reviews.
- **Transcranial focused ultrasound modulates cortical activity in clinical settings.** Tyler 2011 and the subsequent focused-ultrasound clinical literature establish the mechanism in contact-coupled, gel-coupled, or fluid-coupled geometries.

## What the open literature does not support

- **Standoff 30-50 kHz dielectric voice-to-brain coupling at ranges relevant to remote operations.** The free-space wavelength forecloses efficient compact-aperture radiation, the near-field falls off too quickly, and no open report demonstrates voice intelligibility at standoff in this band.
- **Air-coupled ultrasonic standoff voice intelligibility.** The impedance mismatch is too large.
- **Voice-modulated microwave intelligibility at standoff with reported primary documentation.** The Sharp/Grove claim survives in Justesen 1975 reviewer text; the primary WRAIR report has not been located.

## Classified-program caveat (preserved without upgrade)

This matrix preserves an explicit caveat: a classified program could in principle have demonstrated a coupling path not represented in the open literature. That caveat is **not** affirmative evidence of any specific operational capability. To convert the caveat into evidence would require either (a) a declassified primary report describing the coupling geometry and the demonstrated standoff range, modulation method, and intelligibility metric, or (b) a credible technical reconstruction from period contract numbers and surviving correspondence that establishes the same.

Until either path is established, the matrix conclusion stands:
- **Contact-coupled Neurophone-style mechanisms: biologically plausible, period source exists.**
- **Standoff 30-50 kHz dielectric voice-to-brain coupling: not supported by the open literature.**
- **The gap is not closed by a caveat; the caveat is a gap.**
