## 2 · The "intelligible speech" passage — verbatim, primary

Background section, internal page 45, written by Kenneth J. Oscar, Ph.D. (US Army MERADCOM, Fort Belvoir VA 22060):

> "A decoy and deception concept presently being considered is to remotely create the perception of noise in the heads of personnel by exposing them to low power, pulsed microwaves. When people are illuminated with properly modulated low power microwaves the sensation is reported as a buzzing, clicking, or hissing which seems to originate (regardless of the person's position in the field) within or just behind the head. The phenomena occurs at average power densities as low as microwatts per square centimeter with carrier frequencies from 0.4 to 3.0 GHz. **By proper choice of pulse characteristics, intelligible speech may be created.** Before this technique may be extended and used for military applications, an understanding of the basic principles must be developed. Such an understanding is not only required to optimize the use of the concept for camouflage, decoy and deception operations but is required to properly assess safety factors of such microwave exposure."

This passage was previously cited in our matrix from third-hand mirrors. It is now confirmed verbatim against the DTIC-catalog-metadata-bearing PDF and against the document's own embedded NTRL `Subject` field.

## 3 · What the *experimental body* of the report actually measures

The body of AD-A090426 is **not** a speech-transmission protocol. It is a rat local-cerebral-blood-flow (LCBF) study. Faithful summary:

**Methods (internal pp. 45–47):**
- Subjects: male Wistar rats from the Walter Reed colony, 250–320 g.
- Exposure: conscious animals exposed individually for 5, 15, 30, 45, or 60 minutes to pulsed microwaves at **15 mW/cm² average power density**. Sham controls at 5, 30, or 60 minutes.
- Chamber: 2 m wide × 3 m high × 3 m long, 23 ± 2 °C.
- Carrier: **2.8 GHz**, pulse rate **500 pps**, pulse width **2 µs**.
- Source: 40 kW pulsed microwave generator (Applied Microwave Lab. PH40), standard gain horn. Field intensity verified by NBS field-intensity meter and Narda Model 8300; average power density accuracy ±25 %.
- Endpoint: local cerebral blood flow measured via 14C-iodoantipyrine and scintillation counting after exposure.

**Results / discussion (internal pp. 47–53):**
- Low-power pulsed microwave exposure increased LCBF in all 17 sampled brain regions of conscious rats.
- By 60 minutes, every region was up at least 39 %; many were up well over 100 %.
- The first region to respond (at 5 min) was the **inferior colliculus** — the primary auditory midbrain nucleus.
- Largest 60-minute increases: pineal, pituitary, temporal cortex, inferior colliculus, lateral geniculate, medial geniculate.
- Short-exposure increases concentrated in sensory regions; combined with Wilson et al.'s glucose-consumption work, this suggests microwaves increased metabolic activity through direct or indirect excitation of brain tissue.
- 60-minute results indicate gross alteration of brain function "possibly due to microwave-induced temperature rise, fatigue, brain stimulation, biochemical imbalance, neurotransmitter release, etc."
- **Conclusion (Oscar's own words, paraphrased here):** the LCBF increases indicate an alteration of brain activity; the mechanism is **unclear**; possibilities include direct or indirect stimulation of central or peripheral receptors via microwave-induced thermal, mechanical, or electrical disturbances.

## 5 · Interpretive guardrails (carried into the matrix and the deep-dig writeup)

The attached PDF review tightens the language we use about AD-A090426:

1. **"Primary report contains the claim"** — the Background paragraph asserting that "intelligible speech may be created" is now confirmed at primary grade in a DTIC-metadata-bearing copy. This is a federal-command-level US Army acknowledgment of stated intent in 1980.
2. **"Concept presently being considered"** — Oscar's own framing. The Background paragraph reports an *Army research concept under consideration*, not a fielded capability.
3. **"Intelligible speech may be created"** — Oscar's own modal verb. Not "has been created", not "is created"; *may be created*, conditional on "proper choice of pulse characteristics".
4. **"Experimental body measures rat LCBF"** — the methods/results sections of AD-A090426 measure local cerebral blood flow in conscious rats exposed to 2.8 GHz / 500 pps / 2 µs pulsed microwaves at 15 mW/cm². They do **not** report a human or animal speech-transmission demonstration.
5. **"Mechanism unclear"** — Oscar's own conclusion language. The LCBF increases imply altered brain activity; the underlying mechanism is left open (thermal, mechanical, electrical, neurotransmitter-release, fatigue, all listed as possibilities).

What AD-A090426 therefore does NOT establish, even with the body now reviewed:
- It does **not** prove an actual human speech-transmission demonstration was operationalised.
- It does **not** report intelligibility scores, word lists, transcripts, blinding, or human subject counts for any speech demo.
- It does **not** provide the underlying speech-protocol experimental detail (no power level, no modulation scheme, no confusion matrix for any speech corpus).
- It does **not** cite a specific underlying speech-protocol report; the speech sentence is presented as established background, downstream of Sharp 1973 / Frey 1973 / Justesen 1975 lineage.
