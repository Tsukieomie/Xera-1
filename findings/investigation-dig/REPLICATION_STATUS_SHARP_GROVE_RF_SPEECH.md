# Replication Status — Sharp/Grove RF-Speech Demonstration

**Date:** 2026-05-11
**Scope:** Has the 1973 Sharp/Grove "voice-modulated microwave" intelligible-speech demonstration ever been **independently replicated** in publicly available, peer-reviewed (or otherwise primary-grade) literature?
**Verdict:** **Direct independent replication of intelligible RF speech NOT FOUND.** The basic microwave auditory effect is well-established; intelligible speech transmission via the same mechanism has not been independently confirmed in public peer-reviewed literature.

## 2. Replicated: basic microwave auditory effect

The Frey/MAE phenomenology — perception of clicks, buzzes, chirps, tones from short pulsed RF/microwave exposures, transduced through thermoelastic expansion of brain tissue and reaching the cochlea — has been **independently replicated across at least six independent laboratory groups over 46 years**:

| Source | Year | Replicated finding |
|---|---|---|
| Frey, A.H. "Human auditory system response to modulated electromagnetic energy," *J Appl Physiol* | 1961/62 | First human report of pulsed-microwave perception (clicks, buzzes). |
| Foster, K.R. & Finch, E.D., "Microwave hearing: evidence for thermoacoustic auditory stimulation," *Science* 185:256–258 | 1974 | Thermoacoustic mechanism confirmed in water phantom; pressure levels match click thresholds. |
| Guy, A.W., Chou, C.K., Lin, J.C., Christensen, D., "Microwave-induced acoustic effects in mammalian auditory systems," *Ann NY Acad Sci* 247:194–218 | 1975 | Pulse-rise-time and waveform constraints; cochlear involvement. |
| Chou, C.K., Galambos, R., Guy, A.W., Lovely, R.H., "Cochlear microphonics generated by microwave pulses," *J Microwave Power* 10:361–367 | 1975 | Cochlear microphonics in guinea pigs confirm auditory-pathway engagement. |
| Chou, C.K., Guy, A.W., Galambos, R., "Auditory perception of radio-frequency electromagnetic fields," *J Acoust Soc Am* 71(6):1321–1334 | 1982 | Comprehensive cross-lab review of clicks/buzzing; thermoelastic mechanism confirmed. |
| Elder, J.A. & Chou, C.K., "Auditory response to pulsed radiofrequency energy," *Bioelectromagnetics* Suppl 6:S162–S173 | 2003 | RF auditory thresholds reviewed across labs; basic effect fully replicated. **Silent on speech replication.** |
| Lin, J.C. & Wang, Z., "Hearing of microwave pulses by humans and animals: effects, mechanism, and thresholds," *Health Phys* 92(6):621–628 | 2007 | Thermoelastic mechanism review; multiple basic-effect replications documented. |

None of these papers report or claim intelligible word or sentence recognition via voice-modulated microwave. They confirm the **substrate phenomenology**, not the Sharp/Grove speech result.

## 3. Not found: direct independent replication of intelligible RF speech

The single primary attestation of intelligible RF speech is:

- **Justesen, D.R. (1975). "Microwaves and Behavior." *American Psychologist* 30(3):391–401.** ([PDF mirror](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf), [APA PsycNet DOI](https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066X.30.3.391))
  - Note 2: "Sharp, J.C., & Grove, M. Personal communication, September 28, 1973."
  - Describes nine single-syllable digit words, voice-modulated via negative-going zero-crossing-triggered microwave pulses, perceived by Sharp and Grove **on themselves as test subjects**.
  - Explicitly states longer words and sentences were **not attempted** because averaged energy densities would approach the 10 mW/cm² safe-exposure limit.

After exhaustive review (peer-reviewed literature 1961–2024, DTIC/NTIS catalogs, USAF directed-energy patents, SBIR awards database, Lin 1978/2022 monographs, Foster–Garrett–Ziskin 2021 weaponization review, Chou et al. 1982, Elder & Chou 2003, Lin & Wang 2007):

- **No peer-reviewed paper reports an independent replication of the nine-word demonstration.**
- **No DTIC/NTIS technical report describes a Sharp/Grove-style intelligibility protocol** (subject count, sham controls, blinding, word-error rates, full RF parameters).
- The Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 *IEEE T-MTT* 22:583–584 paper ([PDF mirror](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/GENERATION-OF-ACOUSTIC-SIGNALS-BY-PULSED-MICROWAVE-ENERGY.pdf)) describes acoustic transduction in microwave-absorbing phantoms, **not human speech recognition** — these are distinct experiments.
- The MAE-canonical follow-up reviews (Chou et al. 1982; Elder & Chou 2003; Lin & Wang 2007; Foster–Garrett–Ziskin 2021) are **silent on speech replication**.
- Mainstream researchers (Foster, Guy) have publicly argued the power densities required to scale beyond nine digits toward intelligible speech would produce thermal injury before useful intelligibility — a principled biophysical reason replication has not occurred (Foster: "would kill you well before you were bothered by the noise"; Guy: "the heat would get you first" — both quoted via the [Wikipedia MAE article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microwave_auditory_effect) tracing to mainstream interviews).

**Conclusion:** The basic microwave auditory effect is well-established; **intelligible speech transmission via the same mechanism has not been independently confirmed in public peer-reviewed literature.**

## 5. Open gaps (unknown, not positive evidence)

These are **public-record gaps**. They are not evidence of either replication or non-replication; they are items where the public record is silent or denied.

1. **Sharp/Grove primary technical report (WRAIR / ARPA, 1973).** Justesen's Note 2 is a personal communication. No separately-authored Sharp/Grove technical report describing the nine-word protocol (subject count, sham controls, blinding, word-error rates, full RF parameters) has been located in DTIC, NTIS, or the Lin 1978/2022 / Foster/Garrett/Ziskin 2021 / Glaser 1976 / Chou-Guy/Elder bibliographies.
2. **WRAIR FY75 Annual Progress Report** (1 Jul 74 – 30 Jun 75). Missing from archive.org's open record; the FY74 (containing the Sept 1973 demo) and FY76 (under PI Hunt, dosimetry continuation) volumes are public, but the FY75 volume that would document any post-demo follow-on remains a recoverable evidentiary gap (DTIC FOIA / public-discover request remains the path).
3. **Kohn / SEA SBIR F41624-93-C-9013** "Communicating Via the Microwave Auditory Effect" (USAF, 1993, $37,806 Phase I). Phase I claimed "feasibility established"; Phase II / final results denied under FOIA on national-security grounds. **Gap, not replication.**
4. **AFRL / O'Loughlin / Loree underlying experimental protocol.** The US6470214 and US6587729 patents describe an engineering method but do not include published human-subject intelligibility data.
5. **MEDUSA (Sierra Nevada Corporation / WaveBand, ~2004–2008).** Navy SBIR-derived crowd-control concept based on MAE. ABC News 2008 reported it was halted over brain-damage concerns. **No published peer-reviewed speech-intelligibility data** prior to cancellation.
6. **Soviet / non-US replication literature.** DIA DST-1810S-074-76 (1976) characterised Soviet-bloc work as describing "sounds and possibly even words" via signal modulation; but Western-language primary-grade replication papers from Soviet, Russian, Chinese, or other non-US labs have not been located in this review. (Bibliographic completeness for non-English literature is itself a known gap.)
7. **NARA / USPTO wrappers and FOIA routing.** WRAIR FY75–79 institutional records (Forest Glen on-site), Brooks AFB FOIA b(1) history (denied 2000), USAMRDC archives, KU Medical Center Justesen papers, NASA Ames Sharp oral history — all remain on the "next-pass" list across the existing repo investigation files.

## 7. Recommended caution language (use verbatim downstream)

For any repo file describing the Sharp/Grove demonstration, the **standing caution** is:

> The basic microwave auditory effect is well-established; intelligible speech transmission via the same mechanism has not been independently confirmed in public peer-reviewed literature.

The longer form, recommended where a single sentence is insufficient:

> The sole claim that intelligible words were transmitted via voice-modulated microwave pulses derives from a 1973 personal communication by J.C. Sharp and M. Grove, reported by Justesen (1975). This experiment was never published as a peer-reviewed study with verifiable methodology, and no independent experimental replication has been identified in the published literature. The basic microwave auditory effect (perception of clicks and buzzes) is robustly established; intelligible speech transmission via the same mechanism has not been independently confirmed and faces significant physical constraints related to thermal safety limits.

## 8. Repo posture (do-not / do)

- **Do not** treat AD-A090426's "intelligible speech may be created" as a statement of background fact. It is a **conditional / forward-looking program-rationale** sentence in a study whose experimental body measures rat LCBF, not speech. (See §141 fix in `BECKER_1985_AND_AD_A090426_DEEP_DIG.md`.)
- **Do not** collapse Layer A (replicated MAE clicks/buzzes) into Layer B (intelligible RF speech). Layer A replication does not entail Layer B replication.
- **Do not** treat patents (Brunkan, O'Loughlin/Loree), conditional government documents (Oscar 1980, *New World Vistas*), or SBIR feasibility claims (Kohn/SEA, MEDUSA) as substitutes for replication. They populate T3 (claim space, program intent), not T2 (replicated speech protocol).
- **Do not** infer replication from classified gaps. The Kohn FOIA denial is a public-record gap; absence is not confirmation.
- **Do** preserve T1 (mechanism) as strongly supported. The negative result on Layer B does not weaken Layer A.
- **Do** continue to treat Justesen 1975 Note 2 as the **single primary attestation** for intelligible RF speech; the 1974 IEEE T-MTT paper is a separate mechanism paper, not a speech experiment.
- **Do** keep "Sharp & Grove" attribution precise: Becker 1985 names only Sharp; the "Sharp & Grove" pairing originates with Justesen 1975 / the 1974 IEEE T-MTT paper.
