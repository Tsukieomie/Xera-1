# JNLWP/JIFCO 2004-2010 Negative / Contrast Note

Date: April 2026

Scope: targeted scan of accessible archived Joint Non-Lethal Weapons Program (JNLWP) / Joint Intermediate Force Capabilities Office (JIFCO, post-rename) materials in the 2006-2008 window for any surface mention of the WaveBand MEDUSA program family.

## 1) Method

Each accessible archived JNLWP document below was scanned for the following target terms:

- `MEDUSA`
- `WaveBand`
- `Remote Personnel Incapacitation System`
- `microwave auditory effect`
- `silent audio`
- `Sadovnik`
- `M67854-04-C-1012`
- `N03-163`

## 2) Result

**No target-term hits found** in any of the documents listed below.

These documents represent the JNLWP public communications surface most likely to have surfaced a WaveBand MEDUSA reference if the program had been adopted, transitioned, or treated as a flagship non-lethal directed-energy effort in the 2006-2008 window.

## 4) Key contrast (what these documents do contain)

These materials discuss:

- **Active Denial System (ADS)**: a 95 GHz millimeter-wave skin-heating effect device explicitly described as not microwave (mm-wave absorbed in the top ~1/64 inch of skin), framed as a non-lethal counter-personnel directed-energy capability.
- Directed-energy non-lethal applications and the policy framing of bridging the "shouting / shooting" gap.
- The human-effects review infrastructure (independent review boards, bioeffects research history).
- Legal and treaty review processes for non-lethal directed-energy systems.
- ADS user assessments, the Advanced Concept Technology Demonstration (ACTD) phase, and the post-ACTD bridge program.

They do **not** surface WaveBand's MEDUSA (Mob Excess Deterrent Using Silent Audio) microwave-auditory-effect concept.

## 5) Disambiguation reminder: 66mm GD-OTS/USMC "MEDUSA" launcher is unrelated

Some JNLWP-adjacent materials reference a separate munition/launcher program also bearing the "MEDUSA" label. This is a **different program** from WaveBand's microwave-auditory MEDUSA and should not be conflated.

Useful disambiguation sources:

- NDIA 2011 Small Arms briefing (Hartman) discussing the GD-OTS/USMC 66mm launcher line:

- 2015 DoD Non-Lethal Weapons Program annual review:
  - https://defenseinnovationmarketplace.dtic.mil/wp-content/uploads/2018/02/2015_DoD_NLWP_ANNUAL_REVIEW.pdf

## 6) Interpretive impact

The JNLWP public trail in 2006-2008 documents a full transition / safety / legal-review narrative for ADS, but no analogous public JNLWP trace for WaveBand MEDUSA appears in this corpus.

Combined with the existing `MEDUSA_TRANSITION_GAP_ANALYSIS.md` finding that the publicly indexed Phase II continuation under topic `N03-163` belongs to the EPIC / Invocon line (not WaveBand MEDUSA), this scan reinforces the working classification of MEDUSA as an **SBIR Phase I / contractor self-report** program rather than a JNLWP-transitioned or operationalized capability.

- **T3 (program intent / provenance):** unchanged — MEDUSA Phase I narrative still anchored by `medusa-navysbir-wayback.html`.
