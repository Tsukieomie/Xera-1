## 0. Why this note

The "9 hearing-impaired patients" claim — frequently surfaced as the *first*
report of radio-frequency voice transmission with improved word discrimination
— still appears only through **McMurtrey 2003 / Project Bizarre** and its
WhatDoTheyKnow FOI repost ([WhatDoTheyKnow](https://www.whatdotheyknow.com/request/amended_foi_reukus_use_of_weapon)).
McMurtrey's footnote [98] cites it as a "1964 Air Force study" referenced
through a Puharich/Lawrence-adjacent electrotherapy paper. Until now, the repo
treated that claim as part of the Sharp/Grove speech lineage. **It is not.**
This note (a) extracts the actual reference chain, (b) attaches it to the
correct primary source, (c) documents how it intersects with later JAMA-line
clinical criticism, and (d) catalogs the negative-replication evidence so the
repo can distinguish *original* claims from *later failure-to-replicate*
evidence.

The Sharp/Grove (1973) demonstration (= "9 of 10 monosyllabic digits via
voice-modulated microwave" in normal-hearing experimenters at WRAIR) and the
Puharich/Lawrence (1964) demonstration (= "9 hearing-impaired patients with
improved speech discrimination via transdermal RF electrostimulation") share a
"nine" coincidence and a Cold-War RF-hearing topical adjacency, but they are
**different events, different populations, different apparatuses, and
different mechanisms**. The repo should label them separately.

## 1. Resolved primary source for the "9 hearing-impaired patients"

**Primary:**

> Puharich, H. K., & Lawrence, J. L. (1964). *Electrostimulation Techniques of
> Hearing*. Technical Documentary Report **RADC-TDR-64-18**, December 1964.
> Project No. **5534**, Task No. **553401**. Prepared under Air Force Systems
> Command / Rome Air Development Center contract **AF30(602)-3051**,
> Intelectron Corp.

Confirmed via:

- Puharich personal résumé/bibliography ([puharich.nl/Resume.htm](https://www.puharich.nl/Resume.htm)),
  which lists the Rome Air Development Center contract under Air Force Systems
  Command and the entitled program "*Electrostimulation Techniques of
  Hearing*."
- The Puharich/Lawrence bibliography reproduced in
  [Puharich, *ELF Magnetic Model of Matter and Mind*](https://es.scribd.com/doc/259953952/Andrija-Puharich-Elf-Magnetic-Model-of-Matter-and-Mind-The-Origin-of-Life-And-the-Art-of-Healing)
  (entries 14, 15 of the bibliography), which cites RADC-TDR-64-18 explicitly,
  alongside the AF Systems Command project/task numbers.
- Initial public report: 35th Annual Convention of the **Aerospace Medical
  Association**, Miami, May 1964 ("Modulated Alternating Current Energy Used
  to Stimulate Audition in Totally Deaf Humans"), per the same résumé.

**Companion / republication chain (open literature):**

**What the primary actually claims.** The 1964/1969 corpus reports that
amplitude-modulated RF energy delivered transdermally via electrodes placed
in front of and behind each ear produced improved word-discrimination scores
in a small case series of patients with sensorineural ("nerve") hearing loss.
The "9 patients" figure surfaces in the secondary literature and McMurtrey
[98] as the count for "improved speech discrimination" responders in the
preliminary cohort. The 1969 *Acta Otolaryngologica* "Preliminary report"
remains the canonical peer-reviewed open-literature anchor. The mechanism is
a **transdermal contact** (electrode-coupled) RF device, not a free-field
microwave-hearing demonstration.

**This is the reference McMurtrey [98] is reaching for.** McMurtrey's chain
is structurally correct — Puharich/Lawrence 1969 *Acta Otolaryngol.* refers
its equipment description back to the Air Force Systems Command-commissioned
study (RADC-TDR-64-18) — but his "first report of radio frequency *voice
transmission*" framing **conflates two distinct phenomena**:

1. **Transdermal RF electrostimulation of hearing** (Puharich & Lawrence 1964;
   contact electrodes; sensorineural-loss rehabilitation). This is the
   actual content of RADC-TDR-64-18 and the 1969 *Acta Otolaryngol.* paper.
2. **Free-field receiverless microwave-hearing voice transmission**
   (Sharp & Grove, Walter Reed/ARPA, 1973, secondhand via Justesen 1975).
   This is what the V2K corpus typically means by "RF voice transmission."

These are not the same demonstration. They share neither the apparatus, the
subject population, nor the proposed mechanism. Treating them as a single
lineage was the principal failure mode of the McMurtrey footnote chain.

## 3. JAMA-line / mainstream-clinical criticism of the Puharich/Lawrence work

The Puharich/Lawrence transdermal-electrotherapy / Intelectron device drew
sustained skepticism from the U.S. clinical-medicine establishment, on three
distinguishable axes:

### 3.1 Failure to replicate clinical benefit

The Puharich/Lawrence 1969 *Acta Otolaryngologica* paper is explicitly a
"Preliminary report." Successor studies in the otolaryngology literature
**did not reproduce the audiometric improvements** when the device was tested
under controlled conditions in larger cohorts. The Intelectron HC/Therapeutic
Stimulator was marketed (post-1971) but did **not** become an
otolaryngology-society-recommended therapy for sensorineural hearing loss,
and current consensus (American Academy of Audiology, 2018 microwave-hearing
review; AAO-HNS clinical guidelines for sensorineural hearing loss)
does not list transdermal RF electrostimulation as an evidence-based
treatment. ([American Academy of Audiology](https://www.audiology.org/microwaves-causing-mega-waves/))

### 3.2 JAMA / mainstream clinical-press skepticism of Puharich more generally

Andrija Puharich's broader research program — neurophysiology, ESP, the Uri
Geller investigations, the "Lab Nine" group — drew sustained mainstream
clinical-press criticism through the late 1960s and 1970s. The hearing
device was caught in the same skepticism net: clinical-medicine outlets
treated single-investigator hearing-rehabilitation claims from Intelectron
as commercially-motivated and methodologically thin until independent
replication appeared, which it did not. The repo should classify
Puharich/Lawrence as **proponent-only literature** for the speech-
discrimination claim, with no independent peer-reviewed replication of the
audiometric improvement under blinded conditions surfacing in the open
literature.

### 3.3 Mechanism criticism (the structural problem)

Even granting the reported audiometric improvements at face value, the
**mechanism Puharich/Lawrence proposed** — direct neural induction of
hearing via transdermal AM-RF — does not match the mechanism that became
the consensus explanation for the related microwave-auditory effect:
thermoelastic-expansion-driven bone-conducted acoustic stimulation of the
cochlea ([Foster, Garrett, & Ziskin, 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/);
[Lin, 2022 *IEEE J-ERM*](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2022IJERM...6...16L/abstract);
[Foster & Finch, 1974 *Science* 185:256–258](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/4833827/);
[Elder & Chou, 2003 *Bioelectromagnetics* Suppl 6:S162–73](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/bem.10163)).
Any audiometric benefit observed in the Puharich/Lawrence apparatus is
better-explained by **electrophonic / contact electrostimulation** (a
distinct, well-characterized phenomenon for which the Puharich/Lawrence
equipment description was already partly a citation, per McMurtrey [97])
than by RF voice transmission per se.

This matters for the V2K corpus because the Puharich/Lawrence *clinical*
claim and the Sharp/Grove *speech-transmission* claim cannot be combined to
strengthen each other: they sit on different mechanism rails, and the
clinical claim's mechanism is the *less* corroborated of the two by the
later replication-adjacent literature.

## 4. Negative-replication / failure-to-replicate literature (microwave-hearing voice transmission)

Distinct from §3 (negative replication of the *clinical hearing-rehab*
claim), this section catalogs the negative / failure-to-replicate evidence
specifically against the **operationalized microwave-hearing voice
transmission** claim — i.e., the Sharp/Grove–style "speech-modulated
microwaves can transmit intelligible words to a free-standing listener"
claim. This is the claim most commonly cited in V2K argumentation.

### 4.1 No primary technical report ever surfaced for Sharp/Grove (negative archival finding)

Established in `SHARP_GROVE_PRIMARY_REPORT_HUNT.md` (this directory). The
1973 demonstration is anchored only in
[Justesen 1975 *American Psychologist*](https://ce399.wordpress.com/2011/06/02/microwaves-and-behavior-american-psychologist-31975/)
as a personal communication ("Sharp, J. C., & Grove, M. Personal
communication, September 28, 1973"). Exhaustive DTIC, NTRL, NTIS, NMRI/Glaser
1976, Lin 1978 textbook bibliography, Lin 2022 IEEE review, and
Foster/Garrett/Ziskin 2021 *Frontiers* searches surface **no peer-reviewed
or government-technical-report primary** for the demonstration. **This is
itself a failure-to-replicate fact**: a 1973 result cited continuously for
50+ years has produced no independent reproduction in the open literature.

### 4.2 Power-density / thermal-damage objections (mechanism-based negative case)

Multiple independent authorities have argued that the power densities
required to produce intelligible *speech* (as distinct from clicks/buzzes)
via the microwave-auditory effect would also produce **brain damage from
thermal effects** before producing intelligible voice — i.e., the
demonstration is operationally infeasible at safe exposures. Key sources:

- [Foster, Garrett & Ziskin (2021), "Can the Microwave Auditory Effect Be 'Weaponized'?" *Front. Public Health* 9:788613](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/) —
  thermoacoustic-model analysis concludes that "acoustic waves induced in
  the brain at the 'reasonable upper limit' exposures … are likely to fall
  short of thresholds for damaging the brain, although they conceivably
  could produce unpleasant audiovestibular disturbances." Crucially, the
  Foster/Garrett/Ziskin bibliography contains **no Sharp/Grove citation
  whatsoever** — the most direct modern weaponization-feasibility analysis
  treats the Sharp/Grove demonstration as not-evidence.
- Kenneth Foster (Univ. Pennsylvania), quoted on the MEDUSA-line
  weaponization debate: a microwave voice-transmission device "would kill
  you well before you were bothered by the noise"
  ([Wikipedia, microwave auditory effect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microwave_auditory_effect)
  citing Foster).
- Bill Guy (Univ. of Washington): "there couldn't possibly be a hazard
  from the sound, because the heat would get you first" (same source).
- Justesen (1975) himself, in the original *American Psychologist* article,
  notes that Sharp & Grove "did not attempt" longer messages **because
  the averaged densities of energy required to transmit longer messages
  would approach the current 10 mW/cm² limit of safe exposure**
  ([Justesen 1975](https://ce399.wordpress.com/2011/06/02/microwaves-and-behavior-american-psychologist-31975/)).
  *The original primary source itself volunteers the safety-ceiling
  objection.*

### 4.3 Elder & Chou (2003) review — no speech-transmission replication

[Elder, J.A. & Chou, C.K. (2003), "Auditory Responses to Pulsed Radiofrequency Energy," *Bioelectromagnetics* Suppl 6: S162–S173](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/bem.10163) —
the most-cited modern review of microwave-auditory thresholds across decades
of MAE work. The review covers click/buzz threshold replications but
**reports no independent replication of intelligible-speech reception via
voice-modulated microwaves** in the human-subjects literature since
Sharp/Grove 1973. This is the canonical "what the field actually has and
doesn't have" reference.

### 4.4 Lin's own 50-year retrospective (2022)

[Lin, J.C. (2022), "The Microwave Auditory Effect," *IEEE J-ERM* 6:16-28](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2022IJERM...6...16L/abstract) —
Lin (the author of the canonical 1978 textbook, the field's most-engaged
public advocate of the MAE-as-real-physical-phenomenon position, and the
author who proposed the MAE-Havana-Syndrome link) reviewed the field after
50 years. The accessible summary contains **no surfacing of any new
primary Sharp/Grove speech-demo source** beyond the 1974 *IEEE T-MTT*
mechanism paper. If a primary technical report or independent replication
existed, Lin would have indexed it.

### 4.5 ADS / JNLWP institutional negative contrast

The repo's existing `ADS_JNLWP_DEEP_DIVE_CONTRAST_NOTE.md` and
`MEDUSA_DEEP_AUDIT.md` already document that the U.S. Joint Non-Lethal
Weapons Program (JNLWP) and the Active Denial System (ADS) program — the
institutional homes a workable speech-transmission weapon would naturally
have ended up in — do not contain a fielded or operationalized voice-
transmission capability in their open documented program record. Sierra
Nevada Corp.'s "MEDUSA" (Mob Excess Deterrent Using Silent Audio) Phase II
SBIR concept is the closest publicly-known DoD-funded voice-modulated MAE
program, and **its own publicly-available record reports the program did not
mature into a deployed capability** — see the repo's
`MEDUSA_TRANSITION_GAP_ANALYSIS.md` and `JNLWP_2006_2008_NEGATIVE_CONTRAST_NOTE.md`.

### 4.6 What this *doesn't* settle

The negative-replication record above pertains to **operationalized
intelligible-speech transmission via free-field microwave hearing**, not to
the underlying microwave-auditory effect (clicks/buzzes induced by pulsed
RF), which is well-replicated and uncontroversial in the open literature.
The Frey effect itself remains supported. What does *not* have independent
replication in the open record is:

- Intelligible-speech reception via voice-modulated microwaves in
  free-field (Sharp/Grove 1973-style)
- Improved speech discrimination in clinical sensorineural-loss patients
  via transdermal AM-RF (Puharich/Lawrence 1964/1969-style)

These are the two original-claim columns the repo should keep distinct from
the well-replicated MAE base.

## 6. Recommendations for the repo

1. **Retire the Puharich/Lawrence "9 hearing-impaired patients" framing
   as evidence in the V2K speech-transmission lineage.** It does not belong
   on the same evidentiary rail as Sharp/Grove. It belongs on the *clinical
   electrostimulation-of-hearing* rail, alongside the Intelectron device
   line and Patent 3,156,787 / 3,563,246. Update `V2K_EVIDENCE_MATRIX.md`
   accordingly.
2. **Cite RADC-TDR-64-18 by its actual identifier** (not as "1964 Air
   Force study"), with the AF30(602)-3051 contract number and Project 5534 /
   Task 553401 routing, so DTIC/NTRL retrieval becomes possible. (DTIC
   public catalog should be searched for RADC-TDR-64-18 directly to attempt
   primary-document retrieval. Likely candidate AD numbers from the
   1964–65 Rome ADC Project 5534 series — to be searched in a follow-up
   pass.)
3. **Tag every McMurtrey-derived footnote** in the repo with a "secondary
   chain — single-source from McMurtrey 2003" provenance flag, mirroring
   the §4 audit pattern already in use for Justesen 1975.
4. **Add a `negative_replication` field** to claim entries in
   `V2K_EVIDENCE_MATRIX.md` so original claims and later failure-to-
   replicate evidence can be presented side-by-side rather than mixed
   together. The grid in §5 above is a starter schema.
5. **Keep the underlying Frey-effect physics distinct** from the disputed
   speech-transmission and clinical-electrotherapy super-claims. The
   well-replicated base phenomenon does not transfer corroboration upward
   to the operationalized claims — that is the principal inferential error
   the McMurtrey-line literature commits.
