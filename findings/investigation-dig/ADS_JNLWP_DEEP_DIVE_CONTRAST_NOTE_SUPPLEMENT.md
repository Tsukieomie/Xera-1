## 1) ADS human-effects, review, and user-assessment trail

### 1.1 Air Force 2008 article — "Test results show Active Denial System as nonlethal weapon"

- URL: https://www.af.mil/News/Article-Display/Article/122302/test-results-show-active-denial-system-as-nonlethal-weapon/
- Key facts:
  - Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) completed extensive ADS bioeffects research.
  - 94 GHz millimeter-wave ADS. (Other ADS public materials specify 95 GHz; the 94/95 GHz citation variance is itself part of the public record and is preserved here as-stated.)
  - Defined skin and eye exposure thresholds and effective repel levels.
  - Data showed millimeter waves do not promote cancer or cause reproductive problems.
  - ADS could be used operationally with significant safety margin.
  - Sponsors named: AFRL, Joint Nonlethal Weapons Directorate (JNLWD), Air Force Force Protection Battlelab, OSD Advanced Systems and Concepts Office via the Advanced Concept Technology Demonstration (ACTD).
  - Two system instances cited: a mobile demonstrator and a containerized operationally suitable version.

### 1.2 Air Force 2007 article — Moody AFB ADS extended user evaluation

- URL: https://www.af.mil/News/Article-Display/Article/128277/moody-airmen-test-new-nonlethal-method-of-repelling-enemy/
- Key facts:
  - 820th Security Forces Group at Moody AFB selected as first unit for the extended user evaluation portion of the ACTD.
  - ADS described as long-range, nonlethal, 95 GHz millimeter RF.
  - Penetrates approximately 1/64 inch of skin; effect propagates at the speed of light.
  - Operational uses cited: base perimeters, checkpoints, entry control points, peacekeeping / humanitarian assistance, crowd dispersal.
  - >600 volunteers, 10,000 exposures, <0.1% chance of even minor injury.

### 1.3 NDU / ETHZ 2009 paper — Susan LeVine, JNLWD

- URLs:
  - https://www.files.ethz.ch/isn/134720/DTP%2065%20Active%20Defense-%20PO%2060032.pdf
  - https://digitalcommons.ndu.edu/defense-tech-papers/44/
- Key facts:
  - Author: Susan LeVine, JNLWD; published 2009.
  - ADS human-effects work cited as ongoing since 1993.
  - >11,000 exposures on >700 humans cited.
  - 8 second-degree burns reported; 2 medically attended; full recovery in cited cases.
  - Human Effects Advisory Panel (HEAP) established 1998 under Penn State guidance; reviewed ADS in 2002, 2004, and 2007.
  - Cites: "A Narrative Summary and Independent Assessment of the Active Denial System," Dr. John Kenny et al., HEAP, Feb. 11, 2008.
  - Legal / treaty review described: domestic-law analysis with no prohibition cited; LOAC discrimination and unnecessary-suffering analysis; OSD treaty-compliance review.
  - Joint Military Utility Assessments (JMUAs) cited at: Creech (Aug 2005), Fort Benning (Sept 2005), Santa Rosa / Eglin (Apr 2006).
  - System lineage cited: System 1 HMMWV demonstrator; System 2 containerized / armored.
  - Bridge / transition program led by the Air Armament Center, Eglin AFB.
  - **MEDUSA / WaveBand absent.**

## 2) Annual review / capability trail

### 2.1 2012 DoD Non-Lethal Weapons Program Annual Review (mirrored)

- URL: http://www.teargasresource.com/DoD%20Non-Lethal%20Weapons%20Program%20Annual%20Review%2011.19.2012%20HTML%20format%20v1.pdf
- Key facts (ADS / ADT):
  - ADS uses 95 GHz millimeter-wave energy.
  - Two systems: ADS1 and ADS2.
  - ADS1 is a mobile prototype / demonstrator being refurbished.
  - ADS2 is a containerized version suitable for operational deployment, with a plan to deploy / train / support if requested.
  - Range up to ~1,000 m.
  - March 2012 Quantico demo cited.
  - **MEDUSA / WaveBand / Remote Personnel absent.**
- Key facts (acoustic hailing — separate program family; do not conflate with MEDUSA or ADS):
  - Acoustic hailing developed after USS Cole to determine intent of nearby craft.
  - High-intensity directional sound; warning messages, tones, MP3, microphone modes.
  - Navy successfully used acoustic hailing devices since 2006 to protect naval ships.
  - Fielded acoustic hailing device (AHD) cited.
  - This is an **independent acoustic / force-protection capability path**. It is not a microwave auditory effect (MAE) program and is not the MEDUSA concept. It does not appear in the five accessible 2006-2008 JNLWP PDFs scanned in `JNLWP_2006_2008_NEGATIVE_CONTRAST_NOTE.md`.
- Magnetic Acoustic Device:
  - Appears in the April 2012 vehicle checkpoint Military Utility Assessment (MUA) at Fort Benning, alongside LA-9/P, M2 VLAD, and JNL warning munitions.

## 3) Active Denial Technology (ADT) lineage beyond legacy ADS

### 3.1 JNLWD BAA 14-008 (mirrored)

- URL: https://documents2.theblackvault.com/documents/laserinducedplasmaeffect/JNLWD%20BAA%2014-008.pdf
- Key facts:
  - Compact ADT sought to reduce ADS size, weight, and cost.
  - Current ADS1 / ADS2 based on a 100 kW cryogenically cooled commercial gyrotron.
  - Both ADS1 / ADS2 use ~95 GHz, ~1,000 m range, ~2-3 W/cm², spot size ~1.5 m.
  - 2006-2008: JNLWP funded the Laser-ADT project; produced 95 GHz at 100-200 mW; failed to reach ADS power density due to AO modulator inefficiency; JNLWP exercised the FY2008 "No-Go" decision.
  - RF bio-effects section cites ~15+ years of human-effects risk analysis and characterization for the ADS 95 GHz repel effect.
  - **MEDUSA / WaveBand / Remote Personnel absent.**

## 4) Interpretation — preserved and reinforced

### 4.0 Lineage-question recap (one-line)

A direct answer to the recurring question "are ADS and LRAD evolutions or variations of WaveBand MEDUSA?" is: **no.** ADS, LRAD / AHD, and WaveBand MEDUSA are three different capability families with different funding paths, different physical mechanisms, and disjoint public evidence trails. They share the broad non-lethal / counter-personnel ecosystem; they are not parent-child programs. See `MEDUSA_VS_ADS_LRAD_LINEAGE_QUESTION.md` for the consolidated one-stop write-up.


- **ADS has a public, multi-document transition / safety / legal / user-assessment trail**: ACTD, JMUAs, extended user evaluation, dual system versions (ADS1 / ADS2), HEAP / human-effects review (1998-2007 review cycle, 2008 narrative assessment), legal and treaty review, post-ACTD bridge / transition under the Air Armament Center, and later demonstrations (Quantico 2012, Twentynine Palms, Joint Base Langley-Fort Eustis).
- **ADT is a related but distinct lineage**: JNLWD BAA 14-008 documents a compact-ADT path with explicit Go/No-Go gates (Laser-ADT FY2008 No-Go).
- **Acoustic hailing is a separate fielded COTS / force-protection path**: post-USS Cole; fielded since 2006; named devices include AHD, Magnetic Acoustic Device, Mini-DSLA, HALLTS. **Do not conflate with MEDUSA or with ADS.** Acoustic hailing is air-pressure acoustic energy, not RF; MEDUSA's claimed mechanism is a microwave auditory effect. Treating these as the same program family would be a category error.
- **MEDUSA / WaveBand remain absent** from these accessible JNLWP / JNLWD / DoD NLWP public documents (2007 Moody article, 2008 AF article, 2009 LeVine NDU paper, 2012 Annual Review mirror, 2014 JNLWD BAA 14-008, 2015 NLWP Annual Review). MEDUSA should remain classified as **SBIR Phase I / contractor self-report only** unless and until at least one of the following surfaces:
  - Phase I final report (primary).
  - HEAP-like independent human-effects review.
  - JNLWP / DoD NLWP annual report mention.
  - Transition funding line.
  - Documented testing program.
  - Legal / treaty / human-effects record.
