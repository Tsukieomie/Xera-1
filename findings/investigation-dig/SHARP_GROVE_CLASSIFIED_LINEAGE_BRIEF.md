## 0. Claim under review

> **CLAIM (§8.3):** An informal speech-modulation extension of the formal WRAIR WU 057 "Modulated microwave signals" study was conducted by Sharp and Grove on or about **28 September 1973**, demonstrated to a visiting outside scientist (D. R. Justesen), and was **never written into the formal WU 057 / WRAIR Annual Progress Report narrative** — because it fell outside the nominal bioeffects/safety scope, and/or because the formal study was being deliberately wound down during the same reporting period.

The claim is **non-trivial**. If true, it implies:
- A primary technical report does not exist in the open DTIC/NTIS record — not because it was suppressed, but because the experiment was never formally documented as WU-057 output.
- Justesen 1975 *American Psychologist* Note 2 is therefore the *only* unclassified primary attestation and will remain so.
- The speech demonstration was a one-off extension of bioeffects equipment, not a programmatic capability-development effort.
- Any later operationalization (MEDUSA SBIR N03-163, Oscar 1980 USAMERDC AD-A090426, etc.) is a **downstream re-derivation**, not a continuation of a classified speech program rooted in 1973.

This reading has implications for the V2K judicial-brief T2 row: it supports a **qualified T2 grade** ("credible isolated demonstration; no evidence of sustained open-literature capability chain") rather than either T2 ("established capability") or T4 ("demonstration unsupported").

## 1. Positive evidence for the informal-extension reading

Each item below is graded **[P]** primary documentary, **[S]** secondary, **[C]** circumstantial.

### 1.1 FY74 WU 057 "Modulated microwave signals" subsection is framed as bioeffects/safety only [P]

**Source:** AD-A009338, WRAIR Annual Progress Report FY74 Vol 2, printed pp. 985–986 (OCR lines 9476–9489 per `FY74_SPEECH_DEMO_HUNT.md` §4).

**Verbatim (the full subsection):**
> **Modulated microwave signals.** An initial series of studies designed to better understand the so-called hearing effect produced by extremely low average power levels have been completed. Two observations have led to a temporary cessation of this work. These were (a) the observation that non-biologic material could serve as a transducer of the energy to acoustic signals which can be used as a model without exposing biological material and (b) the report that signals similar to those used in the auditory study may cause changes in the blood brain barrier of experimental animals. This latter observation has been investigated during the latter half of the reporting period and, to date, not been replicated. However, the implications of these observations are of such great importance that further work is in progress.

**Interpretation:** The subsection discloses that Sharp/Grove's WRAIR-internal auditory work in the FY74 reporting window (1 Jul 1973 – 30 Jun 1974 — the exact window containing Justesen's 28 Sep 1973 date) was **mechanism characterization** (thermoelastic / electrostrictive) and **BBB-safety follow-on** — not speech transmission. There is no mention of:
- voice, speech, phoneme, word, digit, intelligibility, or human-voice modulation
- human-subject communication
- any September 1973 "demonstration"
- Justesen or any external witness

### 1.2 WRAIR's formal output from this study is the Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT mechanism paper, not a speech paper [P]

**Source:** WU 057 FY74 publication list (Publication #1), as transcribed in `FY74_SPEECH_DEMO_HUNT.md` §3, cross-referenced against Sharp, Grove, Gandhi (1974), *IEEE Trans. Microwave Theory and Techniques* 22:583–584, DOI [10.1109/TMTT.1974.1128293](https://doi.org/10.1109/TMTT.1974.1128293).

**Interpretation:** The 1974 IEEE T-MTT paper is a mechanism paper on acoustic-signal generation by pulsed microwave energy in materials. It is explicitly registered as the formal output of "Modulated microwave signals" — and it says nothing about speech. The WRAIR institution therefore formally published on the mechanism but formally stayed silent on any speech extension.

### 1.3 Justesen 1975 sources the demonstration to a personal communication, not a report [P]

**Source:** Justesen, D. R. (1975). Microwaves and Behavior. *American Psychologist* 30(3):391–401, DOI [10.1037/0003-066X.30.3.391](https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066X.30.3.391), PMID 1137231. Footnote 2: "Sharp, J. C., & Grove, M. Personal communication, September 28, 1973." See `34_JUSTESEN_1975_APA_MICROWAVE_HEARING.md` §§1–3.

**Interpretation:** A published researcher citing a personal communication for a claim of this magnitude (nine-word receiverless speech communication) is evidence that, at time of writing (1974–early 1975), no primary report existed — otherwise the cite convention would be to the report, not to personal communication. This is the single strongest positive indicator for the §8.3 reading.

### 1.4 The demonstration is bounded to a single calendar date [P]

**Source:** Justesen 1975, footnote 2 (above), and narrative on p. 396: "In briefing one of us (Justesen) on their experiment, Sharp and Grove..."

**Interpretation:** "Briefing" language + a specific date (28 Sep 1973) is consistent with a **discrete demonstration event**, not a multi-week experimental protocol. Discrete demos typically produce lab-notebook entries, not technical reports.

### 1.5 Justesen dropped the Sharp/Grove material from his 1979 NYAM review [S]

**Source:** Justesen, D. R. (1979). Behavioral and psychological effects of microwave radiation. *Bulletin of the New York Academy of Medicine* 55(11):1058–1078, [PMID 120208](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/120208/). Cross-ref `FY74_SPEECH_DEMO_HUNT.md` DEEPER_DIG_7.

**Interpretation:** The 1979 NYAM retrospective covers the same biophysical territory as 1975 and cites many of the same mechanism sources (Frey, Lin, Guy, Chou) — but *omits* Sharp, Grove, and voice-modulated microwaves entirely. If Justesen had received any primary report from Sharp/Grove between 1975 and 1979, a retrospective review was the natural venue to promote the citation from "personal communication" to formal reference. He did not. This is consistent with the §8.3 reading that no such report was ever generated.

### 1.6 No replication effort exists in the open literature [P/S]

**Source:** Exhaustive negative searches across Lin 1978 textbook, Lin 2022 IEEE J-ERM review, Foster/Garrett/Ziskin 2021 *Frontiers* (PMC8733248), Glaser NMRI 1976 bibliography, PNL-2634 1978, Elder/Chou 2003 review (cross-ref `SHARP_GROVE_PRIMARY_REPORT_HUNT.md` §§1.2, 6.5 and `AD_A090426_REFERENCE_CHAIN_EVIDENCE.md` §1.8).

**Interpretation:** If the speech demonstration had been operationalized in any programmatic follow-on, a successor replication attempt — successful or failed — would be expected in the 1975–2005 bioeffects literature. None exists. The closest downstream artifacts (Oscar 1980 USAMERDC AD-A090426; Miller 2001 synthetic-telepathy review; MEDUSA SBIR) cite Justesen 1975 or Lin 1978 rather than a primary Sharp/Grove report — and none is itself a speech-transmission replication. This is consistent with the §8.3 reading that the demo was a one-off, not the origin of a sustained capability program.

### 1.7 Topical-scope mismatch between demo and WU 057 formal scope [P]

**Source:** `BERMAN_1985_REF24_CHASE.md` (FY73 WU 057 scope), `FY74_SPEECH_DEMO_HUNT.md` §4.1 (FY74 WU 057 scope).

**WU 057 formal scope (FY73 + FY74):** rat lethality at 3.0 and 2.45 GHz; frequency-dependent operant-performance decrement; ocular damage thresholds; dielectric measurement; mouse/rat/rabbit species lethality comparisons; absorption dosimetry; Frey-effect mechanism characterization; BBB-permeability safety follow-on.

**Interpretation:** A nine-word speech-communication demonstration does not fit the WU 057 formal scope — it is not a lethality study, not a dosimetry study, not an ocular study, and not a BBB study. An informal demonstration of the equipment's upper capability to a visiting researcher is consistent with the formal scope boundary; a formally documented speech-transmission protocol would have required a scope change and a new Work Unit. None is registered in the FY74 WU inventory.

### 1.8 FY74 narrative explicitly marks the work as winding down [P]

**Source:** AD-A009338 (quoted in §1.1 above): "Two observations have led to a *temporary cessation* of this work."

**Interpretation:** The subsection documents that Sharp/Grove were **already closing out the formal auditory-effect program** during the reporting period containing Justesen's 28 Sep 1973 date. This supplies the motive in the §8.3 reading: an informal demonstration of the full equipment capability to an outside visitor is *most likely to occur* at the close of a program, not during active protocol execution, because (a) the equipment is still running and (b) there is no longer protocol-scope risk.

## 2. Alternative readings and why they are weaker

### 2.1 Alternative A — "The primary report exists but has not yet been found"

**Strength:** weak.

**Against:**
- Six independent, topically-appropriate bibliographic filters (DTIC, NTIS, Lin 1978, Glaser 1976, Foster/Garrett/Ziskin 2021, PNL-2634 1978) have all returned null for a primary Sharp/Grove speech report — across both author-field and keyword searches, and across both catalog and full-text strategies where available. Independent failures across six filters with near-zero overlap in indexing policy is a strong negative-evidence ensemble.
- The FY74 WU 057 publication list registers the 1974 IEEE T-MTT mechanism paper as Publication #1 but registers *no* separate speech paper. WRAIR Annual Progress Reports are nearly comprehensive on WU-level publication output in this era.
- Sharp's own primary publication record (see §1.3 of main hunt log) shows no separately-authored speech paper in IEEE, PNAS, *Science*, *Nature*, or *Bioelectromagnetics* between 1973 and his departure from WRAIR.

**Cannot be fully ruled out** because:
- WRAIR 1981 Microwave Research Program Review Vols I–III are on-site only at Gorgas Memorial Library and have not yet been retrieved.
- The 1977 Senate Hearings Serial 95-49 full transcript is not yet captured in primary text.
- Sharp's NASA Ames / post-retirement personal papers location is unconfirmed.

### 2.2 Alternative B — "The report exists but was classified"

**Strength:** moderate but weakening as evidence accumulates.

**Against:**
- If classified, the formal WU 057 registration would still exist in the unclassified annual report as a project title or secure-annex cross-reference. The FY74 narrative explicitly discloses the WU 057 auditory study in the *unclassified* annual report — and discloses its halt. Classified follow-ons typically manifest as continuation Work Units (e.g., WU 058, WU 117) with a redacted-or-classified annex notice; no such WU is registered in the FY75-FY79 window that Thread A is investigating.
- ARPA/DARPA's own 1970s-era program documents — insofar as they have been declassified — do not reference a Sharp/Grove speech program. Brodeur 1977 (*Zapping of America* Ch 9, 24, 25) explicitly attributes the demo to an ARPA-supported WRAIR study but cites only the same Justesen 1975 / personal-communication chain.
- If the demonstration had been classified from inception, the most parsimonious expectation is that Justesen would not have been able to cite it at all, even as a personal communication, in a 1975 open-literature review. He did.

**Cannot be fully ruled out** because:
- Reclassification of informally-collected demonstration records is possible if the capability was later picked up by a classified successor program. Oscar 1980 AD-A090426 (USAMERDC) is the most plausible candidate for such a pickup, but its own narrative cites Justesen 1975 rather than any separate Sharp/Grove classified report (cross-ref `AD_A090426_PRIMARY_REVIEW.md`).

### 2.3 Alternative C — "Justesen conflated the WU 057 hearing-effect study with a separate speech event"

**Strength:** weak.

**Against:**
- Justesen's prose on p. 396 of the 1975 article is specific: "nine words plus a count from 1 to 10" with peak-power, pulse-rate, and average-power parameters; subject count (Sharp and Grove themselves); no trained animal intermediary. This is not phenomenological language; it is experimental-protocol language. Conflation between "characterizing the hearing effect in animals" and "transmitting human-decodable speech to human listeners" is implausible for a researcher of Justesen's stature.
- The specific calendar date (28 September 1973) is not invented; Justesen had no reason to fabricate a date. A date pointer in a footnote is strong evidence of contemporaneous note-taking.

### 2.4 Alternative D — "The demonstration did not occur as described"

**Strength:** weak but requires a brief against for evidentiary completeness.

**Against:**
- Justesen's 1975 article is peer-reviewed in *American Psychologist*, a leading journal; it was not retracted, and the Note 2 citation was not challenged in the subsequent bioeffects literature.
- The author network around the citation (§7.7 of main hunt log; also `SYSTEMS_AND_PATENTS_MASTER_INDEX.md`) is fully primary-source-verified for 1962–1998, with Frey, Sharp, Grove, Hawkins, Oscar, Lin, and Gandhi all independently publishing on adjacent mechanism and threshold work during the window.
- Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT explicitly establishes that the equipment capable of producing intelligible acoustic signals via thermoelastic coupling was in hand at WRAIR during the relevant window.
- The 1971 AD887119 ("WRAIR behavioral radiology bibliography" with Grove) and AD-758136 (Hawkins/Grove/Heiple/Schrot 1973) independently place Grove inside the WRAIR Microwave Research group in 1971–1973 — so the named co-author was a real WRAIR staff member, not an invented attribution.

## 3. Consequences for the V2K evidence matrix

### 3.1 T2 row (Intelligible semantic speech via RF hearing)

**Current grade (per `V2K_EVIDENCE_MATRIX.md`):** T2 — established intelligibility demonstration in the open literature (anchor: Justesen 1975).

**Proposed revised grade:** **T2-qualified** — credible isolated demonstration (Justesen 1975 Note 2) with no separately-authored primary technical report and no open-literature replication. Capability established by single-attestation + adjacent-mechanism corroboration (Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT); capability *not* established as a sustained or reproducible engineering result.

**Supporting language for the matrix cell (draft):**
> T2 is anchored by a single primary attestation: Justesen 1975 *American Psychologist* Note 2 citing a 28 September 1973 personal communication from Sharp and Grove. The WRAIR internal record (AD-776967 FY73 Vol 2; AD-A009338 FY74 Vol 2) confirms that Sharp and Grove conducted formal microwave-auditory-effect research in the window containing the demonstration, but frames the work as bioeffects/safety and documents its halt during FY74. No separately-authored Sharp/Grove speech primary has surfaced across six independent bibliographic filters. The most parsimonious reading is that an informal speech-modulation extension of the formal WU 057 study was demonstrated to Justesen at the close of the formal program and was never written into the annual report. Under this reading, T2 is credible at the demonstration level but *not* at the capability-chain level.

### 3.2 T4 row (Operational speech-transmission weapon)

**Current grade:** T4 — unsupported in the open literature.

**Proposed revised grade:** **T4 unchanged.** The §8.3 reading strengthens T4's unsupported classification: if the 1973 demo was a one-off informal extension, then no open-literature chain links it to any fielded capability. The downstream candidates (Oscar 1980 AD-A090426, MEDUSA SBIR N03-163, O'Loughlin/Loree 2003 Air Force patent US6587729) cite Justesen 1975 rather than any Sharp/Grove classified-report predecessor — i.e., they re-derive the auditory-effect physics rather than continuing a 1973 speech program.

### 3.3 Judicial-brief redline guidance

**Language to use:** "A single primary attestation in a peer-reviewed journal establishes that a nine-word receiverless speech-communication demonstration occurred at Walter Reed on 28 September 1973. The underlying WRAIR project (Work Unit 057) is separately documented in the unclassified Annual Progress Reports, but its formal scope did not include speech transmission, and the study was formally halted during the same reporting period. No separately-authored Sharp/Grove primary technical report on the speech experiment has been located in the open DTIC, NTIS, or NASA Technical Reports Server record."

**Language to avoid:** "The U.S. Army demonstrated operational microwave voice-to-skull capability in 1973" — this overreads the single-attestation demonstration into a programmatic capability claim that the evidence does not support. Downgrade to: "In 1973, two U.S. Army researchers at Walter Reed conducted a demonstration of nine-word speech communication via pulse-modulated microwaves, witnessed by an external scientist (Justesen) and reported by him in the open literature (1975 *American Psychologist*). The experiment was not formally documented by its principals; no replication in the open literature has been identified."

## 4. Confidence statement

- **Claim 1** (no separately-authored Sharp/Grove primary technical report exists in the open unclassified record): **confidence high.** Six independent bibliographic filters, two primary WRAIR annual reports, and Sharp's own publication record all concur.
- **Claim 2** (the 1973 demonstration occurred substantially as Justesen described): **confidence moderate-to-high.** Single primary attestation + verified author network + mechanism paper from the same team in the same window. Not replicable by the standards of experimental physics, but credible by the standards of historical documentary evidence.
- **Claim 3** (the demonstration was an informal extension of WU 057, not a classified program predecessor): **confidence moderate.** Strongly consistent with the FY74 WU 057 narrative, the topical-scope mismatch, and the absence of downstream replication. Cannot be fully ruled out that a classified successor exists; Thread A (FY75–FY79 follow-ons), Thread B (Justesen separate-authored papers), and Thread C (Sharp/Grove archival deposits) may still surface contrary evidence.
- **Claim 4** (Oscar 1980 USAMERDC AD-A090426 and downstream operational candidates re-derive rather than continue the 1973 demo): **confidence moderate-to-high.** Documented by citation-chain analysis in `AD_A090426_REFERENCE_CHAIN_EVIDENCE.md`.

5. **WRAIR 1981 Microwave Research Program Review (Vols I–III, Gorgas Memorial Library on-site only)** — a 1981 retrospective covering 1968–1981 may explicitly address the demo.
