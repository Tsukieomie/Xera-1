**Net implication for the V2K / synthetic-telepathy lineage**: Berman 1985 ref 24 is — exactly as the parent paper's topic (mouse/rat lethality at 2450 MHz) implied — a **lethality / bioeffects citation only**. The chase is therefore closed — Berman 1985 ref 24 is now a *positively identified* but *topically irrelevant* citation for the speech / V2K claim chain.

## 2. The candidate-composite hypothesis (and why it was right)

The Berman ref 24 page reference "pp. 1073-..." implied a multi-thousand-page composite volume — too large for any standalone Sharp/Grove/Hawkins technical report (which would be 30-50 pages, e.g., the Hawkins+Grove+Heiple+Schrot 1973 32-page report at AD-758136). Three pieces of evidence triangulated the correct composite:

1. **Glaser 1976 NMRI bibliography supplementary listing** ([zoryglaser.com](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/BIBLIOGRAPHY-FOR-RADIOFREQUENCY-AND-MICROWAVE-RADIATION.pdf)) refers to WRAIR microwave-bioeffects work as appearing "in **Annual Research Progress Report, Army Research and Development Report RCS MEDDH-288(RI)**, US Army Medical Research and Development Command." This identifies the Army-internal series report number.

2. **govinfo *Monthly Catalog of US Government Publications, July 1976*** ([GOVPUB-GP3-e8c7c9652591e9bb1e7179565ac24072](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/GOVPUB-GP3-e8c7c9652591e9bb1e7179565ac24072/pdf/GOVPUB-GP3-e8c7c9652591e9bb1e7179565ac24072.pdf)) snippet listed: "...annual progress report 1 July 1973 to 30 June 1974. — Washington... Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, 1974. **2 v. (v, 1584 p**...)" — a 1,584-page two-volume composite physically capable of containing pp. 1073-1074.

3. **Internet Archive DTIC catalog query** confirmed the complete RCS MEDDH-288(R1) series spans at least 1965-1979, with exact AD numbers per fiscal year. The FY73 edition (1 July 1972 – 30 June 1973, dated 30 Jun 1973) is **AD-774860 (Vol 1) / AD-776967 (Vol 2)**.

The "1973" date in Berman's ref 24 ("Progress Report... 1973") matched FY73 (dated 30 Jun 1973, covering 1 Jul 1972 – 30 Jun 1973), not FY74 (which would be dated 30 Jun 1974).

**Verification**: Direct OCR full-text examination of AD-776967 Vol II confirmed pp. 1073-1074 are inside Work Unit 057 — Sharp/Grove/Hawkins on microwave bioeffects. Match closed.

## 6. Implications for the V2K / synthetic-telepathy claim chain

**The Sharp/Grove September 1973 speech-into-head claim** has long been the speculative foundation for "voice-to-skull" / "synthetic telepathy" claims. The repo's prior scoring (per `SHARP_GROVE_PRIMARY_REPORT_HUNT.md`) classified the speech claim as:

> **Best primary unclassified evidence**: Justesen 1975 *American Psychologist* (PMID 1137231), itself reporting Sharp & Grove via "Personal communication, September 28, 1973" — i.e., journalistic-grade in primary status, not technical-report-grade.

**This addendum further hardens that classification**:

1. **Berman 1985 ref 24 is now positively identified** as AD-776967 pp. 1073-1074. There is no longer any uncertainty about what document it cites.

2. **The cited document, on the cited pages, contains only lethality/bioeffects content** — no speech demonstration, no auditory effects, no Frey-effect work, no microwave-modulated speech.

3. **Even if one read the entire Sharp/Grove/Hawkins Work Unit 057 narrative (pp. 1073-1077) and the entire 1,633-page Vol II of the FY73 WRAIR composite, no description of a speech-transmission demonstration appears anywhere** under any author for that fiscal year. The September 1973 demonstration that Justesen 1975 described falls **after** the FY73 reporting period closed (30 June 1973) and was therefore administratively too late to be reported in this volume — and a search of the corresponding FY74 volumes (AD-A009337/AD-A009338, dated 30 June 1974, which would cover the period containing the September 1973 demo) is recommended as the natural next step but is **out of scope** for closing Berman ref 24.

4. **Berman 1985's authorial purpose** in citing Sharp/Grove/Hawkins pp. 1073-1074 was to support Berman's own paper's lethality findings — Berman 1985 reports lethality in mice and rats exposed to 2450 MHz, and ref 24 supplies the immediately prior Sharp/Grove/Hawkins lethality data at exactly 2.45 GHz / 3.0 GHz on rats and mice. The citation is **topically appropriate within Berman 1985 as a lethality paper** and in no way connects to speech or V2K mechanisms.

**Net repository impact**: The Berman 1985 ref 24 lead is now **closed as topically irrelevant for the V2K speech lineage**. It strengthens — does not weaken — the existing finding that Justesen 1975 remains the sole unclassified contemporaneous published account of the Sharp/Grove September 1973 speech demo, and that no primary Army-internal technical report describing that specific demonstration has been located in the unclassified record through the DTIC, Internet Archive, NLM, or Glaser 1976 holdings.
