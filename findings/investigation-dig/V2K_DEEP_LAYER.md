## 1. Why Tier A ≠ Tier B (communication-theory framing)

**Tier A — Audible transient ("microwave hearing" / click-class).**

- RF pulse → localized rapid heating / thermoelastic expansion (classical continuum description in Foster–Finch, Sharp–Grove–Gandhi absorber measurements) → pressure transient → cochlea / auditory nerve → **heard** click, buzz, or burst.
- Experimental endpoints historically: threshold detection, loudness vs pulse-width scaling, corroborating hydrophone/analog acoustic measurements.
- This answers: *something that feels like inner-head sound without external acoustic source in some protocols.*

**Tier B — Intelligible speech as a covert channel.**

Semantic speech implies **controlled spectral-temporal modulation** resembling speech **phonemes** at **sufficient bandwidth and signal-to-distortion ratio** for a human to extract **lexical content**, not occasional cue detection.

Problems that Tier A endpoints do **not** automatically solve:

| Issue | Notes |
|--------|------|
| **Information rate vs channel capacity** | Clicks occupy a tiny basis compared with speech-coded audio; ramping repetition rate fights thermal/integration limits and perceptual masking. |
| **Encoding** | Turning text/intent into a pulsed waveform that survives skull/tissue dispersion is a **speech-coding problem** orthogonal to proving a single audible click exists. |
| **SNR under covert power caps** | Real environments add clutter, skull coupling variance, posture, and distance swings; feasibility memos rarely attach **measured BER / word-error** curves. |
| **Blinded intelligibility protocols** | What investigators actually need: reproducible WORD/Sentence lists, sham controls, scorer blinding (matrix Version 11). Secondary anecdotes fail this bar. |

**Net:** The repo can be **physics-honest** on Tier A and **epistemologically strict** on Tier B simultaneously.

## 5. What would falsify Tier A wholesale?

Demonstrating that historical MAE perceptual experiments were purely suggestive sham would take **successful negative replication** across independent labs with calibrated double-blind design—against the consensus of multiple independent primary references. Nothing in current open literature reviewed in Versions 11–13 supports such wholesale falsification.

### What would validate Tier B (partially)?

Would require at least:

1. Published **peer-reviewed** protocol with modulation parameters, subject N, sham, intelligibility scoring, cross-lab repetition.
2. Or **validated declassified** primary document with comparable rigor—not magazine retellings.

Until then, Tier B stays **explicitly unresolved** regardless of claimant testimony volume.
