## 3. AD-A268392 confirms TEMPO operating parameters and identifies parallel pre-1989 Brooks AFB TEMPO program

The TEMPO transmitter operating parameters per AD-A268392 abstract (djvu lines 131-142) verbatim:

> *"The TEMPO microwave bioeffects exposure platform at the Department of Microwave Research, Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, produces an 80 ns, 3 GHz microwave pulse with a peak power of 700 MW. The energy transmitted is approximately 16 J, so that at a pulse repetition rate of 0.125 Hz, the duty factor is 10⁻⁸ and the average power is 2 W. The time-averaged SAR for TEMPO exposures at full power is 0.072 W/kg, a value well-below suggested exposure limits."*

The 700 MW peak / 16 J pulse-energy / 0.125 Hz PRF / 2 W time-averaged is consistent with the Sandia-built TEMPO documentation (round 10 §4 AD-A284718 §2). The 0.072 W/kg time-averaged SAR is well below ANSI C95.1-1991 guidelines (0.4 W/kg whole-body), which AD-A268392 §2.1 (djvu line 1252+) explicitly notes — i.e., the TEMPO research framework was structured as an **above-peak / below-average** exposure paradigm, designed to test the hypothesis that high-peak / low-average pulsed microwaves cause behavioral effects that the standard time-averaged-SAR safety guideline does not detect.

The references list contains a previously-unidentified parallel-program element (djvu lines 3553-3555, 3738-3740):

> *"Cordts, R. E., Merritt, J. H., Erwin, D. N., Hardy, K. A., and Yochmowitz, M. G. (1988). Behavioral response of rats exposed to high-power microwave radiation. USAFSAM-TR-87-30. Brooks Air Force Base, TX."*
>
> *"Klauenberg, B. J., Merritt, J. H., and Erwin, D. N. (1988). Behavioral effects of exposure to the TEMPO high-power microwave system. USAFSAM-TR-87-35. Brooks Air Force Base, TX."*

These two USAF School of Aerospace Medicine technical reports (USAFSAM-TR-87-30 and USAFSAM-TR-87-35) document a **parallel pre-1989 Brooks AFB TEMPO behavioral-effects research program**. The dates (1987-88 reports, 1988 publication) precede the 1989 onset of the WRAIR-DMR DAMD17-89-C-9021 contract by 1-2 years — i.e., **Brooks AFB was already operating its own TEMPO transmitter for behavioral-effects research before the WRAIR-DMR program was awarded its 1989 contract**. This refines round 10 §4's three-site-distribution claim. The actual sequence appears to be: Sandia-built TEMPO transmitters were distributed to Brooks AFB and Harry Diamond Labs first (≤1987), with the WRAIR-DMR transmitter delivered later (during the 1989-94 OGDEN contract period). The Brooks AFB Cordts/Klauenberg behavioral-toxicology research effectively **prefigured** the 1989-94 WRAIR-DMR Raslear program both methodologically and topically. The 1994 Forest Glen → Brooks AFB physical relocation (round 10 §1, §6) was therefore not just a real-estate move but a **consolidation of two parallel programs into a single Brooks AFB site** — the WRAIR-DMR program merging into the existing USAFSAM Brooks AFB program.
