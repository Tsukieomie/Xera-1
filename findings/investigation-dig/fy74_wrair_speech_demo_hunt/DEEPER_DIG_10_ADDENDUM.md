## 4. Sandia National Laboratory — TEMPO transmitter contractor for WRAIR-DMR

The TEMPO (Transformer Energized Megavolt Output) high-peak-power pulsed-microwave transmitter at WRAIR-DMR is documented in AD-A284718 §2 (djvu lines 480-500) as **Sandia National Laboratory-built**:

> *"The Transformer Energized Megavolt Output (TEMPO) transmitter was developed for the WRAIR Department of Microwave Research by the Sandia National Laboratory. This transmitter was designed to provide scientists and researchers with a means of studying biological effects and potential health hazards associated with exposure to high-power pulsed microwaves. The unique exposure parameters which TEMPO provides the research community is an extremely high-peak-power pulse which is comprised of a very intense electrical field. … Since the delivery and installation of TEMPO by Sandia National Labs, the OGDEN engineering staff has been constantly improving and enhancing the transmitter system."*

A second Sandia-WRAIR connection appears at djvu lines 1107-1110:

> *"OGDEN staff is planning to improve further the FDTD code in order to be able to simulate the ultra wide band frequency generator which the Department purchased from Sandia National Laboratories."*

A third connection: TEMPO transmitter distribution (djvu lines 789-792):

> *"TEMPO has been designed as a prototype high pulse transmitter of which three have been manufactured. The transmitters have been distributed to the following organizations: Armstrong Labs at Brooks Air Force Base, Harry Diamond Labs and WRAIR. The OGDEN engineering staff has led the development of TEMPO through an evolution of change, which has clearly established the WRAIR TEMPO as the most reliable and productive machine of the three."*

## 5. Post-1980 microwave-hearing successor program at WRAIR-DMR (Seaman/Beblo/Raslear startle modification + Swearengen et al. cochleotomy)

The most structurally consequential round-10 finding is the documentation of a **post-1980 microwave-hearing successor program at WRAIR-DMR** that operationally tests whether the Frey-effect microwave-auditory mechanism requires a functional cochlea (i.e., is the perception thermoelastic-expansion-mediated through the cochlea, or does it have a CNS-direct pathway?). The body section (djvu lines 2536-2724) verbatim:

> *"Microwave pulses cause the same modification of acoustic startle as do sensory stimuli, and — A functional auditory periphery is necessary for microwave pulse modification of startle. In order to perform these tasks, a new microwave exposure apparatus was designed and constructed, and a surgical procedure for deafening the animal was developed."*

> *"One of the hypotheses that explains microwave-evoked body movements ('startle') was microwave hearing via thermoelastic expansion waves sensed by the cochlea. The movement of the cochlea fluid is essential in generating the auditory sensation through the auditory pathway. A deaf rat model with a bilateral patent cochlea is essential to evaluate the accuracy of this hypothesis. Deaf rats do not occur in any great frequency and ototoxicity of antimicrobials cannot produce consistent deafness by cochlea dysfunction. Therefore, it is necessary to develop a surgical procedure for deafening rats with patent cochlea to obliterate the primary site of microwave hearing. A surgical procedure for bilateral cochleotomy in rats was developed [Swearengen, Kittell, Davis, Raslear, Beblo and Colleton 1993]."*

The reference list confirms the published forms (djvu lines 3070-3110):

> *"Seaman, R.L., D.A. Beblo, and T.G. Raslear [1994]: Modification of acoustic and tactile startle by single microwave pulses. Physiol. Beh. 55: 587-595, 1994."*
>
> *"Seaman, R.L., and D.A. Beblo [1992]: Modification of acoustic startle by microwave pulses in the rat: a preliminary report. Bioelectromagnetics 13: 323-328."*
>
> *"Swearengen, J.R., C.L. Kittell, J.A. Davis, T.G. Raslear, D.A. Beblo, and C.A. Colleton [1993]: A surgical technique for bilateral cochleotomy in the Long-Evans rat. J. Invest. Surg. 6: 431-437."*
>
> *"Raslear, T.G., Y. Akyel, S.-T. Lu, J.R. Swearengen, D. Varle, M.L. DeAngelis, and R.L. Seaman [1992]: CW microwave fields evoked body movements in bilaterally cochleotomized rats. Proc. Ann. Int. Conf. IEEE EMBS 14: 295-296."*
>
> *"Raslear, T.G., Y. Akyel, F. Bates, S.-T. Lu, E.C. Elson, B. Akyel, and S.P. Mathur [1993]: The behavioral toxicology of high-peak, low average power, pulsed microwave irradiation. Technical Report 001-92, Washington, D.C.: Department of Microwave Research, Walter Reed Army Institute of Research."*

This 1992-1994 WRAIR-DMR cluster is the **direct operational successor** of the 1973 Sharp/Grove voice-modulation demonstration:

1. The 1973 Sharp/Grove demonstration (round 0 §1; Justesen 1975 *Am Psychol* Note 2) used the Frey-effect zero-crossing pulse-trigger method to induce **speech perception** in human subjects. The mechanism was open at the time — Frey 1962 had shown microwave-induced auditory perception, but the transduction site (cochlea? CNS-direct?) was unspecified.
2. The 1981 Lin textbook (round 7 §3 indirect; Lin 1978 round 6) and the 1985-1991 WRAIR-DMR thermal-effects literature converged on the **thermoelastic-expansion-via-cochlea** hypothesis: pulsed microwaves cause rapid local heating of head tissues, generating an acoustic pressure wave that the cochlea transduces as sound.
3. The 1992-1994 Seaman/Beblo/Raslear-Swearengen WRAIR-DMR program **directly tested** that hypothesis by performing bilateral cochleotomy on Long-Evans rats and measuring whether microwave-evoked startle and microwave-evoked body movements were abolished when the cochlea was surgically destroyed. Per the §2 quote ("Twelve bilateral cochleotomized rats and two sham bilateral cochleotomized rats were tested. … the majority of the cochleotomized rats failed to show any startle by this intense acoustic stimulus even under repetitive testing"), the cochleotomy was technically successful — and Raslear et al. 1992 IEEE EMBS 14:295-296 ("CW microwave fields evoked body movements in bilaterally cochleotomized rats") then tested whether CW microwave fields evoked body movements **after** cochleotomy. The 1993 Raslear-Akyel-Bates-Lu-Elson-Akyel-Mathur Technical Report 001-92 ("The behavioral toxicology of high-peak, low average power, pulsed microwave irradiation") is the synthesis report.

This 1992-1994 program is **the only documented late-WRAIR-DMR direct experimental test of the microwave-hearing transduction mechanism** the Sharp/Grove 1973 demonstration relied upon. Its publication channels (*Bioelectromagnetics* 13:323-328 in 1992, *Physiol. Behav.* 55:587-595 in 1994, *J. Invest. Surg.* 6:431-437 in 1993) are open peer-reviewed literature — i.e., this work was *not* classified or distribution-limited, in contrast to the 1973 demonstration's near-zero open-literature footprint. The structural significance is that **WRAIR-DMR returned, two decades after Sharp/Grove, to the question of whether the microwave-auditory effect is cochlea-mediated** — and published the work openly. The 1973 demonstration's documentary uniqueness within the post-1980 WRAIR-DMR record (no replication, no follow-up speech-induction experiment) is therefore **not** for lack of the institutional capability or interest in microwave-hearing research, but specifically for lack of further open-literature speech-induction work on the Sharp/Grove zero-crossing voice-pulse method.

## 6. Round-10 structural insight — the Forest Glen → Brooks AFB program-line transfer

Round 2 §6 noted a "FY75-79 documentary gap" at WRAIR-DMR consistent with the Forest Glen reconstruction completion. Round 7 §1 documented the late-1975 WRAIR-DMR roster via URSI. Round 10 now establishes that **the WRAIR-DMR program continued through 1985-1994 under contracted-services management (OGDEN BioServices) and was physically relocated to Brooks AFB Armstrong Labs in San Antonio TX during the 1993-1994 contract closeout**. The 1973 Sharp/Grove demonstration's WRAIR-DMR program-line therefore did not end with the 1981 Oscar/Gruenau/Folker/Rapoport *Brain Research* paper (round 7 §2) — it continued for an additional 13 years at WRAIR Forest Glen and is currently embodied as **Armstrong Labs RFR-bioeffects program at Brooks AFB San Antonio**. This is consistent with the round-7 §3 Brodeur ARPA→WRAIR program-line attribution: the program-line was structurally an Army electronic-warfare-bioeffects research program from inception (1971-72 USAMERDC contracts via Fort Belvoir), institutionally expressed as a WRAIR Walter Reed unit, and post-1994 expressed as an Air Force Armstrong Labs unit at Brooks AFB.

The round-10 reading of the Sharp/Grove 1973 demonstration's institutional trajectory:

> **1971-72 USAMERDC Fort Belvoir** (Frey 1972 / Hawkins-Grove 1973 / Sharp-Grove 1973, all on Project 1J662712AJ21) → **1974-1980 ARPA-WRAIR** (Brodeur 1976/1977 attribution) → **1980 Oscar USAMERDC AD-A090426** ("camouflage, decoy, deception" rationale) → **1981 Oscar/Gruenau/Folker/Rapoport NIH/NIA Brain Research** (peer-reviewed without operational framing) → **1985-1994 WRAIR-DMR Forest Glen** (Wachtel/Akyel/Hunt/Lu DAMD17-85-C-5083 + DAMD17-89-C-9021, thermal-effects + behavioral toxicology) → **1992-1994 Seaman/Beblo/Raslear-Swearengen cochleotomy program** (post-Sharp/Grove direct test of microwave-hearing transduction mechanism) → **1994+ Armstrong Labs Brooks AFB San Antonio TX** (institutional successor; AFRL/RHD).

The 28 September 1973 voice-modulation demonstration is **the only event on this 23-year program-line with a documented operational-deception rationale and a documented zero open-literature successor experiment**. All other events on the line either (a) were sub-rosa (Brodeur 1976/1977 ARPA-WRAIR window) and had no published successor program, or (b) were openly published (1985-1994 WRAIR-DMR thermal-effects + cochleotomy program) without a speech-induction component.
