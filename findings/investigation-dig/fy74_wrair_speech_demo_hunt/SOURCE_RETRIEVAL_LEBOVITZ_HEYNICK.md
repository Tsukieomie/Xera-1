### Author affiliation — verbatim from page 229

This **closes the round-12 §8 / round-13 §9 sustained carry on the Lebovitz, R.M., 1977 *Radio Sci* institutional affiliation** and confirms the round-12 §6 hypothesis that the 1977 senior author is R.M. ### Verbatim abstract content (page 229)

The paper studies the response of single auditory neurons in cats to 915 MHz pulse-modulated microwave radiation (PM-MWR). Total of **133 single auditory units examined; 63 responsive to both acoustic clicks and PM-MWR**. Threshold sensitivity at energy doses **as low as 4 µJ/g per pulse** (verbatim: "Threshold effects were observed at an energy dose as low as 4 μJ/g per pulse"). Auditory units with low characteristic frequencies (CFs below 1.2 kHz) appeared more responsive to pulsed MWR than units with higher CFs. The PSTH (post-stimulus time histogram) responses to pulsed MWR were generally similar to those for acoustic clicks. The authors interpret the data as supporting the view that microwave hearing is "mediated, at least in part, by an electromechanical interaction that is initiated distal to the cochlea" — i.e., consistent with the thermoelastic / mechanical activation of the auditory periphery.

### Methods — verbatim methodological detail (pp. 229-231)

- Adult domestic cats, mongrels, anesthetized with sodium pentobarbital (35 mg/kg IP) plus supplemental IV doses; later urethane (1 g/kg) + alpha-chloralose (40 mg/kg). Posterolateral aspect of the cerebellum aspirated to expose the proximal eighth nerve for single-unit recording.
- Acoustic clicks delivered via pulse-driven condenser earphone, contralateral ear stoppled. Background acoustic noise 40-50 dB SPL.
- PM-MWR generator: MCL Model 15022/6050, 915 MHz, applied via waveguide applicator 2-3 cm from dorsolateral aspect of cat's head. Applicator design provided in personal communication from A. W. Guy (i.e., the Arthur W. Guy line cross-cited in the WRAIR-DMR Lin/Foster/Finch dosimetry chain — round 11 §6).
- Peak output power 70 W; pulse durations 25-300 µs effective; pulse repetition rates ≤10/sec; averaged power density at the cat ≤1.0 mW/cm²; SAR ≤0.5 mW/g averaged medullary; calibration via thermistor probe in medulla after pentobarbital euthanasia (McRee 1974 caloric method).
- Shielded enclosure lined on three sides with Eccosorb Type H-4 microwave-absorbent material; plastic stereotaxic head-holder to minimize MWR-field interaction.
- Single-unit data analyzed by post-stimulus time histograms (PSTHs) on Ortec Model 4621/4620.

### Substantive evidentiary classification

This is **mechanism / physiology support only** — single-unit auditory-neuron responses to pulsed microwave radiation in anesthetized cats. **It is not evidence of speech transmission, intelligible-word transmission, or voice modulation.** The methodological framework studied here (pulse durations of 25-300 µs at 10/sec, peak power 70 W, SAR ≤0.5 mW/g) is the canonical thermoelastic-cochlea-mediated microwave-hearing protocol that supports the Tier A claim only.

The paper itself notes (page 229, end of §1) that "potential implications include the possibility that nonthermal modes of interaction may be neurophysiologically significant" — but the body of the paper concludes the *opposite* of speech-relevant modulation: the responses are click-like, latency-shortened by ~0.75 ms relative to acoustic clicks (consistent with bypassing the earphone-to-tympanum acoustic transit time), with response patterns "not grossly different from those to typical acoustic stimuli." This is the canonical Tier A microwave-auditory-effect mechanism finding, not a speech-induction finding.

Pages 4-8 not exhaustively scanned for this note; this is a partial scan, not a full-text bibliography audit.

### Sharp/Grove/Gandhi citation status — verbatim

Heynick 1987 cites **Sharp, J.C., H.M. Grove, and O.P. Gandhi, *Generation of Acoustic Signals by Pulsed Microwave Energy*, IEEE Trans. Microwave Theory Tech., Vol. 22, No. 5, pp. 583-584 (1974)** in the §3.1.4.2 Auditory Effects reference list (DjVu OCR lines 9265-9269; reproduced again at OCR lines 45842 ff.). Heynick discusses Sharp et al. (1974) twice in the body of §3.1.4.2:

- DjVu OCR lines 7962-7971 (page 116 region): the head-shielding experiment with RFR-absorber that demonstrated transduction of 1.5 GHz pulses to acoustic signals in absorber material outside the body, with measured propagation-delay times for distances 0.3-0.6 m, 14-µs pulses at ~3 pps, computed pulse power densities 7.5-15 kW/m² (750-1500 mW/cm²).
- DjVu OCR lines 7973-7983 (same page region): subsequent Sharp et al. (1974) tests showing carrier-frequency invariance from 1.2 to 1.6 GHz and at 2.45 GHz; absorber size/shape independence (down to 4 mm × 4 mm × 2 mm); aluminum-foil transduction; threshold pulse power ~275 W (estimated pulse-power densities 46-92 mW/cm²); pulse-repetition-rate tracking up to 500 pps. Quoted verbatim by Heynick: *"The sound produced from the absorber seemed to track the repetition rate of the microwave pulses."*

**Heynick's discussion of Sharp et al. (1974) is purely thermoelastic-mechanism content** — RFR-to-acoustic transduction in absorber material, threshold pulse power, carrier-frequency invariance, pulse-rate tracking. Heynick cites the **published** Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE MTT abstract; he does **not** cite, discuss, or allude to the Sharp/Grove September 1973 personal-communication voice-modulation demonstration that Justesen reported in 1975.

### Voice / speech / verbal / intelligible-modulation status — verbatim

The DjVu OCR of §3.1.4.2 Auditory Effects (DjVu lines ~7000-9300, corresponding to pp. 111-138) has **zero matches** for the substrings `voice`, `verbal`, `spoken`, `intellig` (intelligible / intelligibility), or `speech` in any auditory-effect or speech-induction context. Heynick's auditory-effect coverage is exclusively pulse-thermoelastic-mechanism content: Frey 1961, 1962, 1967, 1971; Frey and Messenger 1973; White 1963; Foster and Finch 1974; Sharp, Grove, and Gandhi 1974; Taylor and Ashleman 1974; Guy et al. 1975b; Lin and his collaborators on spherical-head acoustic resonance; Lebovitz and Seaman 1977 (per round-14 §1 above); Tyazhelov et al. 1979; and the click/chirp/Morse perception in Guy et al. 1975b at threshold peak energy density 40 µJ/cm² per pulse for 2.45 GHz in human subjects.

The Guy et al. (1975b) Morse-code perception passage (DjVu OCR lines 8078-8082) is verbatim: *"each pulse was perceived individually as a click and that short pulse trains were heard as chirps of tones corresponding to the pulse recurrence rate. Also, when the pulse generator was keyed manually, digital (Morse) code transmitted thereby could be interpreted accurately by the subject."* This is the upper bound of speech-relevant content in Heynick's §3.1.4.2 — Morse code via on/off keying of pulse trains, not voice modulation, not intelligible-word transmission.

### Substantive evidentiary classification

Heynick 1987 USAFSAM-TR-87-3 is the institutionally canonical Air Force microwave-bioeffects literature review of June 1987 (cited as reference 1 in both Cordts 1988 USAFSAM-TR-87-30 and Klauenberg 1988 USAFSAM-TR-87-35). The retrieved full text confirms:

1. **Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE MTT is cited and discussed** — purely as transduction-mechanism content (RFR-to-acoustic in absorber material, threshold pulse power, carrier-frequency invariance, pulse-rate tracking).
2. **Justesen 1975 *American Psychologist* is not cited.** This is the canonical primary-literature retelling of Sharp/Grove's September 1973 voice-modulation personal communication, and it is absent from a comprehensive 600+-reference USAF synthesis review of microwave bioeffects compiled by a SRI-International microwave-bioeffects researcher (Heynick) for USAFSAM Brooks AFB.
3. **No discussion of voice modulation, intelligible-speech induction, or recorded-words transmission** appears anywhere in §3.1.4.2 Auditory Effects.

This extends the **Sharp/Grove citation-silence pattern** documented in this directory through the canonical 1987 USAF synthesis review: the published Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE MTT abstract is cited as a thermoelastic-mechanism reference, while the Justesen-1975-retold Sharp/Grove 1973 voice-modulation personal communication is not cited. The pattern is now uniform across the USAFSAM 1987 → USAFSAM-TR-87-30/-35 1988 → DoD 2004 (Lin/Wang) institutional synthesis chain. **Tier A microwave-auditory-effect mechanism** (clicks, chirps, Morse perception via thermoelastic pulse-train transduction) is supported. **Tier B intelligible-speech induction** (Sharp/Grove 1973 voice-modulation personal communication, retold in Justesen 1975) is not endorsed, replicated, cited, or discussed in Heynick 1987.
