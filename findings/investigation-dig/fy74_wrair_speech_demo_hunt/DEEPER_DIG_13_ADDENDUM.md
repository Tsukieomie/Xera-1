## 2. Gypsy-vs-TEMPO emitter-generation sequence at AFWL Kirtland AFB

Round 12 §4 closed the Sandia-DOE-Albuquerque attribution of the TEMPO transmitter via AD-A193305 line 472, with AFWL Kirtland AFB identified as the operator-manual / RF-characterization institution. AD-A192199 closes the Gypsy-emitter half of the same lineage. Verbatim (djvu lines 511-516):

> *"Exposures at the Air Force Weapons Laboratory (AFWL) at Kirtland AFB, New Mexico, were made using the Gypsy virtual cathode oscillator (vircator) pulsed-microwave emitter (Fig. 1) (5). The oscillator is driven by a 500-kV water-filled Blumlein pulse power source, with a nominal pulse width of 140 ns, and operated at 1.64 GHz. The microwave energy produced by the oscillator is radiated by a 1.22-m (48 in.) diameter conical horn antenna in the TM01 mode."*

The Gypsy emitter operating parameters as primary-source-attested:

| Parameter | Gypsy (AD-A192199, 1986-87) | TEMPO (AD-A193305, 1987) |
|---|---|---|
| Frequency | 1.64 GHz | 1.3 GHz |
| Pulse width | 140 ns | (similar AFWL vircator class) |
| Drive | 500-kV water-filled Blumlein pulse power source | Sandia DOE Albuquerque transformer-based repetitive pulse power drive |
| Vircator class | axially-extracted | axially-extracted |
| Horn | 1.22-m diameter conical, TM01 mode | (similar) |
| Site of fielding | AFWL Kirtland AFB NM | AFWL Kirtland AFB NM (with WRAIR-DMR Forest Glen + Brooks AFB USAFSAM as recipient sites) |
| Operator team | "Advanced Weapons Branch" Cremer/McLaughlin/Moculeski (acknowledgments, djvu line 1831) | (same Advanced Weapons Branch by inference) |

The sequence is now end-to-end primary-source-attested:

1. **Pre-1986**: AFWL Kirtland Advanced Weapons Branch designs Gypsy axially-extracted vircator (1.64 GHz, 140 ns, Blumlein-driven). Voss/Demuth/Babbitt/Torraca/Miera AFWL-TR characterization in preparation.
2. **1986-87**: USAFSAM Brooks AFB (Cordts/Merritt/Erwin/Hardy/Yochmowitz) conducts behavioral-effects testing of Gypsy at AFWL Kirtland.
3. **February 1988**: USAFSAM-TR-87-30 / AD-A192199 published.
4. **1987**: AFWL Kirtland develops TEMPO axially-extracted vircator successor (1.3 GHz, with Sandia DOE Albuquerque transformer-based repetitive-pulse power drive enabling repeat-pulse capability — Gypsy was single-pulse-per-firing, TEMPO was repeat-pulse).
5. **January-June 1987**: USAFSAM Brooks AFB (Klauenberg/Merritt/Erwin) conducts behavioral-effects testing of TEMPO at AFWL Kirtland.
6. **March 1988**: USAFSAM-TR-87-35 / AD-A193305 published.
7. **20 October 1989**: WRAIR-DMR DAMD17-89-C-9021 contract begins (round 10 §1 / round 11 §1) with TEMPO transmitter installed at Forest Glen.
8. **21 May 1994**: WRAIR-DMR Forest Glen → Brooks AFB physical relocation (round 11 §4); OGDEN→McKesson contractor changeover.
9. **2002**: Brooks AFB → Brooks City-Base BRAC redesignation (round 12 §3 / round 12 §6).

The Gypsy-vs-TEMPO emitter generation differential is structurally significant: TR-87-35 (AD-A193305) line 467 verbatim identifies the TEMPO as *"with repeat pulse power source… similar to the single pulse power source Gypsy simulator"* — i.e., the engineering distinction was the addition of the Sandia transformer-based repeat-pulse drive enabling sustained-pulse-train experiments. Round 12 §4's note that "the Brooks AFB TEMPO had been fired only 212 times prior to the January-June 1987 testing" should be read in light of this: TEMPO was newly-commissioned in early 1987, with the Gypsy single-pulse predecessor having been operational since ≤1986.
