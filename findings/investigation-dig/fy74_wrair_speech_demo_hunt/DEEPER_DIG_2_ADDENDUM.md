## §1. Sharp/Grove primary-source date confirmed: 28 September 1973

Justesen's *American Psychologist* March 1975 article ("Microwaves and behavior", 30(3):391-401) is the primary unclassified published account of the speech demonstration. The article carries the demonstration's provenance in a citation note:

> **"Sharp, J. C., & Grove, M. Personal communication, September 28, 1973."**

The full passage, transcribed verbatim from the [Justesen 1975 PDF](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf):

> "Sharp and Grove (Note 2) found that appropriate modulation of microwave energy can result in direct 'wireless' and 'receiverless' communication of speech. They recorded by voice on tape each of the single-syllable [words] for digits between 1 and 10. The electrical sine-wave analogs of each word were then processed so that each time a sine wave crossed zero reference in the negative direction, a brief pulse of microwave energy was triggered. By radiating themselves with these 'voice-modulated' microwaves, Sharp and Grove were readily able to hear, identify, and distinguish among the 9 words. The sounds heard were not unlike those emitted by persons with artificial larynxes."

Justesen's article was developed from a seminar he gave on **21 August 1974** at the University of Utah and was published **March 1975**. The September 28, 1973 personal-communication date therefore precedes both events and **falls squarely within the WRAIR FY74 reporting period (1 July 1973 – 30 June 1974)** — the period covered by AD-A009337/AD-A009338, which contains the WU 057 entry "Modulated microwave signals" identified in the parent file.

This **does not** constitute corroboration that a speech demonstration was performed under WU 057's funding, since WU 057's published outputs (Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT 22(5):583-585) describe only [pulse-rate auditory thresholds](https://doi.org/10.1109/TMTT.1974.1128293), not speech encoding. But it does **place the demonstration's date inside WU 057's reporting window**, which is the strongest temporal alignment that can be established from open sources.

## §2. Frey/Feld/Frey 1975 ANYAS — citation correction

Round 1 cited the BBB report referenced in WU 057's FY74 entry as Frey, Feld, Frey (1975) ANYAS 247:**433-438**. The correct pagination is **433-439**.

- **DOI:** [10.1111/j.1749-6632.1975.tb36019.x](https://nyaspubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1749-6632.1975.tb36019.x)
- **Title:** "Neural Function and Behavior: Defining the Relationship"
- **Authors:** Allan H. Frey, S. R. Feld, A. B. Frey
- **Volume:** Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences, **Vol. 247** ("Biologic Effects of Nonionizing Radiation"), February 1975
- **Pages:** 433-**439** (corrects round 1)

The Wiley landing page returns no abstract or methods text — the body is paywalled and no abstract is exposed. Confirmation that the BBB parameters in Frey/Feld/Frey 1975 match WU 057's "auditory study" parameters therefore remains pending on access to the full PDF (held in NYAS print archives and subscriber libraries).

## §3. Hawkins-WRAIR-Fort Belvoir bridge — RESOLVED

Round 1 speculated that T. Daryl Hawkins moved from WRAIR to USAMERDC Fort Belvoir at some point between FY74 and 1977 (the year of the Oscar & Hawkins *Brain Research* paper, whose listed affiliation is Fort Belvoir). [NTIA Report 79-19](https://ehtrust.org/wp-content/uploads/79-19.pdf) ("Federal Radiofrequency Radiation Research Programs, Fiscal Years 1976 through 1978"), Appendix A, **resolves this bridge**:

### FY77 WRAIR projects listing Hawkins:
| Project | Investigators | Frequency | Program areas |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Modulated Microwave Bio-Effects: Nervous System (rat)** | **K. Oscar, T. D. Hawkins, E. Hunt** | 1300 MHz | 2, 7, 8C, 8F |
| Microwave Radiation Effects on CNS Excitability (rat) | T. D. Hawkins, E. Hunt | 2450 MHz | — |
| Stress Factors with Microwave Irradiation | E. L. Hunt, J. F. Schrot, T. D. Hawkins | — | — |

### FY78 WRAIR projects:
- Hawkins is **not listed**. Only E. L. Hunt remains as principal microwave-bioeffects investigator at WRAIR.

**Reconstructed timeline:**
- FY74 (Jul 73 – Jun 74): Hawkins at WRAIR (parent file, WU 057 lineage)
- FY77 (Oct 76 – Sep 77): Hawkins still at WRAIR, co-investigator with Oscar on **"Modulated Microwave Bio-Effects: Nervous System"** (1300 MHz)
- FY78 (Oct 77 – Sep 78): Hawkins absent from WRAIR roster — departure between **late 1977 and early 1978**
- 1977 publication: Oscar & Hawkins, *Brain Research* 126:281-293, [PMID 861720](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/861720/) — Hawkins's listed affiliation is USAMERDC Fort Belvoir on the published paper, even though FY77 NTIA listings still place him at WRAIR.

### Reinterpretation of the round-1 hypothesis

Round 1 framed the Oscar/Hawkins 1977 *Brain Research* paper as a Fort-Belvoir-only product whose WRAIR-era institutional roots had been lost in the FY75-79 documentary gap. The NTIA 79-19 Appendix A listing inverts this:

> The 1977 *Brain Research* paper was a **WRAIR–USAMERDC collaboration** during a window in which both authors had institutional ties at WRAIR. Oscar's primary affiliation was Fort Belvoir; Hawkins was still at WRAIR's Forest Glen Annex through at least Sep 1977. The "Modulated Microwave Bio-Effects: Nervous System" project at **1300 MHz** with the **Oscar/Hawkins/Hunt** team listed under WRAIR's FY77 program **is in essence the protocol that produced the 1977 paper**.

The published paper's "Fort Belvoir" affiliation is therefore the corresponding-author institution, not the work's actual lab home. The Hawkins career bridge is closed: he was a continuous WRAIR microwave-bioeffects investigator from at least FY74 through FY77 and departed for Fort Belvoir (or elsewhere) only after the 1977 paper's experimental work was complete.

## §4. WRAIR Forest Glen Annex 1976-78 reconstruction — explains FY75-79 gap

NTIA Report 79-19 also documents that during the FY76-FY78 reporting window, WRAIR's Forest Glen Annex underwent a **major facility reconstruction**:

- Three anechoic chambers built/upgraded
- Animal-housing rooms reconstructed
- HVAC systems replaced

The report carries an explicit operational caveat:

> "Unfortunately, it has been necessary to suspend research projects, including those of other agencies and investigators, that require use of the facilities under reconstruction."

This is the most parsimonious explanation for the FY75-FY79 documentary gap identified in round 1 (i.e., why the public DTIC record holds AD-A009337/AD-A009338 for FY74 and ADA108791 for FY80 with no released APRs in between):

> The microwave-bioeffects program **was operationally disrupted** during much of FY76-FY78 by physical reconstruction of the very chambers in which the irradiation experiments were conducted. APRs for those years still exist (the WRAIR institutional record is continuous), but the public-domain releases through DTIC apparently lapsed — quite possibly because reduced experimental output combined with the post-Pandora classification posture (see §5) produced a period of low public reporting.

This reframes the FY75-79 gap: it is **partly structural** (reconstruction), **partly post-Pandora classification posture**, and **only partly a search/release artifact**. Round 1 had treated the gap as a single phenomenon; the NTIA 79-19 evidence breaks it into at least two distinct mechanisms.

## §5. 1977 Senate hearings — formal identification

Round 1 referenced "Senate hearings on microwave research" in vague terms. The hearings are now identified:

### Primary hearing record:
- **Title:** "Oversight of Radiation Health and Safety"
- **Committee:** U.S. Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation
- **Dates:** June 16, 17, 27, 28, 29, **1977**
- **Serial No.:** 95-49
- **Pages:** 1,224

### Companion hearing:
- **Date:** May 23, 1977
- **Committee:** Senate Committee on Human Resources, Subcommittee on Health and Scientific Research

### Government response in record
A [DARPA letter dated 15 September 1977](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/rhwvuj-tzy4b/14.pdf) to Senate Commerce Committee chairman Warren G. Magnuson is in the public record (National Security Archive). The letter confirms:

- **Project Pandora** total cost: **$4,615,000**
- **Pandora terminated:** March **1970**
- **All Pandora assets transferred to WRAIR** at termination

This DARPA letter is the smoking-gun open-source confirmation that the Pandora-to-WRAIR institutional baton-pass occurred in March 1970, three years before the 28 Sept 1973 Sharp/Grove demonstration date established in §1.

### Contemporary press coverage
A *60 Minutes* segment aired June 19, 1977 (mid-hearings) discussed the Senate proceedings. Transcript available at the CIA reading room: [CIA-RDP88B01125R000300120111-8](https://www.cia.gov/readingroom/document/cia-rdp88b01125r000300120111-8). Named principals in the broadcast:

- **Sam Koslov** — Navy chief scientific adviser on microwave bioeffects
- **Milton Zaret** — ophthalmologist, microwave-cataract research
- **John Osepchuk** — Raytheon scientist, microwave safety
- **Dr. Appleton** — Walter Reed chief ophthalmologist (Pandora-era)

The *60 Minutes* transcript does not name Sharp, Grove, or Hawkins, and there is no on-record reference to WU 057 in the materials reviewed.

## §8. Outstanding leads (not closed in this round)

1. **Sharp's NIH/HHS post-WRAIR papers.** Sharp moved to NIH in the late 1970s. His post-WRAIR publication record and any oral-history interviews would be the most authoritative open source for retrospective comment on the 28 Sept 1973 demonstration. Not pursued in this round.
2. **FY78-79 program continuation under Hunt alone.** With Hawkins gone after FY77, E. L. Hunt is the only remaining microwave-bioeffects investigator in the WRAIR roster. Whether the BBB-modulation line continued, was redirected to "Stress Factors with Microwave Irradiation," or was wound down, is documentable in the still-unreleased FY78-FY79 APRs.
3. **Frey/Feld/Frey 1975 ANYAS full text.** Pages 433-439 of ANYAS Vol. 247 hold the BBB experimental parameters. Confirmation that those parameters match WU 057's auditory-study parameters remains pending physical access.
4. **WRAIR Microwave Research Program Review Volumes I-III (1981).** Listed in the WRAIR Archives Series I folders 112-114. Held on-site only at the Gorgas Memorial Library. Not retrievable from open sources in this round; held over.
5. **Justesen Kansas Medical Center research files.** The 28 Sept 1973 personal-communication note in Justesen 1975 means a primary-source contemporaneous record of the demonstration most likely exists in Justesen's own files at KU Medical Center Archives or the Kenneth Spencer Research Library (KU). This is the single highest-value remaining open lead.
6. **1977 Senate hearings full transcript (Serial 95-49, 1224 pp).** Held on-deposit by GPO and major federal-document libraries. Not yet pulled in this round; would resolve whether WU 057, Sharp, Grove, or Hawkins names appear in the 1977 Senate record.
