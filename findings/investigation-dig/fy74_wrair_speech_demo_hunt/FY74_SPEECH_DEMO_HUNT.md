### 3.2 Narrative outline (FY74 WU 057)

The FY74 narrative is organized into seven sub-sections (in the order the report presents them):

1. **Lethal effects of 2.45 GHz as a function of power density level in mice** — extension of FY73 rat work to mice; mice consistently more sensitive at 100, 150, 200, 300, 400 mW/cm² (lines 9324–9341).
2. **Differential lethal effects of 1.7 GHz and 2.45 GHz** — at 100 mW/cm², 1.7 GHz is more lethal than 2.45 GHz in 150 g rats (median time-to-convulsion 801 s vs 1260 s) (lines 9343–9363).
3. **Lethality as a function of body orientation relative to E and H vectors** — E ‖ a is consistently more lethal than H ‖ a; effect amplifies at lower frequencies (e.g. at 0.985 GHz: 269 s vs 865 s) (lines 9365–9398). This is the first known animal-to-human scaling opportunity for resonant-absorption frequencies (~40–55 MHz scaled to man), thanks to absorption data collected by Dr. Om P. Gandhi during a three-month visit.
4. **Systematic interrelationships of microwave frequency, E-field orientation and size of experimental subject as codeterminants of microwave lethality** — large lethality study at 0.985, 1.7, 2.45, 3.0 GHz with 0.70 GHz projected as last stage; mice (25–30 g), small rats (100–120 g), large rats (380–400 g) (lines 9400–9474).
5. **Modulated microwave signals** — *the section of central interest to this investigation; transcribed in §4 below* (lines 9476–9489).
6. **Determination of dielectric properties of tissue** — fat, brain, muscle, liver, lung from multiple species including man; human tissues not significantly different from animal tissues (lines 9491–9504).
7. **Microwave bioeffects current-awareness digest** — multi-agency (Army/AF/Navy/FDA-BRH/EPA) Franklin Institute Research Laboratories quarterly digest under ARO Grant DAHCO4-74-G-0132; first issue June 1974 (lines 9506–9519).
8. **Ocular effects of microwaves** — Army-installations populations survey + albino-rabbit chronic exposure (15 min/day × 30 days, CW and pulsed); cataracts only at ≥400 mW/cm²; no lenticular changes <300 mW/cm² after one year (lines 9525–9551).

## 4. THE KEY FINDING — "Modulated microwave signals" subsection

**Verbatim transcription from AD-A009338, OCR lines 9476–9489 (printed pp. 985–986):**

> **Modulated microwave signals.**
>
> An initial series of studies designed to better understand the so-called hearing effect produced by extremely low average power levels have been completed. Two observations have led to a temporary cessation of this work. These were (a) the observation that non-biologic material could serve as a transducer of the energy to acoustic signals which can be used as a model without exposing biological material and (b) the report that signals similar to those used in the auditory study may cause changes in the blood brain barrier of experimental animals. This latter observation has been investigated during the latter half of the reporting period and, to date, not been replicated. However, the implications of these observations are of such great importance that further work is in progress.

This is the **first direct, unclassified, Army-internal documentary acknowledgement** that Joseph C. Sharp and H. Mark Grove conducted formal "hearing effect" / "auditory study" research at WRAIR during the FY74 reporting window — i.e. the same window inside which Justesen 1975 places the September 28 1973 "speech demonstration." The FY73 Vol 2 narrative (AD-776967) had not used the words "hearing", "auditory", or "modulated" in the WU 057 sections; the FY74 narrative does, explicitly.

### 4.1 Topical analysis of the "Modulated microwave signals" subsection

**What the WRAIR narrative actually says:**

| Element | Narrative wording | Interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **Phenomenon** | "the so-called hearing effect produced by extremely low average power levels" | The Frey-effect microwave auditory phenomenon (Allan Frey 1962, *J. Appl. Physiol.* 17:689) |
| **Stimulus characterization** | "Modulated microwave signals" | Pulse-modulated RF, consistent with the Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT paper "Generation of acoustic signals of pulsed microwaves" (DOI 10.1109/TMTT.1974.1128293) |
| **Observed output** | "acoustic signals" (transduced from microwave energy) | Acoustic transients, NOT speech, NOT phonemes, NOT verbal stimuli |
| **Disclosed mechanism (a)** | "non-biologic material could serve as a transducer of the energy to acoustic signals" | Confirmation of the thermoelastic-expansion / electrostrictive mechanism — i.e. the effect is *not* a special biological-tissue phenomenon |
| **Disclosed mechanism (b)** | "signals similar to those used in the auditory study may cause changes in the blood brain barrier" | Implicit reference to Frey 1973 / Oscar 1977-era BBB-permeability literature — concerns over health-and-safety, not capability |
| **Status** | "temporary cessation of this work" / "further work is in progress" | Officially halted but framed as a pause, not a termination |

**What the narrative does NOT say:**
- No mention of *speech* being transmitted into a head.
- No mention of *voice*, *verbal*, *phonemes*, *intelligibility*, *spoken* words.
- No mention of *Justesen*, *Sharp's voice*, or any human subject (including any of the investigators themselves) being a recipient of transmitted intelligible content.
- No mention of the date 28 September 1973.
- No description of the modulation scheme that would be required to encode intelligible speech (e.g., the AM voice-modulation Justesen 1975 reports on p. 396 of *American Psychologist*).

**Framing assessment:** The FY74 disclosure positions the Sharp/Grove auditory work as **bioeffects/safety research** — equivalent to the rest of WU 057, which is overwhelmingly concerned with lethality thresholds, ocular damage, dielectric measurement, and absorption dosimetry. The "hearing effect" is presented as a *phenomenon to be characterized and dosed*, not a *capability to be developed*. The two stated reasons for halting (non-bio transducer model adequate + BBB safety concern) are both biophysical/safety justifications, not capability or operational ones.

## 9. Verdict on the FY74 evidence vis-à-vis the speech-demonstration claim

### What FY74 corroborates (positive findings)

1. **Sharp/Grove DID conduct formal microwave-auditory-effect research at WRAIR during FY74** — confirmed by the explicit "Modulated microwave signals / so-called hearing effect / auditory study" narrative on printed pp. 985–986 of AD-A009338. This is the strongest unclassified Army-internal corroboration available that the Sharp/Grove team studied the microwave hearing effect in the period containing Justesen's 28-Sept-1973 date.
2. **The WRAIR-internal auditory work is officially linked to the published mechanism paper** Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT "Generation of acoustic signals of pulsed microwaves" (publication #1 in WU 057's FY74 publications list). The mechanism paper and the WRAIR auditory study are the same line of work.
3. **The work was officially halted in FY74** — for two stated reasons: (a) a non-biological model is adequate (electrostrictive/thermoelastic transducer), and (b) BBB-permeability concerns at similar exposure parameters.

### What FY74 does NOT corroborate (negative findings)

1. **No mention of speech, voice, verbal stimuli, phonemes, words, or intelligible content** anywhere in WU 057.
2. **No mention of human subjects (other than ophthalmic-survey populations)** being exposed to modulated microwaves for auditory purposes.
3. **No mention of the 28 September 1973 demonstration**, no mention of Don Justesen, no mention of any visit by an outside investigator to receive an auditory demonstration.
4. **No mention of voice-modulation schemes** (the "single voice modulation of microwave radiation" Justesen 1975 describes on pp. 396–397 is absent from any WRAIR narrative).

### Synthesis

The FY74 disclosure **partially corroborates** Justesen 1975's claim that Sharp/Grove had auditory work in the relevant period — but **only at the level of "hearing effect / acoustic-signal" research**, not as a speech-transmission demonstration. The Army-internal record describes the work as bioeffects/safety dosimetry of the Frey effect; Justesen's 1975 narrative escalates this to a demonstration in which Sharp's recorded voice was modulated onto a microwave carrier and "heard" intelligibly inside a subject's head.

The two records are not contradictory but they are not the same record either:
- **WRAIR's formal record** = Frey-effect bioeffects characterization, halted, mechanism paper published in IEEE T-MTT.
- **Justesen's published recollection** = an informal speech-modulation demonstration on 28 September 1973, transmitted to him as a personal communication.

The most parsimonious reading consistent with both records is that **a brief, informal speech-modulation extension of the formal "Modulated microwave signals" study was conducted around the time the formal study was being wound down (latter half of FY74 reporting period), demonstrated to a visiting outside scientist (Justesen), and never written into the formal WU 057 narrative because it fell outside the scope of the bioeffects/safety mission and because the formal study was being closed.** The FY74 disclosure does not require this reading, but it is consistent with it; the FY74 disclosure also does not contradict the alternative reading that Justesen 1975 conflates the bioeffects "hearing effect" study with a separate speech demonstration.

The FY74 record therefore **does not constitute a primary technical-report account of the speech demonstration itself**. The Justesen 1975 article remains the primary unclassified published account of the alleged speech demonstration — as already concluded in `SHARP_GROVE_PRIMARY_REPORT_HUNT.md`.

## 14. Successor work — round 3

A third deeper-dive iteration extending this file and `DEEPER_DIG_2_ADDENDUM.md` is at `DEEPER_DIG_3_ADDENDUM.md` in the same directory. Round 3 closes the Sharp post-WRAIR career lead and surfaces a documentary anomaly in Justesen's later writings:

- **Sharp post-WRAIR career RESOLVED, round-1 hypothesis RETRACTED.** Sharp moved to **NASA Ames Research Center** in 1974 (not NIH in the late 1970s as round 1 hypothesized), where he served until 1994, finishing as Director of the Office of Space Research (1989-94). After NASA Ames he taught at Southern Utah University. He did not continue publishing on microwave bioeffects after leaving WRAIR.
- **WRAIR exit window narrowed** to late 1973 / early 1974 — after the 28 Sept 73 demonstration but before Justesen's late-1974 manuscript. Justesen's March 1975 *American Psychologist* article calls Sharp "former director" — consistent with the 1974 NASA Ames start.
- **Justesen's 1979 NYAM retrospective DROPPED the Sharp/Grove speech material.** Justesen's December 1979 *Bulletin of the New York Academy of Medicine* review ("Behavioral and psychological effects of microwave radiation," 55(11):1058-78, [PMID 120208](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/120208/)) covers the same biophysical territory as 1975 but contains **no mention** of Sharp, Grove, or voice-modulated microwaves. This is not dispositive but is a notable documentary anomaly given that Brodeur (1977) and Lin (1978) had by then canonized the citation in trade and academic literature.
- **Brodeur 1977 *Zapping of America* directly attests Sharp's Pandora role:** "Joseph C. Sharp, who had worked on Project Pandora when he was chief of experimental psychology at the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research..." — second open-source primary attestation alongside Justesen 1975.
- **Lin 1978 *Microwave Auditory Effects and Applications*** (Charles C. Thomas) canonized the Sharp/Grove citation in the foundational academic monograph.
- **Mark Grove** identified by Justesen 1975 as "an electronic engineer"; absent from FY77 NTIA 79-19 listings; most likely an instrumentation staff engineer rather than independent PI — explains lack of independent publication trail post-1974.
- **Synthesis:** The Sharp/Grove demonstration is a **single-attestation event** — described once primarily (Justesen 1975 Note 2 personal communication, on Sharp/Grove's own account, 28 Sept 1973), thereafter cited (Brodeur 1977; Lin 1978) but never independently replicated, re-described in primary form, or revisited by Justesen himself in print.

Remaining open leads (carry forward): Justesen's KU Medical Center research files (still highest-value remaining lead); WRAIR FY75-79 institutional records (Forest Glen on-site); WRAIR 1981 Microwave Research Program Review (Gorgas); 1977 Senate hearings Serial 95-49 (GPO); Sharp's NASA Ames or post-retirement personal papers (location unknown; Southern Utah University holdings unverified); Frey/Feld/Frey 1975 ANYAS 247:433-439 full text BBB parameters.
