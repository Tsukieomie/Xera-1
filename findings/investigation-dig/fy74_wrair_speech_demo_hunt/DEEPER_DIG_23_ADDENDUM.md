### 1.2 Lin 1980 abstract — Tier A only

The Lin 1980 abstract, verbatim from the [Academia.edu paper-detail page](https://www.academia.edu/780670/The_microwave_auditory_phenomenon) and consistent with the [IEEE Xplore abstract endpoint](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/1455852/) and the [Wikipedia microwave-auditory-effect article](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microwave_auditory_effect) external-link summary, examines:

> *"electrophysiological auditory activity ... elicited by exposing the brains of laboratory animals to rectangular pulses of microwave energy. These results suggest that a microwave auditory phenomenon is evoked by a mechanism similar to that responsible for conventional sound reception and that the primary site of interaction resides peripheral to the cochlea. A comparison of the pressure amplitudes ... in a homogeneous planar layer of brain matter that is irradiated by a microwave pulse, indicates that the peak pressure due to thermal expansion is much greater than either radiation pressure or electrostriction."*

Vocabulary footprint: animal electrophysiology, peripheral-to-cochlea site of interaction, thermal-expansion pressure, radiation pressure, electrostriction, brain-size acoustic-property dependence of induced sound frequency. **Zero Tier B vocabulary** in the abstract: no "voice," no "speech," no "intelligible," no "spoken," no "communication," no "modulated digit," no "9 of 10," no "Sharp," no "Grove," no "Justesen," no "demonstration."

The abstract framing is **mechanism-and-experiment**: animal-electrophysiology evidence + thermoelastic-vs-radiation-pressure-vs-electrostriction comparison. This is **pure Tier A** territory — exactly the Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT mechanism letter's terrain, extended into peer-reviewed-engineering-review form.

A peer-reviewed 2021 commentary on weaponization-feasibility that does not cite, refute, contextualize, or even mention this evidence is **structurally telling**: either the evidence is not retrievable in primary form (which Round 16 §1-§3 and Round 22 §2 establish), or the evidence is judged by peer-reviewed engineering commentators as not bearing on the question.

2. **The independent-authorship dimension.** Foster, Garrett, and Ziskin are not Lin's co-authors and are not WRAIR/USAFSAM-affiliated. The commentary's silence on Sharp/Grove is therefore a **2021 decision by independent peer-reviewed engineering commentators not to admit the demonstration into their evidence base**, not a pre-Havana-syndrome era artifact.

The Foster/Garrett/Ziskin 2021 silence on Sharp/Grove parallels the Round 11-15 9-document institutional-record silence (1981-2004 WRAIR/USAFSAM/McKesson record, zero Tier B vocabulary) but extends the silence pattern into the **peer-reviewed-engineering-commentary** stratum and into the **post-Havana-syndrome 2021 contemporaneity**.

## 5. Cross-stratum coherence: the Tier B silence is multi-channel, not single-channel

The Round 1-22 finding had been that the Tier B claim is single-source. Round 23 adds the structural-coherence observation: **the silence is multi-channel and consistent across genre strata**. Specifically:

- The 1976 government-bibliography silence (Glaser 1976, Round 22 §1) is a **catalogue-level** silence.
- The 1978 engineering-textbook footprint (Lin 1978 ch.7, Round 22 §2) is the **single positive node** — one passage, single-source.
- The 1980 peer-reviewed-engineering-review silence (Lin 1980, this round §1) is a **review-genre** silence by the same author who carried the claim into the textbook two years earlier.
- The 1981-2004 institutional-record silence (Rounds 11-15) is an **institutional-genre** silence at the very institutions (WRAIR, USAFSAM) that hosted the original demonstration.
- The 2007 peer-reviewed-engineering-update silence (Lin & Wang 2007, this round §2) is an **update-genre** silence by the same lead author who carried the claim into the 1978 textbook 29 years earlier.
- The 2021 peer-reviewed-engineering-commentary-on-weaponization silence (Foster/Garrett/Ziskin 2021, this round §3) is an **independent-authorship + post-Havana-syndrome** silence on the exact question the Tier B demonstration would, if real, naturally answer.

The **same-author dimension** is structurally important: Lin himself, who carried the Tier B retelling into engineering canon in 1978 (Round 22 §2), then chose **not** to carry it into either his 1980 *Proc IEEE* peer-reviewed engineering review (this round §1) or his 2007 *Health Phys* peer-reviewed engineering update (this round §2). The decision-not-to-propagate is the author's own — it is not a downstream-reception artifact. The Tier B claim's confinement to the textbook stratum is therefore a **structural choice by the author** when moving from textbook genre to peer-reviewed-engineering-review genre, not a downstream filtering effect.

This adds an interpretive frame to Round 22's "structural carrier" finding: **Lin 1978 ch.7 is not just the only engineering-canon Tier B carrier — it is the only Tier B carrier that the same author was willing to carry, and the author then declined to propagate it into peer-reviewed engineering-review form in 1980, 2007, and (per chapter-1 citation patterns retrieved this round) the same author's 2021 successor textbook treats Lin 1980 as a Tier-A-mechanism source only.**

## 6. Why this matters for the Tier-A/Tier-B framework

Round 16 had established the Tier A / Tier B distinction as a primary-source-record readout. Round 22 had established that the Tier A / Tier B distinction is preserved at the engineering-textbook and government-bibliography strata, with one Tier-B-positive node (Lin 1978 ch.7). The cumulative round 1-23 finding is therefore:

- **Tier A (clicks/Eccosorb mechanism, thermoelastic theory, animal electrophysiology, perception thresholds)** — propagated through the entire engineering-canon stratum-set: peer-reviewed primary papers (Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974, Foster/Finch 1974, Guy et al. 1973-1975, Frey 1961-1962, Lin 1976), peer-reviewed engineering reviews (Lin 1980), peer-reviewed engineering updates (Lin & Wang 2007), peer-reviewed engineering commentaries on weaponization (Foster/Garrett/Ziskin 2021), engineering textbooks (Lin 1978 ch.4), government bibliographies (Glaser 1976), institutional records (1981-2004 9-doc set), and the Lin 2021 successor textbook chapter 1.

The asymmetry between Tier A and Tier B is therefore now established as **mathematically multiplexed across all engineering-canon strata vs. mathematically minimal at exactly one stratum (textbook), with the same-author decision to confine confirmed by the 1980 + 2007 declines to propagate**.

## 7. Cowan-substack-class citation-fabrication audit (Round 22 §5 carry, advanced one notch)

The Cowan-substack-class fabrication pattern is therefore **a predictable response to the structural-authority gap** between (i) the only real Tier B engineering-canon source (1978 textbook chapter) and (ii) the perceived-needed Tier B citation form (peer-reviewed engineering-review article in a high-authority journal). When a real high-authority Tier B engineering-review citation does not exist, downstream authors invent one.

This is a falsifiable prediction of where future Cowan-substack-class fabrications will cluster: **at fabricated peer-reviewed-engineering-review articles or fabricated Federal-publication identifiers, not at fabricated textbook chapters**. The Round 16 §4 fabricated *FDA 77-8026* citation fits this pattern (a Federal-publication identifier, perceived as higher-authority than a textbook chapter). The systematic-audit carry is open and now has a sharper search-target: peer-reviewed-engineering-review/Federal-publication-identifier-class fabrications in the Tier B narrative ecosystem.
