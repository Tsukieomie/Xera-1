## 1. Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 *IEEE T-MTT* MTT-22(5):583-584 — full primary-text rereading

The **only** Sharp-and-Grove-co-authored peer-reviewed primary-research publication is the [IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques short letter, MTT-22(5):583-584 (May 1974), DOI 10.1109/TMTT.1974.1128293](https://doi.org/10.1109/TMTT.1974.1128293). Title verbatim: *"Generation of Acoustic Signals by Pulsed Microwave Energy."* Authors: J. C. Sharp and H. M. Grove (Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, Department of Microwave Research) with Om P. Gandhi (Department of Electrical Engineering, University of Utah). Manuscript dates printed in the letter: **received October 30, 1973; revised January 11, 1974**. Workspace OCR text is at `findings/investigation-dig/sharp-grove-gandhi-1974.txt` (263 lines, heavily OCR-garbled but readable).

The substantive primary content is **acoustic transduction via an absorbing dielectric**, not transduction in the head. Verbatim experimental setup (cleaned from the OCR):

> *"A sample of carbon-impregnated polyurethane absorber (Emerson and Cuming Eccosorb WG4) was placed in the antenna near-field region. Single rectangular pulses of microwave energy with carrier frequencies of 1200, 1500, and 2450 MHz, pulse widths of approximately 14 µs, and pulse-repetition frequencies of approximately 3 pulses per second were used. Audible acoustic signals could be detected using a stethoscope coupled to the absorber sample."*

Reported parameters: peak power density on the order of **7,500 W/m²**; threshold for stethoscope-detection of the click train approximately **0.46–0.92 kW/m²**. Mechanism speculation in the short discussion section: **radiation pressure, electrostriction in solid dielectric (Eccosorb), and possible direct coupling to the basilar membrane in mammalian-skull experiments**. The letter explicitly frames itself as a **mechanism letter** documenting that pulsed microwave energy can generate audible acoustic signals via a thermoacoustic (rapid-thermal-expansion) pathway in absorbing materials.

**Critical primary-text observation:** the letter contains **zero** mention of voice modulation, speech, intelligible words, word recognition, single-syllable digits, sine-wave-zero-crossing encoding, or any vocabulary in the Tier B semantic-channel class. It is a Tier A mechanism letter exclusively. Specifically:

- The waveform under test is a **fixed 14-µs rectangular pulse at fixed PRF ≈ 3 pps** — not an audio-modulated pulse train.
- The detection target is **clicks audible via stethoscope coupled to an Eccosorb absorber sample**, not phonemic recognition by a human listener radiated by the field.
- The proposed transduction site is **the absorber, primarily, with secondary speculation about basilar-membrane coupling for skull cases** — not a direct cortical or cochlear pathway encoding voice content.

The cumulative round-1-through-15 record had treated this finding as established. Round 16 confirms it by direct primary-text rereading. The Sharp/Grove **published** primary-research record contains **only Tier A mechanism content**.

## 2. Sept 28, 1973 personal-communication date predates Oct 30, 1973 IEEE manuscript-receipt by 32 days

Justesen 1975 *Microwaves and Behavior, Am Psychol* 30(3):391-401, page 396, footnote / Note 2 verbatim (recovered from the [Glaser archive zoryglaser.com PDF](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf)):

> *"Sharp, J. C., & Grove, M., Personal communication, September 28, 1973."*

The IEEE T-MTT 1974 letter manuscript-received date verbatim from the letter (round 16 §1 above): **"Manuscript received October 30, 1973."**

The **personal communication therefore predates the IEEE manuscript receipt by 32 days** — same researchers (J. C. Sharp + H. M. Grove), same institution (Walter Reed Army Institute of Research, Department of Microwave Research), same calendar month range (late September → late October 1973). The conventional reading of the timing is: between the September 28 demonstration and the October 30 IEEE letter submission, Sharp and Grove transitioned from the **Tier B voice-modulation demonstration** described in Justesen's narrative to the **Tier A click-generation mechanism letter** that became their only peer-reviewed primary publication. The same lab, same researchers, **but a different paper** — and the Tier B paper never published.

Rounds 8 and 9 had previously documented (`DEEPER_DIG_8_ADDENDUM.md` / `DEEPER_DIG_9_ADDENDUM.md`) that Frey/Messenger/Eichert 1972 NTIS [AD-747684](https://archive.org/details/DTIC_AD0747684) — funded under **the same WRAIR Project 1J662712AJ21** as Sharp/Grove 1973 — had reported verbatim *"It was found that aspects of vocoder technology could not be used to generate speech."* The Tier B speech-induction problem on the Sharp/Grove project line therefore had a **15-month-prior documented Frey-team negative result** when the Sharp/Grove demonstration occurred. Round 16's timing finding tightens this further: within the same project line, there is a **published Tier A clicks paper** (Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT) and an **unpublished Tier B voice-modulation demonstration** (Sharp/Grove personal communication to Justesen, Sept 28 1973) — the latter never having survived the 32-day gap from demonstration to manuscript drafting.

## 3. Verbatim Justesen 1975 p. 396 — the full negative-zero-crossing encoding scheme

The full verbatim Justesen retelling of the Sharp/Grove voice-modulation demonstration as primary-narrative-source for the Tier B claim, recovered verbatim from [zoryglaser.com](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf), p. 396 (Justesen 1975 *Am Psychol* 30(3)):

> *"Sharp and Grove (Note 2) found that appropriate modulation of microwave energy can result in direct 'wireless' and 'receiverless' communication of speech. They recorded by voice on tape each of the single-syllable words for digits between 1 and 10. The electrical sine-wave analogs of each word were then processed so that each time a sine wave crossed zero reference in the negative direction, a brief pulse of microwave energy was triggered. By radiating themselves with these 'voice-modulated' microwaves, Sharp and Grove were readily able to hear, identify, and distinguish among the 9 words."*

Round 16 forensic anatomy of this paragraph:

| Element | Justesen 1975 verbatim | Tier-A-attestation status | Tier-B-attestation status |
|---|---|---|---|
| "appropriate modulation of microwave energy can result in direct 'wireless' and 'receiverless' communication of speech" | overarching claim | **not in IEEE 1974 letter** | **established only by Justesen narrative**; never primary-published |
| "recorded by voice on tape each of the single-syllable words for digits between 1 and 10" | corpus design | **not in IEEE 1974 letter** | **established only by Justesen narrative** |
| "electrical sine-wave analogs of each word… each time a sine wave crossed zero reference in the negative direction, a brief pulse of microwave energy was triggered" | encoding scheme — **negative-zero-crossing pulse trigger** | **not in IEEE 1974 letter**; pulse train in IEEE letter was fixed-PRF ≈ 3 pps rectangular, not zero-crossing-modulated | **established only by Justesen narrative** |
| "radiating themselves with these 'voice-modulated' microwaves" | self-experimentation by the researchers | **not in IEEE 1974 letter** (which used Eccosorb absorbers, not human skulls) | **established only by Justesen narrative**; no protocol, no IRB record, no replication |
| "readily able to hear, identify, and distinguish among the 9 words" | reported result — note **9** of **10** digits | **not in IEEE 1974 letter** | **established only by Justesen narrative**; the "9 of 10" figure that propagates downstream into 1976/1977 Brodeur and the entire Tier B citation chain originates **here** |

The negative-zero-crossing pulse-trigger encoding scheme is the **specific technical content** of the Tier B claim. It is engineering-plausible as a description (zero-crossing detectors are textbook 1970s analog electronics; pulsed-microwave triggers were within Walter Reed/Microwave-Research-Laboratory hardware capability). What it lacks is **independent primary-source attestation**. The scheme survives in the open literature **only** in Justesen's 1975 retelling and in derivative retellings (Brodeur 1976 *New Yorker*, Brodeur 1977 *The Zapping of America*, Lin 1978 textbook chapter, Bowart 1978, Justesen 1979 *Bull NYAM*, Justesen 1979 *IEEE Spectrum*, the Cowan 2024 substack reference list — and in each retelling the encoding-scheme description is either Justesen-1975-derivative or absent entirely).

The "9 of 10" figure is itself worth noting — Justesen says the digits 1 through 10 were recorded, and that Sharp/Grove distinguished among "the 9 words." Whether this is "9 of 10" (one digit failed) or "9 distinct words" (with one of the ten being phonetically merged or excluded by design) is **not specified** in Justesen's retelling. The downstream propagation of "9 of 10" as a confidence-bearing performance metric is a Justesen-narrative artifact, not a primary-research-protocol-attested result.

## 4. False-citation finding — Cowan substack reference #9 and FDA 77-8026

The [Peter Anthony Cowan substack post "The CIA said the science behind Havana Syndrome 'doesn't add up.' Here's why they're wrong"](https://peteranthonycowan.substack.com/p/the-cia-said-the-science-behind-havana) provides a numbered reference list. **Reference #9** verbatim:

> *"Sharp, J.C. and Grove, H.M. 'Individual Perception of Pulsed Microwave Radiation.' Symposium on Biological Effects and Measurement of Radio Frequency/Microwaves, HEW Publication (FDA) 77-8026, 1973."*

Round 16 verifies this citation against the actual contents of HEW Publication (FDA) 77-8026.

**FDA 77-8026 metadata (primary-source-verified):** the publication is the proceedings of the *Symposium on Biological Effects and Measurement of Radio Frequency/Microwaves*, held at the Bureau of Radiological Health, Rockville, Maryland, **February 16-18, 1977** (not 1973), edited by D. G. Hazzard, published by the U.S. Department of Health, Education, and Welfare, Public Health Service, Food and Drug Administration, Bureau of Radiological Health, **July 1977**.

**FDA 77-8026 actual Table of Contents** (verified via the [GBV/TIB Hannover catalog PDF preview at gbv.de](https://www.gbv.de/dms/tib-ub-hannover/017394619.pdf), which carries the full ToC):

The volume's session structure consists of: opening remarks; **Session I: Behavioral Effects** (chair Justesen) — papers including Galloway, Lobanova, D'Andrea/Gandhi/De Lorge; **Session II: Physical Measurements / Dosimetry** — papers including Schwan, Stuchly, Allen, Gandhi, Bassen; **Session III: Growth and Development** — Berman, Inouye, Stavinoha; **Session IV: Nervous System** — Bawin/Adey, Lin, Lebovitz/Seaman, Servantie; **Session V: Occupational and Ocular Effects** — Cleary, Carpenter, Birenbaum.

**No paper authored by J. C. Sharp or H. M. Grove appears anywhere in the ToC.** No paper titled "Individual Perception of Pulsed Microwave Radiation" appears in the ToC. The closest semantic match in FDA 77-8026 is the **Lin** *Auditory Effects of Pulsed Microwave Radiation* paper in Session IV (Lin's microwave-auditory mechanism paper, which cites Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT for the click-generation mechanism — Tier A, not Tier B).

**The Cowan reference #9 is therefore a false citation.** It misattributes a non-existent Sharp/Grove FDA-symposium paper to a publication number (FDA 77-8026) and date (1973) that do not match the actual document (FDA 77-8026 = February 1977 symposium, July 1977 publication; no Sharp/Grove content). Three forensic possibilities for the error:

1. **Citation chain hallucination** — the citation may have been generated by an LLM or paraphrased without verification from a derivative source that itself confused Sharp/Grove (Walter Reed) with the FDA-symposium speaker roster, or that retroactively attributed a "perceived microwaves" Tier B label to the Sharp/Grove name.
2. **Cross-merge with a different document** — possibly a confusion with a 1973 DOD/NIH/HEW internal document, with a Lin-authored paper that **does** cite Sharp/Grove, or with the IEEE T-MTT 1974 letter renumbered into the FDA-77 series.
3. **Constructed citation** — the bibliographic entry may have been fabricated whole-cloth at some upstream point in the Tier B narrative ecosystem, then propagated as a "primary source" lending false weight to the voice-modulation claim's documentary footing.

This is **the first attested case in this investigation of Tier B speech-claim citation-chain hallucination** — a fabricated or grossly-misattributed primary-source citation specifically supporting the Sharp/Grove voice-modulation claim. The investigation discipline established in `v2k-evidence-matrix.md` (Tier A vs Tier B distinction) and `V2K_DEEP_LAYER.md` (mechanism-vs-semantic-channel separation) is therefore reinforced operationally: when reading downstream Tier B citation chains, **verify primary-source citations against actual ToCs**, because at least one widely-circulated reference list contains a falsified primary-source citation for the Sharp/Grove voice-modulation claim.

The discovered false citation also has a structural implication. The downstream Tier B narrative ecosystem includes serious-form citation chains (Cowan substack references, mind-justice technical packets, V2K-claimant primary-source compilations) that **purport** to point to peer-reviewed or government-published primary sources. Round 16 demonstrates that **at least one such chain points to a non-existent document**. The Tier B narrative ecosystem cannot be treated as a citation aggregator — it must be treated as a citation-verification problem.
