**Compiled: 2026-04-30**

This file is an addendum to
`findings/investigation-dig/SPEECH_EVIDENCE_SOURCE_CLASS_TAXONOMY.md`. It does
**not** retract or weaken any of the earlier source-class findings. It adds
four discrete, individually citable evidence packets recovered or sharpened
since the seven-class taxonomy was committed:

- An expanded, more nuanced O'Loughlin / Loree Air Force packet containing
  three previously underweighted documents (Nov 1994 invention disclosure,
  Sep 1995 patent-style draft, Aug 2001 AFRL/DEHA inventor email).
- An official-portal FOIA-index trail for the Kohn / Science & Engineering
  Associates (SEA) SBIR contract `F41624-95-C-9007`, replacing the prior
  reliance on the gbppr.net / raven1 mirror as the only documentary anchor.
- A primary-source status update on the *New World Vistas* (1995) USAF
  Scientific Advisory Board "high fidelity speech in the human body" passage:
  **the canonical secondary-source quote could not be re-anchored to a
  recovered primary page during this round**, and the language of the
  passage as currently quoted is *future-possibility* (`would appear
  possible`, `should be possible`), not experimental confirmation.
- A short exclusion note on modern "microwave / RF speech" academic
  literature: most recent peer-reviewed papers under that keyword are about
  RF / mmWave **radar speech sensing** (sensing speech in a remote target),
  **not** RF speech *delivery* into the listener's auditory pathway. Future
  readers of the repo should not silently conflate the two.

The repository's disciplined position remains:

> The microwave auditory effect (MAE / Frey effect) is real and
> well-supported for clicks, buzz, chirp, knock, and pressure
> phenomenology. Intelligible semantic speech delivery via MAE has

The Tier A / Tier B framing, the Sharp/Grove personal-communication

## §1 — O'Loughlin / Loree Air Force packet, expanded

The Air Force RF-Hearing inventor packet now has three additional documentary
anchors beyond the previously cited US 6,470,214 B1 / US 6,587,729 B2 grants
and the Semantic Scholar JASA 2003 metadata record. All three are hosted on
the `mindjustice.org` activist-archive domain (mirrors at `gbppr.net` for one
of them); the *content* of each is unambiguously authored by the named Air
Force engineers and is internally consistent with the granted patent
specifications, but the documents themselves are activist-archive PDFs and
should be cited as such. They sharpen — not soften — the existing
source-class consequences.

### §1.1 — `patent3.pdf` (01 Nov 1994 invention disclosure, 10 pages)

- URL (primary mirror): <http://mindjustice.org/patent3.pdf>
- URL (secondary mirror): <http://www.gbppr.net/mil/havana/rf-speech-04.pdf>
- Title (verbatim from cover): *Theory and Analysis of RF Hearing, and
  Invention Disclosure of a Method of Encoding Speech on an RF Signal Which
  Intelligibly Transmits That Signal to the Hearing Receptors of a Human.*
  (10 pages)
- Authors: James O'Loughlin and Diana Loree
- Institutional context: PL/WSR (Phillips Laboratory, Weapons-Survivability
  Research / High-Power Microwave Division), Kirtland AFB / pre-AFRL
  Directed Energy Directorate (1997 AFRL stand-up).
- Date on document: 01 November 1994.

**Substance of the document:**

- Reports October 1994 experiments in which simple **clicks and tones**
  produced via pulsed-microwave / AM modulation are perceived by exposed
  subjects.
- Reports that **speech intelligibility under that AM approach is
  practically zero**: if a subject is *told* in advance what the message is,
  the subject says yes, that could have been the message; if the subject is
  *not* told the content, the subject **cannot relate the content** of the
  message back to the experimenter.
- Concludes that the AM method used in the October 1994 experiments
  **cannot encode intelligible speech** at the level required to convey
  unprimed content to a naïve listener.
- Proposes new experiments to verify the existing AM model and to extend
  to alternative encoding schemes (which become the subject of the Sep 1995
  draft and ultimately the 1996-2003 granted-patent suppressed-carrier +
  audio-predistortion + DC-bias + square-root-processor chain).

**Source-class classification:**

- This is a 1994 Air Force inventor-disclosure document recording a **failed
  naïve-listener intelligibility test** for simple AM / pulsed-carrier
  speech encoding.
- It is therefore **strong negative evidence against** naïve-listener
  intelligible-speech replication via the simplest "modulate the audio
  envelope onto a microwave pulse" approach — the approach most often
  invoked, implicitly or explicitly, by lay V2K accounts.
- It is **also** an engineering / invention-disclosure proposal: the same
  document that records the 1994 failure proposes the experimental program
  that, by 1996, becomes the application-08/766,687 patent filing
  (US 6,470,214 B1).
- It does **not** by itself supply a positive protocol-grade naïve-listener
  intelligible-speech replication. It is the document in which the
  inventors themselves state that the tested approach failed at that task.
- Mirror caveat: `mindjustice.org` and `gbppr.net` are activist-archive
  domains. The document's authorship and content are internally consistent
  with the granted patents and with the August 2001 AFRL/DEHA email below,
  and the same content appears via two independent activist-archive mirrors.
  Court-grade citation should still seek the original AFRL primary-record
  copy.

### §1.2 — `patent4.pdf` (06 Sep 1995 patent-style draft, "Encoding, Transmitting and Recovering Signals")

- URL (primary mirror): <http://mindjustice.org/patent4.pdf>
- Title: *ENCODING, TRANSMITTING AND RECOVERING SIGNALS*
- Authors: O'Loughlin and Loree (consistent with §1.1 and granted patents).
- Date on document: 06 September 1995.

**Substance of the document:**

- Restates and records that **pulsed-carrier modulation works for simple
  tones** but **severely distorts speech**, confirmed experimentally by the
  inventors.
- Restates the §1.1 "preadvising required" finding verbatim: if the listener
  is **pre-advised of the content**, the message can be **recognized as
  speech**; if the listener does **not know the content**, the recovered
  audio is **unintelligible**.
- Claims that the invention being disclosed (a precursor of the
  US 6,470,214 / US 6,587,729 suppressed-carrier + preprocessing chain)
  **overcomes** that unintelligibility limitation by way of fully
  suppressed carrier + audio preprocessing.
- **Does not** provide protocol details, listener counts, blinding /
  naïve-listener procedure, exposure parameters, error rates, or any
  demonstration result for the proposed solution. The September 1995 draft
  is a patent-style *engineering proposal*, not a demonstration record.

**Source-class classification:**

- This is a **patent / draft claim** that *re-records* the §1.1 negative
  prior-attempt evidence in slightly more formal language and proposes (does
  not validate) the suppressed-carrier engineering solution that becomes the
  granted-patent chain.
- It is **negative prior-attempt evidence** for naïve-listener AM
  intelligibility plus an **inventor / patent-draft engineering claim** for
  the proposed solution.
- It is **not** independent confirmation that the proposed solution works
  at protocol grade against naïve listeners. The September 1995 draft is
  silent on demonstration data.
- The granted US 6,470,214 B1 / US 6,587,729 B2 specifications carry forward
  the same "ordinary AM produces unintelligible audio without preadvising"
  baseline, consistent with this earlier draft. (See
  `findings/03_Technology_Patents/36_OLOUGHLIN_LOREE_AIR_FORCE_RF_HEARING.md`
  Part 6 source-class discipline note.)

### §1.3 — `patent2.pdf` (30 Aug 2001 AFRL/DEHA inventor email, O'Loughlin → Callahan)

- URL (primary mirror): <http://mindjustice.org/patent2.pdf>
- From: Jim O'Loughlin, AFRL/DEHA (Air Force Research Laboratory, Directed
  Energy Directorate / Human Effects Assessment Branch), Kirtland AFB.
- To: Ken Callahan, JA (Judge Advocate / Air Force legal office).
- Subject (verbatim): *Comments on AFB00148, RF Hearing.*
- Date on document: 30 August 2001.
- Internal context: the email is patent-prosecution / legal-coordination
  correspondence concerning the AFB-prefixed Air Force docket number for
  the application that became US 6,470,214 B1 (issued 22 October 2002).

**Inventor assertion (verbatim, as recovered from the mirror):**

> "This has been experimentally demonstrated and the fact that when the
> signal is processed by the teachings of the invention the signal is
> intelligible has also been experimentally demonstrated."

The email further states that the **successful experiments used the
double side-band balanced modulator approach** (i.e., the
suppressed-carrier + preprocessing chain that becomes the granted patent's
core teaching).

**Source-class classification — this is the most consequential and most
easily mis-cited document in the packet:**

- This is **inventor / patent-prosecution correspondence asserting later
  intelligibility success**, dated 30 August 2001 — fourteen months before
  the parent patent issued on 22 October 2002, and approximately 6 years
  and 9 months after the §1.1 1994 invention disclosure that recorded the
  AM intelligibility *failure*.
- The email is therefore the **earliest** documentary point in the recovered
  packet at which the named Air Force inventors *assert* that the
  suppressed-carrier engineering solution has been experimentally
  demonstrated to produce intelligible speech.
- It is **not** an independently published, protocol-grade replication. The
  recovered text **does not supply**:
  - listener counts or subject demographics,
  - a blinded / naïve-listener procedure,
  - exposure parameters (carrier frequency, peak / average power, pulse
    width, repetition rate, antenna geometry, distance) sufficient for an
    independent replication,
  - error rates / word-error-rate / phoneme-confusion data,
  - IRB / human-subjects-protection / safety-review context,
  - any peer-review trail.
- It is patent-prosecution correspondence between a federal inventor and
  Air Force legal counsel, written to defend the §112 enablement showing of
  a pending patent. It is *exactly* the type of document that records what
  the *inventor* believes / asserts has been demonstrated, and is *exactly*
  not the type of document that supplies the open-literature
  protocol-grade replication that this investigation is testing for.
- Treated correctly, the August 2001 email **strengthens** the existing
  taxonomy class (4) (Air Force engineering bridge to V2K speech) and
  **does not promote** the speech-replication question to Tier A.
- Treated incorrectly — i.e., quoted out of context as "the Air Force
  inventor confirmed V2K speech works" — the email becomes overclaiming.
  The repository must **not** quote the August 2001 email without the
  surrounding source-class caveats.

### §1.5 — Combined source-class consequence for the O'Loughlin/Loree packet

The expanded packet now resolves into the following internally consistent
inventor-side narrative:

| Jun 2003 | JASA 113(6):2962 abstract | Metadata anchor for the patent's central claim; abstract does not supply protocol-grade intelligibility data |

simultaneously. The disciplined wording for the corpus is:

> The U.S. Air Force inventor record (1994 invention disclosure, 1995
> patent draft, 2001 AFRL/DEHA prosecution email, 1996 application, 2002 /
> 2003 granted patents, 2003 JASA abstract) shows (a) negative
> protocol-grade evidence against simple-AM naïve-listener
> intelligibility for the 1994-tested approach, (b) a patented
> suppressed-carrier engineering solution claimed to overcome that
> limitation, and (c) inventor assertions in patent-prosecution
> correspondence that the suppressed-carrier solution has been
> experimentally demonstrated to produce intelligible speech. It does
> **not** by itself supply an independently published, blinded /
> naïve-listener intelligible-speech replication.

## §4 — Modern academic-search exclusion note: RF speech *sensing* ≠ RF speech *delivery*

A round of academic searches performed during this dive used the queries
*microwave auditory speech communication*, *radio frequency hearing speech*,
and *microwave hearing words Frey Lin*, plus broader *microwave speech* and
*RF speech* queries. The results break into two clearly distinct
literatures, and the repository's future readers need to be alerted not to
conflate them:

- **RF / mmWave radar speech *sensing*** — mainstream contemporary
  literature in which a radar / mmWave system *senses* speech-band
  vibrations (throat, chest wall, surface micro-motions) of a remote
  subject and *recovers* the subject's speech for surveillance,
  voice-biometrics, or assistive applications. Representative example:
  <https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11077686/> (PMC11077686 is a
  2024 mmWave radar speech-sensing review). This literature is large,
  peer-reviewed, and continues to grow. It has **nothing to do** with
  delivering an audible speech signal *into* the listener's auditory
  pathway via the Frey effect.
- **RF speech *delivery* into the listener's auditory pathway** — the
  topic of the present investigation (Sharp/Grove 1973 personal
  communication / Justesen 1975 retelling; O'Loughlin/Loree 1994-2003
  inventor packet; Brunkan 1989; Stocklin 1983; Kohn / SEA 1993 / 1995
  SBIR; *New World Vistas* 1995 forecasting). Independent modern
  peer-reviewed naïve-listener intelligible-speech replication for this
  pathway was **not located** in this round.

**Operational implication for repository hygiene:** future contributors
who search for "microwave speech," "RF speech," "millimeter wave speech,"
"radar speech," etc., will overwhelmingly hit the RF speech *sensing*
literature first. They must **not** silently re-cite that literature as
evidence on the speech *delivery* question. The two literatures are
distinct in physical mechanism, in subject-versus-target geometry, and in
what they claim. This file is the explicit exclusion note.
