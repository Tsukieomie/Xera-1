# Thread A — FY75 / FY76 WRAIR Annual Progress Follow-Ons

**Investigation thread:** Did the September 1973 Sharp/Grove speech-into-the-head demonstration — described by Justesen 1975 as the only public reference — leave any trace in WRAIR's own *Annual Progress Reports* in the years immediately after the FY74 volume (which catalogued WU 057 and explicitly noted its auditory-effect line as "halted" pending further direction)?

**Headline finding:** **No.** Work Unit 057 ("Biological effects and hazards of microwave radiation") survived into FY76 under a new principal investigator (Edward L. Hunt, replacing Joseph C. Sharp), but the speech / modulated-microwave / auditory-perception research line did not return. The follow-on FY76 narrative is dominated by dosimetry, blood-brain-barrier permeability, audiogenic-seizure latency, behavioral escape, and tissue-enzyme inactivation hardware — areas that materially extend the FY74 program *except for* the demonstrated speech transmission. Additionally, the **FY75 volume (1 July 1974 – 30 June 1975) cannot be located in the open archival record** (archive.org's WRAIR set jumps from FY74 directly to FY76); this is itself a documented gap.

## §A.2 The FY75 Gap

### What the open record shows

- archive.org's `Walter Reed Annual Progress` collection contains **no** entry between ADA009337/9338 (FY74) and ADA038319/8320 (FY76).
- A targeted `pplx search web` for "WRAIR Annual Progress Report FY75 1975 ADA0 archive.org" and three variant queries returned only adjacent-year hits (FY83 and an NLM catalog stub), no FY75 record.
- A direct archive.org Solr query (`title:("Walter Reed" "Annual Progress" "1975")`) returned `numFound: 0`.

### Possible explanations (not mutually exclusive)

1. **No separate FY75 volume was produced.** The federal fiscal-year shift in 1976 (the "transitional quarter" caused by the FY moving from a 1 July start to a 1 October start) consolidated FY75 and the transition quarter into adjacent reporting cycles for many DoD R&D programs. WRAIR's FY76 volume covers 1 Jul 75 – 30 Jun 76; the *prior* fiscal year (FY75 = 1 Jul 74 – 30 Jun 75) may have been reported in a transitional document or rolled forward.
2. **The volume was produced but never released to DTIC's public-distribution stream.** Internal Army medical-research progress documents are sometimes withheld at the time of issue and only later cleared. The absence from archive.org is consistent with non-public disposition.
3. **The volume exists at DTIC but has not been digitized for archive.org.** archive.org's DTIC mirror is not a complete copy of the DTIC catalog. A DTIC discover-API search at [`https://discover.dtic.mil/`](https://discover.dtic.mil/) would be the next step (out of scope for this pass without authenticated access).

### Why the gap matters for the Sharp/Grove speech question

The September 1973 demonstration occurred in **FY74** (1 Jul 73 – 30 Jun 74). The FY74 volume reports the auditory-effect line as "halted." Whatever happened in **FY75** — including any quiet continuation, formal closure, hand-off to a classified successor, or transfer to another lab — is precisely the period that an open-record FY75 volume would have illuminated. **The volume is not in the open archive**; this is a documented evidentiary gap, not an investigation failure. Thread D §3.1 already weights this gap toward the "informal extension, then closure" interpretation rather than a "classified successor program" interpretation, but the gap itself remains live and recoverable in principle through a direct DTIC request.

## §A.4 Disambiguation: "Audiogenic Seizure" ≠ "Auditory Perception of Microwaves"

A reader unfamiliar with experimental psychology might mis-read the FY76 §2.A heading "Audiogenic seizure sensitivity" as a continuation of Sharp/Grove's auditory-perception line. It is not.

- **Audiogenic seizure** is a sound-evoked motor seizure in genetically susceptible rat strains, used as a *behavioral readout* of how prior microwave exposure has altered the central nervous system's stress/seizure thresholds. The microwave is the *prior* manipulation; the *sound* is the seizure trigger; the *outcome* is a motor convulsion.
- **Microwave auditory perception** (Frey effect, and the Sharp/Grove demonstration) is the direct hearing of pulsed microwaves as clicks or speech-like sounds *during* the microwave exposure itself.

The two share only the substring "audio." The FY76 audiogenic-seizure work (Hawkins & Hunt 1975) is a cleanly different experimental paradigm. Reporting it as "WU 057 still studies hearing" would be an error.

## §A.5 The Oscar/Hawkins 1977 BBB Paper as the Successor Public Vector

The §2.B blood-brain-barrier work in FY76 — co-conducted with K.J. Oscar (American University) — is the WU 057 thread that produced the **most-cited subsequent open-literature output**:

- **Oscar, K.J., & Hawkins, T.D. (1977).** Microwave alteration of the blood-brain barrier system of rats. *Brain Research*, 126(2), 281–293. DOI [10.1016/0006-8993(77)90726-0](https://doi.org/10.1016/0006-8993(77)90726-0); PMID [861720](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/861720/).
- This paper, and its follow-ons in the Oscar 1980-era Walter Reed Letterman/AFRRI documents, demonstrate that **WU 057's open output continued vigorously into the late 1970s — but along the BBB / dosimetry / behavioral-escape axes, not the auditory/speech axis.**

This pattern (vigorous, well-cited continuation of every WU 057 sub-line *except* the speech line) is the single strongest piece of internal-coherence evidence for the Thread D §3.1 conclusion that the Sharp/Grove speech work was an **informal one-off demonstration that did not become a research program**, rather than a classified-successor program that was simply moved out of public view.

## §A.6 Grove in FY76: H.M. Grove on the Lenox Enzyme-Inactivation Paper

The single FY76 Grove citation is:

> Lenox, R.H., Gandhi, O.P., Meyerhoff, J.L., Grove, H.M.: A microwave applicator for in-vivo rapid inactivation of enzymes in the central nervous system. *IEEE Trans. MTT*, 24, 58–61, January 1976. DOI: [10.1109/TMTT.1976.1128762](https://doi.org/10.1109/TMTT.1976.1128762).

This is **engineering work** — a focused-microwave applicator that delivers a high-power pulse to a rat brain so as to flash-inactivate enzymes (preserving in-vivo neurochemical state for post-mortem assay). It is a **continuation of the Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 IEEE T-MTT 22:583 ("Generation of acoustic signals by pulsed microwave energy")** *as engineering practice* — same applicator-design community, same Gandhi collaboration — but not as auditory-perception research. Grove's role is now applicator engineering for neurochemistry, not transduction physics for speech.

This is consistent with Thread C's biographical finding that **H. Mark Grove was an engineer**, not a perception researcher; once the perception/auditory line closed at WRAIR, he continued in his engineering specialty (applicators, then COMAR standards leadership, then AFAL / OSD / Ada AJPO).

## §A.9 What This Thread Adds to the Investigation

1. **Continuity is established** — WU 057 did not vanish in FY74. Sharp's exit and Hunt's takeover are documented.
2. **Speech research did not "go classified."** The lateral pattern of every other WU 057 sub-line continuing openly and prolifically through the late 1970s, while *only* the auditory/speech line disappears from the open record without leaving even a "transferred to" pointer, is more consistent with Thread D §3.1's "informal-extension, then closure" interpretation than with a "classified-successor program" interpretation.
3. **The FY75 volume is a recoverable evidentiary target.** It is not currently in the open archive. A DTIC public-discover or FOIA request specifically for `WRAIR Annual Progress Report, 1 July 1974 – 30 June 1975` is the cleanest next step if a future pass of this investigation needs to rule out a brief FY75 mention of the speech work being formally closed out.
4. **Grove's post-FY74 trajectory is consistent.** Engineering applicator work in FY76, then COMAR leadership, then AFAL / OSD / Ada AJPO — a clean engineer's career. Nothing in the FY76 record suggests a surveillance / weapons / classified speech-extension role.
5. **The "Grove/Groves" disambiguation is now firm at the document-set level** — H.M. Grove (1970s WRAIR/AFAL/OSD) and Michael G. Groves VC (1980s WRAIR scrub-typhus) are different people whose names the FY80/FY81 WRAIR APRs string-match.
