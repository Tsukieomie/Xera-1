# Air Force RF Hearing Patents → Navy/Marine MEDUSA: Timeline & Interpretation

**Compiled: 2026-04-29**

Bibliographic facts:

- **US 6,470,214 B1**, "Method and device for implementing the radio frequency hearing effect," inventors James P. O'Loughlin and Diana L. Loree, assignee *United States of America as represented by the Secretary of the Air Force*. Describes encoding speech on an RF carrier so that intelligible subjective sound is perceived via the RF Hearing Effect.
- **US 6,587,729 B2**, "Apparatus for audibly communicating speech using the radio frequency hearing effect," same inventor team and Air Force assignment.
- **MEDUSA**: Navy/Marine SBIR contract M67854-04-C-1012, Phase I, topic N03-163 ("Remote Personnel Incapacitation System" line in the SBIR bulk record; see `MEDUSA_TRANSITION_GAP_ANALYSIS.md`).

## 2. Interpretation

The dating relationship the user asked about holds in open-record terms:

- The Air Force RF-hearing patent line (filed 1996, granted 2002–2003) **predates** the Navy/Marine MEDUSA SBIR line (visible 2003–2004 in SBIR records) by roughly **one to seven years**, depending on whether one anchors against the 1996 filing or the 2002–2003 grants.
- That ordering is consistent with a continuity of military R&D interest in RF-hearing / microwave auditory-effect (MAE) technologies moving from Air Force patent work (Kirtland AFRL Directed Energy Directorate) into Navy/Marine personnel-deterrent SBIR research within the Joint Non-Lethal Weapons Program ecosystem.

What the timeline **does not** establish on its own:

- It does **not** prove that any deployed covert "voice-to-skull" (V2K) capability exists.
- It does **not** establish reliable, conversational, individually-targeted speech transmission as a fielded capability.
- It does **not** support case-specific operational use against any individual or population.
- The visible Phase II continuation under topic N03-163 in open SBIR records traces to the **EPIC / Invocon** line, not to MEDUSA itself (see `MEDUSA_TRANSITION_GAP_ANALYSIS.md`); MEDUSA's later operational status is not established by the open record indexed in this corpus.

Safe phrasing for downstream documents:

> Open records show U.S. Air Force RF-hearing patents (US 6,470,214 B1 filed 1996, granted 2002; US 6,587,729 B2 filed 2002, granted 2003) preceding the Navy/Marine MEDUSA SBIR Phase I effort (M67854-04-C-1012, topic N03-163) visible in 2003–2004. The sequence is consistent with continuity of military RF-hearing / microwave auditory-effect R&D interest across services, but does not by itself establish deployed covert V2K, reliable conversational targeting, or case-specific operational use.

## 3. Dual-use characterization

MEDUSA was a dual-use microwave auditory-effect R&D system: its stated defense / non-lethal role was perimeter, crowd, or personnel deterrence, while public descriptions also identified possible assistive or non-combat applications, including microwave audio for people with outer-ear hearing impairment. This dual-use characterization supports institutional interest and potential applicability, but does not by itself establish deployed covert V2K use.
