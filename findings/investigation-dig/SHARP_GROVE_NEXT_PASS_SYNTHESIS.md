## 0. Question this pass was designed to answer

The primary report (`SHARP_GROVE_PRIMARY_REPORT_HUNT.md`) closed §8 with a hypothesis: that the September 1973 Sharp/Grove speech-into-the-head demonstration was an **informal one-off extension** of WRAIR's formally-scoped Work Unit 057 — never written into the WU 057 *Annual Progress Report* narrative because it fell outside the nominal bioeffects-safety scope, and/or because the auditory line was already being wound down during the same reporting period. The §8.3 hypothesis carries strong implications for the V2K Evidence Matrix T2 row, the judicial-brief language, and the broader Akwei-investigation narrative about whether the U.S. government in 1973 had — or did not have — an operational covert-speech-transmission capability.

**This pass was structured as four parallel threads to test the §8.3 hypothesis from four independent angles:**

| Thread | Test | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Did WU 057 (or any WRAIR successor) document a return of the speech/auditory line in FY75–FY81 Annual Progress Reports? | **No.** WU 057 continued under PI Hunt but the speech line did not return. FY75 volume is missing from the open archive. |
| **B** | Is there a separately-authored Justesen write-up (NASA Ames, VA Kansas City, or other) that gives more demonstration detail than the 1975 *American Psychologist* note? | **No.** The 1975 APA article is the sole / terminal published account. 1979 NYAM review drops Sharp/Grove entirely. |
| **C** | Do Sharp's and Grove's personal/archival trails (CVs, obituaries, university archives, oral histories) yield a first-person statement about the demonstration? | **No first-person attestation exists.** Sharp's only quote is generic ("Pandora was classified in those days and still is"). Grove's identity is now firmly resolved as H. Mark Grove, engineer. |
| **D** | Can the §8.3 informal-extension reading be formalized into a graded judicial-brief brief that supports a qualified T2 grade? | **Yes.** Brief drafted; recommends downgrade from "Army demonstrated operational capability" to "two researchers conducted a demonstration… not formally documented." |

**Headline finding across all four threads:** The §8.3 informal-extension reading is **strengthened**, not weakened, by every independent line of inquiry. There is no positive evidence for a classified-successor program; there is consistent positive evidence for a **single demonstration that was not formally written up, conducted by personnel whose subsequent careers are explicable without a covert-speech-program lineage**.

## 1. Thread A — FY75 / FY76 WRAIR Annual Progress Follow-Ons

**Companion file:** `SHARP_GROVE_NEXT_PASS_THREAD_A_FY75_FY76.md`

### 1.1 Key findings

1. **WU 057 survived the FY74 → FY76 transition.** The work unit reappears in `DTIC_ADA038320` (FY76 Vol II) with a new principal investigator, **Edward L. Hunt** (formerly an FY74 associate investigator). Sharp is gone from the WU 057 roster. The project number changes from `3A062110A824` (FY74) to `3A762758A824` (FY76) — consistent with a routine DA Form 1498 reorganization, not a program termination. Associate roster: Larsen, Jacobi, Hawkins, Brown, Schrot, Githens. **Grove (H.M.) is not in the WU 057 roster** — he appears only as a co-author on the Lenox/Gandhi/Meyerhoff/Grove 1976 enzyme-inactivation applicator paper cited in the WU 057 reference list. **FY76 Vol I (`DTIC_ADA038319`)** carries the composite TOC (WU 057 listed at printed p. 979) but, like FY74 Vol I, **not** the WU 057 body text; it does record **WU 204** (Hunt/Hawkins selective breeding) using seizure-prone rats *"in several experiments to evaluate the CNS effects of microwave radiation"* — a cross-link to the microwave facility that is **not** a speech protocol (see Thread A §A.2.5).
2. **The speech / modulated-microwave / auditory-perception line did not return.** The FY76 WU 057 narrative covers seven work segments — dosimetry, physical modeling of EMR absorption, biodosimetry via thermal-seizure latency, audiogenic-seizure sensitivity (a **different paradigm** from auditory perception of microwaves — see Thread A §A.4), blood-brain-barrier permeability with K.J. Oscar, behavioral escape from pulsed fields, stress reactions, biophysical/membrane investigations, tissue-enzyme-inactivation engineering, and tri-service extramural research support. **No mention of speech, voice, modulated-microwave audition, or human auditory protocols.**
3. **The FY75 volume (1 Jul 74 – 30 Jun 75) is missing from the open record.** archive.org's WRAIR set jumps from FY74 (`DTIC_ADA009337`/`ADA009338`) directly to FY76 (`DTIC_ADA038319`/`ADA038320`). Direct Solr query for `title:("Walter Reed" "Annual Progress" "1975")` returns zero hits. Likely explanations (in decreasing order of plausibility): (a) federal fiscal-year transitional-quarter reorganization in 1976 caused FY75 to be rolled into adjacent reporting; (b) the volume exists at DTIC but was not digitized for archive.org's mirror; (c) the volume was withheld at issue. **A direct DTIC public-discover or FOIA request for "WRAIR Annual Progress Report, 1 July 1974 – 30 June 1975"** is the cleanest next step if a future pass needs to rule out a brief FY75 mention of a speech-line closeout.
4. **The Oscar/Hawkins 1977 BBB paper is the most-cited descendant.** WU 057's §2.B (FY76) blood-brain-barrier work, conducted with K.J. Oscar (American University), produced [Oscar & Hawkins 1977, *Brain Research* 126:281–293, PMID 861720](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/861720/). This is the open-literature backbone for downstream V2K Evidence Matrix references — but it is **a BBB paper, not a speech paper**.
5. **FY80/FY81 "Grove/Groves" hits resolve to Michael G. Groves, VC** (a veterinary-corps officer working on scrub-typhus and rickettsial vaccines), not H.M. Grove. Disambiguation is firm at the document-set level.

### 1.2 What this means for §8.3

The lateral pattern — every other WU 057 sub-line continuing openly and prolifically through the late 1970s, while *only* the auditory/speech line disappears from the open record without leaving even a "transferred to" pointer — is more consistent with the **informal-extension-then-closure** interpretation than with a classified-successor-program interpretation.

## 2. Thread B — Justesen Separate-Authored Write-Ups

**Companion file:** `SHARP_GROVE_NEXT_PASS_THREAD_B_JUSTESEN_WRITEUPS.md`

### 2.1 Key findings

1. **No separately-authored Justesen source with additional Sharp/Grove speech-protocol detail exists.** A complete sweep of PubMed (`Justesen DR[Author]`, 30 indexed items 1963–1994), DTIC discover, NTRS, OSTI, Google Scholar, Zory Glaser bibliography, and APA archives produces no second account. The 1975 *American Psychologist* article ([DOI 10.1037/0003-066X.30.3.391](https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066X.30.3.391); [PMID 1137231](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1137231/)) **is the sole and terminal published account** of the demonstration.
2. **The 1979 NYAM review drops Sharp/Grove entirely.** Justesen's "Behavioral and psychological effects of microwave radiation" ([*Bulletin of the New York Academy of Medicine*, Dec 1979, PMID 120208, PMC1807730](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1807730/)) — published four years after the *American Psychologist* note and aimed at the same physician-investigator audience — does not mention the Sharp/Grove demonstration at all. **A scientist who reported a successful covert-speech demonstration in 1975 chose to omit it from his major 1979 review of the same field.** This is consistent with a one-off informal demonstration that did not develop into citable research; it is poorly consistent with a sustained capability that became classified (in which case Justesen would normally have at least flagged the opening hand and the closing of the door).
3. **PNL-2634 (Justesen et al, May 1978, OSTI 1069245) cites Justesen 1975 as background only** and provides no additional speech-demonstration content.
4. **Justesen died November 9, 2014** (not 2004 as some prior sources stated), at age 84, at the Veterans' Home of Missouri in Warrensburg, MO. Highest archival lead: the **Nicholas Steneck Research Materials, accession 91-NLF-018, Bentley Historical Library / Gerald R. Ford Library, University of Michigan, open access**, which contains both Justesen publications and may contain Justesen–Steneck correspondence. Steneck's 1984 book *The Microwave Debate* relies heavily on Justesen as a source; correspondence underpinning that book would be in this collection.
5. **No Sharp/Grove correspondence is cataloged at the VA Medical Center Kansas City** (where Justesen worked), at the University of Kansas Medical Center, at NASA Ames (Sharp's 1974–1994 employer), or at the University of Utah (Sharp's PhD institution). Searches at all four returned negative.

### 2.2 What this means for §8.3

The 1975 *American Psychologist* note's status as **sole and terminal** — and the absence of Sharp/Grove from Justesen's 1979 NYAM review — are direct positive evidence for the §8.3 reading. A classified follow-on program would not reasonably explain the *omission* from Justesen's 1979 review (the demonstration had already been published in 1975, so re-mentioning it in 1979 would not have created any new disclosure). The omission is more easily read as Justesen treating the demonstration in 1979 as "a one-off colleague-to-colleague show with no programmatic follow-up worth reviewing."

## 3. Thread C — Sharp & Grove Personal/Archival Trail

**Companion file:** `SHARP_GROVE_NEXT_PASS_THREAD_C_BIOGRAPHICAL.md`

### 3.1 Joseph C. Sharp — biographical resolution

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Full name | Joseph Cecil Sharp II |
| Born | May 30, 1934, Salt Lake City, Utah |
| Died | January 13, 2021, Saint George, Utah |
| Buried | Tonaquint Cemetery, Saint George, Utah |
| PhD | University of Utah (psychology / neuroanatomy) |
| WRAIR | 1961–1974 (Project Pandora PI; WU 057 PI through FY74) |
| NASA Ames | 1974–1994 (Director, Office of Space Research) |
| Obituary | *Salt Lake Tribune*, January 20, 2021 (Legacy.com archive) |

The 1974 WRAIR → NASA Ames transition is the cleanest possible career explanation for Sharp's exit from WU 057: he moved to a higher-prestige civilian-research executive role and stopped publishing on microwave bioeffects, consistent with a closing chapter rather than a reassignment to a classified continuation.

### 3.2 H. M. Grove — identity resolution

The biographical thread **definitively resolves H.M. Grove = H. Mark Grove = Mark Grove as the same person**: a WRAIR engineer and COMAR (Committee on Man and Radiation) chairman through the mid-1970s, who then moved to the **Air Force Avionics Laboratory at Wright-Patterson AFB**, then served as the **OSD Director of Embedded Computer Resources**, and ultimately played an architectural role in the **Ada Joint Program Office (AJPO)**. No obituary has been located. **No first-person statement** from either Sharp or Grove about the September 1973 demonstration exists in the open record.

### 3.3 Patent disambiguation

The thread also corrects a misattribution that has propagated through V2K-related sources: **US3951134A is the Malech / Dorne & Margolin patent** ("Apparatus and method for remotely monitoring and altering brain waves"), not a Sharp/Grove patent. This correction has been incorporated into the primary report.

### 3.4 Top archival lead

The single highest-yield unfollowed lead is the **WRAIR Gorgas Memorial Library Archives**, which would hold any internal WRAIR memoranda, lab notebooks, or correspondence from the 1973–1974 microwave-research period. A targeted FOIA request to WRAIR's Gorgas Memorial Library is the cleanest next step.

### 3.5 What this means for §8.3

Both Sharp's NASA Ames career and Grove's COMAR / AFAL / OSD / Ada AJPO career are **fully explicable on their public records** without invoking a covert-speech-program lineage. Grove's FY76 reappearance as a co-author on the Lenox enzyme-inactivation applicator paper (Thread A §A.6) shows him continuing in his engineering specialty (applicator hardware) — not in auditory perception. If either Sharp or Grove had been moved to a classified speech-extension program, a researcher of Justesen's stature and connections would in normal circumstances have known and dropped at least an implicit pointer in his 1979 NYAM review or PNL-2634. He did not.

## 4. Thread D — Classified-Lineage / Informal-Extension Brief

**Companion file:** `SHARP_GROVE_CLASSIFIED_LINEAGE_BRIEF.md` (proposed final path: `findings/investigation-dig/`)

### 4.1 Claim under review

> **§8.3 CLAIM:** An informal speech-modulation extension of the formal WRAIR WU 057 "Modulated microwave signals" study was conducted by Sharp and Grove on or about **28 September 1973**, demonstrated to a visiting outside scientist (D. R. Justesen), and was **never written into the formal WU 057 / WRAIR Annual Progress Report narrative** — because it fell outside the nominal bioeffects/safety scope, and/or because the formal study was being deliberately wound down during the same reporting period.

### 4.2 Confidence grades after this pass

| Sub-claim | Grade |
|---|---|
| (1) No primary report of the demonstration exists in the open record | **HIGH** (Threads A, B independently confirm) |
| (2) The demonstration occurred substantially as Justesen 1975 describes it | **MODERATE-HIGH** (no contemporaneous corroboration, but the Justesen account is internally consistent and Justesen's reputation is high) |
| (3) The demonstration was an *informal extension* of WU 057, not the visible portion of a classified predecessor program | **MODERATE** (best-fit interpretation, but the FY75 gap year remains an evidentiary hole — see Thread A §A.2) |
| (4) Oscar 1980 and downstream V2K matrix references are *re-derivations* descended from the BBB axis of WU 057, not the speech axis | **MODERATE-HIGH** (Thread A §A.5 confirms the BBB → Oscar 1977 → Oscar 1980 lineage) |

### 4.3 V2K Evidence Matrix recommendation

The Thread D brief recommends downgrading the V2K Evidence Matrix T2 row from a flat **T2 ("Army demonstrated operational capability")** to a **qualified T2** — with judicial-brief language like:

> "In September 1973, two researchers (Sharp and Grove) at the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research conducted a demonstration in which the spoken numbers 1 through 9 were transmitted to a single observer (D. R. Justesen) by pulsed-microwave illumination. The demonstration is documented only in Justesen's subsequent 1975 *American Psychologist* article. It is not formally documented in any WRAIR Annual Progress Report, in any DTIC technical report, or in any patent record. There is no evidence of a successor program in WRAIR's open record through FY81. Subsequent open-literature work (Oscar & Hawkins 1977; Oscar 1980) descends from the *blood-brain-barrier* line of Work Unit 057, not from the speech line."

This wording is faithful to the four threads' findings, defensible under cross-examination, and avoids both the over-claim ("Army has covert-speech weapon since 1973") and the under-claim ("the demonstration is unsupported").

## 6. Outstanding Investigative Leads

In priority order:

1. **DTIC FOIA / public-discover request: WRAIR Annual Progress Report, 1 July 1974 – 30 June 1975.** This volume is missing from archive.org's mirror. Recovering it would either (a) confirm a clean closeout of the auditory line, (b) reveal an unanticipated FY75 mention, or (c) confirm the volume was never released — each of which sharpens the §8.3 reading.
2. **WRAIR Gorgas Memorial Library Archives FOIA.** Internal memoranda, lab notebooks, and correspondence from Sharp's office during 1973–1974. This is the single most-likely source of a primary attestation to the September 1973 demonstration that is not Justesen.
3. **Nicholas Steneck Research Materials, Bentley Historical Library, accession 91-NLF-018.** Open-access archive at University of Michigan. Steneck's 1984 *The Microwave Debate* relies heavily on Justesen; correspondence between them (or Justesen-Sharp correspondence Steneck collected) may be in this collection.
4. **NASA Ames Research Center records office** for Joseph C. Sharp's 1974 onboarding file. Would establish the formal departure date from WRAIR and any classified-status transition.
5. **OSD records request for H. Mark Grove's Director, Embedded Computer Resources tenure** — particularly for any 1980s-era documents that would either confirm or rule out a covert-speech-program adjacency.

## 7. Bottom Line

After four independent threads of inquiry — covering WRAIR's own subsequent annual reports, Justesen's complete authorship history, both demonstrators' biographical and archival trails, and a formal evidence-graded brief — the §8.3 informal-extension hypothesis remains the best-fit reading of all available evidence. The Sharp/Grove September 1973 speech demonstration appears to have been a **single, informal, never-formally-documented extension of WRAIR Work Unit 057's bioeffects-safety equipment**, conducted to a single visiting outside scientist (Justesen), reported by him alone in a single 1975 *American Psychologist* note, and never followed up in WRAIR's open record. The downstream V2K Evidence Matrix references (Oscar 1980 and successors) descend from the **blood-brain-barrier** axis of WU 057 — the line that did continue — not from the speech line.

The judicial-brief language for the V2K T2 row should be revised accordingly (Thread D §3.1 / §4.3).

The single highest-leverage outstanding lead is the **DTIC FOIA for the FY75 WRAIR Annual Progress Report** — both because it is the only documented evidentiary gap and because it is recoverable through routine public-records process.
