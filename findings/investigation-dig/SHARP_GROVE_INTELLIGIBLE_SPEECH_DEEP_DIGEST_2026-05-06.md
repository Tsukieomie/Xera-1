## 1. What the published claim actually asserts (precision)

Justesen reports **nine of ten** spoken digit-word stimuli could be heard, identified, and distinguished via “voice-modulated” microwaves; **not sentences** and **no naïve listener cohort** separate from Sharp and Grove. He adds that longer stretches were avoided because averaged power would approach **10 mW/cm²** institutional limits (`SHARP_GROVE_NEXT_PASS_THREAD_B_JUSTESEN_WRITEUPS.md`, block quote). That wording supports **digit-level, closed-set discrimination** told by Principals Investigator–subjects familiar with expected words — not conversational intelligibility under blinded protocols.

Treat “intelligible speech” as **graded**: (i) perceptual modulation carrying voice-linked pulse timing, (ii) **closed-set digit labels**, (iii) open-set words/sentences — the claim in the literature is strongest at **(ii)** via a **single-channel published attestation**.
