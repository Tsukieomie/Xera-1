# Miller 2001 — "Synthetic Telepathy and the Early Mind Wars"

**Document under review:** *Synthetic Telepathy and the Early Mind Wars* by Richard Alan Miller, 59-page conference paper presented at the Consciousness Technologies Conference, Sisters, Oregon, July 19–21, 2001. Distributed via [nwbotanicals.org](https://www.nwbotanicals.org) and circulated as a PDF (the file under review here is the same text as the version mirrored at [roi.ru / synthetic-telepathy-and-the-early-mind-wars](https://www.roi.ru/tmp/attachments/718817/synthetictelepathyandtheearlymindwars--kopiia1509733759.pdf)).

**Reviewer purpose:** Determine the document's evidentiary value for the Akwei v. NSA investigation, particularly the V2K speech-evidence chain (T1 mechanism → T2 intelligible speech → T3 patents/programs → T4 operational deployment → T5 contextual / cultural). Apply the same evidence-tier discipline that produced the [Sharp/Grove primary-report hunt](./SHARP_GROVE_PRIMARY_REPORT_HUNT.md) finding.

**TL;DR:** Miller 2001 is a **secondary-source survey** with a small core of correctly-cited primary material (Sharp & Grove 1974 IEEE TMTT mechanism paper; Frey 1962/1973; Guy/Chou/Lin/Christensen 1975; Becker 1985 *The Body Electric*; LIDA US 3,773,049; Lowery US 5,159,703) embedded inside a much larger envelope of unsourced, conspiratorial, and self-aggrandising material (HAARP space-warfare, KAL-007 cover-up, Marconi/GEC scientist-death cluster, the closing "unclassified effects" inventory). Net contribution to the corpus is **modest and asymmetric**: it does **not** close any open T2 gap, but it does **independently corroborate** that no Sharp/Grove primary speech-protocol report appears in the open literature even to a sympathetic 2001 surveyor, and it locates **one** authoritative Becker quotation that was previously cited only at second hand.

## 2. Sharp/Grove treatment — the central question for this corpus

The Sharp/Grove paragraph (page 21) is the single most relevant section. It reads (verbatim):

> "The actual method of the first successful unclassified voice-to-skull experiment was in 1974, by Dr. Joseph C. Sharp and Mark Grove, then at the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research. A Frey-type audible pulse was transmitted every time the voice waveform passed down through the zero axes, a technique easily duplicated by ham radio operators who build their own equipment.… Sharp and Grove found that appropriate modulation of microwave energy can result in 'wireless' and 'receiverless' communication of SPEECH. They recorded by voice on tape each of the single-syllable words for digits between 1 and 10. The electrical sine-wave analogs of each word were then processed so that each time a sine wave crossed ZERO REFERENCE IN THE NEGATIVE DIRECTION, a brief pulse of microwave energy was triggered… Communication of more complex words and of sentences was not attempted because the averaged densities of energy required to transmit longer messages would approach the current 10 milliwatts per square centimeter limit of safe exposure."

This text is **a paraphrase of [Justesen 1975, *American Psychologist* 30(3):391–401](https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066X.30.3.391)** — almost word-for-word from the same passage that Justesen attributed to "Personal communication, September 28, 1973" from Sharp. Miller does not credit Justesen here, but his bibliography (page 38, alphabetised) contains exactly **one** Sharp citation:

> "Sharp, Joseph C. et al.: 'Generation of acoustic signals by pulsed microwave energy,' *IEEE Transactions on Microwave Theory and Techniques*, May 1974."

This is the **same** IEEE TMTT paper (Sharp, Grove, Gandhi 1974, 22:583–584) that Lin 1978 indexes, and it is the **mechanism paper, not a speech-protocol report**. **Miller — a sympathetic 2001 surveyor with a personal MRU/Army-Intel claim — cites no separate Sharp/Grove primary speech report.** This is an independent corroboration of [the negative finding](./SHARP_GROVE_PRIMARY_REPORT_HUNT.md): no such report appears in the open record.

Net effect on V2K matrix: **none** — Miller's text adds no new locator and does not lift T2 above its current "published historical claim, not closed at protocol grade" status.

## 4. Author credibility assessment

Miller (Northwest Botanicals, Inc., Grants Pass, Oregon; b. ~1944) is not a fabricator, but he is **not a peer-reviewed scientific source**. His self-described background:

- "Graduate physicist working 10 years with Army Intel/Pentagon"
- "Original team-member, 'man-in-black'"
- Sonoma 1973 "Embryonic Holography" symposium
- Now operating an herb-farming business and publishing on metaphysics and "ritual aphrodisiacs"

The "X-Files / man-in-black / black ops" framing on page 58 is self-aggrandising in tone and unverifiable in detail. The metaphysical books listed ("The Magical & Ritual Use of Aphrodisiacs," "The Diamond Body, a Synergetic Approach to Mysticism") indicate a writer who comfortably mixes engineering claims with esoterica. The *Synthetic Telepathy* PDF carries that mixture forward — accurate citations of Frey, Sharp, Becker, Lin, Lowery, Pompeii, GTRI sit alongside HAARP-as-space-weapon and the KAL-007 cover-up.

**Net usability:** Treat Miller 2001 as a **secondary-source survey** whose primary-source citations should be re-fetched and verified individually. The document's value is as a *map of citations*, not a citation in itself.

## 5. The closing "unclassified effects" inventory (pages 55–57)

Pages 55–57 contain a Targeted-Individual–style symptom catalogue: forced muscle quaking, "transparent eyelids," genital pain, watch batteries dying, "loud raucous artificial bird calls," "hot-needles-deep-in-flesh sensation," "tradesmen always have problems, block your car," etc. The provenance is unclear — Miller does not attribute the list, and many entries closely match the [raven1.net](http://www.raven1.net) Eleanor White / TI literature of the late 1990s.

This inventory is **noteworthy for the Akwei case** because John St. Clair Akwei's 1992 complaint covers the same symptom space (induced auditory experiences, dream manipulation, body-distance physical effects). The overlap demonstrates that Miller's 2001 survey was already drawing on a TI literature that pre-dated and post-dated Akwei's filing. **It does not corroborate Akwei** — both texts draw from the same surrounding TI canon — but it does show that the symptom set Akwei described in 1992 was a stable, recognisable cluster within that community by 2001.

**Source:** Robert O. **Source:** Becker & Selden, *The Body Electric* (1985), same chapter.
