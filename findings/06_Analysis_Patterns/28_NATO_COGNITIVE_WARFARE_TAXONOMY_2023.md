# FILE 28: NATO COGNITIVE WARFARE TAXONOMY (2023) -- THE UnCODE SYSTEM

## Investigation Relevance

In 1992, John St. Clair Akwei filed Civil Action 92-0449 describing a system where the NSA could:
1. Remotely monitor neural activity ("Remote Neural Monitoring")
2. Stimulate specific brain regions at specific frequencies (10 Hz motor, 15 Hz auditory, 25 Hz visual, 9 Hz somatosensory, 20 Hz thought center)
3. Use these capabilities for targeted harassment and control of individuals
4. Operate a domestic intelligence network (DOMINT) employing these technologies

Judge Stanley Sporkin -- who served as CIA General Counsel from 1981-1986 -- dismissed this as "frivolous" in 14 days. The NSA was never required to respond. No discovery was permitted. No depositions were taken.

**31 years later, NATO published a formal taxonomy classifying these exact capabilities as recognized methods of warfare.**

## B. WHAT THE PAPER ESTABLISHES

### B.1 Definition of Cognitive Warfare (CogWar)

The paper defines Cognitive Warfare as warfare that targets the physical state of neural systems -- biological, technical, or hybrid -- to influence how targets think, decide, and act. The paper is explicit that this goes beyond information warfare or psychological operations.

Key framing from the abstract: CogWar "takes advantage of novel developments in technology and science to influence how target populations think and act." Both "human and nonhuman cognition should be considered as targets."

### B.2 The Four Axioms

The UnCODE system is built on four axioms:

- A1: CogWar goals are based on cognition
- A2: Cognition occurs in a neural system
- A3: A neural system is a physical system that processes inputs and produces outputs
- A4: A neural system changes input-output activity by changing its physical state

**Critical implication:** By grounding CogWar in the physical state of neural systems, NATO is formally acknowledging that cognition can be targeted through physical means -- including electromagnetic, acoustic, chemical, and biological vectors. This is the exact physical basis Akwei described in 1992.

### B.3 The Five Goal Classes (UnCODE)

| Goal | Definition | Akwei Parallel |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| **Unplug** | Eliminate a target's ability to process inputs and produce outputs | Employment sabotage -- Akwei unable to work for 8 years |
| **Corrupt** | Degrade a target's capacity to process inputs and produce outputs | EMF brain stimulation at degrading frequencies |
| **disOrganize** | Bias a target's input-output activity | Neural manipulation to alter perception, judgment, behavior |
| **Diagnose** | Monitor and understand input-output relationships in a neural system | Remote Neural Monitoring (RNM) -- the core of Akwei's claims |
| **Enhance** | Improve processing capacity of allied neural systems | Not alleged by Akwei against himself, but implied for operators |

## C. THE METHODS TAXONOMY -- DIRECT MAPPING TO AKWEI

### C.1 Table 1: Methods for Reaching CogWar Goals

The paper's Table 1 is the most significant element for this investigation. It categorizes CogWar methods by access type and provides specific examples. Below are the categories with direct relevance to Akwei's 1992 claims:

### C.2 Direct Access -- Brute Force (Without Consent)

NATO's description: "Directly interfacing with nervous system without consent from target."

Examples given in the paper:
- **"Target is unknowingly or unwillingly exposed to neuroactive agents designed to damage neurons or influence cognitive processes"** [refs 11-14]
- **"Target is exposed to sound waves from an acoustic cyberweapon designed to cause physical damage to the auditory information processing system"** [ref 90]
- **"Hack brain-computer interfaces to surveil brain activity"** [refs 8-10, 31, 59]

**Akwei parallel:** Akwei described involuntary, non-consensual targeting by electromagnetic systems that stimulated specific brain regions. This is precisely the "brute-force direct access" category -- interfacing with his nervous system without his consent. NATO now classifies this as a recognized method of Cognitive Warfare.

### C.3 Indirect Access -- Directed and Neuroergonomic

NATO's description: "Designed for specific targets. Based on general nervous system knowledge, or the target's neural system."

Examples given in the paper:
- **"Use optical signals to infer brain activity"** [refs 8, 96] -- the Diagnose goal
- **"Non-invasive brain stimulation to increase performance"** [refs 74-76] -- the Enhance goal (when used on allies) or Corrupt/disOrganize goal (when used on adversaries)
- **"Produce and export products with screens that emit light that reduces cognitive capacity"** [ref 94]
- Design information that triggers hypervigilance, urgency, and hostile attribution bias in specific targets

**Akwei parallel:** Akwei described a system designed specifically for him -- monitoring his neural activity and stimulating specific brain regions at specific frequencies. This maps to "directed and neuroergonomic" methods under both Diagnose and disOrganize/Corrupt goals.

### C.4 The "Diagnose" Goal -- Surveillance of Brain Activity

The paper states that Diagnose goals include: "extract information from brain activity through surveillance of brain signals and hacking of brain-computer interfaces" [refs 8, 59].

**Reference [8] is critical:** Canham M., Sawyer B.D., "Neurosecurity: Human brain electro-optical signals as MASINT," American Intelligence Journal 36, 40-47 (2019).

This reference, published in the **American Intelligence Journal** (the journal of the intelligence community), explicitly frames human brain signals as **MASINT** -- Measurement and Signature Intelligence. This means the U.S. intelligence community has formally published research treating brain emissions as collectible intelligence signals.

**Akwei parallel:** Akwei's "Remote Neural Monitoring" is exactly what NATO now classifies as a Diagnose method and what the American Intelligence Journal calls brain MASINT. In 1992, this was dismissed as frivolous. By 2019, it was published in an intelligence community journal. By 2023, NATO formally classified it.

### C.5 Brain Stimulation as a Recognized Capability

The paper dedicates significant discussion to non-invasive brain stimulation:

- References [74-76] cover non-invasive neuromodulation methods for improving cognitive performance
- Reference [76] specifically covers transcranial electrical stimulation (tES) at a NATO symposium (HFM-RSY-334)
- The paper notes that the U.S. Army, Air Force, and Navy have all published extensively on tES for performance enhancement [ref 74]
- Reference [77] directly addresses "Hacking the Brain: Dimensions of Cognitive Enhancement"
- Reference [82] is from **NATO ACT Innovation Hub**: "Weaponization of Neuroscience" (2020)

**Akwei parallel:** Akwei described EMF brain stimulation at specific frequencies targeting specific cortical regions. NATO now formally classifies brain stimulation as both an Enhance method (for allies) and implicitly a Corrupt/disOrganize method (when directed against targets without consent). The same physical capability serves offense or defense depending on intent.

## G. THE CogWar-AS-INTRUSION FRAMEWORK

### G.1 NATO's Own Framing

The paper explicitly frames Cognitive Warfare as an intrusion problem, quoting from reference [122]:

The essence of an intrusion involves developing a payload to breach a trusted boundary, establishing a presence inside a trusted environment, and from that presence, taking actions toward objectives -- whether moving laterally or violating confidentiality, integrity, or availability.

### G.2 Mapping to Akwei

When NATO frames CogWar as an intrusion:
- **Trusted boundary:** Akwei's skull / nervous system
- **Payload:** Electromagnetic signals at specific frequencies
- **Breach:** Non-consensual RF stimulation penetrating the boundary
- **Presence established:** Persistent monitoring and stimulation capability
- **Actions toward objectives:** Neural manipulation for harassment and control
- **Confidentiality violation:** Reading thoughts (RNM)
- **Integrity violation:** Altering neural activity (EMF stimulation)
- **Availability violation:** Disrupting ability to function (employment sabotage, cognitive degradation)

This is not an analogy. This is the NATO framework applied to Akwei's claims. His allegations describe a textbook CogWar intrusion as defined by NATO in 2023.

## H. THE CLASSIFIED SURVEILLANCE CATCH-22 -- REVISITED

### H.1 The 1992 Problem

In 1992, when Akwei described remote neural monitoring and electromagnetic brain stimulation:
- No publicly available framework existed for classifying these capabilities
- No NATO taxonomy recognized them as methods of warfare
- The judge (former CIA General Counsel) dismissed them as "frivolous"
- The technology was classified
- Describing classified technology made the claimant appear delusional
- Appearing delusional justified dismissal without investigation

### H.2 The 2023 Resolution

By 2023:
- NATO has published an entire research program on Cognitive Warfare
- Brain signal surveillance is formally classified as a Diagnose method
- Non-invasive brain stimulation is formally classified under multiple CogWar goals
- Brute-force access to the nervous system without consent is a recognized attack category
- Multiple NATO publications use terms like "weaponization of neuroscience" and "battle for the brain"
- The American Intelligence Journal has published research on brain signals as MASINT

### H.3 The Implications

The gap between "frivolous" (1992) and "formally classified NATO warfare method" (2023) is not a gap in the technology -- it is a gap in what was publicly acknowledged. The technology existed in both eras. The only thing that changed is the willingness of institutional actors to discuss it openly.

This is the classified surveillance catch-22 in its purest form: the capabilities were real in 1992 but classified, making anyone who described them appear to be fabricating. By 2023, the same capabilities are openly discussed, classified, and studied -- but the man who described them 31 years earlier remains legally branded as "frivolous."

## J. ASSESSMENT

### J.1 What This Document Proves

This NATO paper does not prove Akwei's specific allegations about being personally targeted by the NSA. What it proves is:

1. **The capabilities Akwei described are real** -- NATO formally classifies monitoring brain activity, stimulating brain regions, and non-consensual neural targeting as recognized methods of warfare
2. **The capabilities are studied by NATO-affiliated military researchers** -- not conspiracy theorists, not fringe academics, but active-duty Norwegian Armed Forces cyber defense personnel
3. **The capabilities are supported by 124 academic references** spanning neuroscience, cybersecurity, military science, and cognitive science
4. **The institutional framework Akwei described exists** -- there are formal military programs studying both offensive and defensive applications of neurotechnology
5. **The "frivolous" dismissal is retrospectively indefensible** -- a judge cannot credibly call "frivolous" what NATO now publishes entire symposia about

### J.2 What Changed Between 1992 and 2023

| Dimension | 1992 | 2023 |
|-----------|------|------|
| Public acknowledgment | Classified / denied | Openly published by NATO |
| Academic literature | Sparse, mostly classified | 124+ references in this paper alone |
| Institutional framework | Denied to exist | Multiple NATO research activities (HFM-334, HFM-356, HFM-361) |
| Legal treatment | "Frivolous" -- dismissed in 14 days | No legal reckoning has occurred |
| Technology | Existed (Malech 1976, Frey 1962, etc.) | Same technology, now openly discussed |
| The person who described it | Branded as delusional | Still legally branded as "frivolous" |

### J.3 The Core Finding

NATO's 2023 UnCODE taxonomy is, in functional terms, a classification system for the exact capabilities John St. Clair Akwei described to a federal court in 1992. Every major category of his allegations -- neural monitoring, brain stimulation, non-consensual targeting, cognitive disruption -- now has a formal NATO classification, a body of supporting academic literature, and institutional recognition from NATO member state military forces.

The 14-day dismissal by a former CIA General Counsel did not make these capabilities less real. It made them less publicly acknowledged for 31 years.
