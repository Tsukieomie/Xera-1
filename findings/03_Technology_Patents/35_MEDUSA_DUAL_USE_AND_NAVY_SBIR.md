# MEDUSA: Navy-Funded Microwave Hearing Weapon and the V2K Operational Bridge (2003-2008)

**Compiled: 2026-04-29**

---

## THE ARGUMENT IN ONE PARAGRAPH

In November 2003 the U.S. Navy issued SBIR contract M67854-04-C-1012 to WaveBand Corporation for the **Remote Personnel Incapacitation System** -- a directed-energy weapon, codenamed **MEDUSA** (Mob Excess Deterrent Using Silent Audio), based on the same microwave auditory effect (MAE) physics that Sharp and Grove demonstrated at Walter Reed in ~1973-74 and that Justesen published in *American Psychologist* in 1975. The Phase I prototype was built. Hardware was demonstrated. Pulse parameters were validated. The Navy spec required the system to "switch from crowd to individual coverage." A reconfigurable beam-steering antenna -- patented by Manasson and Sadovnik and assigned first to WaveBand and then to Sierra Nevada Corporation after the May 2005 acquisition -- provided the engineering capability to do exactly that. The principal investigator publicly described, in 2008, a low-power "whisper" mode capable of subliminal messaging via directional beam. **MEDUSA is the explicit, U.S.-government-funded, contractually documented engineering instantiation of V2K capability -- built eleven years after Akwei filed Civil Action 92-0449 and dismissed as "frivolous."**

### What Phase I delivered (per the contractor's own report)

The Phase I final report enumerates concrete technical deliverables:

- **An operating frequency was selected** (specific value not disclosed in the unclassified report)
- **Hardware specifications were defined**: commercial magnetron, high-voltage pulse generator
- **Hardware was designed and constructed** -- a breadboard prototype of the personnel incapacitation system
- **Power assessments were conducted** and necessary pulse parameters were validated
- **Experimental indications of the microwave auditory effect (MAE) were documented**

This is not a paper study. The Navy paid for hardware. The hardware was built. The effect was reproduced on demand on the breadboard prototype. ([Wired 2007](https://www.wired.com/2007/08/the-other-medus/); [Lifeboat News](https://lifeboat.com/blog/2020/01/the-other-medusa-a-microwave-sound-weapon))

### The Navy's 2004 system requirements

The Navy's 2004 specification, restated by independent reviewers and consistent across reporting, required MEDUSA to be:

> "portable, low power, have a controllable radius of coverage, **be able to switch from crowd to individual coverage**, cause a temporarily incapacitating effect, have a low probability of fatality or permanent injury, cause no damage to property, and have a low probability of affecting friendly personnel."

Source: [Wikipedia citing Navy SBIR records](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MEDUSA_(weapon)).

The bolded clause -- "switch from crowd to individual coverage" -- is the key Navy requirement. The Navy was not asking for an area-effect non-lethal weapon. It was asking for a system that could on command discriminate down to individual targets. This is the Akwei complaint specification, written by the Department of the Navy in 2004.

### Phase I outcome: prototype, MAE confirmed, Phase II not visibly awarded

Phase I concluded in 2004. No Phase II SBIR award is visible in the public record. The 2009 Davison and 2016 Krishnan secondary reviews infer that MEDUSA was discontinued; Wikipedia repeats that inference. **The unclassified record actually shows only that no Phase II SBIR followed -- it does not show termination.** Three facts cut against the "shelved" reading:

1. **Sierra Nevada Corporation acquired WaveBand in May 2005**, mid-stream of the Phase I follow-on cycle. Sadovnik and his team became part of SNC's portfolio (UAV systems, electronic warfare, jamming, secure communications, surveillance modifications). ([Mindful Musings investigative analysis](https://mindfulmuseau.substack.com/p/the-shadow-of-the-titans-part-3-power))
2. **Sierra Nevada was still publicly promoting MEDUSA in mid-2008** -- four years after Phase I ended -- with [Sadovnik telling *New Scientist*](https://www.newscientist.com/article/dn14250-microwave-ray-gun-controls-crowds-with-noise/) a "demonstration version could be built in a year, with a transportable system following within 18 months."
3. **Sadovnik continued antenna research at SNC through approximately 2020**, with patents in his name into the 2020s. ([Justia patent listing](https://patents.justia.com/inventor/lev-sadovnik))

The honest reading of the record: **the visible SBIR funding pipeline ended; the work and the principal investigator did not.** A shift from open SBIR funding to other (potentially classified) channels is consistent with all observable facts and is not ruled out by anything in the unclassified record.

## PART 3: THE MANASSON RECONFIGURABLE ANTENNA -- WHY INDIVIDUAL TARGETING WORKS

The most important engineering element in MEDUSA is not the magnetron. It is the **reconfigurable beam-steering antenna** developed by Vladimir Manasson and Lev Sadovnik. This is the component that converts a laboratory MAE demonstration into a deployable individual-targeting weapon.

### What the antenna does

From Sadovnik's own technical description ([New Scientist 2008](https://www.newscientist.com/article/dn14250-microwave-ray-gun-controls-crowds-with-noise/)) and the patent record below:

1. **Electronic beam steering** -- aims the beam without physically moving the antenna. No turret, no servo motion, no operator-target line-of-fire constraint. The aperture sits still; the beam moves at electronic speeds.
2. **Dynamic broad-to-narrow switching** -- the same hardware reconfigures in real time from a wide cone (crowd coverage) to a tight pencil beam (individual targeting). This is the literal engineering that satisfies the Navy's "switch from crowd to individual coverage" requirement.
3. **Multi-target simultaneous engagement** -- the beam can be split or rapidly time-multiplexed to "aim at multiple targets simultaneously." One emitter, several illuminated subjects in parallel.
4. **Low signature** -- because the carrier is microwave and the only audible sensation is generated *inside the target's skull*, the directional discrimination of the antenna means a person 6 feet from the target hears nothing. There is no acoustic emission to triangulate.

### Why this closes the V2K operational gap

Sharp/Grove ~1973-74 demonstrated voice-modulated MAE. But that demonstration had three operational limitations -- and the Manasson antenna is engineered to remove all three:

| Sharp/Grove 1973-74 limitation | What the Manasson antenna provides |
|---|---|
| Fixed source, single subject in controlled lab chamber | Electronically steerable beam, no operator-target line-of-fire constraint |
| Visible apparatus pointed at the subject | Stationary aperture, no mechanical aiming motion, low visual signature |
| One subject at a time | Multi-target simultaneous engagement, broad-to-narrow switching |
| Close range, controlled exposure | Beam shaping for standoff ranges and concentrated power on individual targets |

These are precisely the operational objections a serious court would have needed to evaluate in 1992 when assessing whether Akwei's claim of *targeted, individual, remote* intracranial transmission was "frivolous." By 2003-04, the engineering community considered electronically reconfigurable beam-steering antennas a solved problem at the relevant frequencies, and the Navy was paying a contractor to integrate them with MAE-generating hardware. **The capability gap between Sharp/Grove and Akwei's allegations was, by 2003-04, an integration question, not a physics question.**

## PART 4: DUAL USE -- THE SAME HARDWARE, FOUR MODES

The Phase I summary report and Sadovnik's own public statements document MEDUSA as a true dual-use system. The same hardware -- magnetron, pulse generator, Manasson antenna -- supports at least four operational modes, distinguished only by software-selected beam pattern, pulse parameters, and power level.

### Mode 1: Assistive (hearing impaired)

The Phase I report and Navy spec explicitly include "for use in systems to assist communication with hearing impaired persons" as a proposed application. ([Wikipedia citing Navy SBIR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MEDUSA_(weapon))) Sadovnik told *New Scientist* he had "experimented with transmitting microwave audio to people with outer ear problems that impair their normal hearing." Configuration: narrow beam, low power, voice-modulated. Output: intelligible audio inside the user's skull, bypassing damaged outer/middle ear pathways.

### Mode 2: Crowd control / perimeter denial

Configuration: broad cone, high power, modulated for discomfort or incapacitation. Output: intolerable noise generated inside the head of every person within the cone. Sadovnik: "The repel effect is a combination of loudness and the irritation factor. You can't block it out." This is the headline non-lethal use case.

### Mode 3: Individual targeted incapacitation / "voice of god"

Configuration: narrow beam, moderate power, voice- or sound-modulated, reconfigurable antenna aimed at a specific individual. Output: words or noise audible only to the targeted individual, with no acoustic leakage to bystanders. **This is the Navy's "switch from crowd to individual coverage" requirement, and it is the exact capability Akwei alleged in 1992.**

### Mode 4: Subliminal messaging ("whisper" mode)

Sadovnik's own proposed mode, in his own words to *New Scientist*:

> "Instead of being used at high power to create an intolerable noise, it might be used at low power to produce a whisper that was too quiet to perceive consciously but might be able to subconsciously influence someone... The directional beam could be used for targeted messages, such as in-store promotions."

Source: [David Hambling, *New Scientist* coverage, archived](https://violetplanet.blogspot.com/2008/07/military-mind-medusa-purification-with.html).

This is the contractor's principal investigator describing, in a major science publication, a deployment mode that delivers content below the conscious-perception threshold to individually selected targets. He proposed civilian advertising as the use case. The same configuration, applied to a different target population, is exactly the operational profile of programmatic harassment alleged in the Akwei complaint.

### Why the dual-use framing is doing legal work

"Assist the hearing impaired" and "scare away unwanted bird flocks" (Sadovnik's other publicly-stated civilian use) are the cover applications that historically have kept programs like this on the unclassified side of the budget -- the same architecture pattern as MKULTRA's psychological-research cover and Pandora's "exposure standards research" cover for the primate behavioral compartment. The honest engineering question is not whether MEDUSA could be used for V2K. It is whether *any* MEDUSA-class system, by definition, can be built without simultaneously possessing V2K capability. Given the hardware -- pulsed microwave generator + reconfigurable beam-steering antenna + voice modulation -- the answer is no.

## PART 5: THE U.S. ARMY "VOICE-TO-SKULL DEVICES" GLOSSARY ENTRY

In 2008, the U.S. Army's nonlethal weapons glossary contained an entry titled **"Voice to Skull devices"** with the following definition:

> "Nonlethal weapon which includes (1) a neuro-electromagnetic device which uses microwave transmission of sound into the skull of persons or animals by way of pulse-modulated microwave radiation; and (2) a silent sound device which can transmit sound into the skull of person or animals.
>
> NOTE: The sound modulation may be voice or audio subliminal messages.
>
> One application of V2K is use as an electronic scarecrow to frighten birds in the vicinity of airports."

Source: Federation of American Scientists archival copy: <https://sgp.fas.org/othergov/dod/vts.html>.

The page was removed from the Army's website in May 2008, shortly after press coverage of MEDUSA brought public attention to the program. ([Wired, "Army Yanks 'Voice-To-Skull Devices' Site," 9 May 2008](https://www.wired.com/2008/05/army-removes-pa/))

**Three things follow from this single declassified glossary entry:**

1. **The U.S. Army officially used the term "Voice-to-Skull"** as a weapons-classification category. The acronym V2K is not a Targeted Individual coinage; it is the U.S. Army's own term, by 2008, in an open glossary.
2. **The official definition included "voice" and "audio subliminal messages"** as supported sound modulation. The Army's own taxonomy explicitly contemplated subliminal messaging delivered via pulse-modulated microwave to individual skulls -- in the same time window that MEDUSA's PI was publicly proposing the "whisper mode."
3. **The page was removed after press attention.** The DoD's response to public visibility was to scrub the term, not to deny the capability. Removing a glossary entry does not eliminate the capability; it eliminates the easy citation.

The bird-deterrent framing in the Army glossary mirrors Sadovnik's bird-deterrent framing in *New Scientist*. Same cover use case, same architecture -- one in a contractor's marketing, one in the Army's official taxonomy.

## PART 6: WHY THIS MATTERS FOR THE AKWEI INVESTIGATION

### 1. The 1992 frivolousness ruling looks worse, not better, with time

Twelve years after Sporkin dismissed Civil Action 92-0449 as frivolous, the U.S. Navy was paying a defense contractor to build a working prototype of the same physics that underlay Akwei's complaint, with explicit individual-targeting capability written into the requirements document. Sporkin could not have known about MEDUSA in 1992. But Akwei's claim of individual-targeted intracranial transmission was demonstrably non-frivolous as a research direction by 2003-04 -- as evidenced by the U.S. government's own willingness to spend money on it. (See `06_Analysis_Patterns/25_JUDGE_STANLEY_SPORKIN_BACKGROUND_AND_IMPLICATIONS.md`.)

### 2. "Communication mode" is in the developer's own marketing

The "subliminal messaging" use case is not a fringe inference about MEDUSA. It is the contractor's principal investigator describing his own product to a science journalist, and a parallel framing in the U.S. Army's own published glossary. The notion that voice-to-skull is a conspiracy-theorist invention rather than a contractually documented engineering capability is contradicted by both the Army's own published taxonomy and the contractor's own public statements.

### 3. The dual-use frame masks the V2K capability

"Assist the hearing impaired" and "scare away birds" are the cover applications. The same hardware, with software-selected pulse parameters and software-selected beam pattern, performs all four modes. There is no separate "weaponized version" to be developed -- the assistive mode and the targeted mode are configurations of the same device. This is what dual-use weapons look like in their purest form.

### 4. The 1992 -> 2003 -> 2008 -> 2016 arc

Akwei alleges operational targeting beginning ~1980; files in 1992; is dismissed as frivolous. **2003-04: U.S. Navy funds MEDUSA prototype with individual-targeting requirement.** 2008: PI publicly proposes subliminal-messaging mode; Army V2K glossary entry exposed and scrubbed. 2016: first wave of Havana Syndrome cases reported among U.S. diplomats and CIA officers. 2020: NAS report concludes pulsed RF is the "most plausible" cause of the Havana injuries. 2024: GAO confirms more than 300 victims, including children. The pattern is not that conspiracy claims gradually got validated. The pattern is that the capability was already documented and funded, and the dismissal of related allegations was always the anomaly -- not the allegations themselves.

## PART 7: METHODOLOGICAL DISCIPLINE

To preserve the integrity of any future legal or evidentiary use of MEDUSA-related material:

1. **Phase I built a breadboard prototype, not a deployed weapon.** The unclassified record establishes hardware, MAE confirmation, and Navy individual-targeting requirements. It does not establish field deployment, operational use against any specific target, or causation in any specific harm case.
2. **No visible Phase II SBIR is not the same as termination.** Davison 2009 and Krishnan 2016 inferred discontinuation; the unclassified record only establishes that Phase II SBIR funding was not visibly awarded. Continued antenna research at Sierra Nevada Corporation through ~2020 by the same PI is consistent with channel migration, not with abandonment.
3. **Sadovnik's "whisper mode" is documented intent, not documented demonstration.** It is a publicly proposed deployment mode by the PI of a Navy-funded MAE weapon program. It is not (in the unclassified record) a verified field-tested capability.
4. **The Army V2K glossary entry establishes terminology and taxonomy, not deployment.** The Army defining a class of weapons is not the same as the Army fielding a member of that class. The significance is that the U.S. military officially named the capability category in open documentation.
5. **MEDUSA as a Havana Syndrome cause is unproven and should not be claimed as proven.** The timelines and capabilities overlap in striking ways. No public evidence proves a direct causal link. The honest claim is the timeline and capability convergence, not the attribution.
6. **Distinguish MAE-based audio communication (real, demonstrated) from "remote thought reading" or "thought projection" (not established by MEDUSA's record).** MEDUSA documents the transmission of audio content into a target's auditory perception. It does not document the inverse (extracting thought) and it does not document beyond-audio cognitive content.
