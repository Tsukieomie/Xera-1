# JASON Advisory Group and the Havana Syndrome / AHI Investigations

---

## 1. What Is JASON?

JASON is an independent scientific advisory group that provides consulting services to the U.S. government on matters of defense science and technology. It was founded in December 1959 and launched as a formal program in 1960, growing out of a summer study initiative — Project 137 — organized by physicists John Archibald Wheeler, Eugene Wigner, and others in the aftermath of Sputnik ([Federation of American Scientists](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/); [Wikipedia / JASON advisory group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JASON_(advisory_group))). The name was suggested by Mildred Goldberger, wife of founding member Murph Goldberger, in honor of the mythological leader of the Argonauts; the group rejected the Pentagon's original working title, "Project Sunrise," as presumptuous ([Science, June 2019](https://www.science.org/content/article/jason-secretive-group-cold-war-science-advisers-fighting-survive-21st-century)).

JASON has between 30 and 65 members at any given time — physicists, biologists, chemists, mathematicians, and computer scientists — who hold full security clearances and are selected exclusively by existing members. Over the decades, eleven members have won Nobel Prizes. Sponsors have included the Department of Defense (DoD), the Department of Energy (DoE), the National Nuclear Security Administration (NNSA), and the U.S. Intelligence Community. For administrative purposes, JASON's activities are run through the MITRE Corporation, a non-profit in McLean, Virginia ([Wikipedia / JASON advisory group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JASON_(advisory_group))).

The group performs most of its work during an annual summer study. Roughly half of all resulting reports are classified. Notable early work includes the design of the McNamara Line electronic barrier during the Vietnam War, early modeling of CO₂-driven global warming (1979), and the invention of adaptive optics for astronomical telescopes. In April 2019, DoD abruptly terminated its MITRE contract, forcing JASON into a temporary existential crisis that was resolved when the NNSA offered emergency support and Congress, via the National Defense Authorization Act for FY2020, directed DoD to re-engage JASON on an ongoing basis ([FAS / "Jason Abides," June 2024](https://fas.org/publication/jason-abides/); [Science, April 2019](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.364.6437.220)).

## 2. The 2018 JASON Study on the Havana Incidents

Starting in late 2016, U.S. and Canadian diplomatic personnel stationed in Havana, Cuba, began reporting a cluster of unusual symptoms following perceived exposure to localized sounds or pressure: acute vertigo, ear pain, cognitive impairment, and persistent concussion-like deficits. The State Department commissioned several investigations in parallel, including one from JASON.

JASON's study — formally titled *Acoustic Signals and Physiological Effects on U.S. Diplomats in Cuba* — was finalized and submitted to the State Department on **November 29, 2018** ([NDU Press / *Joint Force Quarterly*, January 2023](https://ndupress.ndu.edu/Media/News/News-Article-View/Article/3262785/havana-syndrome-directed-attack-or-cricket-noise/)). The report analyzed **eight audio recordings** from the original 21 Havana cases and **two cellphone videos** taken by one patient ([Wikipedia / Havana syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Havana_syndrome)). Its findings were first reported publicly in July 2019. The full text remained restricted as a sensitive-but-unclassified government document until September 2021, when portions were declassified and made available via a Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) request. The full declassified document is accessible via the Federation of American Scientists at [irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf) and was posted on DocumentCloud by BuzzFeed News ([DocumentCloud / JASON Report 2018](https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/21068770-jason-report-2018-havana-syndrome/)).

**Classified status:** The 2018 report was *not* formally classified (Top Secret / SCI); it was issued at a restricted-distribution level and withheld from public release by the State Department for approximately three years. The declassification in September 2021 came specifically through a FOIA request — it was not proactively released. This distinction matters: the State Department's prolonged non-disclosure of a report that cleared Cuba of sonic-weapon culpability drew significant criticism ([BuzzFeed News / Dan Vergano, September 30, 2021](https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/danvergano/havana-syndrome-jason-crickets); [MuckRock FOIA filing](https://www.muckrock.com/foi/united-states-of-america-10/fbi-otdbau-2018-135010/)).

## 3. JASON's Core Findings: Crickets, Not Microwave Weapons

The JASON 2018 report reached seven main findings, drawn from careful acoustic analysis ([FAS / JASON report full text](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf)):

1. **No plausible single energy source** could produce *both* the recorded audio/video signals *and* the reported medical effects. Neither radio/microwave nor sonic energy satisfies both constraints simultaneously.

2. **Sound pressure levels too low to cause harm.** The observed pressure levels of the recorded sounds were not sufficient, by themselves, to produce long-term neurological injury. Sounds loud enough to harm would have caused victims to cover their ears immediately — and they did not.

3. **Objective medical suffering is real.** JASON acknowledged that neurological evaluations showed genuine objective impairment in some individuals, including saccadic dysfunction in 10 of 21 patients and vestibular asymmetry in others. However, the absence of pre-deployment baseline data and comparable control groups made causal attribution scientifically untenable.

4. **Recorded sounds most likely biological/mechanical.** With high confidence, JASON concluded the sounds were "mechanical or biological in origin, rather than electronic. The most likely source is the Indies short-tailed cricket, *Anurogryllus celerinictus*." The call of this species — known to inhabit Cuba and the West Indies — matched the spectral properties of the recordings in "nuanced detail" once room echoes were accounted for, including pulse repetition frequency and spectral comb structure. A University of Florida reference recording from Big Pine Key, Florida, at 27°C, was used for comparison. A secondary match was found with the robust conehead katydid (*Neoconocephalus robustus*), though its pulse repetition frequency was less stable and its presence in Cuba uncertain.

5. **High-power RF ruled out with high confidence.** The recorded audio signal was "with high confidence, not produced by the nonlinear detection of high-power radio frequency or ultrasound pulses."

6. **Frey effect (microwave auditory effect) judged highly unlikely.** JASON assessed as "highly unlikely" the hypothesis that pulsed RF mimicked acoustic signals in both the brain (via the Frey effect) and in electronic recording devices.

7. **Deception scenario not excluded.** The report explicitly noted it "cannot be ruled out that the perceived sounds, while not harmful, are introduced by an adversary as deception so as to mask an entirely unrelated mode of causing illness." This caveat was notable: JASON left open the theoretical possibility that a weapon operated on a different mechanism while using cricket-like noise as cover.

On psychogenic effects, JASON concluded that "psychogenic effects may serve to explain important components of the reported injuries," noting that persistent postural-perceptual dizziness (PPPD) — a recognized functional vestibular disorder — is triggered and sustained by exactly the kind of anxiety-laden environment the Havana diplomats experienced ([FAS / JASON full text](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf)).

## 4. How JASON's Conclusions Compared to the JAMA 2018 Study and the 2020 NAS Report

### The JAMA 2018 Swanson Study

In February 2018 — months before the JASON report was completed — a team at the University of Pennsylvania's Center for Brain Injury and Repair published a landmark case series in *JAMA*: Swanson et al., "Neurological Manifestations Among US Government Personnel Reporting Directional Audible and Sensory Phenomena in Havana, Cuba" ([JAMA Network, February 2018](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2673168)). Based on multidisciplinary evaluations of 21 affected individuals (conducted on average 203 days after exposure), the study found persistent cognitive, vestibular, oculomotor, and auditory dysfunction — with impairments in 6 cognitive domains in all patients who underwent neuropsychological testing. The authors concluded that the subjects "appeared to have sustained injury to widespread brain networks without an associated history of head trauma," coining the concept of a novel acquired brain injury.

JASON's 2018 report directly challenged this framing. While JASON acknowledged the medical findings were "real," it argued the study's conclusions were compromised by the absence of pre-deployment baseline data and lack of an appropriate control group. JASON further explicitly stated that "psychogenic effects" — which the JAMA paper dismissed — could explain "important components" of the reported injuries, particularly vestibular symptoms ([FAS / JASON full text](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf)). Subsequent criticism of the Swanson study, published in academic reviews and a later *International Journal of Social Psychiatry* post-mortem, noted that the threshold for "impairment" in the neuropsychological battery was set unusually low, such that "just about anybody would be diagnosed with brain injury" ([PMC / *Int. J. Social Psychiatry*, December 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10913303/)).

A 2019 follow-on JAMA study by Verma et al. using advanced diffusion MRI claimed differences in white matter volume and cerebellar microstructural integrity in 40 personnel compared to 48 healthy controls ([JAMA Network, July 2019](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2738552)). This was also later challenged: the controls had no history of prior concussions, whereas 12 of the AHI cohort did, which alone could account for imaging differences.

### The 2020 NAS Report

In December 2020, the National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine (NASEM) released *An Assessment of Illness in U.S. Government Employees and Their Families at Overseas Embassies*, chaired by Stanford's Dr. David Relman ([National Academies press release, December 2020](https://www.nationalacademies.org/news/new-report-assesses-illnesses-among-us-government-personnel-and-their-families-at-overseas-embassies)). The NAS panel concluded that "directed, pulsed radio frequency energy appears to be the most plausible mechanism" for the distinctive early symptoms — particularly the sudden onset of a perceived directional sound, intense head pressure, and ear pain.

This directly contradicted JASON's 2018 findings on two fronts. First, the NAS endorsed RF energy as a plausible cause where JASON had rated it "highly unlikely." Second, the NAS committee had not reviewed JASON's 2018 report as part of its work — a gap the State Department confirmed to BuzzFeed News ([BuzzFeed News / Dan Vergano, September 30, 2021](https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/danvergano/havana-syndrome-jason-crickets)). The NAS panel was reviewing a different body of evidence and operated under different mandate; the failure to share findings across investigatory bodies was itself criticized by the State Department's own Accountability Review Board ([NSArchive, February 2021](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/cuba/2021-02-10/secrets-havana-syndrome-how-trumps-state-department-cia-mishandled-mysterious-maladies-cuba)).

The contrast between the two reports created a fractured scientific landscape: JASON (acoustics-focused, reviewing original recordings) concluded crickets and psychogenic effects; NAS (clinically focused, reviewing medical records and existing literature) concluded directed RF energy. Neither body had access to all of the other's evidence.

## 5. Public Release Status: FOIA Attempts and Media Reporting

The timeline of public knowledge about the JASON 2018 report unfolded in stages:

- **July 2019**: The report's existence and its cricket hypothesis were first reported publicly, though the document itself remained withheld.
- **September 2021**: BuzzFeed News reporter **Dan Vergano** published the full story after obtaining the JASON report through a Freedom of Information Act request. The headline: ["A Declassified State Department Report Says Microwaves Didn't Cause 'Havana Syndrome'"](https://www.buzzfeednews.com/article/danvergano/havana-syndrome-jason-crickets) (September 30, 2021). Vergano was the key reporter on multiple JASON-related documents, having previously (January 2021) obtained and published a buried CDC report on the same incidents ([NSArchive, February 2021](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/cuba/2021-02-02/cdc-report-havana-syndrome-medical-mystery-remains-unresolved)).
- **September 2021**: The full declassified JASON report was posted on DocumentCloud and subsequently mirrored by FAS at [irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf).
- **February 10, 2022**: The State Department proactively released unclassified portions of JASON's *second* report on AHIs (see Section 7 below) ([Wikipedia / Havana syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Havana_syndrome)).

The three-year delay in releasing the 2018 report was widely cited as an example of the broader government information management failures around Havana Syndrome. A MuckRock FOIA filing on related FBI documents cited the JASON report's availability as evidence that withholding comparable FBI documents served no national security purpose ([MuckRock FOIA filing](https://www.muckrock.com/foi/united-states-of-america-10/fbi-otdbau-2018-135010/)). The State Department, when approached by BuzzFeed, expressed gratitude to JASON for its contribution but did not explain why the findings had not been published for three years.

## 6. Pushback from Victims, Congress, and Other Scientists

### Victims

The cricket hypothesis was, as the *NDU Press* review diplomatically observed, "not well received by some, particularly when those affected had chronic health outcomes" ([NDU Press / *Joint Force Quarterly*, January 2023](https://ndupress.ndu.edu/Media/News/News-Article-View/Article/3262785/havana-syndrome-directed-attack-or-cricket-noise/)). Victims and their attorneys rejected the psychogenic framing categorically. One affected CIA officer, writing in *War on the Rocks* (March 2026), described a pattern of "gaslighting" by agency leadership — including accusations of fabricating symptoms for financial gain — and argued that the JASON/IC skeptical narrative "caused victims to suffer without care, unable to access government medical facilities" ([War on the Rocks, March 10, 2026](https://warontherocks.com/we-were-right-about-havana-syndrome/)). Attorney Mark Zaid, representing multiple victims, stated that his clients were "very demoralized" by government assessments dismissing foreign involvement ([Politico, March 2023](https://www.politico.com/newsletters/politico-nightly/2023/03/01/the-havana-syndrome-report-hasnt-convinced-everyone-00085112)).

### Congress

Congressional pushback was bipartisan and sustained. In September 2021, Representatives Mike Turner (R-OH), Jason Crow (D-CO), and Brad Wenstrup (R-OH) introduced the *Havana Syndrome Service Member Support Act*, which directed cross-agency response and explicitly characterized AHIs as attacks ([Turner press release, September 2021](https://turner.house.gov/2021/9/reps-turner-crow-wenstrup-act-to-mitigate-havana-syndrome-support-affected-service-members)). In October 2021, the **HAVANA Act** was signed into law by President Biden, authorizing compensation payments of up to $187,300 for State Department and CIA personnel with qualifying brain injuries — implicitly acknowledging an external cause ([NDU Press / JFQ, January 2023](https://ndupress.ndu.edu/Media/News/News-Article-View/Article/3262785/havana-syndrome-directed-attack-or-cricket-noise/)).

In December 2024, the House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence's CIA Subcommittee released an unclassified interim report concluding that the 2023 IC Assessment "lacked analytic integrity and was highly irregular in its formulation," and that "it appears increasingly likely that a foreign adversary is behind some cases" ([HPSCI Interim Report, December 2024](https://intelligence.house.gov/uploadedfiles/unclassified_ahi_report.pdf)). The Subcommittee alleged that the IC had attempted to "shape" the 2023 assessment to produce a "politically palatable conclusion," had impeded congressional investigations, and had issued a subpoena to the NSA in May 2024 to obtain non-cooperating evidence. A criminal referral was sent to the Department of Justice in late 2025 over potential illegal conduct by intelligence officials ([Just the News, September 2025](https://justthenews.com/government/congress/house-intel-inquiry-anomalous-health-incidents-sent-doj-criminal-referral)).

### Other Scientists

The Cuban Academy of Sciences released a 21-author report in September 2021 concluding that U.S. scientific investigations were biased, that evidence had been cherry-picked to support an attack narrative, and that mass psychogenic illness was the most likely explanation ([Wikipedia / Havana syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Havana_syndrome)). This broadly aligned with JASON's psychogenic framing, though from a different political vantage point.

A *Scientific American* analysis (March 2024) sided with JASON, arguing that "after a seven-year investigation, it is time to close the book on 'Havana syndrome'" and that the congressional investigation "rests on politics, not science" ([Scientific American, March 2024](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/a-havana-syndrome-investigation-in-congress-rests-on-politics-not-science/)). Meanwhile, the 2024 NIH-led study — the most comprehensive clinical evaluation yet, using advanced neuroimaging — found no consistent MRI-detectable brain injury signature across AHI patients, undermining the Swanson/Verma brain-damage framing ([Psychology Today, March 2024](https://www.psychologytoday.com/us/blog/its-catching/202403/havana-syndrome-study-finds-no-foul-play)).

## 7. JASON's Follow-up Work Post-2018

JASON conducted at least one, and possibly two, follow-on studies.

### 2022 Report (State Department)

On **February 10, 2022**, the State Department publicly released unclassified portions of a second JASON report, titled *An Analysis of Data and Hypotheses Related to the Embassy Incidents* ([State Department PDF, February 2022](https://www.state.gov/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/JASON-Study-Revised_10-February-2022-Redacted_V1.1.pdf)). Key findings:

- **80–90% of reported incidents** could be readily explained by everyday occurrences or other unrelated medical/environmental factors.
- For the **remaining 10–20%** that could not be easily explained, JASON was unable to perform a statistically meaningful analysis due to the absence of comparable background population data, small sample sizes, and generally poor data quality.
- On directed energy: JASON concluded that ionizing radiation, acoustic energy, and RF energy below 500 MHz or above 30 GHz could be ruled out. The intermediate frequency band (500 MHz–30 GHz) was deemed a "highly unlikely" cause, though it could not be conclusively excluded.
- Overall, the 2022 report reaffirmed the 2018 skepticism, emphasized the data quality problem, and warned against over-confident attributions in either direction ([Wikipedia / Havana syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Havana_syndrome); [North American Community Hub summary, January 2026](https://nchstats.com/what-is-havana-syndrome/)).

Wikipedia's entry on JASON also notes that "JASON again found no compelling evidence that the anomalous health incidents were caused by a deliberate attack using a radio-frequency or any other directed energy weapon" in the 2021/2022 period ([Wikipedia / JASON advisory group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JASON_(advisory_group))). The State Department listed the JASON reports among "several studies by JASON, an independent group of expert scientists" in congressional testimony ([House Homeland Subcommittee hearing transcript, May 2024](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CHRG-118hhrg58217/html/CHRG-118hhrg58217.htm)).

## 8. How JASON's Findings Have Been Treated in the 2023 IC Assessment and Beyond

### The March 2023 ODNI Assessment

On March 1, 2023, the Office of the Director of National Intelligence released the *Intelligence Community Assessment: Updated Assessment of Anomalous Health Incidents* (ICA 2023-02286), jointly prepared by seven U.S. intelligence agencies. The assessment concluded: "it is 'very unlikely' a foreign adversary is responsible for the reported AHIs." Five agencies assessed this at moderate-to-high confidence; two assessed it as "unlikely" with low confidence due to collection gaps ([Wikipedia / Havana syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Havana_syndrome)). The report stated there is "no credible evidence that a foreign adversary has a weapon or collection device that is causing AHIs."

This conclusion was broadly consonant with JASON's skeptical stance — particularly the 2022 finding that 80–90% of cases had mundane explanations — though the IC Assessment was more categorical and drew on intelligence collection that JASON did not have access to. Both JASON and the IC effectively converged on heterogeneity: most cases explainable by ordinary factors, a small subset unexplained but not clearly attributable to a weapon.

### The December 2024 ODNI Update

A further update — *Updated Assessment of Anomalous Health Incidents, as of December 2024* (ICA 2025-00008-B), released January 9, 2025 — reaffirmed the 2023 conclusions: most IC components continued to assess "very unlikely" foreign adversary involvement. However, two components shifted assessments due to new reporting indicating foreign RF research programs had been making progress: one assessed it as "likely" a foreign actor has an RF antipersonnel capability, and another assessed "a roughly even chance" such a capability could have caused some reported experiences ([ODNI December 2024 update PDF](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/assessments/NIC-Unclassified-ICA-Updated-Assessment-AHI-December2024.pdf)). The IC simultaneously noted that recent limited lab studies on RF bioeffects produced "mixed preliminary results" — not the clean negative result of prior years.

This represents a subtle but significant divergence from the JASON posture: JASON's analyses were consistently more skeptical about the RF mechanism and focused heavily on acoustic/bioacoustic explanations for the recorded signals. The IC Expert Panel released in February 2022 — a separate body from JASON — went further than JASON in the other direction, finding that pulsed electromagnetic energy was a "plausible" mechanism for cases with the four "core characteristics" ([ODNI IC Experts Panel Executive Summary, February 2022](https://www.dni.gov/files/ODNI/documents/assessments/2022_02_01_AHI_Executive_Summary_FINAL_Redacted.pdf)).

As of early 2026, the evidentiary landscape remains contested. *60 Minutes* (March 2026) reported that the U.S. government had acquired a portable pulsed RF device through a Russian criminal network and was testing it on animals — a development that, if confirmed, would not by itself prove attribution to historical AHI cases but would partially validate the technical feasibility argument JASON had consistently discounted ([CBS News, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/60-minutes-havana-syndrome-report-finds-u-s-government-tested-energy-weapon/)).

## Summary Assessment

JASON's 2018 and 2022 reports represent the most technically rigorous government-commissioned acoustic and electromagnetic analysis of the Havana Syndrome episode. Their core technical conclusions — that the recorded sounds match the Indies short-tailed cricket with high confidence, that a single RF or acoustic mechanism cannot explain both the recordings and the medical findings, and that psychogenic effects likely contributed — have proved durable within the mainstream scientific community and broadly consistent with the 2023/2024 IC Assessments.

Where JASON has been most contested is not on acoustics but on the question of whether its data-limitation argument forecloses investigation. Critics — including victims, some members of Congress, and the December 2024 House Intel Subcommittee — argue that the very absence of baselines and controls is itself the product of institutional failure, not proof of normal causation. The 2020 NAS report's endorsement of directed RF as the "most plausible mechanism" for a clinically distinct subset remains in tension with JASON's skepticism, though the 2024 NIH study weakened one of the NAS report's empirical pillars (the claim of consistent MRI-detectable brain injury). What JASON never claimed — and what its critics sometimes imply it claimed — is that crickets caused the neurological symptoms. JASON said crickets explained the *sounds*. What caused the injuries, if real injuries occurred, the reports explicitly stated "remains undetermined."
