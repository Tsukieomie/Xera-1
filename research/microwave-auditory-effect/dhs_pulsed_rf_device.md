# DHS Covert Acquisition of a Russian-Origin Pulsed-RF Antipersonnel Device

*Research memo — compiled from open-source reporting, January–April 2026*

---

## 1. Original Reporting: Date of Disclosure and Reporter

The story broke in two waves. The **first disclosure** came on **January 12, 2026**, when independent intelligence journalist **Sasha Ingber** — a former State Department and NPR reporter — published an exclusive on her Substack newsletter *HUMINT*, reporting that the U.S. government possessed and was testing a device associated with Havana Syndrome, citing four anonymous sources ([Ingber, HUMINT Substack, Jan. 12, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/exclusive-us-has-been-testing-a-captured)). That same day, **CNN** published a corroborating story by reporter Kylie Atwood (with four independent sources) describing the Pentagon-funded undercover purchase by the Department of Homeland Security's Homeland Security Investigations (HSI) division ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)). **CBS News** followed hours later with its own parallel reporting, sourced from multiple people briefed on the matter ([CBS News, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/device-havana-syndrome-obtained-by-u-s-government/)). The Hill also reported the story the same day, noting that initial disclosure originated with Ingber and CNN ([The Hill, Jan. 13, 2026](https://thehill.com/policy/defense/5686939-device-russian-components-havana-syndrome/)).

The **second, more detailed wave** came on **March 8–9, 2026**, when CBS's *60 Minutes* broadcast a deep-dive segment by correspondent **Scott Pelley**, produced by **Oriana Zill de Granados and Michael Rey**, laying out technical details of the device and animal test results ([CBS News / 60 Minutes transcript, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/)). The *New York Post* summarized the broadcast on March 9 ([NY Post, March 9, 2026](https://nypost.com/2026/03/09/us-news/us-military-tests-on-secret-weapon-bought-from-russian-criminal-network-reveal-havana-syndrome-like-symptoms-report/)).

**No Politico byline** has been identified in connection with this story based on available reporting. The *Washington Post* contributed the Norwegian self-experiment disclosure (see §8).

## 2. Acquisition Details: Cost, Country of Origin, Date

The device was purchased **clandestinely in late 2024**, in the final weeks of the Biden administration, by HSI — the investigative arm of DHS — using funds provided by the Pentagon ([CBS News, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/device-havana-syndrome-obtained-by-u-s-government/)).

**Cost:** The *60 Minutes* broadcast put the mission cost at **approximately $15 million**, funded by the Pentagon ([60 Minutes, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/)). Earlier CNN and CBS reporting cited only "eight figures" — in excess of $10 million — declining to give a more precise number ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)). A subsequent account by investigative journalist Michael Weiss (The Insider), cited in Ingber's April 2026 follow-up, placed the cost at **approximately $20 million** ([Ingber, HUMINT Substack, April 16, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/the-story-isnt-over-inside-havana)). The discrepancy between $15 million and $20 million has not been publicly reconciled and may reflect different elements counted in the total mission cost.

**Country of origin:** The device is not described as wholly Russian-manufactured. CNN's sourcing was most precise: "Although the device is not entirely Russian in origin, it contains Russian components" ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)). *60 Minutes* reported that "the vital components were made in Russia" and that "the key is not the hardware but the software" — meaning the RF pulse-shaping programming ([60 Minutes, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/)). The House Homeland Security Committee's letter to DHS Secretary Kristi Noem similarly noted it is "described as capable of producing pulsed radio waves and containing Russian components, though it is supposedly not entirely Russian in origin" ([House Homeland Security Committee, Jan. 15, 2026](https://homeland.house.gov/2026/01/15/homeland-republicans-investigate-reports-of-havana-syndrome-device-acquired-by-dhs-under-the-biden-administration/)).

## 4. How DHS Obtained It

According to three independent anonymous sources cited by *60 Minutes*, "U.S. agents who investigate illicit arms dealers heard that a **Russian criminal network was selling a microwave weapon**," and undercover HSI agents then purchased it in 2024 ([60 Minutes, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/)). CNN's sourcing was consistent but noted a gap: "It was also not clear how the US government learned of the existence of the device in order to purchase it" ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)).

The Insider's Michael Weiss, as relayed by Ingber, offered additional texture: the Russian criminal network is "a euphemism, as we're given to understand, for an organization that doesn't necessarily specialize in arms dealing — the implication being that these were operatives of the Russian special services who were doing some kind of side action," i.e., potential GRU or FSB-affiliated individuals engaged in black-market proliferation ([Ingber, April 16, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/the-story-isnt-over-inside-havana)).

Importantly, Ingber's original January 12 report initially described the device as potentially "**seized by U.S. Special Forces during an operation**," though subsequent sourcing — including national security attorney Mark Zaid (on record) — corrected this, saying it was **purchased, not captured** ([Ingber, Jan. 12, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/exclusive-us-has-been-testing-a-captured)). The purchase-vs.-seizure discrepancy in early reporting is a notable sourcing inconsistency.

## 5. Testing: Classified and Unclassified Evaluation

The device has been held at a **secure U.S. military laboratory and tested for more than a year** before the January 2026 disclosure — meaning testing began in late 2023 or early 2024, prior to the purchase date, which suggests either a staged acquisition or overlapping timelines in sourcing ([CBS News, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/device-havana-syndrome-obtained-by-u-s-government/)). The House Homeland Security letter confirmed the device was transferred to the "Department of War" (DoW — DoD) for testing after acquisition ([House Homeland Security Committee, Jan. 15, 2026](https://homeland.house.gov/2026/01/15/homeland-republicans-investigate-reports-of-havana-syndrome-device-acquired-by-dhs-under-the-biden-administration/)).

*60 Minutes* reported, citing three sources, that **animal tests on rats and sheep** produced injuries consistent with those described by Havana Syndrome victims ([60 Minutes, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/)). Additionally, *60 Minutes* reported that **classified security footage** has been gathered showing American individuals being affected by the weapon, including a described incident at a restaurant in Istanbul where FBI agents and their families reportedly reacted simultaneously when a man carrying a backpack entered ([NY Post, March 9, 2026](https://nypost.com/2026/03/09/us-news/us-military-tests-on-secret-weapon-bought-from-russian-criminal-network-reveal-havana-syndrome-like-symptoms-report/)).

The Pentagon's DoD Intelligence & Security unit reportedly holds the device at a **secure military installation** ([Ingber, Jan. 12, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/exclusive-us-has-been-testing-a-captured)). Findings were briefed to House and Senate Intelligence Committees in late 2025 ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)). No unclassified test report has been published.

## 6. FY2025 NDAA AHI Provisions and the Service Member Support Act

The **FY2025 NDAA** (enacted December 2024) included a provision **directing a DoD briefing on anomalous health incidents** and **establishing the Defense Intrepid Network for Traumatic Brain Injury and Brain Health as a program of record** within DoD — both provisions with direct relevance to AHI victims ([FY2025 NDAA Conference Executive Summary, Senate Armed Services Committee](https://www.armed-services.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/fy25_ndaa_executive_summary.pdf)).

The **Havana Syndrome Servicemember Support Act** (H.R. 5132, 117th Congress) is an earlier legislative vehicle, not the FY2025 NDAA itself. It would have established a cross-functional team for emerging health threats, codifying care obligations for servicemembers affected by AHIs ([GovInfo, H.R. 5132](https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/BILLS-117hr5132ih/html/BILLS-117hr5132ih.htm)). The **FY2022 NDAA** was more consequential in actually establishing the DoD Cross-Functional Team (CFT) for AHIs and authorizing $30 million for AHI care capacity ([CRS, FY2022 NDAA AHI Provisions](https://www.everycrsreport.com/files/2022-02-07_IN11850_a8b9f30e315b69684276fce88afd599ec465cd98.pdf)). By February 2026, internal documents obtained by Ingber showed the CFT was being **transitioned to DoD's Research and Engineering directorate** — which some officials feared would reduce its focus on medical care ([Ingber, Feb. 12, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/cross-functional-team-for-havana)).

## 7. Official Confirmations and Denials

**No on-record confirmation from DoD or DHS has been given.** In January 2026, CNN reported that "the Pentagon and DHS did not immediately reply to requests for comment. The CIA declined to comment" ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)).

**ODNI** has issued a statement that is confirmatory in tone without confirming the device specifically: "The team conducting the review of AHI intelligence has been and continues to be relentless in its work... DNI Gabbard has provided the time, resources, and support needed to ensure the review is fulsome and accurate. We remain committed to delivering the truth that the American people deserve" ([ODNI statement, reported in 60 Minutes, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/)). A separate ODNI spokesperson told Ingber that DNI Gabbard believes "the mistreatment and dismissal of Americans impacted by AHIs is unacceptable" and that the team is doing "relentless" work, but added "we are not going to rush to put out incomplete information" ([Ingber, Jan. 12, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/exclusive-us-has-been-testing-a-captured)).

**On-record confirmation by a non-government party:** National security attorney **Mark Zaid**, representing nearly 30 federal AHI victims, confirmed publicly via social media that he had "the same information of USG possessing one or more such devices — and from different sources" ([Ingber, Jan. 12, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/exclusive-us-has-been-testing-a-captured); [The Hill, Jan. 13, 2026](https://thehill.com/policy/defense/5686939-device-russian-components-havana-syndrome/)). Former CIA officer **Marc Polymeropoulos** said on record that if the government has confirmed such devices, "the CIA owes all the victims a fucking major and public apology" ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)).

**Congressional action:** House Homeland Security Chairman **Andrew Garbarino** (R-NY) sent a formal letter to DHS Secretary Noem demanding the procurement process, cost, and test findings ([House Homeland Security Committee, Jan. 15, 2026](https://homeland.house.gov/2026/01/15/homeland-republicans-investigate-reports-of-havana-syndrome-device-acquired-by-dhs-under-the-biden-administration/)).

## 8. The Norwegian Self-Experiment (February 2026)

On **February 14, 2026**, the **Washington Post** reported (with reporter not specified in available summaries) that an unnamed **Norwegian government scientist**, described as initially skeptical of the directed-energy hypothesis, built his own electromagnetic pulse-emitting device in 2024 using classified information and **tested it on himself** to prove it would be harmless ([Washington Post, Feb. 14, 2026](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/2026/02/14/havana-syndrome-cia-norway-experiment/)). The experiment backfired: the researcher developed **neurological symptoms consistent with Havana Syndrome** ([Jerusalem Post, Feb. 16, 2026](https://www.jpost.com/health-and-wellness/article-886767); [Audacy/KRLD, Feb. 17, 2026](https://www.audacy.com/krld/news/world/norwegian-scientist-suffers-brain-injury)).

A U.S. official familiar with the case cautioned that the researcher's symptoms were "not an exact match for a 'classic' case of Havana Syndrome," and officials stopped short of concluding that the self-experiment proved foreign attacks on diplomats ([Audacy, Feb. 17, 2026](https://www.audacy.com/krld/news/world/norwegian-scientist-suffers-brain-injury)). Separately, the Reddit summary of a February 2026 press account noted that "in 2024, Pentagon officials traveled to Norway to inspect his device, followed by visits from intelligence and White House representatives in December of the same year" ([Reddit/worldnews, Feb. 15, 2026](https://www.reddit.com/r/worldnews/comments/1r5emki/cia_pentagon_reviewed_secret_havana_syndrome/)) — suggesting the Norwegian experiment predated and may have informed the U.S. government's acquisition mission.

The Washington Post reported the experiment under an exclusive, citing "individuals familiar with the experiment." No Norwegian government official has been identified by name.

## 9. The IC Assessment Retraction — January–March 2026

The 2023 Intelligence Community Assessment (ICA), led by the CIA and six other agencies, concluded it was **"very unlikely" that a foreign adversary** was responsible for reported Havana Syndrome cases. That conclusion was reaffirmed in January 2025, though two agencies dissented — assessing a "roughly even chance" of foreign involvement ([CBS News, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/device-havana-syndrome-obtained-by-u-s-government/); [CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)).

Following the January 2026 disclosures, House Intelligence Committee Chairman **Rick Crawford** (R-Ark.) publicly called for the IC to **retract the 2023 ICA**, calling it "fatally flawed," charging that it had "inflicted serious damage on some of our nation's bravest individuals," and describing the IC's management of AHIs as "fundamentally flawed" ([NY Post, Jan. 26, 2026](https://nypost.com/2026/01/26/us-news/house-intel-chair-demands-recall-of-flawed-report-saying-us-foes-didnt-cause-havana-syndrome/); [Newsmax, Jan. 26, 2026](https://www.newsmax.com/us/havana-syndrome-house/2026/01/26/id/1243616/)). Crawford's call was prompted in part by the death of a former NSA counterintelligence official, Mike Beck, who had been an AHI victim ([NY Post, Jan. 26, 2026](https://nypost.com/2026/01/26/us-news/house-intel-chair-demands-recall-of-flawed-report-saying-us-foes-didnt-cause-havana-syndrome/)).

By **March 10, 2026**, Crawford escalated further, publicly stating that intel agencies were "**definitely implicated in a cover-up**" concerning Havana Syndrome ([NY Post, March 10, 2026](https://nypost.com/2026/03/10/us-news/house-gop-chair-rick-crawford-says-intel-agencies-definitely-implicated-in-havana-syndrome-cover-up/)). The *Washington Examiner* reported on March 20 that Crawford was "forcing the intelligence community to touch the third rail on Havana Syndrome" ([Washington Examiner, March 20, 2026](https://www.washingtonexaminer.com/opinion/beltway-confidential/4498095/rick-crawford-drags-intelligence-community-toward-havana-syndrome-truth/)). Crawford did acknowledge that not all AHI cases can be attributed to foreign attack — some, he noted, may have been malingered.

As of April 2026, the ODNI review under DNI Gabbard remained **incomplete and unreleased**. There is no confirmed formal retraction or withdrawal of the 2023 ICA. Ingber noted in April that "don't forget the retraction requests" are ongoing and that the situation represented what sources are calling "the CIA's biggest cover-up in modern times" — though this characterization is itself from anonymous insider sources, not on-record ([Ingber, April 16, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/the-story-isnt-over-inside-havana)).

## 10. Skeptical Evaluation: Source Reliability and Evidentiary Weight

**Source basis:** Every major claim in this story rests on anonymous sources. The core CNN January 13 story cited **four unnamed sources briefed on the matter**, with two confirming the eight-figure purchase cost ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)). CBS News January 13 relied on **multiple people briefed on the matter** and **two people familiar with the matter** — distinct from but consistent with CNN's sourcing ([CBS News, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/device-havana-syndrome-obtained-by-u-s-government/)). *60 Minutes* March cited **three independent sources from different agencies** for the device purchase, plus additional named subject-matter experts (Dr. Relman, Polymeropoulos) for contextual and historical claims ([60 Minutes, March 8, 2026](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/)).

**Multi-outlet corroboration** is genuine: CNN, CBS, *60 Minutes*, the *Washington Post* (on Norway), and *Der Spiegel* have all run parallel reporting, which is a meaningful signal. National security attorney Zaid provided **on-record third-party confirmation** of government possession of such a device, from his own independent sources ([Ingber, Jan. 12, 2026](https://sashaingber.substack.com/p/exclusive-us-has-been-testing-a-captured)).

**Caveats and gaps:**

- **No government official has confirmed on the record** that the acquisition occurred, that the testing results are as described, or that the device can reproduce AHI symptoms.
- **The cost figure has not been officially confirmed** and appears in two variants ($15M and $20M) across different accounts.
- The initial Ingber report contained an error (capture vs. purchase) that required correction, illustrating that even well-sourced national security reporting can introduce factual noise.
- **CNN explicitly noted** it was "not clear how the US government learned of the existence of the device" — a critical gap in the acquisition narrative ([CNN, Jan. 13, 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/01/13/politics/havana-syndrome-device-pentagon-hsi)).
- The animal testing results (rats and sheep showing AHI-like injuries) are significant but remain classified. No peer-reviewed publication or public lab report is available.
- **No specific frequency, pulse width, or power level** has been disclosed. The public cannot independently evaluate whether the described waveform is technically consistent with existing directed-energy physics.
- The Istanbul "restaurant footage" claim appears in *60 Minutes* and the *NY Post* summary but has not been confirmed by any other outlet, and the device's presence in that situation is inferred, not established.
- The Norwegian self-experiment, while significant in principle, involves a single anonymous subject, a self-built device using unclear specifications, and a U.S. official's caution that symptoms were "not an exact match" for a classic AHI case.

**Bottom line on sourcing:** The reporting is **solid by mainstream national security journalism standards** — multiple major outlets, independently sourced, over a multi-month period, with one on-record third-party corroboration and congressional action that implicitly treats the claims as credible. It is **not definitive proof** of the full causal chain (Russian state → device → AHI attacks on U.S. personnel). The device's existence and its pulsed-RF characteristics appear well-sourced. Its capability to cause the full range of AHI symptoms, and its operational use against U.S. officials, remain inferential. The intelligence community's posture — with ODNI reviewing and DNI Gabbard expressing public sympathy for victims — suggests internal validation without external confirmation.
