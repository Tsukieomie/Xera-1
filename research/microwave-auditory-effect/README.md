# Microwave Auditory Effect: Sharp & Grove → Havana Syndrome Research Trail

Research compiled May 2, 2026, tracing the citation chain from the 1973 Sharp & Grove demonstration at the Walter Reed Army Institute of Research through Cold War embassy programs and into the modern Havana Syndrome / Anomalous Health Incident discourse.

## Core findings

**On the physics:** The famous "voice transmission via pulsed microwaves" demonstration is held up by a single citation chain — one personal communication (Sharp & Grove to Justesen, September 28, 1973), one synthesis paper (Justesen 1975 in *American Psychologist*), and one peer-reviewed acoustic-physics paper (Sharp/Grove/Gandhi 1974 in IEEE Trans. MTT) **which does not discuss speech transmission at all**. The microwave auditory effect itself is real and thermoelastic; weaponization at injury thresholds remains physically contested.

**On the embassy data:** The 1978 Lilienfeld Johns Hopkins study of Moscow embassy staff was a properly designed cohort with a null result. The Goldsmith reanalyses claiming cancer excess violate the original cohort design (wrong comparator, wrong exposure assumptions). Elwood 2012 is the most rigorous peer-reviewed re-examination and supports Lilienfeld.

**On the institutional posture:** As of May 2026, the March 2023 IC assessment ruling foreign adversary involvement "very unlikely" has been formally challenged. In December 2024 the HPSCI CIA Subcommittee found the assessment "lacked analytic integrity"; a January 2025 ODNI update partially reversed on the capability question; and in March 2026 House Intel, ODNI, and FBI reportedly agreed to seek retraction.

## Key facts established

### On the founding demonstration

1. **The 1974 IEEE paper used inanimate absorbers only.** Eccosorb WG4 polyurethane foam and crumpled aluminum foil. No tissue, no phantoms, no in vivo measurement. 1500 MHz carrier, 14 µs pulses, ~75 kW/m² peak — about 750× the contemporary 10 mW/cm² average safety limit.
2. **The speech demonstration was never published.** It exists only as Note 2 ("Personal communication, September 28, 1973") in Justesen 1975.
3. **Joseph C. Sharp (1934–2021) left the field in 1974.** Twenty years at NASA Ames, finishing as Director of the Office of Space Research. No post-1974 microwave publications. No public commentary on the demo or Havana Syndrome despite living until 2021.
4. **"Mark Grove" is unverified.** Primary sources call him H.M. Grove. He has no archival footprint after 1975.

### On the Cold War context

5. **The Moscow Signal was continuous-wave.** This disqualifies it as a Frey-effect / MAE source. Citing Moscow as physical precedent for Havana misstates the underlying physics.
6. **Project Pandora was ended in 1969** after a RAND review demolished the data. CIA's initial power-density measurements were 10–20× too high, which produced the early false positives.
7. **The 1978 Lilienfeld study was null.** The cancer-excess reanalysis (Goldsmith 1995, 1997) depends on analytical moves that violate the original cohort design — Goldsmith himself called his 1995 paper "not a balanced presentation."

### On Frey

8. **Frey was right about MAE** (1961, 1962 — replicated almost immediately).
9. **Cardiac-arrhythmia claims didn't replicate.**
10. **The blood-brain barrier claim is in genuine scientific limbo** — Salford lab replicated through the 2000s; 2017 *Scientific Reports* on 1,120 rats found no low-SAR effect.
11. **In September 2018, age 83, Frey told the NYT he had been questioned by federal investigators about Cuba** and considered microwave radiation plausible.

### On Lin vs. Foster

12. **Both helped build modern MAE science** — Lin developed thermoelastic theory in the 1970s; Foster co-authored the 1974 *Science* paper that confirmed it.
13. **Lin has the better case** for the acute auditory phase of Havana incidents matching MAE phenomenology.
14. **Foster has the better case** that a covert, injury-causing weapon at the reported geometries is implausible with publicly known technology — antenna size, power requirements, lack of detected exposure, and a more conservative injury threshold.

### On JASON and the IC chain

15. **JASON 2018 found crickets** (*Anurogryllus celerinictus*) for the audio recordings, ruled out RF for what they could analyze, called Frey effect "highly unlikely," and acknowledged psychogenic factors for "important components" of injuries — but reviewed only 8 recordings + 2 videos from 21 cases.
16. **JASON 2022 follow-up:** 80–90% of AHIs explainable by mundane factors; 10–20% un-assessable due to data quality.
17. **NAS 2020 (Relman) found pulsed RF "most plausible"** but never reviewed the JASON report.
18. **March 2023 ODNI ICA:** 5 of 7 agencies "very unlikely" foreign adversary; 1 "unlikely" with low confidence; 1 abstained.
19. **December 2024:** HPSCI CIA Subcommittee found the 2023 assessment "lacked analytic integrity."
20. **January 2025:** ODNI update — one IC component now assesses foreign pulsed-RF antipersonnel capability "likely"; another "roughly even chance." Both still rate *use* as unlikely with low confidence.
21. **Late 2025:** DOJ criminal referral on the 2023 assessment.
22. **Late 2024 / disclosed January 2026:** DHS reportedly spent ~$15M acquiring a Russian-origin pulsed-RF device.
23. **March 2026:** House Intel, ODNI, FBI reportedly agree to seek retraction of the 2023 and 2025 assessments.

### On the 2024 medical evidence

24. **March 18, 2024 NIH JAMA neuroimaging study (Pierpaoli et al.):** No significant structural brain differences between AHI patients (n=81) and matched controls on MRI/DTI. Direct contradiction of the 2018/2019 UPenn (Smith/Verma) findings.
25. **March 18, 2024 NIH JAMA clinical study (Chan et al.):** 41% of AHI patients met criteria for Functional Neurological Disorder; no consistent neurological injury pattern.
26. **The 2024 results do not rule out injury**, but they remove the strongest published structural-imaging evidence cited in 2018–2019 for an external causative agent.

27. **March 31, 2024 (corrected date):** *60 Minutes* / *Der Spiegel* / *The Insider* joint broadcast tying GRU Unit 29155 (the same unit linked to the 2018 Skripal poisoning) to multiple AHI cases via travel-record correlation. Lead reporter: Christo Grozev.
28. **January 12–13, 2026 disclosure:** Sasha Ingber's HUMINT Substack first reported DHS spent ~$15M in late 2024 acquiring a Russian-origin pulsed-RF device; CNN (Atwood), CBS, and *60 Minutes* (Pelley, March 8, 2026) followed. February 2026: Norwegian self-experiment with the device documented in *Washington Post* exclusive.

### On capability vs. mechanism (Ghost Murmur)

36. **April 2026 disclosure:** CIA used "Ghost Murmur" — reportedly NV-diamond quantum magnetometry plus AI noise filtering — to locate a downed F-15 weapons systems officer in southern Iran. Lockheed Martin Skunk Works developer; first known operational use.
37. **Physics dispute:** The reported ~64 km / 40-mile range is ~10¹⁰ below known physical limits for cardiac magnetic detection. Multiple physicists (Wikswo, Roth, Orzel) have argued the disclosure is partially misdirection, with the actual locating method involving the airman's CSEL beacon.
38. **Implication for RNM:** Ghost Murmur detects CARDIAC magnetic fields, which are ~1000× stronger than neural (MEG) signals. If detecting cardiac at distance is at the edge of physics, neural decoding at distance is far past it. The capability validates the *category* of "passive remote bioelectromagnetic detection" that Akwei flagged in 1992, but does not validate his specific RNM / synthetic-telepathy claims.

### On Operation Reduktor

39. **The name's provenance is suspect.** "Operation Reduktor" appears almost exclusively in Western secondary literature (1980s–2000s non-lethal-weapons polemics) and is not robustly attested in Russian-language primary sources or declassified U.S. intelligence assessments.
40. **Documented Soviet research existed** (Kholodov microwave neuroscience; Smirnov psychocorrection; LIDA device) but the gap between "research interest" and "deployable weapon" was as wide for the Soviets as for the U.S.
41. **Cheryl Rofer's critique applies:** Much of the Western Operation Reduktor / psychotronics literature was source-amplified by U.S. defense contractors seeking funding, then recycled into TI / V2K narratives without methodological scrutiny.

## Two strata, both supportable

The body of evidence supports two distinct conclusions that are not in tension:

1. **The physics is essentially settled.** MAE is real, thermoelastic, low-power for perception, very-high-power for plausible injury. Embassy-era data do not show a non-thermal harm signal in well-designed studies.
2. **The institutional posture is in active reversal.** The 2023 IC assessment has lost its standing. Capability ≠ deployment ≠ injury mechanism — and capability is what shifted in 2024–2026.

"Havana Syndrome was largely crickets and stress" and "a foreign RF antipersonnel capability likely exists" are both consistent with the current evidence.

## What this means for the Akwei investigation

The Sharp/Grove demonstration is one of the most-cited primary sources in synthetic-telepathy / V2K discourse. This research establishes:

- The physical phenomenon (microwave auditory effect via thermoelastic expansion) is real and replicated
- The specific speech-transmission claim has a single, thin source chain
- The lead investigator went silent for 47 years
- The peer-reviewed paper does not support what it is widely cited as supporting
- Modern weaponization claims at injury thresholds remain physically contested
- The strongest non-thermal effect claims (Frey BBB, Goldsmith cancer reanalysis) have not held up under proper methodological scrutiny

This intersects with the Akwei complaint's technological claims and the broader V2K evidence matrix in this repo.
