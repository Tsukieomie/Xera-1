## Preamble: How to Read This Document

John St. Clair Akwei filed Civil Action 92-0449 against the National Security Agency on February 20, 1992. U.S. District Judge Stanley Sporkin dismissed it as frivolous under 28 U.S.C. §1915(d) on March 9, 1992 — seventeen days after filing, without NSA response, discovery, or depositions. Akwei subsequently elaborated his technical claims in a 1996 article in *Nexus Magazine* (the "technical dossier"), which has circulated widely in online communities for three decades and is the document most people mean when they cite "the Akwei complaint."

Three things must be true simultaneously for this document to be useful:

1. Some of the technologies Akwei references do exist, at least in laboratory or classified form. Wholesale dismissal is lazy and wrong.
2. The specific complaint — as a working description of a deployed NSA program targeting private U.S. citizens with satellite-based remote neural monitoring and voice transmission in 1992 — is not supported by current evidence.
3. The V2K/MAE question is genuinely hard to settle because the mechanism that would enable covert voice transmission also makes verification by an isolated target nearly impossible. This is a methodological problem, not a proof of or against the claim.

The Xera-1 repository's [V2K Evidence Matrix](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/V2K_EVIDENCE_MATRIX.md) correctly identifies five tiers of evidence: T1 (physical mechanism), T2 (intelligible speech, lab-scale), T3 (patents and named programs), T4 (operational deployment evidence), T5 (symptom reports and media context). The central methodological failure in weak V2K arguments — treating T1 or T3 as proof of T4 — is called "tier collapse." This document tracks every Akwei claim against the appropriate tier.

## Section 2: Claim-by-Claim Mapping Against MAE Evidence

### 2.1 — 400 MHz Carrier Frequency Claim

**Akwei's claim:** 400 MHz is cited as a primary frequency for the NSA's electromagnetic targeting system.

**The MAE evidence:**

The Frey effect — thermoacoustic conversion of pulsed microwaves into perceived sound — is real and replicated. However, its efficiency is strongly frequency-dependent. [Frey (1962)](https://www.physiology.org/doi/10.1152/jappl.1962.17.4.689) established auditory perception thresholds at **1.245 GHz**, with incident power densities below 80 mW/cm² peak and repetition rates of 50 Hz, pulse widths 10–70 µs. [Sharp, Grove, and Gandhi (1974)](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/GENERATION-OF-ACOUSTIC-SIGNALS-BY-PULSED-MICROWAVE-ENERGY.pdf) worked at **1,200, 1,500, and 2,450 MHz** (all well above 400 MHz). Foster & Finch (1974, *Science*) confirmed the thermoelastic mechanism at similar frequencies.

The physical problem with 400 MHz: at this frequency, the free-space wavelength is approximately 75 cm — larger than the diameter of the human head (~18 cm). This mismatch between wavelength and target geometry reduces coupling efficiency substantially. The thermoacoustic conversion efficiency improves at higher frequencies (1–10 GHz) where wavelengths approach or are smaller than head dimensions, allowing more localized absorption. Nothing in the published MAE literature identifies 400 MHz as a preferred or efficient frequency for either auditory induction or tissue coupling; it sits at or below the lower boundary of effective MAE delivery.

**Verdict on frequency claim:** Akwei's specification of 400 MHz contradicts the MAE physics literature. The experimentally validated range for MAE induction runs approximately from 300 MHz to 3 GHz, with published threshold measurements clustered in the 1–2.5 GHz range. Using 400 MHz would require substantially higher peak power densities than the 1–2 GHz range to reach perception threshold.

**The 30–50 Hz claim:** Akwei also cites 30–50 Hz as the range of bioelectrical emissions to be monitored. This is a real EEG frequency range (gamma-band approximates 30–80 Hz; beta-band 13–30 Hz). But ELF electromagnetic emissions at 30–50 Hz from neural tissue are on the order of femtoteslas — irrelevant to the 400 MHz RF carrier claim. These are two different physics regimes being conflated.

### 2.2 — Remote Neural Monitoring

**Akwei's claim:** NSA can remotely intercept and decode individual neural electromagnetic emissions at 30–50 Hz, identifying individuals by their unique bioelectrical resonance frequency.

**The physics:**

The magnetic fields generated by cortical neuron populations (not individual neurons) during normal brain activity range from approximately 10–100 femtoteslas (fT) to, for strong epileptiform discharges, 1 picoTesla (pT). Earth's background magnetic field is approximately 50,000 nT (nanoteslas) = 5 × 10^-5 T. A 100 fT neural signal embedded in a 50 µT geomagnetic field represents a signal-to-noise ratio of approximately 1 part in 5 × 10^8 before any urban electromagnetic interference is added.

Magnetoencephalography (MEG) — the clinical technology for measuring cortical magnetic fields — requires:
- A magnetically shielded room (MSR) reducing the external field by a factor of approximately 10^7
- Arrays of 100–300 SQUID (superconducting quantum interference device) sensors cooled to 4 Kelvin (liquid helium)
- Sensor-to-scalp distance of 1–3 centimeters
- Cooperative, motionless subject

The claim that neural emissions are interceptable from satellite distance — or from any distance beyond the few centimeters required for MEG — has no physical basis. The signal falls as 1/r² with distance, the geomagnetic noise is constant, and no mechanism exists for coherent amplification or extraction of individual neural emissions through intervening tissue, skull, air, and Earth's ionosphere.

**The "bioelectrical resonance frequency" biometric:** Akwei asserts that individual humans have unique bioelectrical resonance frequencies serving as targeting biometrics. The EEG does show individual variation, and resting-state EEG patterns have biometric potential in controlled settings. But this biometric has never been demonstrated to be detectable or usable remotely. It conflates a real laboratory observation (individual EEG variation) with a claimed remote-surveillance capability that requires a physically implausible detection apparatus.

**Relationship to Havana research:** Remote neural monitoring — in the sense of reading thought content from a distance — does not appear in any credible AHI/Havana Syndrome hypothesis. The [JASON advisory panel (2022)](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf), the [NAS (2020) report](https://www.nationalacademies.org/news/new-report-assesses-illnesses-among-us-government-personnel-and-their-families-at-overseas-embassies), and [Foster, Garrett & Ziskin (2021)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/) all concern *incoming* directed energy causing perceptual or physiological effects — not outgoing detection of neural signals.

### 2.3 — Voice-to-Skull / Synthetic Telepathy

**Akwei's claim:** The NSA can transmit intelligible speech and voices covertly into a target's head via pulsed RF, at operational distances.

**What the MAE record establishes:**

**Tier 1 (mechanism — strongly supported):** The Frey effect is real. Pulsed microwave radiation, when absorbed by head tissue, causes thermoacoustic expansion and generates a pressure wave conducted via bone to the cochlea. This is perceived as a click, buzz, or knock. It is documented in [Frey (1961, *Aerospace Medicine*)](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/1120267bcdb970f75a13bbdafa9a68c70683397a), confirmed mechanistically by Foster & Finch (1974), and independently replicated in multiple laboratories. The [Sharp, Grove & Gandhi (1974) IEEE paper](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/GENERATION-OF-ACOUSTIC-SIGNALS-BY-PULSED-MICROWAVE-ENERGY.pdf) measured acoustic signal generation in inanimate absorbers (polyurethane foam, aluminum foil) at 1,200, 1,500, and 2,450 MHz using peak power of 4,500 W/pulse and 14 µs pulse widths. It confirmed pressure wave generation but tested no biological subjects and contained no mention of voice modulation or speech.

**Tier 2 (intelligible speech, lab-scale — historically claimed but not protocol-grade):** The claim that the Frey effect can carry intelligible speech rests on a single secondary source: a personal communication footnote in [Justesen (1975, *American Psychologist* 30(3):391–401)](https://zoryglaser.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/05/MICROWAVES-AND-BEHAVIOR.pdf). Justesen reported that Sharp and Grove (the same researchers as the 1974 IEEE paper) had demonstrated, in an unpublished experiment dated September 28, 1973, that the words for nine of ten single-syllable spoken digits could be heard and identified by Sharp and Grove themselves (experimenter = subject) using zero-crossing modulation of a pulsed microwave source.

This is the single best primary evidence for MAE-based speech, and its limitations must be clearly stated: (a) no peer-reviewed paper by Sharp, Grove, or Gandhi describes this experiment; (b) the subjects were the experimenters, creating a maximally favorable identification context; (c) only single-syllable words were tested — Justesen notes that longer transmission would approach the 10 mW/cm² continuous-wave safety limit; (d) it was conducted in a controlled laboratory, not at covert distance.

**The engineering gap between lab demo and covert deployment (Tier 4):**

[Foster, Garrett & Ziskin (2021)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8733248/) provide the decisive quantitative analysis. Peak acoustic pressure from MAE scales with pulse fluence (energy per pulse per unit area). At auditory perception threshold fluences of approximately 0.02–0.4 J/m² per pulse, induced brain pressures are approximately 0.1–3 Pa. These are perceptible but non-injurious. Achieving pressures Lin claimed (~20 Pa, at which "hair cell injury" would occur) requires pulse fluences that correspond to high-power radar installations — large, visible apertures with peak transmitter powers in the kilowatt-to-megawatt range, immediately detectable by spectrum monitoring and thermometric measurement. Foster et al. conclude: "covert, building-penetrating weaponization is implausible under known physics for injury-level effects."

The [JASON (2022) panel](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf) was more direct: practical difficulties of covert pulsed-RF deployment capable of causing injury, even at building range, are "daunting." JASON assessed the 500 MHz–30 GHz range as "highly unlikely" to cause the persistent neurological effects associated with Havana Syndrome. The panel found no RF exposure confirmed in any AHI case.

The additional challenge for intelligible speech is fidelity at distance. The Sharp/Grove demo required the subjects to identify single-syllable words in a controlled setting; covert delivery through walls, at ranges of tens to hundreds of meters, with no calibrated exposure measurement, would face severe signal degradation and no mechanism for quality verification.

**Relationship to 400 MHz claim:** Akwei's invocation of 400 MHz as the V2K carrier is doubly problematic: it contradicts the MAE threshold literature (Frey: 1.245 GHz; Sharp/Grove: 1.2–2.45 GHz) and is near the lower boundary of frequencies at which thermoacoustic coupling to head geometry is efficient.

### 2.4 — Bioelectric Frequency Table and the Adey Window

**Akwei's claim:** By targeting specific ELF modulation frequencies (9–25 Hz), the NSA can selectively stimulate or decode specific cortical functions.

**The relevant science:**

The Adey Window is real. W. Ross Adey and Suzanne Bawin's work in the 1970s demonstrated calcium efflux from chick brain tissue exposed to ELF-modulated 147 MHz RF at power densities well below thermal thresholds. The specific modulation frequencies that produced maximum effect were approximately 6–20 Hz — overlapping with Akwei's table. This is the best-documented window effect in the bioelectromagnetics literature.

However, the calcium efflux results: (a) were observed in tissue cultures and isolated tissue preparations, not in intact human subjects at behavioral resolution; (b) demonstrated a non-specific ionic perturbation, not selective activation of specific cognitive functions; (c) have not been replicated consistently enough to build a therapeutic or diagnostic application; (d) do not constitute a mechanism for decoding thought content from a distance.

Akwei's frequency table — assigning specific cognitive functions to specific Hz ranges — is a plausible-sounding but unfounded extrapolation. EEG correlates of different brain states are real, but they represent statistical patterns across electrode arrays on the scalp, not discrete functional channels that can be "tuned to" from a distance. The visual cortex does not emit a unique 25 Hz beacon; neither does the auditory cortex "broadcast" at 15 Hz.

### 2.5 — Satellite-Based Delivery

**Akwei's claim:** The NSA targets individuals via satellite-based RF systems, operating at intercontinental distances.

**Engineering constraints:**

The problems with satellite-based MAE delivery compound all the frequency-power constraints above. Free-space path loss at 1 GHz from low Earth orbit (200 km altitude) is approximately 132 dB. To deliver even a fraction of the peak power densities required for MAE perception threshold at Earth's surface from a satellite, the transmitter would require apertures of impractical size and transmitter powers that would be trivially detectable by any spectrum monitoring system. The physics does not prohibit it in the absolute sense; it prohibits it in any sense compatible with "covert" or "undetected" operation.

The [JASON (2022) report](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf) noted that even building-range covert pulsed-RF deployment faces "daunting" practical difficulties. Extending this to satellite range multiplies those difficulties by many orders of magnitude.

No Havana Syndrome hypothesis — including Lin's most aggressive weaponization arguments — proposes satellite delivery. All credible AHI hypotheses concern close-proximity devices.

### 2.6 — DOMINT and Domestic EM Targeting

**Akwei's claim:** The NSA's DOMINT program uses RF-based remote neural monitoring to surveil all U.S. citizens, with targeted individuals subject to more intensive RNM and voice transmission.

**What is and is not documented:**

What is documented: the NSA conducted mass surveillance of U.S. citizens' electronic communications, as confirmed by the Snowden disclosures (2013) and congressional investigations. This surveillance is digital signals intelligence — collection of phone calls, emails, and internet traffic — not electromagnetic targeting of the human nervous system. The legal and political dimensions of DOMINT-style surveillance Akwei describes were validated in a different sense by Snowden, but the specific technology (RNM, V2K) was not.

What is not documented: any NSA program for electromagnetic targeting of the nervous system of U.S. citizens under DOMINT or any other program name. No FOIA-disclosed, leaked, or court-disclosed document references an NSA electromagnetic neural monitoring program. The [NSA Archive's Moscow Signals declassification (2022)](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave) explicitly notes that "information on nonbiological testing that followed the discovery of the Moscow signal is still classified" — which is a genuine gap in the record, but not evidence that the gap contains what Akwei describes.

### 2.7 — Named Historical Programs

**Akwei's implicit argument:** The existence of Pandora, MKULTRA, and Soviet psychotronics programs demonstrates the institutional continuity of a government electromagnetic targeting capability.

**The historical record (assessed in [moscow_signal_pandora.md](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/moscow_signal_pandora.md) and [Xera-1 FINDINGS.md](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/FINDINGS.md)):**

- **Project Pandora (ARPA, 1965–1969):** Real. Established by ARPA official Richard Cesaro, October 1965, in response to the Moscow Signal. WRAIR primary laboratory; Sharp (behavioral psychologist) and Grove (electronic engineer) as principal investigators. Classified primate experiments (Project BIZARRE). Concluded 1969: RAND peer review was "devastating"; no behavioral effect at corrected power levels (50 µW/cm²). Sharp left in 1968; moved to NASA Ames 1974. Pandora ended and was not continued as an offensive targeting program.

- **MKULTRA (CIA, 1950s–1970s):** Real. Documented through the Church Committee (1975), the Rockefeller Commission, and FOIA releases. Included drug-based (LSD, mescaline), hypnosis-based, and sensory manipulation approaches. Some electromagnetic experiments are referenced in partially redacted documents. The program was terminated and the CIA Director destroyed most records in 1973; surviving documentation does not describe anything resembling the RNM/V2K system Akwei describes.

- **Mankind Research Unlimited (MRU, Silver Spring, MD):** Real. CAGE code 59737, operated 1973–1999. Located approximately 3 miles from Akwei's residence. Confirmed IC-connected personnel. The [Xera-1 FINDINGS.md](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/FINDINGS.md) notes MRU's confirmed existence and IC connections without establishing that it conducted the specific operations Akwei attributes to NSA.

- **Soviet psychotronics ("Operation Reduktor"):** Real in a different sense. The 60 Minutes / Insider / Der Spiegel reporting (2024) on GRU Unit 29155 described Soviet-era electromagnetic effects research beginning in 1984 at a Kharkiv institute, with the program involving acoustic and microwave effects on personnel — consistent with Akwei's general framing of Cold War-era militarized EM research but concerning Russian, not NSA, programs.

- **"Sleeping Beauty" (DIA):** Referenced in TI discourse via MRU connections but not independently documented in the declassified record at the same level as Pandora or MKULTRA.

## Section 4: Where the 2024–2026 Developments Strengthen or Weaken Specific Akwei Claims

### 4.1 — The DHS Pulsed-RF Device Acquisition (January–March 2026)

In late 2024, DHS/HSI covertly purchased a backpack-sized pulsed-RF device from a Russian criminal network for approximately $15 million using Pentagon funding. The device contains Russian components, is programmable, is described as silent, has an operational range of approximately several hundred feet, and reportedly penetrates walls. Animal tests on rats and sheep reportedly produced injuries described as consistent with AHI reports. CBS News (January 2026) and 60 Minutes (March 2026) reported this; the [dhs_pulsed_rf_device.md](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/dhs_pulsed_rf_device.md) source file covers this in detail.

**Effect on Akwei claims:**

*Strengthens (weakly):* The device existence confirms that compact, portable, pulsed-RF devices capable of biological effects do exist and have been developed (apparently by Russian actors). This weakly supports Akwei's general claim that such devices exist and can be concealed. It strengthens the Tier 3 claim that governments research and possess this category of technology.

*Does not support:* The device operates at close range (hundreds of feet), not at satellite distances. Nothing about it supports remote neural monitoring, thought decoding, or satellite-based voice transmission. The frequency is undisclosed. Its waveform is described as "rises and falls abruptly and pulses rapidly" — consistent with pulsed-RF but not with the specific claims in Akwei's dossier. The device provides T3/T4 evidence for a *close-range disorienting or injuring device* — a very different claim from Akwei's *satellite-based thought-monitoring system*.

### 4.2 — NIH JAMA 2024 Brain Injury Studies

[Pierpaoli et al. (2024, *JAMA*)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10883276/) examined 81 AHI patients versus 48 matched controls using advanced diffusion tensor imaging and functional MRI. After applying multiple-comparisons correction, **no significant MRI-detectable structural brain injury was found** in the AHI group. [Chan et al. (2024, *JAMA*)](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2815538) found no significant differences in objective auditory, vestibular, or cognitive measures; approximately 28% of AHI patients met criteria for Functional Neurological Disorders (FND/PPPD).

**Effect on Akwei claims:**

*Weakens:* Akwei's implied claim of sustained, structurally detectable brain injury from repeated EM targeting is not supported by the best available neuroimaging data. The NIH studies undermine the model in which sustained RNM/V2K exposure produces measurable brain injury.

*Does not fully exculpate the acute event hypothesis:* Chan et al. explicitly note that the NIH studies do not rule out a transient acute injury at the time of initial exposure that subsequently resolved, leaving no structural trace. This is consistent with how Havana researchers interpret the results.

*Irrelevant to the RNM claim:* The NIH studies assess injury, not monitoring or voice transmission. They do not address whether remote neural monitoring is physically possible — they were never designed to do so.

### 4.3 — HPSCI December 2024 Interim Report

The House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence December 2024 interim report concluded that the 2023 ODNI IC Assessment "lacked analytic integrity and was highly irregular," that the IC had "stonewalled, slow-walked, and cherry-picked information," and that "it appears increasingly likely a foreign adversary is behind some AHIs."

**Effect on Akwei claims:**

*Strengthens (institutionally, not physically):* The HPSCI finding that IC analysts reached a "predetermined conclusion" that AHIs were probably not caused by a foreign adversary — and suppressed contrary evidence — is the most significant institutional development in the AHI discourse. It shifts the picture from "experts concluded this is probably mass psychogenic illness" to "experts were pressured to reach that conclusion." This finding strengthens the general case that government agencies can obscure directed-energy research and its operational use.

*Does not validate Akwei's specific claims:* The HPSCI finding concerns AHI causation in diplomatic and IC personnel at overseas and domestic posts — not a domestic targeting program directed at private U.S. citizens. It does not implicate NSA domestic operations. It does not provide evidence for RNM, satellite delivery, or synthetic telepathy.

### 4.4 — ODNI March 2026 Assessment Retraction

The ODNI's updated assessment (ICA 2025-00008-B, December 2024, publicly released March 2026) walked back the prior "low confidence, probably not foreign adversary" finding and acknowledged "it appears increasingly likely that at least some AHIs were caused by a foreign adversary using a directed energy device."

**Effect on Akwei claims:**

*Strengthens (weakly, narrow):* The retraction confirms the institutional acknowledgment that directed-energy devices capable of causing AHI symptoms probably exist and have been deployed against U.S. personnel. This is T3/T4 confirmation for a close-range directed-energy weapon — the narrowest version of what Akwei describes.

*Does not validate the architecture:* The retraction says nothing about RNM, satellite delivery, domestic mass surveillance via EM means, or voice transmission. It concerns episodic acute incidents against known targeted government personnel — a very different operational profile from Akwei's description of sustained covert domestic targeting of a private citizen.

### 4.5 — GRU Unit 29155 Reporting (2024)

The 60 Minutes / Insider / Der Spiegel investigation (March 2024) identified GRU Unit 29155 as a probable author of some AHI incidents. Deputy commander Terentiev received a military bonus specifically for research into "potential capabilities of non-lethal acoustic weapons in combat activities in urban settings." Albert Averyanov was geolocated near Tbilisi AHI incidents in 2021.

**Effect on Akwei claims:**

*Strengthens (historically):* The GRU Unit 29155 evidence supports Akwei's general framework that Russia/Soviet Union developed and operationally used directed-energy harassment devices. The "Kharkiv institute" origin story described in the 60 Minutes reporting, beginning in 1984, is temporally consistent with Akwei's claim of 1980s-era program development.

*Does not support NSA angle:* The GRU attribution is, if anything, the opposite of Akwei's thesis: it suggests Russia used such devices against U.S. government personnel — not that NSA used them against U.S. private citizens.

## Section 5: Where Akwei Is Partially Correct

### 5.1 — The Cold War Research Lineage Is Real

The most defensible parts of Akwei's complaint are historical:

- Project Pandora (1965–1969) was a real classified ARPA program investigating whether the Moscow Signal could cause behavioral effects. [The ARPA Cesaro Memorandum](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/document/28787-document-3-arpa-richard-s-cesaro-memorandum-justification-memorandum-project-pandora) — "Justification Memorandum for Project Pandora," October 1965 — is declassified and confirms the program's intent was to investigate "whether the Soviets could be transmitting information to or affecting the brain."

- Walter Reed Army Institute of Research (WRAIR), with Joseph Sharp as chief of its Department of Microwave Research, was the primary Pandora laboratory. Sharp is the same person who later conducted the 1973 speech transmission demonstration. The [sharp_grove_careers.md](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/sharp_grove_careers.md) source file confirms Sharp's career trajectory: WRAIR Pandora → 1973 speech demo → NASA Ames 1974.

- MKULTRA included behavioral and psychological modification research. The extent to which electromagnetic approaches were part of classified MKULTRA subprograms remains unclear due to record destruction, but the Church Committee confirmed "radiation" (non-ionizing EM) was among the methods investigated.

- Soviet psychotronics research was real and was known to U.S. intelligence. The Moscow Signal (2.5–4.0 GHz, continuous wave) was real, was received at the U.S. Embassy Moscow from approximately 1953 to 1976, and prompted real defensive and investigative programs. The Soviet-era "Kharkiv institute" programs described in the 2024 GRU reporting confirm parallel development on the Russian side.

- Mankind Research Unlimited (MRU) is documented as a real entity with IC connections in Silver Spring, Maryland. Akwei's awareness of this institution, 3 miles from his residence, is at minimum consistent with genuine observation of a defense-sector entity — not pure fabrication.

### 5.2 — The Physical Mechanism Akwei Invokes Is Real

The microwave auditory effect is a real, replicated, peer-reviewed phenomenon. Akwei's complaint correctly identifies this mechanism as the basis for voice transmission technology. The Frey effect — confirmed 1961–1974 — establishes that pulsed RF can produce perceived sound in humans. This is T1-level evidence. Akwei's claim that this mechanism underlies V2K technology is not wrong in principle; it is wrong in the extrapolation from lab-scale perception to covert operational deployment.

### 5.3 — The Patent Chain Shows Real Technology Development Trajectory

The Malech patent (US3951134, "Apparatus and Method for Remotely Monitoring and Altering Brain Waves," 1974, [Xera-1 V2K Evidence Matrix](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/V2K_EVIDENCE_MATRIX.md) — correctly attributed to Robert Malech / Dorne & Margolin Inc., not to Sharp and Grove) is a real U.S. patent making real claims about electromagnetic brain wave monitoring and alteration. The Stocklin, Brunkan, and Mardirossian patents make claims in the same technological neighborhood. Patents are not proof of working technology, and patent claims are frequently aspirational rather than demonstrated. But the existence of this patent chain does show that the general technological direction Akwei describes attracted real engineering investment in the 1970s–1990s.

### 5.4 — "U.S. Government Researched Weaponization of EM Effects" Is True

The [AFRL Directed Energy Futures 2060 report](https://www.afrl.af.mil/Portals/90/Documents/RD/Directed_Energy_Futures_2060_Final29June21_with_clearance_number.pdf) confirms that directed energy (including high-power microwave) research began in the 1970s and that 31 nations possessed DE weapons systems by 2021. The [NSA Archive (2022)](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/intelligence-russia-programs/2022-09-13/moscow-signals-declassified-microwave) notes that post-Pandora nonbiological testing information remains classified. Akwei's general claim that the U.S. government researched electromagnetic effects on human neurophysiology and never publicly closed this research direction is historically accurate.

## Section 6: Where Akwei Is Clearly Wrong

### 6.1 — The 400 MHz Frequency Claim

As established in Section 2.1: no published MAE research identifies 400 MHz as a preferred, efficient, or experimentally validated frequency for auditory induction. Published threshold measurements are at 1.245 GHz (Frey) and 1.2–2.45 GHz (Sharp/Grove). At 400 MHz, the wavelength-to-head-geometry mismatch reduces coupling efficiency substantially. Akwei's specific frequency claim contradicts the MAE literature rather than drawing from it.

### 6.2 — Remote Neural Monitoring / Thought Decoding at Distance

The physics of this claim fail at the most fundamental level. The magnetic fields produced by neural populations are 10–100 fT — ten orders of magnitude weaker than Earth's geomagnetic background. No physical mechanism permits their detection beyond a few centimeters without superconducting sensor arrays in magnetically shielded rooms. The claim that a satellite or remote transmitter could "decode thoughts" by intercepting these signals has no basis in any tier of evidence — not in classified programs, not in the patent record, not in the basic physics. This is the most clearly wrong claim in the complaint.

### 6.3 — Specific NSA Program Names for Electromagnetic Domestic Targeting

DOMINT as a program for electromagnetic neural monitoring of U.S. citizens is not supported by any FOIA disclosure, congressional testimony, whistleblower account, or documentary evidence. The NSA's confirmed domestic surveillance programs (PRISM, MUSCULAR, XKeyscore) are digital SIGINT programs — collection of electronic communications — not electromagnetic neural monitoring. The institutional capability Akwei describes (NSA as an electromagnetic targeting agency conducting RNM on private citizens) has no documented parallel in any country's known intelligence programs.

### 6.4 — Satellite-Based Delivery at Operational Covert Range

The engineering constraints are definitive. Free-space path loss from LEO to Earth surface at 1 GHz exceeds 130 dB. Delivering MAE-threshold peak power densities from orbit requires transmitter apertures and peak power levels incompatible with concealment. This is not a classified constraint — it is standard radio engineering. The JASON (2022) panel's characterization of *building-range* covert deployment as "daunting" makes satellite-range covert delivery, by implication, impossible under any power level consistent with undetected operation.

### 6.5 — "Decoded Thoughts at Distance" Has No Validated Mechanism at Any Tier

This claim is not merely unsupported; it requires two physically untenable steps — remote detection of neural EM emissions and algorithmic decoding of cognitive content from them — each of which independently fails. No Tier 1, Tier 2, or Tier 3 evidence supports thought decoding from a distance. The claim does not appear in any government program documentation, any academic research program, or any serious engineering proposal in the open literature.

### 6.6 — The Complaint's Operational Timeline

Akwei implies the system was deployed against him continuously from approximately 1988 to 1992 and beyond — four or more years of sustained satellite-based RNM and V2K targeting. Even granting (counterfactually) that such a system existed in principle, the operational logistics of continuously devoting a classified satellite-based directed-energy system to monitoring a single private citizen with no apparent intelligence value are implausible on organizational grounds, independent of the physics.

## Section 7: The V2K Unfalsifiability Problem

### 7.1 — The Core Methodological Problem

The Microwave Auditory Effect creates a genuinely intractable verification problem. The Frey effect is real. The Sharp/Grove speech demonstration, while unpublished as a peer-reviewed paper, is a credible historical claim. MAE-induced auditory percepts are physically indistinguishable from internally generated auditory experiences from the subject's perspective. There is no subjective phenomenological marker that would allow a person experiencing a click, tone, or voice via MAE to distinguish it from a verbal hallucination — both originate as bone-conducted pressure waves or as auditory cortex activity, with no visual, proprioceptive, or contextual cue differentiating external RF induction from internal generation.

This is not an argument for the reality of V2K targeting. It is an argument about the structure of the evidence problem.

### 7.2 — The Absence of Negative Evidence

No study has conclusively demonstrated that a claimed V2K experience was *not* MAE-induced, because doing so would require: (a) continuous RF spectrum monitoring in the target environment with sufficient sensitivity to detect sub-threshold pulsed microwave signals; (b) simultaneous physiological recording capable of detecting bone-conducted pressure waves at perception threshold; (c) controlled comparison of the subject's reported experiences under monitored and unmonitored conditions. No such study has been conducted on any self-reported TI case.

The [JASON (2022) panel](https://irp.fas.org/agency/dod/jason/havana.pdf) noted that no RF exposure has been confirmed in any AHI case — but this absence of confirmation is not the same as confirmation of absence. The practical detection difficulties JASON acknowledges for building-range covert pulsed-RF deployment mean that negative spectrum monitoring results are not conclusive.

### 7.3 — What the Unfalsifiability Means

The V2K problem occupies an evidential position unlike the RNM claim. RNM (thought decoding at distance) is falsifiable in principle — it predicts a physical detection mechanism that does not exist — and it fails that test. V2K (MAE-induced voice perception) is not falsifiable in the same way because the mechanism is real; only the operational deployment claim is unverified, and operational deployment at covert scale is exactly what could not be confirmed even if it existed.

This methodological structure means: the V2K claim cannot be definitively refuted for any individual case, but the population of cases in which MAE-level targeting is feasible and covert is very small — constrained by the engineering requirements (high peak power, large aperture, short range, non-trivial spectral signature). The Akwei scenario — satellite-based, sustained, covert, at operational distance over years — is at the far end of the feasibility spectrum, well outside what the physics permits for undetected operation.

### 7.4 — The Patent-Program-Symptom Tier Collapse

The most common error in both credulous and dismissive treatments of the Akwei complaint is tier collapse: using T1 (mechanism is real) + T3 (patents exist, programs existed) + T5 (symptoms reported) to conclude T4 (operational deployment is established). The [Xera-1 V2K Evidence Matrix](https://github.com/Tsukieomie/Xera-1/blob/main/V2K_EVIDENCE_MATRIX.md) correctly identifies this as the central methodological failure. The MAE mechanism being real does not make the Akwei program description real. The Malech patent being real does not mean the patent was reduced to a deployed NSA system. Reported auditory experiences being real does not establish their cause. Each tier requires its own evidence.

### 7.5 — The Honest Summary of the V2K Problem

The MAE mechanism permits, in principle, a scenario in which a person exposed to a close-range pulsed microwave source perceives voices or sounds without being able to identify the external cause. This scenario is not physically impossible. It is not operationally established. The gap between "not impossible" and "deployed against this specific person" is not bridged by symptom reports, historical program documentation, or patent filings — only by controlled RF exposure measurement in the specific environment. In the absence of that measurement, individual V2K claims remain in the category of: *consistent with known physics at lab scale, not confirmed at operational scale, not refutable by available methods in the field.*

## Section 8: Synthesis — The Akwei Complaint in Historical and Scientific Context

The Akwei complaint and 1996 dossier represent a specific genre: a technically sophisticated attempt to give formal legal and scientific structure to a subjective persecution experience. Akwei correctly identified real science (Frey effect), real programs (Pandora, MKULTRA), real institutions (MRU), and real technology development lines (the patent chain). He embedded them in a framework — satellite-based remote neural monitoring, thought decoding, sustained covert domestic targeting — that the physics does not support and that no documentary record corroborates.

Three decades after the filing, the emerging Havana Syndrome evidence base provides a partial, asymmetric retrospective. The plausibility of a close-range pulsed-RF device causing acute auditory and vestibular disturbance in a targeted individual has been substantially strengthened by:

1. The [NAS (2020) "most plausible mechanism" finding](https://www.nationalacademies.org/news/new-report-assesses-illnesses-among-us-government-personnel-and-their-families-at-overseas-embassies) pointing to pulsed RF as the best candidate for a subset of AHI cases.
2. The [DHS acquisition of a portable pulsed-RF device (January 2026)](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/device-havana-syndrome-obtained-by-u-s-government/) with Russian components, tested against animals.
3. The [HPSCI December 2024 finding](https://www.hpsci.gov/) that prior IC assessments dismissing foreign adversary causation "lacked analytic integrity."
4. The [GRU Unit 29155 / Operation Reduktor](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/60-minutes-havana-syndrome-gru-unit-29155/) reporting linking Soviet-era electromagnetic effects research to a still-active Russian military intelligence unit.

What the Havana evidence does *not* support is the specific architecture Akwei describes: satellite delivery, thought decoding, domestic mass surveillance via EM means, or the sustained multi-year targeting of a private U.S. citizen. The Havana incidents concern episodic acute exposure events against known government personnel — a far cry from the DOMINT program Akwei describes.

The honest accounting is this: Akwei was correct that the U.S. and Soviet governments researched weaponization of microwave effects on human neurology, correct that at least some of this research was classified and ongoing beyond publicly known endpoints, correct that the technology his complaint invokes (MAE-based auditory perception) is physically real. He was wrong — not merely unproven but contradicted by physics — about the most technically specific claims: the 400 MHz frequency, remote neural monitoring at distance, thought decoding, and satellite delivery. And he was wrong in a way that is consistent with a broader pattern: the genuine creepiness and opacity of Cold War electromagnetic research makes it a natural scaffold for constructing explanatory frameworks for subjective experiences of persecution, regardless of the actual physics.

The V2K question remains methodologically open in the narrow sense that close-range MAE-based voice induction cannot be ruled out for any specific case without controlled RF measurements. Everything beyond that narrow open question — the architecture, the distance, the continuity, the thought decoding — falls outside what the evidence supports.
