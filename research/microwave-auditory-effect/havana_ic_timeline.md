# U.S. Intelligence Community Assessments and Major Reports on Havana Syndrome / Anomalous Health Incidents (AHIs): 2016–2026

---

## Overview

"Havana Syndrome" — formally designated **Anomalous Health Incidents (AHIs)** by the U.S. government — refers to a cluster of neurological symptoms (sudden head pressure, vertigo, ear pain, tinnitus, cognitive impairment, and visual disturbances) first reported in late 2016 by U.S. and Canadian diplomatic and intelligence personnel stationed in Havana, Cuba. As of 2026, more than 1,500 cases have been reported across 96 countries. The phenomenon has generated the largest and most resource-intensive investigation in CIA history, produced six major scientific or intelligence assessments, triggered landmark legislation, and remains the subject of live inter-agency and congressional dispute.

### Late December 2016 — First Reported Case, Havana, Cuba

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Document/Event** | Initial report to U.S. Embassy medical unit, Havana |
| **Issuing Body** | CIA / State Department (internal) |
| **Key Findings** | A CIA officer under diplomatic cover reported strange high-pitched sound, head pressure, and dizziness at his Havana residence. | **Availability** | Classified at time; extensively reconstructed in later reporting |
| **Citations** | [ProPublica reconstruction (2018)](https://www.propublica.org/article/the-strange-case-of-american-diplomats-in-cuba-as-the-mystery-deepens-so-do-divisions-in-washington); [Heritage Foundation timeline](https://www.heritage.org/technology/report/anomalous-health-incidents-the-havana-syndrome-update) |

Through early 2017, additional CIA and State Department personnel — eventually totaling approximately 26 Americans and 14 Canadian diplomats — reported similar incidents at private residences and two hotels (Hotel Nacional, Hotel Capri). The State Department initially disclosed incidents to embassy staff in late March 2017, prompting widespread medical screenings. Early diagnosis by Dr. Michael Hoffer (University of Miami) identified inner-ear damage consistent with external force exposure. Personnel were described as having experienced "a concussion without an impact."

### March 20, 2018 — JAMA Swanson/UPenn Study

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Document/Event** | *Neurological Manifestations Among US Government Personnel Reporting Directional Audible and Sensory Phenomena in Havana, Cuba* |
| **Issuing Body** | University of Pennsylvania Center for Brain Injury and Repair (lead authors: Randel Swanson DO, PhD; Douglas H. Smith MD) |
| **Key Findings** | Case series of 21 diplomats evaluated a median of 203 days post-exposure. Found persistent cognitive (76%), vestibular (81%), oculomotor (71%), auditory (68%), and sleep (86%) dysfunction — a "constellation" consistent with acquired brain injury, in the complete absence of blunt head trauma. Authors concluded patients appeared to have "sustained injury to widespread brain networks" from a "directional exposure of undetermined etiology." Mass psychogenic illness was discounted based on isolation of cases and objectivity of vestibular/oculomotor findings. MRI scans were largely normal, but diffusion tensor imaging (DTI) was planned. |
| **Availability** | Fully public (peer-reviewed) |
| **Citations** | [JAMA Network full text](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2673168); [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29450484/) |

> **Note:** A follow-up JAMA study by Ragini Verma et al. (July 2019) examined 40 personnel via advanced MRI, finding statistically significant differences in white matter volume, cerebellar microstructure, and functional connectivity in auditory and visuospatial networks compared to 48 healthy controls — a pattern unlike any previously described pathology. ([PubMed 2019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31334794/))

### November 2018 (Declassified 2021) — JASON Panel Report

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Document/Event** | JASON Advisory Group Report on Havana AHI Sounds |
| **Issuing Body** | JASON (independent science advisory group to U.S. government; contracted through MITRE Corp.) |
| **Key Findings** | JASON analyzed audio recordings from 8 of the original 21 AHI cases and two cellphone videos. ### December 5, 2020 — National Academies of Sciences (NAS/NASEM) Report

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Document/Event** | *An Assessment of Illness in U.S. Government Employees and Their Families at Overseas Embassies* |
| **Issuing Body** | National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine (NASEM); Committee chaired by Dr. David Relman (Stanford University); 19-member expert panel including neurologists, radiologists, electrical engineers |
| **Key Findings** | Commissioned by the State Department. Reviewed cases from Cuba, China, and other locations. Considered four causal mechanisms: (1) directed RF energy, (2) chemical exposures, (3) infectious diseases, and (4) psychological/social factors. **Concluded that directed, pulsed radiofrequency (RF) energy is "the most plausible mechanism" for the cases, especially those with distinct early-onset symptoms** (sudden loud sound, intense head pressure, directional localization). Cited significant historical Soviet/Russian research on pulsed RF effects on humans. Did not address perpetrator attribution. Recommended standardized protocols and further research. Notably, the committee's only mass psychogenic illness (MPI) specialist was removed from the panel after publicly suggesting psychogenic factors could be involved. |
| **Availability** | Fully public |
| **Citations** | [National Academies press release](https://www.nationalacademies.org/news/new-report-assesses-illnesses-among-us-government-personnel-and-their-families-at-overseas-embassies); [NYT (Dec. 2020)](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/12/05/business/economy/havana-syndrome-microwave-attack.html); [NPR (Dec. 2020)](https://www.npr.org/2020/12/06/943531538/microwave-radiation-most-plausible-cause-of-diplomats-ailments-report-says) |

### February 2022 — IC Expert Panel Report (ODNI/DNI Haines)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Document/Event** | IC Expert Panel on AHI Causal Mechanisms (Declassified Summary) |
| **Issuing Body** | ODNI-convened panel; composed of experts from inside and outside government; led by Dr. David Relman; reviewed 1,000+ classified documents |
| **Key Findings** | Panel focused on a core subset of cases displaying four clinical characteristics (directional sound, head pressure, vertigo, other neurological sequelae). ### March 1, 2023 — Full IC Assessment (ODNI)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Document/Event** | *Updated Assessment of Anomalous Health Incidents* (ICA 2023-02286-B) |
| **Issuing Body** | Office of the Director of National Intelligence (ODNI) / National Intelligence Council; seven IC agencies participated |
| **Key Findings** | Most comprehensive multi-agency IC assessment to date. Reviewed ~1,500 reported incidents since 2016 across 96 countries. Three lines of inquiry: (1) foreign actor involvement, (2) feasibility of a deliberate causal mechanism, (3) medical analysis. **Conclusions:** — **Five of seven agencies** assessed it is "very unlikely" a foreign adversary is responsible; two of those five held moderate-to-high confidence, three held moderate confidence. — **One agency** assessed it is only "unlikely" (not "very unlikely") with low confidence, citing collection gaps. — **One agency abstained.** — No agency found a foreign adversary responsible. IC found "no credible evidence" of a foreign weapon causing AHIs. Most symptoms "probably resulted from" pre-existing conditions, conventional illnesses, and environmental factors. Intelligence showed foreign adversaries considered the AHI theory to be "a U.S. plot." Investigators reviewed petabytes of data, used Dark Web searches, CCTV footage, 3D modeling, and conducted real-time scene investigations. |
| **Availability** | Partially declassified; unclassified summary released publicly |
| **Citations** | [ODNI unclassified summary (CBS News reporting)](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/havana-syndrome-foreign-adversary-very-unlikely-american-intelligence-review/); [NYT (Mar. 2023)](https://www.nytimes.com/2023/03/01/us/politics/havana-syndrome-intelligence-report.html); [Reuters (Mar. 2023)](https://www.reuters.com/world/us/havana-syndrome-not-caused-by-foreign-adversary-us-intel-finds-2023-03-01/) |

> **Dissent and pushback:** Mark Zaid stated the assessment "lacks transparency" and had done "damage" to victims' morale. Marc Polymeropoulos described it as ongoing victim shaming. House Intelligence Committee leaders Turner (R) and Himes (D) called on the IC to remain open to modifying conclusions based on new evidence. DNI Haines and CIA Director Burns issued public statements affirming victims' real suffering while standing behind analytic conclusions.

### March 2024 — NIH Longitudinal Study (JAMA)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Document/Event** | Two peer-reviewed NIH studies on AHI patients (JAMA, March 2024) |
| **Issuing Body** | NIH Clinical Center; lead authors Dr. Leighton Chan and Dr. Carlo Pierpaoli |
| **Key Findings** | Nearly 5-year prospective study. Advanced MRI of 81 AHI participants vs. 48 matched controls. **Found no significant evidence of MRI-detectable brain injury and no significant differences in most clinical or biomarker measures.** About 41% of AHI participants met criteria for functional neurological disorders (FNDs). Self-reported fatigue, PTSD, and depression were significantly elevated. The studies contradicted the earlier (smaller) UPenn/Swanson findings of white matter changes. Researchers noted absence of long-term imaging differences was "reassuring" but did not rule out transient injury at time of AHI. The NIH studies did not examine cause attribution. |
| **Availability** | Fully public (peer-reviewed) |
| **Citations** | [NIH press release (Mar. 2024)](https://www.nih.gov/news-events/news-releases/nih-studies-find-severe-symptoms-havana-syndrome-no-evidence-mri-detectable-brain-injury-or-biological-abnormalities); [NPR (Mar. 2024)](https://www.npr.org/sections/health-shots/2024/03/18/1239087164/nih-studies-no-pattern-harm-havana-syndrome-patients-brains) |

> **Controversy:** Victims and their advocates, including Polymeropoulos and Zaid, alleged that CIA officers were verbally directed to participate in NIH studies as a condition for receiving treatment at Walter Reed — a potential ethical violation investigated by the NIH Institutional Review Board. The NIH confirmed the IRB was reviewing the allegations.

## THEMATIC THREAD: Victim Advocacy and the Policy Response

### Marc Polymeropoulos

A 26-year CIA career officer, Polymeropoulos became one of the most publicly prominent AHI victims after suffering a debilitating AHI in Moscow in December 2017 — awakening in his hotel room to severe vertigo, head pain, and what he described as "jackhammers in my ears." He retired from the CIA in the summer of 2019 at age 50 due to ongoing symptoms (chronic migraines). He subsequently became a leading public advocate for AHI victims, writing the book *Clarity in Crisis: Leadership Lessons from the CIA* (2021), testifying to Congress, and providing sustained pressure on the CIA and broader IC to take the matter seriously. He criticized both the 2022 interim CIA assessment and the 2023 IC assessment as insulting and counterproductive, and alleged that CIA officers were coerced into NIH studies as a condition of care. He welcomed the 60 Minutes GRU Unit 29155 reporting as vindicating victims. ([CNN (May 2024)](https://www.cnn.com/2024/05/01/politics/havana-syndrome-victims-cia-russia); [El País (Oct. 2021)](https://english.elpais.com/usa/2021-10-25/havana-syndrome-the-mysterious-illness-affecting-us-spies-and-diplomats.html))

### Mark Zaid

A national security attorney with security clearances, Zaid has represented more than two dozen AHI victims across CIA, NSA, State, DoD, and Commerce. He filed a classified whistleblower complaint against the CIA's interim 2022 assessment, calling it neither coordinated with the interagency nor reflective of the full CIA's view. He has consistently pressed for transparency and for the government to acknowledge that evidence of foreign responsibility exists but is being suppressed. In 2024, he raised ethical concerns about the NIH study protocols. He testified before the House Homeland Security Committee's "Silent Weapons" hearing (May 8, 2024). ([CNN (2024)](https://www.cnn.com/2024/05/01/politics/havana-syndrome-victims-cia-russia); [NPR (Oct. 2021)](https://www.npr.org/2021/10/15/1046519741/new-cases-of-havana-syndrome-grow-as-cause-remains-a-mystery); [Homeland Security hearing](https://homeland.house.gov/hearing/silent-weapons-examining-foreign-anomalous-health-incidents-targeting-americans-in-the-homeland/))

### Greg Edgreen

Retired DIA Lieutenant Colonel who ran the Pentagon's internal AHI investigation 2020–2023. He provided access to cross-agency classified intelligence and was one of the first officials to note that AHI victims disproportionately represented the "top 5–10% performing officers" at DIA, consistently with a "Russia nexus" in their work assignments. He went public on 60 Minutes in 2024, offered his "eat my tie" pledge if Russia attribution was wrong, and founded his own company (Advanced Echelon LLC) to pursue government contracts for AHI victim care. ([The Insider (Mar. 2024)](https://theins.press/en/politics/270425); [CNN (May 2024)](https://www.cnn.com/2024/05/01/politics/havana-syndrome-victims-cia-russia))

### Dr. David Relman

Stanford University professor of medicine who chaired both the 2020 NAS panel and the 2022 IC Expert Panel — the two most authoritative scientific bodies to find pulsed RF/microwave energy the most plausible cause of core AHI cases. Both of his panels' findings were effectively contradicted by subsequent IC analytic conclusions on foreign adversary involvement. Relman continued to publicly maintain his panel's findings and provided the scientific framework that the 60 Minutes investigation drew upon in 2024 and 2026. ([NAS 2020 report](https://www.nationalacademies.org/news/new-report-assesses-illnesses-among-us-government-personnel-and-their-families-at-overseas-embassies); [60 Minutes transcript (Mar. 2026)](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/us-military-tested-device-that-may-be-tied-to-havana-syndrome-60-minutes-transcript/))

## NOTES ON ANALYTICAL DISPUTES

The AHI/Havana Syndrome record illustrates a fundamental conflict within U.S. government epistemology:

1. **Scientific panels (NAS 2020, IC Expert Panel 2022)** focused on causal mechanism (what physical agent could produce the symptoms) and concluded pulsed RF energy was plausible for a core subset of cases.

2. **Intelligence assessments (CIA 2022, ODNI 2023, ODNI 2025)** focused on adversary attribution (did a foreign actor do this?) and found no intelligence linking any specific incident to a foreign actor — a different question with a different evidentiary standard.

3. **Congressional oversight (HPSCI 2024)** found the IC assessment process itself to be analytically compromised, and suggested the two prior IC conclusions may have been shaped by political considerations.

4. **Open-source investigative journalism (Insider/60 Min/Spiegel 2024)** provided the first documented evidence placing a known foreign intelligence unit (GRU Unit 29155) at the scene of specific AHIs, though this evidence does not meet the IC's classified intelligence standard.

5. **Late 2024–2026 physical evidence** (DHS acquisition of portable pulsed-RF device; Norwegian self-experiment) shifted the debate from "could such a weapon exist" toward "does such a weapon exist and has it been used" — but neither piece of evidence directly links any specific AHI incident to a foreign actor.

As of May 2026, no U.S. government assessment has publicly attributed any specific AHI to a specific foreign actor with high confidence.
