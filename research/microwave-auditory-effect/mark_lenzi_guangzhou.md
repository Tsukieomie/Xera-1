## 1. Background and Career

### Education and Pre-State Department Work

Mark A. Lenzi graduated from the [University of New Hampshire](https://www.unh.edu/unhtoday/2019/11/home-heal) in 1997 with a Bachelor of Science in Civil Engineering and minors in Political Science, Hydrology, and Water Resource Management. At UNH he was a standout sprinter; he dropped Division I track on the advice of his coach Jim Boulanger in order to concentrate on academics, but would later return to UNH's facilities during his brain-injury rehabilitation. He was born at Wentworth-Douglass Hospital in Dover, New Hampshire, and his family owns property on Swain's Lake in Barrington, a part of his identity he has frequently cited during his public advocacy.

After graduating, Lenzi served two years as a [Peace Corps](https://www.unh.edu/unhtoday/2019/11/home-heal) volunteer in Poland as an environmental engineer. He subsequently won a U.S. Department of State–administered Fulbright scholarship to the Republic of Lithuania, where he developed fluency in Russian and deepened his knowledge of the former Soviet space. He is fluent in Polish, Russian, and Ukrainian, and by his own account has worked in all fifteen former Soviet republics and "been in a couple revolutions that were anathema to the Kremlin," a reference to his later work with the [International Republican Institute](https://www.unh.edu/unhtoday/2019/11/home-heal) during the democratic transitions in Georgia and Ukraine.

Lenzi served as deputy spokesperson for Senator John McCain's presidential campaign in 2007–2008. He then worked as a spokesperson for the [New Hampshire Republican Party](https://thehill.com/opinion/national-security/5521856-why-im-testifying-today-on-havana-syndrome/). His admiration for McCain has shaped his self-presentation throughout the AHI controversy; he often invokes McCain's maxim that "the best thing in life that you can do is serve a cause greater than yourself."

### Entry into the Foreign Service and DSS Career

In 2011, Lenzi joined the State Department's [Bureau of Diplomatic Security (DS)](https://www.courthousenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/lenzi-state-complaint.pdf) as a Security Engineering Officer (SEO), starting at grade level FP-06. His earliest performance reviews praised him for performing "at a high level," "set[ting] a high standard for managerial performance," and possessing language and analytical skills "of significant value to the U.S. Government." He was recommended for early tenure and promotion at the first available opportunity.

His career path through the Diplomatic Security Technical Surveillance and Countermeasures branches included: an initial posting at the Emanations Countermeasures Branch in Rosslyn, Virginia (2011–2013), with temporary duty (TDY) assignments in Afghanistan (Embassy Kabul and consulates in Herat and Mazar-i-Sharif); promotion to FP-05 in approximately August 2012; promotion to FP-04 and assignment to the Technical Surveillance and Countermeasures Branch (2013–2014), with DS missions to Russia and China; and a stretch assignment at FP-02 level at the [U.S. Consulate Frankfurt, Germany](https://theins.press/en/politics/270425) (approximately 2014–2016), with TDY assignments to Moldova, Ukraine, and Africa, and special countermeasures support at The Hague.

According to *The Insider*'s March 2024 investigation, Lenzi was working at the U.S. Consulate in Frankfurt in [November 2014](https://theins.press/en/politics/270425) when colleagues (including a colleague identified as "Taylor") collapsed after experiencing intense pressure from torso to head and a high-pitched squeal — symptoms later diagnosed as traumatic brain injury. Lenzi has described the [Frankfurt events as the earliest known AHI case](https://theins.press/en/politics/270425), saying, "The 2014 Frankfurt attacks were always key in that they came before the Cuba and China hits and should have received the most attention from the U.S. Government."

He was promoted to FP-03 in 2016 and assigned to the [U.S. Consulate Guangzhou, China](https://www.courthousenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/lenzi-state-complaint.pdf), beginning in August 2016. At Guangzhou he led the year-long, multimillion-dollar Technical Security Upgrade for U.S. Consulate General Hong Kong, modernizing every security system across that post. His April 2017 performance review — written while AHI symptoms had not yet fully presented — described his performance as "excellent in all areas" and noted he was "well on his way to performing at an FP-02 level and should be promoted at the next opportunity."

### Security Clearance

According to the [legal complaint he filed in December 2021](https://www.courthousenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/lenzi-state-complaint.pdf), Lenzi holds a **Top Secret/Sensitive Compartmented Information (TS/SCI)** security clearance. His work as an SEO used top-secret equipment to analyze electronic threats to diplomatic missions — a fact he has cited as a likely reason he was targeted. As of October 2021, the [State Department had revoked his access](https://www.politico.com/news/2021/10/25/state-department-2018-directed-energy-exposure-517055) to his classified computer system as an alleged retaliatory measure, even though his clearance itself remained valid.

### Family

Lenzi is married to Kristina Lenzi. They have two children. The family's ages at the time of the 2017–2018 Guangzhou incident place the children at approximately 3 and 7 years old. His lawsuit, his public testimony, and numerous media interviews confirm that all four family members were ultimately medevaced from Guangzhou, having all failed brain injury tests, and that all four were diagnosed with traumatic brain injuries. Lenzi has spoken at length about parental guilt over not evacuating his family sooner, saying: ["When I think about both of my kids having bloody noses a lot there at night, which ceased as soon as we evacuated our apartment, I still wrestle with the fact that I should have gotten us all out of that apartment earlier than I did."](https://www.unh.edu/unhtoday/2019/11/home-heal)

## 2. The Guangzhou Incident

### Assignment and Living Situation

Lenzi arrived at the U.S. Consulate Guangzhou in August 2016. He and his family were housed in the **Canton Place** apartment complex, a cluster of high-rise residential towers in Guangzhou containing restaurants and galleries around a central plaza. Based on Lenzi's public accounts and the lawsuit complaint, the family lived in **Tower 7** of the Canton Place complex. His closest American neighbor — identified in contemporaneous reporting as Catherine Werner, a U.S. Commerce Department commercial trade officer — lived [one floor above him in the same complex](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/brain-trauma-suffered-by-u-s-diplomats-abroad-could-be-work-of-hostile-foreign-government-60-minutes/).

### Onset of Symptoms: October–November 2017

Lenzi has consistently dated the beginning of his symptoms to **October 2017** in some accounts and **November 2017** in others. In his [GBH/WGBH interview in May 2019](https://www.wgbh.org/news/national/2019-05-14/mark-lenzi-on-the-still-unsolved-silent-attack-on-dozens-of-foreign-diplomats), he stated: "For me it was October of 2017 when I first remember having headaches." In the 60 Minutes March 2019 broadcast, he said, "For me it was November of 2017, when I started to feel lightheaded a lot." The [legal complaint](https://www.courthousenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/lenzi-state-complaint.pdf) states "in or about November 2017" as the onset. CBS News's original May 2018 reporting noted the official State Department formulation that the first evacuated employee had experienced symptoms "from late 2017 through April 2018."

Initial symptoms Lenzi and his wife attributed to Guangzhou's [notorious smog](https://www.vice.com/en/article/havana-syndrome-vice-podcast/): headaches, lightheadedness, nausea, sleeplessness. But the symptom that alarmed him most was **short-term memory loss**: he was forgetting the names of tools he used daily, to the degree that colleagues noticed and commented. He told VICE World News: ["As 2017 turned into 2018, my most distressing symptom… was short-term memory loss."](https://www.vice.com/en/article/havana-syndrome-vice-podcast/) He has explicitly stated that the memory loss could not be ascribed to air pollution, and it began to make him fear early-onset dementia — his grandfather had been diagnosed with Alzheimer's at approximately 45, the age Lenzi was approaching.

Both children experienced **frequent, severe nosebleeds** that soaked their sheets. The family initially attributed these to the smog as well. Both children's nosebleeds ceased immediately upon evacuation, a fact Lenzi has repeatedly emphasized to counter mass-psychogenic explanations: ["How do they explain that a three-year-old kid was part of this? They can't."](https://www.wgbh.org/news/national/2019-05-14/mark-lenzi-on-the-still-unsolved-silent-attack-on-dozens-of-foreign-diplomats)

### The Sounds: "Marbles Rolling into a Funnel"

Lenzi reported hearing a **bizarre, unmistakable sound** on approximately three or four occasions, always in the same spot — directly over his son's crib — and always at the same time: right before the family went to bed at night. He gave the most detailed description of this sound in the [March 2019 60 Minutes](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/brain-trauma-suffered-by-u-s-diplomats-abroad-could-be-work-of-hostile-foreign-government-60-minutes/) broadcast with Scott Pelley:

> "Picture holding a marble. Then, picture if you had like a six-foot-in-diameter funnel, metal funnel. The sound that marble would make as it goes around and it progressively gets faster as it gets, goes down towards the hole at the end. It's a sound like I've never heard before."

He described the sound as **"fairly loud"** and said it was "not subtle." In a separate interview, he described it as ["marbles bouncing and hitting a floor, then rolling on an incline with a static sound."](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/a-mystery-illness-is-still-affecting-diplomats-in-cuba-and-china) In the [2024 60 Minutes segment](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/5-year-havana-syndrome-investigation-finds-new-evidence-of-who-might-be-responsible-60-minutes/), he summarized it as "a marble circling down a metal funnel." 60 Minutes producer Michael Rey later said: ["He told us a lot of things when we first met him that we kind of couldn't believe. And now, years later, we believe everything he told us."](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/5-year-havana-syndrome-investigation-finds-new-evidence-of-who-might-be-responsible-60-minutes/)

### The First Evacuee Neighbor and the May 2018 Discovery

In the lawsuit complaint and multiple media interviews, Lenzi describes learning — only in [May 2018, roughly a month after the fact](https://www.wgbh.org/news/national/2019-05-14/mark-lenzi-on-the-still-unsolved-silent-attack-on-dozens-of-foreign-diplomats) — that his immediate neighbor had been quietly medevacked on **April 11, 2018**. The State Department had kept this secret from other consulate personnel.

Lenzi tracked down the neighbor's contact information and called her in the United States. ["I'm back [in the US] diagnosed with brain injury,"](https://www.vice.com/en/article/havana-syndrome-vice-podcast/) he recalled her saying. "The same thing that was going on in Cuba." She told him she had pleaded with the Guangzhou consulate leadership on **three separate occasions** to immediately evacuate all American personnel from the apartment building, and that the State Department had ignored her requests.

Lenzi confronted consulate leadership about why his family had continued to be housed in the same building where his neighbor had fallen ill. He was stonewalled. On **May 27, 2018**, he moved his family out of the apartment and sent an [unclassified email to nearly 200 colleagues](https://www.vice.com/en/article/havana-syndrome-vice-podcast/), as well as State Department leadership in Beijing and Washington, warning them of the health and safety risk and accusing U.S. officials of "an attempted cover-up." This email triggered a State Department-ordered psychiatric evaluation, a counseling session, and what Lenzi describes as the beginning of a sustained retaliatory campaign.

### The April 2018 Health Alert: What Triggered It

On **May 23, 2018**, the State Department issued a health alert for U.S. citizens in China following the [first publicly disclosed case at Guangzhou Consulate](https://www.voanews.com/a/us-issues-alert-in-china-abnormal-sensations-of-sound-/4406189.html) — the case of Catherine Werner. That same day, Secretary of State Mike Pompeo told the House Foreign Affairs Committee: ["We had an incident in Guangzhou that the medical indications are very similar — and entirely consistent with — the medical indications that have taken place to Americans working in Cuba."](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/china-us-government-employee-abnormal-sensations-of-sound-state-department-2018-5-23/) The alert described the affected employee as having experienced "subtle and vague, but abnormal, sensations of sound and pressure."

Werner had been physically evacuated in **April 2018**; the public announcement came in late May only after her diagnosis — mild traumatic brain injury (MTBI) — was confirmed. By the time of the alert, Werner had already been at the University of Pennsylvania for assessment. [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/china-us-government-employee-abnormal-sensations-of-sound-state-department-2018-5-23/) confirmed the embassy learned of her MTBI diagnosis on May 18, 2018. The alert did not mention other affected personnel, as the State Department was presenting the case as isolated.

## 3. The Other Guangzhou Cases

### Catherine Werner (U.S. Commerce Department)

Catherine Werner, age 31 at the time, was a commercial trade officer with the [U.S. Commercial Service](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/brain-trauma-suffered-by-u-s-diplomats-abroad-could-be-work-of-hostile-foreign-government-60-minutes/) (USCS, housed within the Commerce Department's International Trade Administration) promoting American business from the Guangzhou Consulate. She lived one floor above Mark Lenzi in the Canton Place complex.

Werner's symptoms began in October 2017 and included hives, daily headaches, severe fatigue, oscillating pressure in her temples, a low humming sound, tinnitus, nosebleeds, and eventually vomiting and difficulty recalling words. Her visiting mother — an Air Force veteran who stayed nearly three months — developed the same symptoms. In the [2019 60 Minutes segment](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/brain-trauma-suffered-by-u-s-diplomats-abroad-could-be-work-of-hostile-foreign-government-60-minutes/), Werner described her acute onset: "I woke up in the middle of the night. I could feel this sound in my head. Um, it was intense pressure on both of my temples. At the same time, I heard this low humming sound, and it was oscillating. And I remember looking around for where this sound was coming from, because it was painful."

Werner's dogs were also throwing up blood, a detail she shared with Scott Pelley on 60 Minutes. She was medevacked in **April 2018** and was subsequently diagnosed with an "organic brain injury" by University of Pennsylvania doctors. Secretary Pompeo publicly confirmed her case on May 23, 2018. The State Department [labeled her the only China officer](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/19/us/politics/diplomat-attacks-havana-syndrome.html) with the "full constellation" of symptoms consistent with the Cuba cases, a distinction that became a source of intense frustration for Lenzi and other China evacuees.

According to Lenzi's account (relayed in the [2020 *New York Times* investigation](https://www.shaheen.senate.gov/news/in-the-news/us-diplomats-and-spies-battle-trump-administration-over-suspected-attacks)), Werner's visiting mother — the Air Force veteran — used a **personal device to record elevated microwave radiation levels** in Werner's apartment. This recording, Lenzi has argued, prompted the State Department to conduct the (admittedly inadequate) technical inspection of the apartment that preceded Werner's evacuation.

### Robyn and Britta Garfield (U.S. Commerce Department, Shanghai)

Robyn Garfield, a U.S. Commerce Department officer stationed in Shanghai, was evacuated in **June 2018** with his wife Britta and their two young children. University of Pennsylvania doctors told Garfield his injuries were similar to those of the Americans in Cuba, according to a [Facebook post he wrote to a U.S. diplomat group in March 2019](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/10/19/us/politics/diplomat-attacks-havana-syndrome.html). But the State Department's medical bureau dismissed his case, attributing his symptoms to a **17-year-old baseball injury** — a characterization that could not explain his wife and children's simultaneous symptoms. He appeared with Werner in the [March 2019 60 Minutes](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/brain-trauma-suffered-by-u-s-diplomats-abroad-could-be-work-of-hostile-foreign-government-60-minutes/) segment, telling Scott Pelley that the State Department's refusal to classify the incident as an attack had "significant impact on our, our life. Our finances. My career as well, likely."

### Other China Cases

According to multiple sources including the [*New York Times*](https://www.shaheen.senate.gov/news/in-the-news/us-diplomats-and-spies-battle-trump-administration-over-suspected-attacks) and the [HAVANA Act press release from Senator Warner's office](https://www.warner.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/bill-to-support-havana-syndrome-victims-unanimously-passes-senate/), at least **15 individuals in China** (including Lenzi) failed the HABIT test and were evacuated. A State Department internal letter acknowledged that 15 people in Guangzhou, Shanghai, and Beijing had symptoms "similar to those" in Cuba but stopped short of granting full Havana Syndrome recognition.

*The Insider*'s March 2024 investigation additionally identifies a **U.S. Commerce Department official stationed in Beijing** who began experiencing strange sounds and head pressure at home in October 2017, just before President Trump's November 2017 visit to China; she was also medevaced. The investigation notes that the State Department's final tally was more than ten evacuations from Guangzhou specifically.

No public source has confirmed a named "Adams family" among the Guangzhou cluster, and this profile notes that reference as unverified.

## 4. Medical Findings

### The HABIT Test

Beginning in mid-2018, the State Department administered its own internal diagnostic tool, the **Havana Acquired Brain Injury Tool (HABIT)**, to diplomats who reported symptoms consistent with AHI. The HABIT was described by the State Department as "a clinical assessment tool designed with clinical researchers to evaluate medical findings associated with directed energy exposure in certain foreign environments." It tests brain function and eye movement.

Lenzi was [administered the HABIT on June 1, 2018](https://www.politico.com/news/2021/10/25/state-department-2018-directed-energy-exposure-517055), more than six months after he had begun reporting symptoms to supervisors. His wife was tested the same day. Both failed. Of 41 diplomats tested (26 from Cuba, 15 from China), all who failed were medically evacuated. Lenzi provided the HABIT disclosure forms to *Politico*, which constituted some of the first documented evidence that the State Department had internally framed the evaluations in terms of "directed energy exposure" years before acknowledging this publicly to Congress.

### Medevac and University of Pennsylvania Evaluation

Lenzi and his family were medically evacuated from Guangzhou on **approximately June 6–7, 2018**, arriving via Los Angeles to Philadelphia. According to [*The New York Times*](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/more-americans-screened-over-mystery-health-issues-in-china), Lenzi himself described his apartment as being in the Canton Place complex and confirmed he had resided in the same tower as the first evacuee.

On **June 20, 2018**, Lenzi enrolled in a University of Pennsylvania research study titled ["Investigational Link Between Uncharacterized Environmental Exposure and Acquired Brain Injury,"](https://www.politico.com/news/2021/10/25/state-department-2018-directed-energy-exposure-517055) led by Dr. Douglas Smith, a neurologist at Penn's Perelman School of Medicine. The study was initially U.S. government-sponsored; by August 24, 2018, sponsorship had shifted to Penn's Department of Neurosurgery. It was designed to run five years and include multiple blood draws and MRI scans.

On **June 22, 2018**, Lenzi received a formal diagnosis of **"acquired brain injury/concussion"** from Dr. Teena Shetty, a neurologist at the Hospital for Special Surgery in New York. Symptoms recorded at diagnosis included light sensitivity, insufficient eye convergence, headaches, lightheadedness, nausea, difficulty sleeping, and memory loss. Shetty recommended physical restrictions including no sports, no strenuous exercise, and reduced reading and screen time. Lenzi told UNH: ["It's been said we're the only known people in human history to receive a diagnosis of traumatic brain injury without a blow to the head."](https://www.unh.edu/unhtoday/2019/11/home-heal)

The [UNH profile from November 2019](https://www.unh.edu/unhtoday/2019/11/home-heal) notes that Lenzi's MRI showed a "precise match between cognitive symptoms and damaged brain regions responsible for those functions," though the specific imaging metrics are not described in publicly available sources.

### The JAMA 2018 and 2019 Studies: Cuba-Focused, China Context

The landmark first JAMA publication — [Swanson et al., JAMA, March 2018](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2673168) — covered 21 U.S. government personnel stationed in Cuba (evaluated between August 2017 and June 2018). It found a "new syndrome" with persistent cognitive, vestibular, oculomotor, auditory, and sleep dysfunction, noting that "the diplomats appear to have sustained injury to widespread brain networks." The cohort included personnel evaluated at Penn's Center for Brain Injury and Repair, but was composed exclusively of Cuba personnel; Lenzi was not part of this first study, having not yet been medevaced or enrolled at the time of data cutoff.

The [Verma et al. JAMA neuroimaging study (July 2019)](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6652163/) covered 40 U.S. government personnel, including personnel evaluated from August 2017 through June 2018 — again Cuba-centric, predating Lenzi's June 20, 2018 enrollment in the broader Penn study. Penn's imaging study found significant differences in whole-brain white matter volume, cerebellar tissue microstructural integrity, and functional connectivity in auditory and visuospatial subnetworks compared to healthy controls. Lenzi enrolled in the Penn five-year study, but sources do not confirm whether his specific imaging data was incorporated into the 2019 JAMA publication.

The UNH profile (November 2019) states that Lenzi and his 14 China colleagues were "part of a UPenn study of more than 40 U.S. diplomats with 'acquired brain injury,'" which suggests the broader study population used in later analyses included China evacuees, but this confirmation does not appear in the published 2018 or 2019 JAMA papers.

### State Department's Contradictory Medical Assessment

In a note sent to Lenzi and revealed publicly during the [March 2019 60 Minutes broadcast](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/brain-trauma-suffered-by-u-s-diplomats-abroad-could-be-work-of-hostile-foreign-government-60-minutes/), the State Department's medical office stated: **"We have reached the decision that your symptoms and findings do not correlate with the Havana cohort."** This contradicted both his Penn diagnosis and the HABIT results. Lenzi's response on screen: "They tried to deny it. They tried to cover it up. They tried to minimize it." The [lawsuit complaint](https://www.courthousenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/lenzi-state-complaint.pdf) documents a State Department physician (Dr. Behzad Shahbazian) who made this determination but later reconsidered and acknowledged similarity.

The [2021 Steptoe law firm press release](https://www.steptoe.com/en/news-publications/Steptoe-Reaches-Settlement-for-Pro-Bono-Client-in-Havana-Syndrome-Disability-Discrimination-and-Retaliation-Case.html) notes that "Government and private physicians determined that Mr. Lenzi had suffered a Traumatic Brain Injury from external exposure."

## 5. State Department Treatment and Institutional Response

### Initial Dismissal and Retaliation

Before the medevac, Lenzi's attempts to alert colleagues and supervisors were met with a pattern of institutional obstruction that he has documented extensively in legal filings and public statements:

- **April 11, 2018**: Catherine Werner (his upstairs neighbor) was medevacked. The State Department [did not inform Lenzi or other consulate personnel](https://www.wgbh.org/news/national/2019-05-14/mark-lenzi-on-the-still-unsolved-silent-attack-on-dozens-of-foreign-diplomats).

- **April 2018**: SEO Rahim Theriot conducted a technical inspection of Werner's apartment using — according to Lenzi's lawsuit — inferior and outdated microwave detection equipment. According to Lenzi, Theriot told him this was a "check-the-box exercise" directed by DS Deputy Assistant Secretary John Fitzsimmons, and that Fitzsimmons preferred the classified report not be filed in the normal State Department system.

- **April 19, 2018**: Lenzi's supervisor Brian Hayes conducted an "urgent official counseling session" — normally reserved for serious misconduct — telling Lenzi he was being "too emotional" about a security equipment issue. This contrasted with Lenzi's years of performance reviews praising his composure.

- **May 23, 2018**: At a consulate town hall, management represented Werner's medevac as an isolated case with no anomalies found. This account was directly contradicted by the technical inspection findings Lenzi had reviewed.

- **May 27, 2018**: Lenzi moved his family out. He sent an [unclassified email to nearly 200 colleagues](https://www.vice.com/en/article/havana-syndrome-vice-podcast/) accusing officials of "an attempted cover-up." This email, which the State Department apparently cleared before he sent it — or which he sent without clearance — triggered a mandatory psychiatric evaluation. The psychiatric report questioned his judgment and noted that "leadership at post wants to make sure a serious mental illness has not been missed."

- **May 29, 2018**: State Department ordered Lenzi to undergo a psychiatric evaluation; the report surfaced concerns that his email warranted examination for a "serious mental illness."

Lenzi told the [*New York Times*](https://www.shaheen.senate.gov/news/in-the-news/us-diplomats-and-spies-battle-trump-administration-over-suspected-attacks): "They didn't find anything, because they didn't want to find anything." And: "This is a deliberate, high-level cover-up. They have hung us out to dry."

### Medevac: June 2018

Lenzi and his family were medevacked in [early June 2018](https://www.steptoe.com/en/news-publications/Steptoe-Reaches-Settlement-for-Pro-Bono-Client-in-Havana-Syndrome-Disability-Discrimination-and-Retaliation-Case.html), days after failing the HABIT tests. His arrival in the United States is consistent with PBS and *New York Times* reporting from June 6–7, 2018, identifying him by name as "security engineering officer Mark A. Lenzi" who had been evacuated with his wife and two children. At the time, he publicly described experiencing "neurological symptoms."

### Post-Medevac Employment Treatment

After diagnosis, Lenzi was assigned **"Overcomplement" (DS/NOC) status** in November 2018 — a designation meant to be temporary — and was assigned to a domestic Government Technical Monitor position in Portsmouth, New Hampshire, in December 2018. This position involved reviewing contractor work orders and overseeing "thousands of dollars" of project labor — a dramatic downgrade from his prior management of multimillion-dollar security upgrade programs.

Despite being granted [medical clearance for overseas posts](https://www.courthousenews.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/12/lenzi-state-complaint.pdf) in September 2019, the State Department repeatedly denied his applications for foreign assignments (including Frankfurt, Belgrade, Athens, and Warsaw). In late 2017, before the injuries manifested, Lenzi had already been selected for an FP-02 Officer in Charge position at U.S. Embassy Baku, Azerbaijan — a promotion that came with Russian-language incentive pay. His medical evacuation prevented him from taking it.

The [Politico October 2021 investigation](https://www.politico.com/news/2021/10/25/state-department-2018-directed-energy-exposure-517055) revealed that in October 2021, the State Department had revoked his **administrative leave without explanation**, forcing him to use sick leave or unpaid leave to attend therapy and medical study appointments. He had accrued 1,248 hours of administrative leave over two and a half years. On the same day the department became aware he was preparing to file a federal lawsuit (September 29, 2021), he was informed he was being reassigned against his wishes to the Field Support Branch — a move he characterized as retaliation timed to the anticipated litigation.

### Workers' Compensation (FECA)

Federal Employees Compensation Act claims are not publicly disclosed in detail. Public reporting does not confirm the specific status of a FECA claim for Lenzi. What is documented is that, as of 2021, China-based AHI personnel were [treated differently from Cuba-based personnel](https://www.shaheen.senate.gov/news/in-the-news/us-diplomats-and-spies-battle-trump-administration-over-suspected-attacks) with respect to administrative leave and rehabilitation support — China officers were required to use sick days while Cuba officers received administrative leave. Lenzi's lawsuit alleged this disparate treatment constituted disability discrimination.

The 2021 HAVANA Act provided compensation authority separate from FECA, and [State Department guidelines published in June 2022](https://www.nytimes.com/2022/06/24/us/politics/havana-syndrome-compensation-payments.html) specified up to $140,475 for confirmed brain injuries and up to $187,300 for those with severe injuries preventing work. As of 2022, no payments had yet been made to State Department employees (unlike the CIA, which began paying under the Act). The [Steptoe settlement in March 2023](https://www.steptoe.com/en/news-publications/Steptoe-Reaches-Settlement-for-Pro-Bono-Client-in-Havana-Syndrome-Disability-Discrimination-and-Retaliation-Case.html) eventually resolved the first lawsuit; combined with HAVANA Act payments, Lenzi has publicly stated he received [more than $1 million total](https://theins.press/en/politics/270425) from the government in civil litigation settlements and HAVANA Act compensation.

## 10. The 2024 NIH/JAMA Reassessment

In **March 2024**, the National Institutes of Health published two papers in JAMA reporting the results of a nearly five-year study of 81–86 AHI patients (recruited between June 2018 and November 2022) from Cuba, China, Austria, and the U.S. The NIH [Clinical Center study](https://www.cbc.ca/news/world/havana-syndrome-nih-study-1.7147124) found **no significant MRI-detectable evidence of brain injury** in AHI patients compared to controls. A second paper found no significant differences in blood, vision, hearing, or cognitive tests except for self-reported measures of imbalance, fatigue, PTSD, and depression. A subset of approximately 28% of patients were diagnosed with persistent postural-perceptual dizziness (PPPD), described as a maladaptive response, possibly linked to inner-ear problems and stress.

Participants and their advocates disputed the study's integrity. According to [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/world/havana-syndrome-study-halted-review-finds-some-patients-coerced) and [Miami Herald](https://www.miamiherald.com/news/nation-world/world/americas/cuba/article286861020.html) reporting, an NIH internal review board found that some participants were coerced to join the study (by parties outside the NIH research team), violating informed consent standards. In September 2024, NIH halted the study, though it maintained the published findings were unaffected.

Tim Bergreen, a lawyer at Hogan Lovells representing some participants, sent a letter to JAMA's editorial board before publication documenting concerns, including that the CIA-provided control group was not adequately matched to the patient population.

A [January 2026 re-analysis](https://ijirms.in/index.php/ijirms/article/view/2152) of the NIH data in the *International Journal of Innovative Research in Medical Science* — focused specifically on the validated AHI1 subcohort (43 patients meeting neuro-vestibular-otolithic criteria) — found reduced functional connectivity in the salience network and trend-level white-matter microstructural abnormalities, arguing these paralleled the 2019 Verma/Penn JAMA findings and "strengthen the evidence that AHI1 phenotype represents a genuine diffuse brain injury syndrome."

Public sources do not confirm whether Lenzi was personally enrolled in the NIH study (he was enrolled in the separate Penn study beginning June 2018). There is no documented public statement by Lenzi specifically about the March 2024 NIH/JAMA findings; however, his broader position — that the U.S. government is actively suppressing evidence of brain injury — is consistent with the criticisms lodged by other participants and advocates.

## 11. The China Attribution Question

### Why Guangzhou Cases Are Geopolitically Distinct

The Guangzhou cluster created a unique political predicament that distinguished it from Cuba cases. When AHI cases emerged in Havana beginning in late 2016, the Trump administration's interest in reversing Obama's Cuba normalization policy aligned with an aggressive response: mass evacuations, expulsion of Cuban diplomats, travel warnings. The administration labeled Cuba a hostile actor even while nominally declining to assign direct blame for the attacks.

China was different. As the [*New York Times* 2020 investigation documented](https://www.shaheen.senate.gov/news/in-the-news/us-diplomats-and-spies-battle-trump-administration-over-suspected-attacks), "China diplomats began reporting strange symptoms in spring 2018, as U.S. officials stationed there were trying to coax their Chinese counterparts into a trade deal that Mr. Trump had promised to deliver. The president was also looking to Beijing for help in clinching nuclear talks with North Korea and consistently lavished praise on Xi Jinping." According to half a dozen U.S. officials, State Department leaders recognized that pursuing the same course as Cuba — mass evacuations and hostile framing — "could cripple diplomatic and economic relationships." The department instead labeled what happened in China as "health incidents," downgraded Pompeo's initial comparable-to-Cuba framing, and identified only Catherine Werner as meeting the full Havana Syndrome criteria.

Lenzi told Scott Pelley in March 2019: "Because it's China, because we have such a large trade relationship with them. You can push around Cuba. Their trade, you know, relations are minimal. With China, that's a different beast." The *NYT* investigation cited "the president's desire to strengthen relations with Russia" and "win a trade deal with China" as twin motivations for suppression.

### The Russia Attribution for China-Based Cases

Although the predominant media narrative about Havana Syndrome attribution centers on GRU Unit 29155 and incidents in Havana, Vienna, and European posts, *The Insider*'s March 2024 investigation directly linked Unit 29155 to the China cases through circumstantial travel evidence. The investigation found that:

- **Alexander Mishkin**, one of the Skripal poisoners, traveled via Beijing in [July 2016](https://theins.press/en/politics/270425), shortly before AHI reports began emerging from U.S. officials in China.
- **Sergey Avdeenko** (Unit 29155) entered China on July 12, 2017, traveling under cover as a car technician with the Silk Way Rally (identified as a GRU front organization), and flew from Xi'an to Novosibirsk on July 23, 2017 — the same summer that Lenzi's and Werner's symptoms began.

The Silk Way Rally, an off-road race running from Russia to China, was identified by *The Insider* as having been used to insert Unit 29155 operatives into China. The investigation does not prove these operatives directly targeted the Guangzhou consulate cluster, but places them in-country during the symptom onset period.

Lenzi has consistently told journalists that Russia — not China — perpetrated the attacks in Guangzhou, and has called his Russia-focused background the likely reason for his targeting. The GRU 29155 narrative is structurally consistent with a Russia perpetrator using China as an operational theater.

### Attribution of the Symptom Onset: "Early 2017" vs. "Late 2017"

Official State Department language, as reported in May–June 2018, described the Guangzhou employee as experiencing symptoms "from late 2017 through April 2018." Lenzi himself gives both "October 2017" (GBH interview) and "November 2017" (60 Minutes 2019, complaint) as onset dates. *The Insider* characterizes the Guangzhou cases as beginning in "summer of 2017" for Werner and Lenzi. Werner's public account places her symptom onset at October 2017. The most defensible synthesis is that symptoms for the primary cluster began in **October–November 2017**, with Lenzi's most severe phase running through spring 2018.
